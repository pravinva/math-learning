The Key Connection: When expanding (a + b)ⁿ, each term a^(n−r)·b^r arises from choosing which r of the n brackets contribute b. The count of those choices is C(n, r) — and Pascal's Triangle geometrically stores all these counts: every cell is the sum of the two directly above it (Pascal's Rule), because C(n,r) = C(n−1,r−1) + C(n−1,r). All three — the triangle cell, the formula result, and the binomial coefficient — are exactly the same number.
