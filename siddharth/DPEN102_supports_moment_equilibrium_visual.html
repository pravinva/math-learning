DPEN102 Visual Guide

Supports, reactions (Rx, Ry), moment, and equilibrium shown as diagrams first, equations second.

Goal: visualize support types Goal: understand unknown reactions Goal: pick a smart moment point

1) Support Types and Their Unknown Reactions

Pin / Hinge Support

Hinge blocks translation in x and y, but allows rotation. Unknowns: Rx and Ry.

Roller Support

Roller gives one normal reaction (usually vertical in basic beams). Unknown: Ry only.

Fixed Support

Fixed support blocks x, y, and rotation. Unknowns: Rx, Ry, and moment M.

Cable Support

Cable can only pull along its own direction. Unknown: cable tension T (single scalar).

2) Free Body Diagram: Where Rx and Ry Come From

3) Deformation Animations: Shear, Rotation, Strain

These are exaggerated so motion is easy to see. In real structures, deformation is usually much smaller.

Shearing Action (γ)

Shear means adjacent layers slide relative to each other.

Rotation (Moment Effect)

A non-zero net moment makes the body rotate about a point/axis.

Normal Strain (ε)

Normal strain is change in length divided by original length.

Shear strain: γ ≈ lateral shift / height   |   Normal strain: ε = ΔL / L

4) What Is a “Smart Point” for Moments?

A smart point is a moment center chosen so unknown reactions through that point disappear from the equation.
