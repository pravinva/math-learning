Week 11 Deep Dive: Composites

Outline mapping: Ch16 §16.1-16.15.

Scaffold

  1. Identify matrix and reinforcement roles.
  2. Use loading direction to choose longitudinal/transverse model.
  3. Check fiber length against critical length for load transfer.
  4. Interpret anisotropy and laminate orientation effects.

Core explanation

Longitudinal modulus for aligned continuous fibers: EL=Ef*Vf+Em*Vm. Transverse modulus is much lower and often approximated by inverse rule. Reinforcement is effective only if fiber length exceeds critical length.

Worked example

Ef=230 GPa, Em=3 GPa, Vf=0.55, Vm=0.45 -> EL=127.85 GPa. This shows how small matrix modulus contributes less in fiber direction.

Quiz (with solutions)

  1. Why can the same composite be stiff in one direction and compliant in another?
  2. What does critical fiber length represent physically?
  3. If Vf increases, what is the expected trend in EL (all else fixed)?
Solution 1

Because reinforcement orientation creates anisotropy: load transfer is strongest when load aligns with stiff fibers.

Solution 2

Minimum fiber length needed for effective stress transfer from matrix to fiber before fiber can approach its potential strength contribution.

Solution 3

EL generally increases because the higher-modulus fiber phase contributes more to rule-of-mixtures term.
