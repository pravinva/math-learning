Week 7 Deep Dive: Polymers Structure and Properties

Outline mapping: Ch14 §14.1-14.12; Ch15 §15.1-15.16.

Scaffold

  1. Classify polymer: thermoplastic, thermoset, elastomer.
  2. Locate service temperature relative to Tg and Tm.
  3. Map molecular structure (linear/branched/crosslinked) to mechanical response.
  4. Explain time-dependent behavior (viscoelasticity).

Core explanation

Polymer behavior is controlled by chain mobility. Below Tg many polymers are glassy and brittle; above Tg they become more compliant. Crystallinity and crosslinking increase stiffness and thermal resistance but can reduce ductility.

Worked example

Given Mn=120,000 g/mol and repeat unit m=100 g/mol, degree of polymerization DPn=Mn/m=1200. Higher DP generally increases entanglement and can raise toughness/viscosity.

Quiz (with solutions)

  1. Why can one polymer grade feel rubbery while another feels rigid at room temperature?
  2. How does crosslinking affect melt processability?
  3. Why are viscoelastic effects important in long-term loading?
Solution 1

Because their Tg values differ relative to room temperature; above Tg is more rubbery, below Tg more glassy.

Solution 2

Heavy crosslinking suppresses melt flow, so thermoset-like behavior limits remolding/reprocessing.

Solution 3

Strain can continue to grow under constant load (creep) and stress can relax under fixed strain, affecting dimensional stability and reliability.
