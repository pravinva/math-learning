Week 5 Deep Dive: Creep, Phase Basics, Thermal Properties

Outline mapping: Ch8 §8.9-8.15; Ch9 §9.1-9.6; Ch19 §19.1-19.4.

Full scaffold

  1. Check whether service temperature is high enough for creep concern.
  2. Identify creep stage controlling design life (usually steady-state).
  3. Define phase/microstructure state before and after thermal exposure.
  4. Compute thermal strain first, then decide whether constraints convert strain to stress.
  5. Evaluate heat-flow direction and magnitude with conductivity equation.

Core equations

Thermal expansion: DeltaL = alpha * L0 * DeltaT
Fourier conduction (1-D): qx = -kA(dT/dx)

Thermal strain exists whenever temperature changes. Thermal stress appears only when expansion/contraction is constrained.

Worked example 1: free expansion

L0=2 m, alpha=12e-6 /C, DeltaT=100 C:

DeltaL = 12e-6 * 2 * 100 = 2.4e-3 m = 2.4 mm.

If bar is free, this is mostly strain without large stress.

Worked example 2: constrained expansion idea

If the same bar is fully constrained, thermal strain cannot occur freely. This creates internal stress. In linear elastic approximation, thermal stress magnitude scales with E*alpha*DeltaT.

Design consequence: joints, bolts, and interfaces need stress-relief features when thermal mismatch exists.

Quiz (with solutions)

  1. Why is secondary creep stage emphasized in long-service design calculations?
  2. A part has high thermal expansion but is mounted with sliding support. What happens to thermal stress trend?
  3. How do phase and microstructure differ, and why does the distinction matter for properties?
Solution 1

Secondary creep approximates stable long-term deformation rate under constant stress/temperature, so it anchors service-life predictions.

Solution 2

Thermal strain is accommodated by sliding support, so thermal stress is greatly reduced compared with fixed-fixed restraint.

Solution 3

Phase is a homogeneous state. Microstructure is how phases are arranged (size, shape, distribution). Same phases with different microstructures can show very different properties.
