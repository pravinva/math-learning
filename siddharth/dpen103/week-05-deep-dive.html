Week 5 Deep Dive: Creep, Phase Basics, Thermal Properties

Outline mapping: Ch8 §8.9-8.15; Ch9 §9.1-9.6; Ch19 §19.1-19.4.

Scaffold

  1. For high-temperature loading, evaluate creep stage behavior.
  2. Use phase vocabulary correctly (phase, microstructure, equilibrium).
  3. Apply thermal expansion and conductivity equations with unit checks.

Core explanation

Creep has primary, secondary, and tertiary stages. Secondary (steady-state) creep rate is often the design parameter for long service. Thermal expansion can induce stress when deformation is constrained. Thermal conductivity captures heat flow response to temperature gradients.

Worked example

A steel bar, L0=2 m, alpha=12e-6 /C, heated by 100 C: free expansion ΔL=alpha*L0*ΔT=2.4 mm. If fully constrained, this expansion translates to thermal stress risk.

Quiz (with solutions)

  1. Why is secondary creep central for life estimation?
  2. What happens to thermal stress if expansion is unconstrained?
  3. Define phase vs microstructure in one sentence each.
Solution 1

Secondary creep approximates stable long-term deformation rate under constant stress/temperature, so it anchors service-life predictions.

Solution 2

If unconstrained, thermal strain occurs with little/no thermal stress (aside from minor gradients or local constraints).

Solution 3

Phase: homogeneous region with uniform composition/structure. Microstructure: spatial arrangement and morphology of one or more phases.
