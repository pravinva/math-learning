Week 10 Deep Dive: Magnetic Properties

Outline mapping: Ch20 §20.1-20.12.

Scaffold

  1. Identify magnetic class: dia/para/ferro/ferri/antiferro.
  2. Interpret B-H loop: remanence Br, coercivity Hc, loop area.
  3. Match application needs: soft magnetic vs hard magnetic materials.

Core explanation

Soft magnetic materials are easy to magnetize/demagnetize and have low hysteresis losses; useful for AC cores. Hard magnetic materials retain magnetization and resist demagnetization; useful for permanent magnets.

Worked example

If two candidate core materials have similar saturation but one has much smaller loop area, that one dissipates less energy per AC cycle and is preferred for transformer cores.

Quiz (with solutions)

  1. Why is low coercivity preferred in transformer steels?
  2. What does large hysteresis loop area mean physically?
  3. Why are permanent magnets designed with high coercivity?
Solution 1

Low coercivity means less field needed for reversal and lower hysteresis losses in cyclic operation.

Solution 2

Large loop area indicates more energy lost per magnetization cycle (heat generation).

Solution 3

High coercivity helps resist external demagnetizing fields and retain useful residual flux.
