Week 6 Deep Dive: Binary Phase Diagrams and Lever Rule

Outline mapping: Ch9 §9.7-9.14.

Scaffold

  1. Locate alloy composition and temperature on phase diagram.
  2. Identify phase region and tie-line endpoints.
  3. Use mass balance to compute phase fractions.
  4. Interpret microstructure development during cooling.

Core explanation

In a two-phase alpha+beta region, tie-line endpoints define phase compositions C_alpha and C_beta at that temperature. Overall composition C0 must satisfy mass conservation, giving the lever rule.

W_alpha=(C_beta-C0)/(C_beta-C_alpha), W_beta=(C0-C_alpha)/(C_beta-C_alpha).

Worked example

If C_alpha=20 wt%, C_beta=80 wt%, C0=35 wt%:

W_alpha=(80-35)/(80-20)=0.75, W_beta=(35-20)/(80-20)=0.25.

Quiz (with solutions)

  1. Why must tie-line endpoints be read at the same temperature as C0?
  2. If C0 moves toward C_beta in alpha+beta region, what happens to W_beta?
  3. What is the most common lever-rule exam error?
Solution 1

Phase compositions are temperature dependent; using another temperature gives wrong C_alpha/C_beta and wrong fractions.

Solution 2

W_beta increases because composition is closer to beta endpoint.

Solution 3

Using wrong boundary points or mixing composition units and temperature line references.
