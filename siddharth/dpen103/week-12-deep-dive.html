Week 12 Deep Dive: Ceramics

Outline mapping: Ch12 §12.2-12.4, §12.8-12.11.

Scaffold

  1. Recognize ceramic bond/structure implications for deformation.
  2. Treat flaw size as a primary strength variable.
  3. Use fracture-toughness logic for allowable stress estimates.
  4. Propose reliability improvements via processing/design.

Core explanation

Ceramics are often stiff and high-temperature capable, but tension performance is flaw-sensitive and brittle. Limited plasticity means crack-tip blunting is weaker than in ductile metals.

Worked example

If flaw size increases by 4x, allowable stress from sigma~1/sqrt(a) drops by half. This is why processing quality and surface integrity strongly affect ceramic reliability.

Quiz (with solutions)

  1. Why are ceramics commonly used in compression-dominant applications?
  2. How can compressive surface treatments improve ceramic reliability?
  3. Name one design strategy to reduce stress concentration in brittle materials.
Solution 1

Because tensile cracks drive brittle failure; compression tends to close cracks and lowers crack-opening driving force.

Solution 2

Compressive surface stress counteracts applied tensile stress at flaws, increasing effective crack-opening threshold.

Solution 3

Use generous fillet radii/smoother geometry transitions to reduce local stress intensification.
