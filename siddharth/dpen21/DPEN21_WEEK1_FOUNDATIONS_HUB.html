Section 2 — Lesson Blocks

Lesson A (Week 1A): Algebra Basics + Substitution + Simplification

  • Variables represent unknown numbers; same letter means same value throughout one expression/equation.
  • Expression vs equation, terms/coefficient/constant, and like terms recognition.
  • Substitution routine: bracket negative values, apply order of operations, then simplify.
  • Simplification with like terms and sign discipline: the minus sign belongs to the term immediately after it.
  • Multiplication/division conventions with variables and fractions.

Lesson B (Week 1B): Expansion Techniques + Pascal Triangle

  • Distributive law once and twice: \((a+b)(c+d)=ac+ad+bc+bd\).
  • Binomial squares and difference-of-squares expansions.
  • Further expansions with 3+ factors/terms using repeated distribution.
  • Pascal row selection and power-tracking for \((a+b)^n\) and \((a-b)^n\).

Lesson C (Week 1C): Factorisation Core Methods

  • Reverse distributive law: always check HCF first.
  • Simple and compound common factors, including bracket common factors.
  • Difference of two squares: \(a^2-b^2=(a-b)(a+b)\).
  • Sum/difference of cubes with SOAP sign memory.

Lesson D (Week 1D): Quadratic Factorisation

  • Monic trinomials: find two numbers with correct sum/product.
  • Non-monic trinomials: try HCF first, then splitting/grouping methods.
  • Mixed strategy flow: HCF -> special products -> trinomial -> grouping.
