Section B — Long Answer

  1. \(m=\frac{-6-2}{1-(-3)}=-2\). Using \((1,-6)\): \(-6=-2(1)+b\Rightarrow b=-4\). So \(y=-2x-4\).
  2. \(3(4)-2a=12\Rightarrow 12-2a=12\Rightarrow a=0\).
  3. \(4x-2y+7=0\Rightarrow y=2x+\tfrac72\), slope \(2\); perpendicular slope \(-\tfrac12\). Through \((2,5)\): \(y-5=-\tfrac12(x-2)\Rightarrow x+2y-12=0\).
  4. Add: \(8x=24\Rightarrow x=3\); then \(9+2y=16\Rightarrow y=\tfrac72\).
  5. \(g(-2)=(-2)^2+3=7\Rightarrow f(7)=2(7)-1=13\). \((g\circ f)(x)=g(2x-1)=(2x-1)^2+3=4x^2-4x+4\).
  6. \(f(-x)=(-x)^3-4(-x)=-x^3+4x=-(x^3-4x)=-f(x)\). Odd.
  7. \(y=-x^2+4x-3\): vertex \(x=2,\ y=1\Rightarrow(2,1)\); x-intercepts \(x=1,3\); y-intercept \((0,-3)\).
  8. \(x^2-5x+6=(x-2)(x-3)=0\Rightarrow x=2,3\). \(\Delta=25-24=1>0\) (and a perfect square) — two distinct rational roots.
  9. \(x^2+y^2-6x+8y+9=0\Rightarrow (x-3)^2+(y+4)^2=16\). So centre \((3,-4)\), radius \(4\). Intercepts: x-axis (\(y=0\)) gives \((x-3)^2=0\Rightarrow (3,0)\) (tangent). y-axis (\(x=0\)) gives \(9+(y+4)^2=16\Rightarrow y=-4\pm\sqrt7\). Sketch below.
  10. \(y=2|x-1|-3\): vertex \((1,-3)\). x-intercepts from \(2|x-1|-3=0\Rightarrow |x-1|=\frac32\Rightarrow x=-\frac12,\ \frac52\). y-intercept: \(x=0\Rightarrow y=-1\). Sketch below.
  11. \(3^{x+1}=20\Rightarrow x+1=\log_3 20=\frac{\ln 20}{\ln 3}\Rightarrow x=\frac{\ln 20}{\ln 3}-1\approx1.727\).
  12. \(\log_{3}81=4\) (since \(3^4=81\)).
  13. \(\ln[(x-2)(x+1)]=\ln 10\Rightarrow x^2-x-2=10\Rightarrow x^2-x-12=0\Rightarrow (x-4)(x+3)=0\). Domain requires \(x>2\), so \(x=4\) (reject \(x=-3\)).
  14. \(y=-x^2+4x-3\): vertex \((2,1)\), x-intercepts \(1,3\), y-intercept \((0,-3)\), opens downward. Sketch below.
  15. \(y=e^{-x}+1\): y-intercept \((0,2)\); horizontal asymptote \(y=1\); decreasing. Sketch below.
  16. \(y=\ln(2x-4)\): x-intercept where \(2x-4=1\Rightarrow x=2.5\); vertical asymptote where \(2x-4=0\Rightarrow x=2\). Sketch below.
