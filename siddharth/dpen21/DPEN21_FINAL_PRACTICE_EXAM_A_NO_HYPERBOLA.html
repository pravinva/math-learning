DPEN21 Final Practice Exam A (No Hyperbola)

Time allowed: 2 hours | Total: 50 marks | 28 questions | Calculator allowed
No hyperbola questions included. Worked solutions are in a separate file: Exam A Worked Answers.

Section A — Multiple Choice (12 marks)

  1. Find the gradient of the line through \((2,-1)\) and \((8,11)\).
    (A) \(2\)   (B) \(1\)   (C) \(\tfrac12\)   (D) \(-2\)
  2. The graph \(x=-3\) is:
    (A) horizontal line   (B) vertical line   (C) parabola   (D) circle
  3. Find the domain and range of \(y=\sqrt{x+1}-2\).
    (A) D: \(x\ge -1\), R: \(y\ge -2\)   (B) D: \(x\gt -1\), R: \(y\ge -2\)   (C) D: \(x\ge 1\), R: \(y\ge 2\)   (D) D: all \(x\), R: all \(y\)
  4. For \(f(x)=\begin{cases}x+2,&x<0\\x^2-1,&x\ge0\end{cases}\), evaluate \(f(-3)+2f(2)\).
    (A) \(5\)   (B) \(7\)   (C) \(9\)   (D) \(3\)
  5. Find the turning point of \(y=3x^2-12x+7\).
    (A) \((2,-5)\)   (B) \((-2,-5)\)   (C) \((2,5)\)   (D) \((1,-2)\)
  6. Which equation has x-intercepts at \(-2\) and \(5\)?
    (A) \((x+2)(x-5)\)   (B) \((x-2)(x+5)\)   (C) \((x+2)(x+5)\)   (D) \((x-2)(x-5)\)
  7. Write the equation of the absolute-value graph with vertex \((1,-4)\), opening upward with scale \(2\).
    (A) \(2|x-1|-4\)   (B) \(|x-1|-4\)   (C) \(2|x+1|-4\)   (D) \(-2|x-1|-4\)
  8. Circle with centre \((2,-3)\), radius \(4\): write standard form equation.
    (A) \((x-2)^2+(y+3)^2=16\)   (B) \((x+2)^2+(y-3)^2=16\)   (C) \((x-2)^2+(y-3)^2=4\)   (D) \((x+2)^2+(y+3)^2=4\)
  9. Simplify to one logarithm: \(\log_a x + 2\log_a y - \log_a z\).
    (A) \(\log_a\!\left(\frac{xy^2}{z}\right)\)   (B) \(\log_a(xy^2z)\)   (C) \(\log_a\!\left(\frac{xz}{y^2}\right)\)   (D) \(\log_a\!\left(\frac{y^2}{xz}\right)\)
  10. Solve \(\log_{10}(x-1)=2\).
    (A) \(x=101\)   (B) \(x=99\)   (C) \(x=100\)   (D) \(x=10\)
  11. If \(g(x)=e^x\), write the equation after shifting 2 left and 3 down.
    (A) \(y=e^{x+2}-3\)   (B) \(y=e^{x-2}-3\)   (C) \(y=e^{x+2}+3\)   (D) \(y=e^{-x+2}-3\)
  12. Find x-intercept of \(y=\ln(x+4)-1\).
    (A) \(x=e-4\)   (B) \(x=1-4\)   (C) \(x=4-e\)   (D) \(x=e+4\)

Section B — Long Answer (Questions 13–28, 38 marks)

  1. [2] Find equation of the line through \((-1,4)\) and \((3,-2)\) in gradient-intercept form.
  2. [2] The line with equation \(2x+3y=7\) passes through the point \((2,a)\). Find the value of \(a\).
  3. [3] Find equation of line through \((2,5)\) perpendicular to \(4x-2y+7=0\). Leave in general form.
  4. [2] Solve simultaneously: \[ \begin{cases} 3x+2y=17\\ 5x-2y=7 \end{cases} \]
  5. [2] Given \(f(x)=2x-1,\ g(x)=x^2+3\), find \(f(g(-2))\) and \((g\circ f)(x)\).
  6. [2] State whether \(f(x)=x^4-2x^2\) is odd, even, or neither (show check).
  7. [3] For \(y=-x^2+6x-5\), find vertex, x-intercepts, and y-intercept.
  8. [2] Solve \(x^2-7x+12=0\) and state nature of roots using discriminant.
  9. [3] Sketch the circle \(x^2+y^2-6x+8y+9=0\). Show centre, radius, and axis intercepts.
  10. [2] Sketch \(y=2|x-1|-4\). Show vertex, x-intercepts and y-intercept.
  11. [3] A culture starts with \(150\) cells and doubles every \(40\) minutes, so \(N=150\cdot 2^{t/40}\). Find \(t\) when \(N=2400\).
  12. [2] Evaluate \(\log_{4}\!\left(\frac1{16}\right)\).
  13. [2] Solve \(\ln(2x-1)+\ln(x+3)=\ln 20\), including domain restriction.
  14. [3] Sketch \(y=-x^2+6x-5\). Show the vertex, intercepts and shape.
  15. [3] Sketch \(y=2e^{-x}-1\): y-intercept, horizontal asymptote, and one extra point.
  16. [2] Sketch \(y=\ln(x-2)+1\). Find x-intercept and vertical asymptote.
