DPEN21 Final Practice Exam D (No Hyperbola)

Time allowed: 2 hours | Total: 48 marks | Calculator allowed
No hyperbola questions included. Worked solutions are in a separate file: Exam D Worked Answers.

Section A — Multiple Choice (12 marks)

  1. Find the gradient of the line through \((2,-4)\) and \((5,2)\).
  2. The graph \(x=1\) is: (A) horizontal line (B) vertical line (C) parabola (D) circle.
  3. Find the domain and range of \(y=\sqrt{x+3}-1\).
  4. For \(f(x)=\begin{cases}-x,&x<1\\2x-1,&x\ge1\end{cases}\), evaluate \(f(-2)+f(3)\).
  5. Find the turning point of \(y=x^2-6x+5\).
  6. Which equation has x-intercepts at \(1\) and \(6\)?
  7. Write the equation of an absolute-value graph with vertex \((-1,2)\), opening downward with scale 1.
  8. Circle with centre \((3,-1)\), radius \(2\): write the standard-form equation.
  9. Simplify to one logarithm: \(3\log_a x + \log_a y - 2\log_a z\).
  10. Solve \(\log_{10}(2x)=2\).
  11. If \(g(x)=e^x\), write the equation after shifting 3 right and reflecting in the x-axis.
  12. Find the x-intercept of \(y=\ln(x-3)\).

Section B — Long Answer (36 marks)

  1. [3] Find the equation of the line through \((1,2)\) and \((5,10)\) in gradient-intercept form.
  2. [3] Find the equation of the line through \((4,3)\) perpendicular to \(2x-y+1=0\).
  3. [3] Solve simultaneously: \[ \begin{cases} x+y=7\\ 2x-y=5 \end{cases} \]
  4. [3] State whether \(f(x)=x^2+1\) is odd, even, or neither (show the check).
  5. [3] Given \(f(x)=x+3,\ g(x)=2x^2\), find \(f(g(-1))\) and \((g\circ f)(x)\).
  6. [4] For \(y=x^2-2x-8\), find the vertex, x-intercepts, y-intercept, and sketch the key features.
  7. [3] Solve \(x^2+4x+4=0\) and state the nature of the roots using the discriminant.
  8. [3] Find the centre and radius of \(x^2+y^2+2x-4y-4=0\).
  9. [3] Find the intercepts of the circle in Q20.
  10. [4] Solve \(5^{x}=40\) for \(x\) (3 d.p.).
  11. [4] Solve \(\ln x+\ln(x-3)=\ln 4\), including the domain restriction.
  12. [3] Sketch \(y=e^{-x}-1\): y-intercept, horizontal asymptote, and one extra point.
