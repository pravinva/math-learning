DPEN21 Final Practice Exam D (No Hyperbola)

Time allowed: 2 hours | Total: 50 marks | 28 questions | Calculator allowed
Questions 1–12 are multiple choice (1 mark each). Questions 13–28 are long answer; the value of each is indicated. No hyperbola questions included. Worked solutions are in a separate file: Exam D Worked Answers.

Section A — Multiple Choice (Questions 1–12, 1 mark each = 12 marks)

  1. Find the gradient of the line through \((-3,-5)\) and \((3,7)\).
    (A) \(2\)   (B) \(-2\)   (C) \(\tfrac12\)   (D) \(-\tfrac12\)
  2. The graph \(x=-2\) is:
    (A) sloping line   (B) horizontal line   (C) vertical line   (D) circle
  3. Find the domain and range of \(y=-\sqrt{x-1}+2\).
    (A) D: \(x\ge 1\), R: \(y\le 2\)   (B) D: \(x\ge 1\), R: \(y\ge 2\)   (C) D: \(x\ge -1\), R: \(y\le 2\)   (D) D: all \(x\), R: all \(y\)
  4. For \(f(x)=\begin{cases}-x,&x\le 0\\x^2+1,&x>0\end{cases}\), evaluate \(f(-3)+f(2)\).
    (A) \(6\)   (B) \(8\)   (C) \(10\)   (D) \(5\)
  5. The graph of \(y=x^2+6x+5\) has a turning point at:
    (A) \((-3,-4)\)   (B) \((3,-4)\)   (C) \((-3,4)\)   (D) \((-6,5)\)
  6. Which equation has x-intercepts at \(-5\) and \(2\)?
    (A) \((x-5)(x+2)\)   (B) \((x+5)(x-2)\)   (C) \((x+5)(x+2)\)   (D) \((x-5)(x-2)\)
  7. Which equation describes an absolute-value graph with vertex \((-1,3)\), opening downward, scale 1?
    (A) \(-|x+1|+3\)   (B) \(|x+1|+3\)   (C) \(-|x-1|+3\)   (D) \(-|x+1|-3\)
  8. What is the equation of the circle with centre \((4,-2)\) and radius \(3\)?
    (A) \((x-4)^2+(y+2)^2=9\)   (B) \((x+4)^2+(y-2)^2=9\)   (C) \((x-4)^2+(y+2)^2=3\)   (D) \((x-4)^2+(y-2)^2=9\)
  9. Expressed as a single logarithm, \(2\log_a x + \log_a y - 3\log_a z\) equals:
    (A) \(\log_a\frac{x^2 y}{z^3}\)   (B) \(\log_a\frac{x^2 z^3}{y}\)   (C) \(\log_a\frac{x^2 y}{z}\)   (D) \(\log_a(x^2 y z^3)\)
  10. Solve \(\log_2(x-1)=3\).
    (A) \(x=7\)   (B) \(x=9\)   (C) \(x=4\)   (D) \(x=6\)
  11. The graph of \(g(x)\) is obtained by reflecting \(f(x)=e^x\) in the x-axis and shifting two units up. Find \(g(x)\).
    (A) \(e^{-x}+2\)   (B) \(-e^{x}+2\)   (C) \(-e^{x}-2\)   (D) \(e^{x}+2\)
  12. Find the x-intercept of \(y=2^{x}-8\).
    (A) \(x=3\)   (B) \(x=4\)   (C) \(x=2\)   (D) \(x=8\)

Section B — Long Answer (Questions 13–28, 38 marks)

  1. [2] Find the equation of the line through \((-3,-5)\) and \((3,7)\) in gradient-intercept form.
  2. [2] The line with equation \(2x+y=10\) passes through the point \((a,4)\). Find the value of \(a\).
  3. [3] Find the equation of the line through \((3,2)\) perpendicular to \(3x+y-6=0\). Leave your answer in general form.
  4. [2] Solve simultaneously, showing all working: \[ \begin{cases} x+2y=11\\ 3x-y=5 \end{cases} \]
  5. [2] Given \(f(x)=3x+2\) and \(g(x)=x^2-1\), evaluate \(f(g(3))\) and find \((f\circ g)(x)\).
  6. [2] Is \(f(x)=2x^4-x^2\) odd, even, or neither? Show all working.
  7. [3] For \(y=x^2-2x-3\), find the vertex, x-intercepts and y-intercept.
  8. [2] Find the value(s) of \(k\) for which \(x^2+kx+9=0\) has exactly one (repeated) root.
  9. [3] Sketch the circle \(x^2+y^2+4x-6y-3=0\). Show centre, radius, and axis intercepts.
  10. [2] Sketch \(y=-|x+2|+4\). Show vertex, x-intercepts and y-intercept.
  11. [3] Evaluate \(\log_5 125\), and hence solve \(5^{2t}=125\) for \(t\).
  12. [2] A colony of bacteria satisfies \(P=500\cdot 2^{t}\), where \(t\) is in hours. Find the time \(t\) at which \(P=4000\).
  13. [2] Solve \(\ln(x+3)+\ln(x-1)=\ln 12\), including any domain restriction.
  14. [3] Sketch \(y=x^2-2x-3\). Show the vertex, intercepts and shape.
  15. [3] Sketch \(y=-e^{x}+2\). Find the y-intercept and write the equation of the horizontal asymptote.
  16. [2] Sketch \(y=\ln(x+1)\). Find the x-intercept and write the equation of the vertical asymptote.
