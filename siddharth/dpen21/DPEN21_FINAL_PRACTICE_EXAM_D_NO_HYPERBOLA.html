DPEN21 Final Practice Exam D (No Hyperbola)

Time allowed: 2 hours | Total: 50 marks | 28 questions | Calculator allowed
Questions 1–12 are multiple choice (1 mark each). Questions 13–28 are long answer; the value of each is indicated. No hyperbola questions included. Worked solutions are in a separate file: Exam D Worked Answers.

Section A — Multiple Choice (Questions 1–12, 1 mark each = 12 marks)

  1. Find the gradient of the line through \((2,-4)\) and \((5,2)\).
    (A) \(2\)   (B) \(-2\)   (C) \(\tfrac12\)   (D) \(-\tfrac12\)
  2. The graph \(x=1\) is:
    (A) sloping line   (B) horizontal line   (C) vertical line   (D) parabola
  3. Find the domain and range of \(y=\sqrt{x+3}-1\).
    (A) D: \(x\ge 3\), R: \(y\ge -1\)   (B) D: \(x\ge -3\), R: \(y\ge -1\)   (C) D: \(x\ge -3\), R: \(y\ge 1\)   (D) D: all \(x\), R: all \(y\)
  4. For \(f(x)=\begin{cases}-x,&x<1\\2x-1,&x\ge1\end{cases}\), evaluate \(f(-2)+f(3)\).
    (A) \(5\)   (B) \(7\)   (C) \(3\)   (D) \(9\)
  5. The graph of \(y=x^2-6x+5\) has a turning point at:
    (A) \((3,4)\)   (B) \((-3,-4)\)   (C) \((3,-4)\)   (D) \((6,-4)\)
  6. Which equation has x-intercepts at \(1\) and \(6\)?
    (A) \((x-1)(x-6)\)   (B) \((x+1)(x+6)\)   (C) \((x-1)(x+6)\)   (D) \((x+1)(x-6)\)
  7. Which equation describes an absolute-value graph with vertex \((-1,2)\), opening downward, scale 1?
    (A) \(|x+1|+2\)   (B) \(-|x+1|+2\)   (C) \(-|x-1|+2\)   (D) \(-|x+1|-2\)
  8. What is the equation of the circle with centre \((3,-1)\) and radius \(2\)?
    (A) \((x-3)^2+(y+1)^2=4\)   (B) \((x+3)^2+(y-1)^2=4\)   (C) \((x-3)^2+(y+1)^2=2\)   (D) \((x-3)^2+(y-1)^2=4\)
  9. Expressed as a single logarithm, \(3\log_a x + \log_a y - 2\log_a z\) equals:
    (A) \(\log_a\frac{x^3 z^2}{y}\)   (B) \(\log_a\frac{x^3 y}{z^2}\)   (C) \(\log_a\frac{x^3 y}{z}\)   (D) \(\log_a(x^3 yz^2)\)
  10. Solve \(\log_{10}(2x)=2\).
    (A) \(x=5\)   (B) \(x=100\)   (C) \(x=50\)   (D) \(x=25\)
  11. The graph of \(g(x)\) is obtained by shifting \(f(x)=e^x\) three units right and reflecting in the x-axis. Find \(g(x)\).
    (A) \(-e^{x-3}\)   (B) \(e^{x-3}\)   (C) \(-e^{x+3}\)   (D) \(e^{x-3}+3\)
  12. Find the x-intercept of \(y=\ln(x-3)\).
    (A) \(x=3\)   (B) \(x=1\)   (C) \(x=4\)   (D) \(x=e\)

Section B — Long Answer (Questions 13–28, 38 marks)

  1. [2] Find the equation of the line through \((1,2)\) and \((5,10)\) in gradient-intercept form.
  2. [2] The line with equation \(y+4x=14\) passes through the point \((2,a)\). Find the value of \(a\).
  3. [3] Find the equation of the line through \((4,3)\) perpendicular to \(y-2x=1\). Leave your answer in general form.
  4. [2] Solve simultaneously, showing all working: \[ \begin{cases} x+y=7\\ 2x-y=5 \end{cases} \]
  5. [2] Determine the domain and range of \(y=\dfrac{1}{x+1}\), in interval/set notation.
  6. [2] Is \(f(x)=x^2+1\) odd, even, or neither? Show all working.
  7. [2] Given \(g(x)=\dfrac{1}{x^2-x}\) and \(h(x)=2x\), evaluate \(g(h(-2))\).
  8. [2] Find all values of \(a\) for which \(x^2+ax+25=0\) has exactly one real root.
  9. [3] Graph \(y=-x^2+9\). Show all important features including intercepts and vertex.
  10. [2] Sketch the graph of \(y=|x-2|+1\). Show all important features.
  11. [3] Find the centre and radius of the circle \(x^2+y^2-4x=5\).
  12. [2] Sketch the circle \(x^2+y^2-4x=5\) and find its intercepts.
  13. [2] Evaluate \(\log_{2} 64\).
  14. [3] Solve \(2e^{x-3}+1=9\) for \(x\), giving your answer to 3 decimal places.
  15. [3] Sketch \(y=\ln(x-1)\). Find the x-intercept and write the equation of the vertical asymptote.
  16. [3] Sketch \(y=e^{x}+3\). Find the y-intercept and write the equation of the horizontal asymptote.
