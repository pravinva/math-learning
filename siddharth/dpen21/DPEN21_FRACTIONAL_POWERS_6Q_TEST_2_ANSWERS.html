DPEN21 Fractional Powers Mini Test 2 - Worked Answers

  1. \(5+(3a+2)^{2/5}=14\Rightarrow (3a+2)^{2/5}=9\).
    \((\sqrt[5]{3a+2})^2=9\Rightarrow \sqrt[5]{3a+2}=\pm3\) (even numerator gives two cases).
    \(3a+2=(\pm3)^5=\pm243\Rightarrow 3a=241\) or \(3a=-245\).
    \(\therefore a=\frac{241}{3}\) or \(a=-\frac{245}{3}\).
    Correct option: (C).
  2. \(2(x+4)^{3/4}+1=17\Rightarrow 2(x+4)^{3/4}=16\Rightarrow (x+4)^{3/4}=8\).
    \((\sqrt[4]{x+4})^3=8\Rightarrow \sqrt[4]{x+4}=2\Rightarrow x+4=16\Rightarrow x=12\).
    Correct option: (B).
  3. \((2y-1)^{4/3}-7=74\Rightarrow (2y-1)^{4/3}=81\).
    \((\sqrt[3]{2y-1})^4=81\Rightarrow \sqrt[3]{2y-1}=\pm3\).
    \(2y-1=(\pm3)^3=\pm27\Rightarrow y=14\) or \(y=-13\).
    Correct option: (C).
  4. \(4(m+1)^{1/3}-2=10\Rightarrow 4(m+1)^{1/3}=12\Rightarrow (m+1)^{1/3}=3\).
    \(m+1=3^3=27\Rightarrow m=26\).
    Correct option: (B).
  5. \((p-5)^{5/2}=1024\Rightarrow (\sqrt{p-5})^5=1024=4^5\).
    \(\sqrt{p-5}=4\Rightarrow p-5=16\Rightarrow p=21\).
    (Single real solution here due to odd numerator and square-root domain.)
    Correct option: (C).
  6. \(3(t+6)^{2/3}+4=79\Rightarrow 3(t+6)^{2/3}=75\Rightarrow (t+6)^{2/3}=25\).
    \((\sqrt[3]{t+6})^2=25\Rightarrow \sqrt[3]{t+6}=\pm5\).
    \(t+6=(\pm5)^3=\pm125\Rightarrow t=119\) or \(t=-131\).
    Correct option: (C).
