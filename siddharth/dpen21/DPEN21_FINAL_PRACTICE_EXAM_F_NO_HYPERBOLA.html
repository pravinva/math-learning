DPEN21 Final Practice Exam F (No Hyperbola)

Time allowed: 2 hours | Total: 50 marks | 28 questions | Calculator allowed
Questions 1–12 are multiple choice (1 mark each). Questions 13–28 are long answer; the value of each is indicated. No hyperbola questions included. This paper is the most challenging in the set. Worked solutions are in a separate file: Exam F Worked Answers.

Section A — Multiple Choice (Questions 1–12, 1 mark each = 12 marks)

  1. Find the gradient of the line through \((-4,3)\) and \((2,-9)\).
    (A) \(2\)   (B) \(-2\)   (C) \(-\tfrac12\)   (D) \(\tfrac12\)
  2. The graph \(x=0\) is:
    (A) the x-axis   (B) the y-axis   (C) a parabola   (D) a circle
  3. Find the domain and range of \(y=2-\sqrt{4-x}\).
    (A) D: \(x\le 4\), R: \(y\le 2\)   (B) D: \(x\ge 4\), R: \(y\le 2\)   (C) D: \(x\le 4\), R: \(y\ge 2\)   (D) D: all \(x\), R: all \(y\)
  4. For \(f(x)=\begin{cases}2x+3,&x<-1\\1-x^2,&x\ge-1\end{cases}\), evaluate \(f(-3)+f(1)\).
    (A) \(-3\)   (B) \(3\)   (C) \(0\)   (D) \(-1\)
  5. The graph of \(y=-x^2+2x+3\) has a turning point at:
    (A) \((1,4)\)   (B) \((-1,4)\)   (C) \((1,-4)\)   (D) \((2,3)\)
  6. Which equation has a double root at \(x=3\)?
    (A) \((x-3)^2\)   (B) \((x+3)^2\)   (C) \((x-3)(x+3)\)   (D) \(x^2-9\)
  7. Which equation describes an absolute-value graph with vertex \((-2,-1)\), opening downward, scale 2?
    (A) \(-2|x+2|-1\)   (B) \(2|x+2|-1\)   (C) \(-2|x-2|-1\)   (D) \(-|x+2|-1\)
  8. A circle has equation \((x-2)^2+(y+3)^2=4\). Its centre and radius are:
    (A) centre \((2,-3)\), \(r=2\)   (B) centre \((-2,3)\), \(r=2\)   (C) centre \((2,-3)\), \(r=4\)   (D) centre \((2,3)\), \(r=2\)
  9. Expressed as a single logarithm, \(3\log_a x - \tfrac12\log_a y\) equals:
    (A) \(\log_a\frac{x^3}{\sqrt{y}}\)   (B) \(\log_a\frac{x^3}{y^2}\)   (C) \(\log_a\frac{\sqrt{y}}{x^3}\)   (D) \(\log_a(x^3\sqrt{y})\)
  10. Solve \(\log_4 x = \tfrac32\).
    (A) \(x=6\)   (B) \(x=8\)   (C) \(x=12\)   (D) \(x=64\)
  11. The graph of \(g(x)\) is obtained by reflecting \(f(x)=e^x\) in the y-axis and then scaling vertically by 2. Find \(g(x)\).
    (A) \(2e^{-x}\)   (B) \(2e^{x}\)   (C) \(e^{-2x}\)   (D) \(-2e^{x}\)
  12. Find the x-intercept of \(y=e^{x}-e^{2}\).
    (A) \(x=2\)   (B) \(x=e^2\)   (C) \(x=e\)   (D) \(x=\ln 2\)

Section B — Long Answer (Questions 13–28, 38 marks)

  1. [2] Find the equation of the line through \((5,-2)\) and \((-1,4)\) in gradient-intercept form.
  2. [2] Find the distance between the points \((-1,2)\) and \((4,14)\).
  3. [3] Find the equation of the line through \((2,-1)\) parallel to \(3x-y+5=0\). Leave your answer in general form.
  4. [3] Solve simultaneously, showing all working: \[ \begin{cases} y=x^2\\ y=2x+3 \end{cases} \]
  5. [2] Given \(f(x)=\sqrt{x}\) and \(g(x)=x^2+4\), evaluate \(f(g(0))\) and find \((g\circ f)(x)\), stating its domain.
  6. [2] Is \(f(x)=x^2-|x|\) odd, even, or neither? Show all working.
  7. [3] For \(y=3x^2-12x+7\), find the vertex, and state whether it is a maximum or minimum.
  8. [2] Find the values of \(k\) for which \(x^2-kx+4=0\) has two distinct real roots.
  9. [3] Sketch the circle \(x^2+y^2-4x+6y+9=0\). Show centre, radius, and axis intercepts.
  10. [2] Sketch \(y=-2|x-1|+5\). Show vertex, x-intercepts and y-intercept.
  11. [2] Evaluate \(\log_2 8 + \log_2 2 - \log_2 4\), showing each step.
  12. [2] A radioactive sample decays according to \(N=200(0.5)^{t}\), where \(t\) is in days. Find the time \(t\) at which \(N=25\).
  13. [2] Solve \(\ln x + \ln(x-3)=\ln 4\), including any domain restriction.
  14. [3] Sketch \(y=-x^2+2x+3\). Show the vertex, intercepts and shape.
  15. [3] Sketch \(y=2e^{-x}\). Find the y-intercept and write the equation of the horizontal asymptote.
  16. [2] Sketch \(y=\ln x + 2\). Find the x-intercept and write the equation of the vertical asymptote.
