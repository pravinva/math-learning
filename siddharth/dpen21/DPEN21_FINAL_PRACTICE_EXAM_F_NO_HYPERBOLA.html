DPEN21 Final Practice Exam F (No Hyperbola)

Time allowed: 2 hours | Total: 48 marks | Calculator allowed
No hyperbola questions included. Worked solutions are in a separate file: Exam F Worked Answers.

Section A — Multiple Choice (12 marks)

  1. Find the gradient of the line through \((1,-5)\) and \((4,1)\).
  2. The graph \(x=-2\) is: (A) horizontal line (B) vertical line (C) parabola (D) circle.
  3. Find the domain and range of \(y=\sqrt{x}-3\).
  4. For \(f(x)=\begin{cases}x+4,&x<-1\\x^2-1,&x\ge-1\end{cases}\), evaluate \(f(-3)+f(2)\).
  5. Find the turning point of \(y=x^2+4x+1\).
  6. Which equation has x-intercepts at \(-1\) and \(3\)?
  7. Write the equation of an absolute-value graph with vertex \((3,0)\), opening upward with scale 2.
  8. Circle with centre \((-3,1)\), radius \(2\): write the standard-form equation.
  9. Simplify to one logarithm: \(\log_a x - 2\log_a y + \tfrac12\log_a z\).
  10. Solve \(\log_{10}x=3\).
  11. If \(g(x)=e^x\), write the equation after shifting 1 left and 4 down.
  12. Find the x-intercept of \(y=\ln(x-1)-1\).

Section B — Long Answer (36 marks)

  1. [3] Find the equation of the line through \((2,-3)\) and \((6,5)\) in gradient-intercept form.
  2. [3] Find the equation of the line through \((5,2)\) perpendicular to \(5x+y-3=0\).
  3. [3] Solve simultaneously: \[ \begin{cases} 4x+y=14\\ 2x-y=4 \end{cases} \]
  4. [3] State whether \(f(x)=x^4+1\) is odd, even, or neither (show the check).
  5. [3] Given \(f(x)=x-2,\ g(x)=x^2\), find \(f(g(3))\) and \((g\circ f)(x)\).
  6. [4] For \(y=x^2+2x-3\), find the vertex, x-intercepts, y-intercept, and sketch the key features.
  7. [3] Solve \(x^2-6x+9=0\) and state the nature of the roots using the discriminant.
  8. [3] Find the centre and radius of \(x^2+y^2+6x-2y+6=0\).
  9. [3] Find the intercepts of the circle in Q20.
  10. [4] Solve \(3^{2x}=50\) for \(x\) (3 d.p.).
  11. [4] Solve \(\ln x+\ln(x+3)=\ln 10\), including the domain restriction.
  12. [3] Sketch \(y=e^{x+1}-2\): y-intercept, horizontal asymptote, and one extra point.
