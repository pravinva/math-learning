DPEN21 Final Practice Exam F (No Hyperbola)

Time allowed: 2 hours | Total: 50 marks | 28 questions | Calculator allowed
Questions 1–12 are multiple choice (1 mark each). Questions 13–28 are long answer; the value of each is indicated. No hyperbola questions included. Worked solutions are in a separate file: Exam F Worked Answers.

Section A — Multiple Choice (Questions 1–12, 1 mark each = 12 marks)

  1. Find the gradient of the line through \((2,-3)\) and \((6,5)\).
    (A) \(2\)   (B) \(-2\)   (C) \(\tfrac12\)   (D) \(-\tfrac12\)
  2. The graph \(y=-1\) is:
    (A) sloping line   (B) horizontal line   (C) vertical line   (D) parabola
  3. Find the domain and range of \(y=\sqrt{x-1}+3\).
    (A) D: \(x\ge -1\), R: \(y\ge 3\)   (B) D: \(x\ge 1\), R: \(y\ge 3\)   (C) D: \(x\ge 1\), R: \(y\ge -3\)   (D) D: all \(x\), R: all \(y\)
  4. For \(f(x)=\begin{cases}3x,&x<0\\x^2+1,&x\ge0\end{cases}\), evaluate \(f(-2)+f(3)\).
    (A) \(-6\)   (B) \(10\)   (C) \(4\)   (D) \(16\)
  5. The graph of \(y=x^2-2x-3\) has a turning point at:
    (A) \((1,-4)\)   (B) \((-1,4)\)   (C) \((1,4)\)   (D) \((2,-4)\)
  6. Which equation has x-intercepts at \(-4\) and \(2\)?
    (A) \((x-4)(x+2)\)   (B) \((x+4)(x-2)\)   (C) \((x-4)(x-2)\)   (D) \((x+4)(x+2)\)
  7. Which equation describes an absolute-value graph with vertex \((-2,1)\), opening downward, scale 1?
    (A) \(-|x+2|+1\)   (B) \(|x+2|+1\)   (C) \(-|x-2|+1\)   (D) \(-|x+2|-1\)
  8. What is the equation of the circle with centre \((-1,-2)\) and radius \(3\)?
    (A) \((x-1)^2+(y-2)^2=9\)   (B) \((x+1)^2+(y+2)^2=3\)   (C) \((x+1)^2+(y+2)^2=9\)   (D) \((x+1)^2+(y-2)^2=9\)
  9. Expressed as a single logarithm, \(\log_a x + 2\log_a y - 3\log_a z\) equals:
    (A) \(\log_a\frac{x y^2}{z^3}\)   (B) \(\log_a\frac{x z^3}{y^2}\)   (C) \(\log_a\frac{x^2 y}{z^3}\)   (D) \(\log_a(x y^2 z^3)\)
  10. Solve \(\log_{10}(x-4)=1\).
    (A) \(x=14\)   (B) \(x=6\)   (C) \(x=10\)   (D) \(x=\tfrac{14}{1}\)
  11. The graph of \(g(x)\) is obtained by shifting \(f(x)=e^x\) four units right and one unit up. Find \(g(x)\).
    (A) \(e^{x+4}+1\)   (B) \(e^{x-4}+1\)   (C) \(e^{x-4}-1\)   (D) \(e^{x-1}+4\)
  12. Find the x-intercept of \(y=\ln(x+1)\).
    (A) \(x=0\)   (B) \(x=1\)   (C) \(x=-1\)   (D) \(x=e\)

Section B — Long Answer (Questions 13–28, 38 marks)

  1. [2] Find the equation of the line through \((2,-3)\) and \((6,5)\) in gradient-intercept form.
  2. [2] The line with equation \(y+5x=12\) passes through the point \((2,a)\). Find the value of \(a\).
  3. [3] Find the equation of the line through \((0,3)\) perpendicular to \(y-5x=3\). Leave your answer in general form.
  4. [2] Solve simultaneously, showing all working: \[ \begin{cases} 4x+y=14\\ 2x-y=4 \end{cases} \]
  5. [2] Determine the domain and range of \(y=\dfrac{1}{x+2}\), in interval/set notation.
  6. [2] Is \(f(x)=x^4+1\) odd, even, or neither? Show all working.
  7. [2] Given \(g(x)=\dfrac{1}{x^2-2x}\) and \(h(x)=3x\), evaluate \(g(h(-2))\).
  8. [2] Find all values of \(a\) for which \(x^2+ax+49=0\) has exactly one real root.
  9. [3] Graph \(y=-x^2+4\). Show all important features including intercepts and vertex.
  10. [2] Sketch the graph of \(y=|x+2|+1\). Show all important features.
  11. [3] Find the centre and radius of the circle \(x^2+y^2+2x=8\).
  12. [2] Sketch the circle \(x^2+y^2+2x=8\) and find its intercepts.
  13. [2] Evaluate \(\log_{2} 128\).
  14. [3] Solve \(5e^{x-4}+1=11\) for \(x\), giving your answer to 3 decimal places.
  15. [3] Sketch \(y=\ln(x-1)\). Find the x-intercept and write the equation of the vertical asymptote.
  16. [3] Sketch \(y=e^{x}+1\). Find the y-intercept and write the equation of the horizontal asymptote.
