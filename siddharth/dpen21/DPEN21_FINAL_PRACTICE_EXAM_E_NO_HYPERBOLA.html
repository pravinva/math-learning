DPEN21 Final Practice Exam E (No Hyperbola)

Time allowed: 2 hours | Total: 50 marks | 28 questions | Calculator allowed
Questions 1–12 are multiple choice (1 mark each). Questions 13–28 are long answer; the value of each is indicated. No hyperbola questions included. Worked solutions are in a separate file: Exam E Worked Answers.

Section A — Multiple Choice (Questions 1–12, 1 mark each = 12 marks)

  1. Find the gradient of the line through \((2,-3)\) and \((6,5)\).
    (A) \(2\)   (B) \(\tfrac12\)   (C) \(-2\)   (D) \(4\)
  2. The graph \(y=3\) is:
    (A) sloping line   (B) horizontal line   (C) vertical line   (D) parabola
  3. Find the domain and range of \(y=\sqrt{x-3}+1\).
    (A) D: \(x\ge 3\), R: \(y\ge 1\)   (B) D: \(x\ge -3\), R: \(y\ge 1\)   (C) D: \(x\ge 1\), R: \(y\ge 3\)   (D) D: all \(x\), R: all \(y\)
  4. For \(f(x)=\begin{cases}x+1,&x<1\\x^2-2,&x\ge1\end{cases}\), evaluate \(f(-2)+f(3)\).
    (A) \(4\)   (B) \(6\)   (C) \(8\)   (D) \(5\)
  5. The graph of \(y=2x^2-8x+5\) has a turning point at:
    (A) \((2,-3)\)   (B) \((-2,-3)\)   (C) \((2,3)\)   (D) \((4,5)\)
  6. Which equation has x-intercepts at \(-3\) and \(1\)?
    (A) \((x-3)(x+1)\)   (B) \((x+3)(x-1)\)   (C) \((x+3)(x+1)\)   (D) \((x-3)(x-1)\)
  7. Which equation describes an absolute-value graph with vertex \((3,2)\), opening upward, scale 3?
    (A) \(3|x-3|+2\)   (B) \(|x-3|+2\)   (C) \(3|x+3|+2\)   (D) \(-3|x-3|+2\)
  8. What is the equation of the circle with centre \((1,2)\) and radius \(3\)?
    (A) \((x-1)^2+(y-2)^2=9\)   (B) \((x+1)^2+(y+2)^2=9\)   (C) \((x-1)^2+(y-2)^2=3\)   (D) \((x-1)^2+(y+2)^2=9\)
  9. Expressed as a single logarithm, \(\tfrac12\log_a x + \log_a y - \log_a z\) equals:
    (A) \(\log_a\frac{\sqrt{x}\,y}{z}\)   (B) \(\log_a\frac{x^2 y}{z}\)   (C) \(\log_a\frac{\sqrt{x}\,z}{y}\)   (D) \(\log_a\frac{y}{\sqrt{x}\,z}\)
  10. Solve \(\log_3(x+2)=2\).
    (A) \(x=7\)   (B) \(x=6\)   (C) \(x=1\)   (D) \(x=9\)
  11. The graph of \(g(x)\) is obtained by shifting \(f(x)=e^x\) one unit down. Find \(g(x)\).
    (A) \(e^{x}-1\)   (B) \(e^{x-1}\)   (C) \(e^{x}+1\)   (D) \(e^{-x}-1\)
  12. Find the x-intercept of \(y=3^{x}-9\).
    (A) \(x=2\)   (B) \(x=3\)   (C) \(x=9\)   (D) \(x=\tfrac13\)

Section B — Long Answer (Questions 13–28, 38 marks)

  1. [2] Find the midpoint and the length of the interval joining \((2,3)\) and \((8,11)\).
  2. [2] The line with equation \(y=2x-3\) meets the y-axis at a point \(P\). Find the coordinates of \(P\).
  3. [3] Find the equation of the line through \((1,4)\) perpendicular to \(2x-4y+3=0\). Leave your answer in general form.
  4. [2] Solve simultaneously, showing all working: \[ \begin{cases} 3x-2y=4\\ x+2y=12 \end{cases} \]
  5. [2] Given \(f(x)=x^2+1\) and \(g(x)=2x-3\), evaluate \(g(f(2))\) and find \((f\circ g)(x)\).
  6. [2] Is \(f(x)=x^3+2x\) odd, even, or neither? Show all working.
  7. [3] For \(y=2x^2-8x+6\), find the vertex, x-intercepts and y-intercept.
  8. [2] Find the value of \(c\) for which \(x^2-6x+c=0\) has exactly one (repeated) root.
  9. [3] Sketch the circle \(x^2+y^2-2x-4y-4=0\). Show centre, radius, and axis intercepts.
  10. [2] Sketch \(y=3|x-2|-6\). Show vertex, x-intercepts and y-intercept.
  11. [3] Evaluate \(\log_2 64\), and hence solve \(2^{3t}=64\) for \(t\).
  12. [2] A population grows according to \(N=100\,e^{0.5t}\), where \(t\) is in years. Find, to 3 decimal places, the time \(t\) at which \(N=300\).
  13. [2] Solve \(\ln(2x-1)=\ln(x+4)\), including any domain restriction.
  14. [3] Sketch \(y=2x^2-8x+6\). Show the vertex, intercepts and shape.
  15. [3] Sketch \(y=e^{x}-1\). Find the y-intercept and write the equation of the horizontal asymptote.
  16. [2] Sketch \(y=\ln(x-1)\). Find the x-intercept and write the equation of the vertical asymptote.
