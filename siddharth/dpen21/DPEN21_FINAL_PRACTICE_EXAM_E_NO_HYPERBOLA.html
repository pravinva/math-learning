DPEN21 Final Practice Exam E (No Hyperbola)

Time allowed: 2 hours | Total: 48 marks | Calculator allowed
No hyperbola questions included. Worked solutions are in a separate file: Exam E Worked Answers.

Section A — Multiple Choice (12 marks)

  1. Find the gradient of the line through \((1,3)\) and \((7,9)\).
  2. The graph \(y=-2\) is: (A) horizontal line (B) vertical line (C) parabola (D) circle.
  3. Find the domain and range of \(y=\sqrt{x-1}+2\).
  4. For \(f(x)=\begin{cases}x^2,&x<2\\3x-2,&x\ge2\end{cases}\), evaluate \(f(1)+f(4)\).
  5. Find the turning point of \(y=3x^2+6x-1\).
  6. Which equation has x-intercepts at \(-4\) and \(2\)?
  7. Write the equation of an absolute-value graph with vertex \((0,1)\), opening upward with scale 3.
  8. Circle with centre \((4,-1)\), radius \(3\): write the standard-form equation.
  9. Simplify to one logarithm: \(\log_a x + \log_a y - 3\log_a z\).
  10. Solve \(\log_{10}(x-5)=1\).
  11. If \(g(x)=e^x\), write the equation after shifting 2 up and reflecting in the y-axis.
  12. Find the x-intercept of \(y=\ln(2x)\).

Section B — Long Answer (36 marks)

  1. [3] Find the equation of the line through \((-2,-1)\) and \((2,7)\) in gradient-intercept form.
  2. [3] Find the equation of the line through \((1,1)\) perpendicular to \(x+2y-4=0\).
  3. [3] Solve simultaneously: \[ \begin{cases} 3x+y=10\\ x-y=2 \end{cases} \]
  4. [3] State whether \(f(x)=x^3+x\) is odd, even, or neither (show the check).
  5. [3] Given \(f(x)=2x,\ g(x)=x^2+1\), find \(f(g(2))\) and \((g\circ f)(x)\).
  6. [4] For \(y=-x^2+2x+3\), find the vertex, x-intercepts, y-intercept, and sketch the key features.
  7. [3] Solve \(2x^2-4x+5=0\) and state the nature of the roots using the discriminant.
  8. [3] Find the centre and radius of \(x^2+y^2-8x+2y+8=0\).
  9. [3] Find the intercepts of the circle in Q20.
  10. [4] Solve \(4^{x-1}=30\) for \(x\) (3 d.p.).
  11. [4] Solve \(\ln(x+5)+\ln(x-1)=\ln 16\), including the domain restriction.
  12. [3] Sketch \(y=e^{x}+2\): y-intercept, horizontal asymptote, and one extra point.
