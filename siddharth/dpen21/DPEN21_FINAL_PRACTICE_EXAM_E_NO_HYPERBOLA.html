DPEN21 Final Practice Exam E (No Hyperbola)

Time allowed: 2 hours | Total: 50 marks | 28 questions | Calculator allowed
Questions 1–12 are multiple choice (1 mark each). Questions 13–28 are long answer; the value of each is indicated. No hyperbola questions included. Worked solutions are in a separate file: Exam E Worked Answers.

Section A — Multiple Choice (Questions 1–12, 1 mark each = 12 marks)

  1. Find the gradient of the line through \((-2,-1)\) and \((2,7)\).
    (A) \(2\)   (B) \(-2\)   (C) \(\tfrac12\)   (D) \(4\)
  2. The graph \(x=-2\) is:
    (A) sloping line   (B) horizontal line   (C) vertical line   (D) parabola
  3. Find the domain and range of \(y=\sqrt{x+1}+2\).
    (A) D: \(x\ge 1\), R: \(y\ge 2\)   (B) D: \(x\ge -1\), R: \(y\ge 2\)   (C) D: \(x\ge -1\), R: \(y\ge -2\)   (D) D: all \(x\), R: all \(y\)
  4. For \(f(x)=\begin{cases}x+3,&x<1\\x^2,&x\ge1\end{cases}\), evaluate \(f(-2)+f(3)\).
    (A) \(1\)   (B) \(9\)   (C) \(11\)   (D) \(10\)
  5. The graph of \(y=x^2-4x+1\) has a turning point at:
    (A) \((2,-3)\)   (B) \((-2,3)\)   (C) \((2,3)\)   (D) \((4,-3)\)
  6. Which equation has x-intercepts at \(-2\) and \(5\)?
    (A) \((x-2)(x+5)\)   (B) \((x+2)(x-5)\)   (C) \((x-2)(x-5)\)   (D) \((x+2)(x+5)\)
  7. Which equation describes an absolute-value graph with vertex \((3,-2)\), opening upward, scale 1?
    (A) \(|x-3|-2\)   (B) \(|x+3|-2\)   (C) \(-|x-3|-2\)   (D) \(|x-3|+2\)
  8. What is the equation of the circle with centre \((2,-3)\) and radius \(4\)?
    (A) \((x-2)^2+(y+3)^2=4\)   (B) \((x-2)^2+(y+3)^2=16\)   (C) \((x+2)^2+(y-3)^2=16\)   (D) \((x-2)^2+(y-3)^2=16\)
  9. Expressed as a single logarithm, \(2\log_a x + 3\log_a y - \log_a z\) equals:
    (A) \(\log_a\frac{x^2 y^3}{z}\)   (B) \(\log_a\frac{x^2 z}{y^3}\)   (C) \(\log_a\frac{x^3 y^2}{z}\)   (D) \(\log_a(x^2 y^3 z)\)
  10. Solve \(\log_{10}(3x)=2\).
    (A) \(x=\tfrac{100}{3}\approx33.33\)   (B) \(x=30\)   (C) \(x=\tfrac{2}{3}\)   (D) \(x=300\)
  11. The graph of \(g(x)\) is obtained by shifting \(f(x)=e^x\) two units right and reflecting in the x-axis. Find \(g(x)\).
    (A) \(e^{x-2}\)   (B) \(-e^{x+2}\)   (C) \(-e^{x-2}\)   (D) \(-e^{2-x}\)
  12. Find the x-intercept of \(y=\ln(x-2)\).
    (A) \(x=2\)   (B) \(x=3\)   (C) \(x=1\)   (D) \(x=e^2\)

Section B — Long Answer (Questions 13–28, 38 marks)

  1. [2] Find the equation of the line through \((-2,-1)\) and \((2,7)\) in gradient-intercept form.
  2. [2] The line with equation \(y+x=8\) passes through the point \((3,a)\). Find the value of \(a\).
  3. [3] Find the equation of the line through \((1,1)\) perpendicular to \(y-x=2\). Leave your answer in general form.
  4. [2] Solve simultaneously, showing all working: \[ \begin{cases} 3x+y=10\\ x-y=2 \end{cases} \]
  5. [2] Determine the domain and range of \(y=\dfrac{1}{x-3}\), in interval/set notation.
  6. [2] Is \(f(x)=x^3+x\) odd, even, or neither? Show all working.
  7. [2] Given \(g(x)=\dfrac{1}{x^2+x}\) and \(h(x)=2x\), evaluate \(g(h(-2))\).
  8. [2] Find all values of \(a\) for which \(x^2+ax+16=0\) has exactly one real root.
  9. [3] Graph \(y=-x^2+25\). Show all important features including intercepts and vertex.
  10. [2] Sketch the graph of \(y=|x-3|+1\). Show all important features.
  11. [3] Find the centre and radius of the circle \(x^2+y^2-6x=7\).
  12. [2] Sketch the circle \(x^2+y^2-6x=7\) and find its intercepts.
  13. [2] Evaluate \(\log_{3} 81\).
  14. [3] Solve \(4e^{x-2}+1=13\) for \(x\), giving your answer to 3 decimal places.
  15. [3] Sketch \(y=\ln(x-3)\). Find the x-intercept and write the equation of the vertical asymptote.
  16. [3] Sketch \(y=e^{x}+2\). Find the y-intercept and write the equation of the horizontal asymptote.
