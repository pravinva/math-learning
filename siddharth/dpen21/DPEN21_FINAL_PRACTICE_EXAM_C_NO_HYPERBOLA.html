DPEN21 Final Practice Exam C (No Hyperbola)

Time allowed: 2 hours | Total: 48 marks | Calculator allowed
No hyperbola questions included. Worked solutions are in a separate file: Exam C Worked Answers.

Section A — Multiple Choice (12 marks)

  1. Find the gradient of the line through \((-2,5)\) and \((4,-7)\).
  2. The graph \(y=4\) is: (A) horizontal line (B) vertical line (C) parabola (D) circle.
  3. Find the domain and range of \(y=\sqrt{x-2}+1\).
  4. For \(f(x)=\begin{cases}2x+1,&x<0\\x^2,&x\ge0\end{cases}\), evaluate \(f(-2)+f(3)\).
  5. Find the turning point of \(y=2x^2-8x+3\).
  6. Which equation has x-intercepts at \(-3\) and \(4\)?
  7. Write the equation of an absolute-value graph with vertex \((2,-3)\), opening upward with scale 1.
  8. Circle with centre \((-1,2)\), radius \(3\): write the standard-form equation.
  9. Simplify to one logarithm: \(2\log_a x - \log_a y + \log_a z\).
  10. Solve \(\log_{10}(x+3)=1\).
  11. If \(g(x)=e^x\), write the equation after shifting 1 right and 2 up.
  12. Find the x-intercept of \(y=\ln(x+2)-2\).

Section B — Long Answer (36 marks)

  1. [3] Find the equation of the line through \((0,-3)\) and \((4,5)\) in gradient-intercept form.
  2. [3] Find the equation of the line through \((3,1)\) perpendicular to \(3x+y-6=0\).
  3. [3] Solve simultaneously: \[ \begin{cases} 2x+3y=12\\ 4x-3y=6 \end{cases} \]
  4. [3] State whether \(f(x)=x^3-x\) is odd, even, or neither (show the check).
  5. [3] Given \(f(x)=3x+2,\ g(x)=x^2-1\), find \(f(g(-2))\) and \((g\circ f)(x)\).
  6. [4] For \(y=-x^2+4x-3\), find the vertex, x-intercepts, y-intercept, and sketch the key features.
  7. [3] Solve \(x^2-5x+6=0\) and state the nature of the roots using the discriminant.
  8. [3] Find the centre and radius of \(x^2+y^2-4x+6y-3=0\).
  9. [3] Find the intercepts of the circle in Q20.
  10. [4] Solve \(2^{x+1}=15\) for \(x\) (3 d.p.).
  11. [4] Solve \(\ln(x-1)+\ln(x+2)=\ln 10\), including the domain restriction.
  12. [3] Sketch \(y=e^{x-1}+1\): y-intercept, horizontal asymptote, and one extra point.
