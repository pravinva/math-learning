DPEN21 Final Practice Exam C (No Hyperbola)

Time allowed: 2 hours | Total: 50 marks | 28 questions | Calculator allowed
Questions 1–12 are multiple choice (1 mark each). Questions 13–28 are long answer; the value of each is indicated. No hyperbola questions included. Worked solutions are in a separate file: Exam C Worked Answers.

Section A — Multiple Choice (Questions 1–12, 1 mark each = 12 marks)

  1. Find the gradient of the line through \((-3,2)\) and \((1,-6)\).
    (A) \(-2\)   (B) \(2\)   (C) \(-\tfrac12\)   (D) \(\tfrac12\)
  2. The graph \(x=4\) is:
    (A) sloping line   (B) horizontal line   (C) vertical line   (D) parabola
  3. Find the domain and range of \(y=\sqrt{x+2}-3\).
    (A) D: \(x\ge -2\), R: \(y\ge -3\)   (B) D: \(x\ge 2\), R: \(y\ge 3\)   (C) D: \(x\ge -3\), R: \(y\ge -2\)   (D) D: all \(x\), R: all \(y\)
  4. For \(f(x)=\begin{cases}x^2,&x<1\\3x-2,&x\ge1\end{cases}\), evaluate \(f(-2)+f(2)\).
    (A) \(6\)   (B) \(8\)   (C) \(4\)   (D) \(10\)
  5. The graph of \(y=2x^2-4x-1\) has a turning point at:
    (A) \((1,-3)\)   (B) \((-1,3)\)   (C) \((1,3)\)   (D) \((2,-1)\)
  6. Which equation has x-intercepts at \(-1\) and \(4\)?
    (A) \((x-1)(x+4)\)   (B) \((x+1)(x-4)\)   (C) \((x-1)(x-4)\)   (D) \((x+1)(x+4)\)
  7. Which equation describes an absolute-value graph with vertex \((2,-1)\), opening upward, scale 2?
    (A) \(2|x-2|-1\)   (B) \(|x-2|-1\)   (C) \(2|x+2|-1\)   (D) \(-2|x-2|-1\)
  8. What is the equation of the circle with centre \((-3,1)\) and radius \(5\)?
    (A) \((x-3)^2+(y+1)^2=25\)   (B) \((x+3)^2+(y-1)^2=5\)   (C) \((x+3)^2+(y-1)^2=25\)   (D) \((x+3)^2+(y+1)^2=25\)
  9. Expressed as a single logarithm, \(3\log_a x - 2\log_a y + \log_a z\) equals:
    (A) \(\log_a\frac{x^3 z}{y^2}\)   (B) \(\log_a\frac{x^3 y^2}{z}\)   (C) \(\log_a\frac{x^3 z}{y}\)   (D) \(\log_a(x^3 y^2 z)\)
  10. Solve \(2^{x}=32\).
    (A) \(x=4\)   (B) \(x=5\)   (C) \(x=6\)   (D) \(x=16\)
  11. The graph of \(g(x)\) is obtained by reflecting \(f(x)=e^x\) in the y-axis and shifting one unit up. Find \(g(x)\).
    (A) \(e^{x}+1\)   (B) \(e^{-x}+1\)   (C) \(-e^{x}+1\)   (D) \(e^{-x}-1\)
  12. Find the x-intercept of \(y=\ln(2x-4)\).
    (A) \(x=2.5\)   (B) \(x=2\)   (C) \(x=3\)   (D) \(x=\tfrac52 e\)

Section B — Long Answer (Questions 13–28, 38 marks)

  1. [2] Find the equation of the line through \((-3,2)\) and \((1,-6)\) in gradient-intercept form.
  2. [2] The line with equation \(3x-2y=12\) passes through the point \((4,a)\). Find the value of \(a\).
  3. [3] Find the equation of the line through \((2,5)\) perpendicular to \(4x-2y+7=0\). Leave your answer in general form.
  4. [2] Solve simultaneously, showing all working: \[ \begin{cases} 3x+2y=16\\ 5x-2y=8 \end{cases} \]
  5. [2] Given \(f(x)=2x-1\) and \(g(x)=x^2+3\), evaluate \(f(g(-2))\) and find \((g\circ f)(x)\).
  6. [2] Is \(f(x)=x^3-4x\) odd, even, or neither? Show all working.
  7. [3] For \(y=-x^2+4x-3\), find the vertex, x-intercepts and y-intercept.
  8. [2] Solve \(x^2-5x+6=0\) and state the nature of the roots using the discriminant.
  9. [3] Sketch the circle \(x^2+y^2-6x+8y+9=0\). Show centre, radius, and axis intercepts.
  10. [2] Sketch \(y=2|x-1|-3\). Show vertex, x-intercepts and y-intercept.
  11. [3] Solve \(3^{x+1}=20\) for \(x\), giving your answer to 3 decimal places.
  12. [2] Evaluate \(\log_{3} 81\).
  13. [2] Solve \(\ln(x-2)+\ln(x+1)=\ln 10\), including any domain restriction.
  14. [3] Sketch \(y=-x^2+4x-3\). Show the vertex, intercepts and shape.
  15. [3] Sketch \(y=e^{-x}+1\). Find the y-intercept and write the equation of the horizontal asymptote.
  16. [2] Sketch \(y=\ln(2x-4)\). Find the x-intercept and write the equation of the vertical asymptote.
