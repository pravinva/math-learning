DPEN21 Final Practice Exam C (No Hyperbola)

Time allowed: 2 hours | Total: 50 marks | 28 questions | Calculator allowed
Questions 1–12 are multiple choice (1 mark each). Questions 13–28 are long answer; the value of each is indicated. No hyperbola questions included. Worked solutions are in a separate file: Exam C Worked Answers.

Section A — Multiple Choice (Questions 1–12, 1 mark each = 12 marks)

  1. Find the gradient of the line through \((-2,5)\) and \((4,-7)\).
  2. The graph \(y=4\) is: (A) sloping line (B) horizontal line (C) vertical line (D) parabola.
  3. Find the domain and range of \(y=\sqrt{x-2}+1\).
  4. For \(f(x)=\begin{cases}2x+1,&x<0\\x^2,&x\ge0\end{cases}\), evaluate \(f(-2)+f(3)\).
  5. The graph of \(y=2x^2-8x+3\) has a turning point at:
  6. Which equation has x-intercepts at \(-3\) and \(4\)?
  7. Which equation describes an absolute-value graph with vertex \((2,-3)\), opening upward, scale 1?
  8. What is the equation of the circle with centre \((-1,2)\) and radius \(3\)?
  9. Expressed as a single logarithm, \(2\log_a x - \log_a y + \log_a z\) equals:
  10. Solve \(\log_{10}(x+3)=1\).
  11. The graph of \(g(x)\) is obtained by shifting \(f(x)=e^x\) one unit right and two units up. Find \(g(x)\).
  12. Find the x-intercept of \(y=\ln(x+2)-2\).

Section B — Long Answer (Questions 13–28, 38 marks)

  1. [2] Find the equation of the line through \((0,-3)\) and \((4,5)\) in gradient-intercept form.
  2. [2] The line with equation \(y+2x=10\) passes through the point \((3,a)\). Find the value of \(a\).
  3. [3] Find the equation of the line through \((1,1)\) perpendicular to \(y-3x=1\). Leave your answer in general form.
  4. [2] Solve simultaneously, showing all working: \[ \begin{cases} 2x+3y=12\\ 4x-3y=6 \end{cases} \]
  5. [2] Determine the domain and range of \(y=\dfrac{1}{x-2}\), in interval/set notation.
  6. [2] Is \(f(x)=x^4-2x^2\) odd, even, or neither? Show all working.
  7. [2] Given \(g(x)=\dfrac{1}{x^2+2x}\) and \(h(x)=3x\), evaluate \(g(h(-2))\).
  8. [2] Find all values of \(a\) for which \(x^2+ax+36=0\) has exactly one real root.
  9. [3] Graph \(y=-x^2+16\). Show all important features including intercepts and vertex.
  10. [2] Sketch the graph of \(y=|x+1|+2\). Show all important features.
  11. [3] Find the centre and radius of the circle \(x^2+y^2-2y=3\).
  12. [2] Sketch the circle \(x^2+y^2-2y=3\) and find its intercepts.
  13. [2] Evaluate \(\log_{\sqrt{2}} 32\).
  14. [3] Solve \(3e^{x-5}+1=10\) for \(x\), giving your answer to 3 decimal places.
  15. [3] Sketch \(y=\ln(x-2)\). Find the x-intercept and write the equation of the vertical asymptote.
  16. [3] Sketch \(y=e^{x}+4\). Find the y-intercept and write the equation of the horizontal asymptote.
