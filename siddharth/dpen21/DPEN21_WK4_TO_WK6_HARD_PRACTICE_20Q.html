DPEN21 Hard Practice (Weeks 4, 5, 6)

20 harder mixed questions targeting the high-yield end of Weeks 4-6: line geometry, functions/parabolas/hyperbolas, and exponential-logarithmic equations and graphs. Several questions are adapted from Strand 1 - Fundamentals (coordinate geometry + equation-solving style).
Recommended time: 75 minutes. Mode: no notes first attempt, then use answer key for correction round. Source blend: Week 4-6 notes + Strand 1 question styles.

Questions (20) Harder Level

  1. [W4, Strand 1 Q21 adapted] Find the equation of the line through \((1,2)\) and \((-1,3)\). State gradient and axis intercepts.
  2. [W4, Strand 1 Q22 adapted] Determine \(k\) so that lines \(y=(k-1)x+5\) and \(2x+6y-7=0\) are perpendicular.
  3. [W4, Strand 1 Q22(c) style] Show whether the line through \(A(1,2),B(-5,6)\) is perpendicular to the line through \(C(-2,3),D(2,9)\).
  4. [W4] A line cuts axes at \((a,0)\) and \((0,b)\). If it passes through \((4,3)\) and slope is \(-\frac{3}{4}\), find \(a,b\).
  5. [W4, Strand 1 Q23(b) adapted] Find the point dividing segment joining \(P(1,3)\) and \(Q(9,-7)\) in ratio \(3:2\) internally.
  6. [W4+W5] Find all intersections of \(y=2x-3\) with \(y=x^2-4x+1\).
  7. [W5] For \(f(x)=x^2-6x+5\): find vertex, axis, intercepts, and range.
  8. [W5] Solve inequality \(2x^2-7x-4\ge 0\) in interval notation.
  9. [W5] For \(g(x)=|2x-5|-3\), find x-intercepts, y-intercept, vertex, and range.
  10. [W5] Circle: \(x^2+y^2-6x+8y+9=0\). Find centre and radius; state if it intersects x-axis.
  11. [W5] Rewrite \(y=\dfrac{3}{x-2}+1\) as \((x-a)(y-b)=c\). State centre, asymptotes, domain, range.
  12. [W5, Strand 1 Q18(i) style] Solve \(\dfrac{x-2}{3x+4}=\dfrac{2x-3}{x+1}\) and check restrictions.
  13. [W5] If \(f(x)=\dfrac{1}{x-4}\), find \(f^{-1}(x)\) and state domains of \(f\) and \(f^{-1}\).
  14. [W6] Solve \(3e^{2x}-5=40\) (exact then 3 d.p.).
  15. [W6] Solve \(10^{3x-1}=7\) for \(x\) in log form and decimal form.
  16. [W6] Solve \(2^{x+1}=5^{x-2}\) in exact log form.
  17. [W6] State domain, range, and asymptote of \(y=e^{x-3}-4\).
  18. [W6] State domain, range, and vertical asymptote of \(y=\ln(x+2)-1\).
  19. [W6, Strand 1 Q14(i) style] If \(\ln y=-2x\), express \(y\) in terms of \(x\).
  20. [W6 mixed] Solve \(\log_{10}(x-1)+\log_{10}(x+1)=1\) with domain check.

Answer Key

  1. Line: \(y=-\frac12x+\frac52\), so \(x+2y-5=0\). Gradient \(m=-\frac12\). Intercepts: \((5,0)\), \((0,\frac52)\).
  2. \(m_1=k-1,\ m_2=-\frac13,\ (k-1)\!\left(-\frac13\right)=-1\Rightarrow k=4\)
  3. \(m_{AB}=\frac{6-2}{-5-1}=-\frac23\), \(m_{CD}=\frac{9-3}{2-(-2)}=\frac32\), product \(=-1\Rightarrow\) perpendicular.
  4. \(y=-\frac34x+6\Rightarrow a=8,\ b=6\)
  5. \(\left(\frac{2\cdot1+3\cdot9}{5},\frac{2\cdot3+3\cdot(-7)}{5}\right)=\left(\frac{29}{5},-3\right)\)
  6. \(x=\frac{6\pm\sqrt{48}}{2}=3\pm2\sqrt3\), points \((3\pm2\sqrt3,\ 3\pm4\sqrt3)\)
  7. Vertex \((3,-4)\), axis \(x=3\), x-ints \(1,5\), y-int \(5\), range \([ -4,\infty)\)
  8. Roots \(-\frac12,4\), solution \((-\infty,-\frac12]\cup[4,\infty)\)
  9. Vertex \((\frac52,-3)\), x-ints \(1,4\), y-int \(2\), range \([ -3,\infty)\)
  10. \((x-3)^2+(y+4)^2=16\), centre \((3,-4)\), radius \(4\), intersects x-axis (yes)
  11. \((x-2)(y-1)=3\), centre \((2,1)\), asymptotes \(x=2,\ y=1\), domain \(x\ne2\), range \(y\ne1\)
  12. \(\frac{x-2}{3x+4}=\frac{2x-3}{x+1}\Rightarrow (x-2)(x+1)=(2x-3)(3x+4)\Rightarrow 5x^2+2x-10=0\).
    \(x=\frac{-1\pm\sqrt{51}}{5}\), with restrictions \(x\ne-\frac43,-1\), so both valid.
  13. \(y=\frac{1}{x-4}\Rightarrow x=4+\frac1y\Rightarrow f^{-1}(x)=4+\frac1x\); domains: \(x\ne4\), \(x\ne0\)
  14. \(e^{2x}=15\Rightarrow x=\frac12\ln15\approx1.354\)
  15. \(3x-1=\log 7\Rightarrow x=\frac{1+\log7}{3}\approx0.615\)
  16. \((x+1)\ln2=(x-2)\ln5\Rightarrow x=\frac{-(2\ln5+\ln2)}{\ln2-\ln5}\)
  17. Domain \(\mathbb{R}\), range \((-4,\infty)\), asymptote \(y=-4\)
  18. Domain \((-2,\infty)\), range \(\mathbb{R}\), vertical asymptote \(x=-2\)
  19. \(\ln y=-2x\Rightarrow y=e^{-2x}\)
  20. \(\log[(x-1)(x+1)]=1\Rightarrow x^2-1=10\Rightarrow x=\pm\sqrt{11}\); domain \(x>1\Rightarrow x=\sqrt{11}\)
