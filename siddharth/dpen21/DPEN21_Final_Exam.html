Find the coefficient of \(x^3y^4\) in the expansion of \((x+y)^7\).

Solution
1
The general term of \((x+y)^7\) is \(\dbinom{7}{r}x^{7-r}y^r\).
2
We need the term with \(y^4\). Set \(r=4\).
3
\(\dbinom{7}{4} = \dfrac{7!}{4!\,3!} = \dfrac{7\times6\times5}{6} = 35\)
ANSWER Coefficient = 35

Write \(\dfrac{2+i}{3-2i}\) in the form \(a+bi\) where \(a,b\in\mathbb{R}\).

Solution

ANSWER \(\dfrac{4}{13}+\dfrac{7}{13}i\)

Q11

Expand \(\left(2x - \dfrac{1}{x}\right)^5\) completely, simplifying each term.

Solution
1
General term: \(\dbinom{5}{r}(2x)^{5-r}\!\left(-\frac{1}{x}\right)^r = \dbinom{5}{r}2^{5-r}(-1)^r x^{5-r} \cdot x^{-r} = \dbinom{5}{r}2^{5-r}(-1)^r x^{5-2r}\)
2
Row 5 of Pascal's Triangle: 1, 5, 10, 10, 5, 1
3
r=0: \(32x^5\)
r=1: \(\binom{5}{1}(16)(-1)x^3 = -80x^3\)
r=2: \(\binom{5}{2}(8)(1)x = 80x\)
r=3: \(\binom{5}{3}(4)(-1)x^{-1} = -40x^{-1}\)
r=4: \(\binom{5}{4}(2)(1)x^{-3} = 10x^{-3}\)
r=5: \(\binom{5}{5}(1)(-1)x^{-5} = -x^{-5}\)
ANSWER \(32x^5 - 80x^3 + 80x - \dfrac{40}{x} + \dfrac{10}{x^3} - \dfrac{1}{x^5}\)

Find the inverse function of \(f(x) = \dfrac{2x+3}{x-1}\), \(x\neq 1\), and state its domain and range.

Solution

Swap x and y: \(x = \dfrac{2y+3}{y-1}\)
2

Consider the polynomial \(P(x) = 2x^4 - x^3 - 13x^2 + 5x + 15\).

(a)Use the Factor Theorem and synthetic division to show that \((x-\sqrt{5})\) is not needed — instead, show that \((x^2-5)\) divides \(P(x)\) by finding a value of \(x\) that is a root. [Hint: test \(x=\pm 1,\pm 3,\pm 5,\pm\frac{1}{2},\pm\frac{3}{2}\).][3]
(b)Divide \(P(x)\) by the factor found in (a) using synthetic division and write \(P(x)\) as a product of a linear and a cubic factor.[3]
(c)Fully factorise \(P(x)\) over the reals and find all real roots.[2]
(d)The cubic factor from (b) has two complex (non-real) roots. Find these roots and verify using Vieta's formulas that the sum of all four roots equals \(\frac{1}{2}\).[2]
Solution
a
\(P\!\left(\frac{3}{2}\right) = 2\cdot\frac{81}{16}-\frac{27}{8}-13\cdot\frac{9}{4}+\frac{15}{2}+15\). Let's try \(x=-\frac{3}{2}\): \(2\cdot\frac{81}{16}+\frac{27}{8}-13\cdot\frac{9}{4}-\frac{15}{2}+15\). Actually test integer: \(P(-1)=2+1-13-5+15=0\) ✓. So \((x+1)\) is a factor.
b
Synthetic division by \(a=-1\), coefficients 2,−1,−13,5,15:
2 | −1 | −13 | 5 | 15
   | −2 | 3 | 10 | −15
2 | −3 | −10 | 15 | 0
Quotient: \(2x^3-3x^2-10x+15\). So \(P(x)=(x+1)(2x^3-3x^2-10x+15)\)
c
Factorise \(2x^3-3x^2-10x+15\) by grouping: \(x^2(2x-3)-5(2x-3)=(x^2-5)(2x-3)\). So:

ANSWER \(P(x)=(x+1)(2x-3)(x-\sqrt{5})(x+\sqrt{5})\). Roots: \(x=-1,\;\frac{3}{2},\;\pm\sqrt{5}\). Sum = \(\frac{1}{2}\) ✓
Q20
10 marks

This question concerns trigonometric equations, identities, and applications.

(a)Prove the identity: \(\dfrac{1+\sin\theta}{\cos\theta} + \dfrac{\cos\theta}{1+\sin\theta} = 2\sec\theta\)[3]
(b)Solve the equation \(\sin 2\theta - \cos\theta = 0\) for \(\theta\in[0°, 360°]\), giving exact answers.[4]
(c)A surveyor observes the top of a cliff from two points A and B, both on level ground, on the same side of the cliff. Point A is 50 m closer to the cliff than point B. The angles of elevation from A and B to the top of the cliff are 52° and 38° respectively. Find the height of the cliff, correct to the nearest metre. Draw and label a clear diagram.[3]
Solution
a
LHS — combine fractions over common denominator \(\cos\theta(1+\sin\theta)\):
\(\dfrac{(1+\sin\theta)^2+\cos^2\theta}{\cos\theta(1+\sin\theta)} = \dfrac{1+2\sin\theta+\sin^2\theta+\cos^2\theta}{\cos\theta(1+\sin\theta)}\)
\(= \dfrac{2+2\sin\theta}{\cos\theta(1+\sin\theta)} = \dfrac{2(1+\sin\theta)}{\cos\theta(1+\sin\theta)} = \dfrac{2}{\cos\theta} = 2\sec\theta\) = RHS ✓
b
Replace \(\sin 2\theta = 2\sin\theta\cos\theta\):
\(2\sin\theta\cos\theta-\cos\theta=0 \Rightarrow \cos\theta(2\sin\theta-1)=0\)
\(\cos\theta=0\): \(\theta=90°, 270°\)
\(\sin\theta=\frac{1}{2}\): \(\theta=30°, 150°\)
All solutions: \(30°, 90°, 150°, 270°\)
c
Let h = height, d = distance from A to cliff base.
From A: \(\tan 52° = h/d \Rightarrow h = d\tan 52°\)
From B: \(\tan 38° = h/(d+50) \Rightarrow h = (d+50)\tan 38°\)
Equal: \(d\tan 52° = (d+50)\tan 38°\)
\(d(\tan 52°-\tan 38°) = 50\tan 38°\)
\(d = \dfrac{50\tan 38°}{\tan 52°-\tan 38°} = \dfrac{50\times0.7813}{1.2799-0.7813} = \dfrac{39.07}{0.4986} \approx 78.37\text{ m}\)
\(h = 78.37\times\tan 52° \approx 78.37\times1.2799 \approx 100\text{ m}\)
ANSWER (b) θ = 30°, 90°, 150°, 270°    (c) Height ≈ 100 m

This question integrates exponential growth, the difference quotient, and rates of change.

(a)A population of bacteria grows according to \(N(t) = 500\,e^{0.3t}\), where \(t\) is time in hours.
(i) What is the initial population?   [1]
(ii) After how many hours does the population first exceed 5000? Give your answer correct to 2 decimal places.   [2]
(iii) Find the average rate of change of population between \(t=2\) and \(t=5\) hours. Give your answer correct to 1 decimal place.   [2][5]
(b)Using the difference quotient definition, find \(f'(x)\) for \(f(x) = \dfrac{1}{2x+1}\).
Leave your answer in simplified exact form.[3]
(c)A quantity \(Q\) satisfies the equation \(Q = 1200e^{kt}\). At \(t=0\), \(Q=1200\), and at \(t=4\), \(Q=2400\).
(i) Find the exact value of \(k\).   [1]
(ii) Find the value of \(t\) when \(Q=3600\), correct to 3 decimal places.   [1][2]
Solution
a(i)
\(N(0) = 500\,e^0 = 500\) bacteria.
a(ii)
\(500e^{0.3t}>5000 \Rightarrow e^{0.3t}>10 \Rightarrow 0.3t>\ln 10 \Rightarrow t>\frac{\ln 10}{0.3} = \frac{2.3026}{0.3} \approx 7.68\) hours.
a(iii)
\(N(2)=500e^{0.6}\approx500\times1.8221=911.1\). \(N(5)=500e^{1.5}\approx500\times4.4817=2240.8\).
AROC \(= \frac{2240.8-911.1}{5-2} = \frac{1329.7}{3} \approx 443.2\) bacteria/hour.
b
\(f(x+h)=\frac{1}{2(x+h)+1}=\frac{1}{2x+2h+1}\)
\(f(x+h)-f(x)=\frac{1}{2x+2h+1}-\frac{1}{2x+1}=\frac{(2x+1)-(2x+2h+1)}{(2x+2h+1)(2x+1)}=\frac{-2h}{(2x+2h+1)(2x+1)}\)
Divide by h: \(\frac{-2}{(2x+2h+1)(2x+1)}\)
Limit as \(h\to0\): \(f'(x)=\frac{-2}{(2x+1)^2}\)
c(i)
\(2400=1200e^{4k} \Rightarrow 2=e^{4k} \Rightarrow k=\frac{\ln 2}{4}\)
c(ii)
\(3600=1200e^{kt} \Rightarrow 3=e^{kt} \Rightarrow t=\frac{\ln 3}{k}=\frac{4\ln 3}{\ln 2}\approx\frac{4\times1.0986}{0.6931}\approx 6.340\)
ANSWER a(i) 500   a(ii) t > 7.68 h   a(iii) ≈ 443.2 bacteria/h   b) \(\frac{-2}{(2x+1)^2}\)   c(i) \(k=\frac{\ln 2}{4}\)   c(ii) t ≈ 6.340
