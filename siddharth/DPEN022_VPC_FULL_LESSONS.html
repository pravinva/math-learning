Core Lessons

L1. Polynomial Baseline: Degree, Roots, Multiplicity, Leading Coefficient

Motivation: you need a fast sketch of "what the polynomial can do" before solving it.

Discussion

Degree gives max roots and end behavior class. Root multiplicity controls touch/cross behavior. Leading coefficient controls up/down direction at large |x|.

Solved Example

f(x)=2(x-1)^2(x+3) has degree 3, roots x=1 (mult 2, touches), x=-3 (mult 1, crosses), leading coefficient 2, so left tail down, right tail up.

Practice + Why

  • Classify -(x+2)^3(x-4)^2 (tests multiplicity + tails together).
  • From given roots and multiplicities, construct a polynomial (tests reverse thinking).

L2. Factor and Remainder Theorem + Integer Root Tests

Motivation: fastest filter before long division or formula-based solving.

Discussion

f(a)=0 implies (x-a) is a factor. Use integer candidates first when coefficients are integers.

Solved Example

For f(x)=x^3-4x^2-x+4, test x=1: f(1)=0 so (x-1) factor. Grouping gives (x-1)(x^2-3x-4)=(x-1)(x-4)(x+1).

Practice + Why

  • Find a root of x^3+2x^2-5x-6 quickly (builds root filtering speed).
  • Compute remainder of 2x^4-3x+7 on division by x+2 using theorem only.

L3. Quadratic Discriminant + Vieta (including reverse build)

Motivation: classify roots and reconstruct equations without solving every time.

Discussion

For ax^2+bx+c=0, discriminant D=b^2-4ac tells real/distinct, repeated, or complex roots. Vieta gives r1+r2=-b/a, r1r2=c/a.

Solved Example

Roots sum to 5, product 6 gives x^2-5x+6=0. Here D=1 so two real distinct roots.

Practice + Why

  • Build quadratic with roots 2 and -7 (reverse Vieta fluency).
  • Classify roots of 3x^2+6x+5 with no solving (fast exam decision).

L4. Synthetic Division and Deflation

Motivation: once one root is known, strip degree fast and keep momentum.

Discussion

Synthetic division gives quotient and remainder compactly for divisors x-a. Deflation turns cubic to quadratic or quartic to cubic.

Solved Example

Deflate x^3-6x^2+11x-6 by x-1: synthetic result is x^2-5x+6, then roots are 1,2,3.

Practice + Why

  • Deflate x^4-5x^3+6x^2+4x-8 by known root 2 (multistep control).
  • Interpret nonzero remainder (error detection).

L5. Rational Root Theorem + Structured Cubic Search

Motivation: avoid random guessing in cubic solving.

Discussion

Possible rational roots are p/q where p|constant and q|leading coefficient. Test small magnitudes first.

Solved Example

For 2x^3-3x^2-8x+12, candidates include 1,2,3,4,1/2,3/2. x=2 works. Deflate to 2x^2+x-6, so full roots are 2,3/2,-2.

Practice + Why

  • Use the theorem to list (not solve) candidates for 3x^3+x^2-10x-8 (candidate discipline).
  • Solve a cubic with at least one fractional root.

L6. Mixed Polynomial Flow

Motivation: exam questions combine tools; you need a trigger chain.

Discussion

Flow: quick structure check -> candidate roots -> theorem test -> synthetic division -> Vieta cross-check.

Solved Example

Given x^3-7x+6=0, test integers: x=1 works. Deflate to x^2+x-6. Remaining roots 2,-3.

Practice + Why

  • Write your own 5-step flow on a sample cubic (metacognitive control).
  • Solve one cubic under 7-minute time cap (speed adaptation).

L7. Complex Intro: i^2, Powers of i, Standard Form

Motivation: all higher complex work collapses without instant i-cycle recall.

Discussion

Powers repeat mod 4: i, -1, -i, 1. Standard form is a+bi.

Solved Example

i^37 = i^(4*9+1)=i. Also (3-2i)+(5+i)=8-i.

Practice + Why

  • Simplify i^53+i^22 (cycle fluency).
  • Convert expressions to a+bi only (format discipline).

L8. Complex Arithmetic with Conjugates

Motivation: division and rationalization appear constantly in later topics.

Discussion

Conjugate of a+bi is a-bi. Product gives real norm: (a+bi)(a-bi)=a^2+b^2.

Solved Example

(2+3i)/(1-2i) multiply by (1+2i)/(1+2i) gives (-4+7i)/5 = -4/5 + (7/5)i.

Practice + Why

  • Compute (4-i)(-2+5i) (sign control).
  • Divide (3+7i)/(2+i) (conjugate routine).

L9. Modulus, Argument, and Argand Geometry

Motivation: links algebra to geometry and unlocks polar form.

Discussion

For z=a+bi, modulus |z|=sqrt(a^2+b^2). Argument is angle from positive real axis (respect quadrant).

Solved Example

For z=-1+i, |z|=sqrt(2), principal argument is 3pi/4.

Practice + Why

  • Find modulus/argument for points in Q2 and Q3 (quadrant consistency).
  • Sketch 3 complex numbers on Argand plane (visual fluency).

L10. Polar Conversion and Multiplication/Division in Polar Form

Motivation: products/divisions become cleaner as modulus-angle operations.

Discussion

z=r(cos theta + i sin theta). Multiply by multiplying moduli and adding angles; divide by dividing moduli and subtracting angles.

Solved Example

2cis(30deg) * 3cis(20deg) = 6cis(50deg). In radians this is 6(cos(5pi/18)+i sin(5pi/18)).

Practice + Why

  • Convert -sqrt(3)+i to polar (conversion accuracy).
  • Compute quotient of two polar numbers and back-convert to a+bi.

L11. De Moivre Powers + Principal Argument Consistency

Motivation: fast powers and angle discipline in one move.

Discussion

(r cis theta)^n = r^n cis(n theta). Reduce resulting angle to chosen principal interval.

Solved Example

(2cis(pi/6))^4 = 16cis(2pi/3). Equivalent rectangular form is -8 + 8sqrt(3)i.

Practice + Why

  • Compute (cis 40deg)^9 (angle wrap handling).
  • Write all equivalent arguments for one complex number (argument family awareness).

L12. n-th Roots and Roots of Unity

Motivation: central for complex equations and pattern recognition.

Discussion

Roots of re^(i theta) are r^(1/n) e^{i(theta+2kpi)/n}, k=0,...,n-1. Roots of unity are equally spaced on unit circle.

Solved Example

Solve z^3=8: roots are 2cis(0), 2cis(2pi/3), 2cis(4pi/3).

Practice + Why

  • Find fourth roots of -16 (multi-root bookkeeping).
  • Sketch fifth roots of unity (geometry memory).

L13. Conjugate-Pair Theorem for Real-Coefficient Polynomials

Motivation: if one complex root appears, the partner is free information.

Discussion

For real coefficients, if a+bi is a root then a-bi is also a root.

Solved Example

If a cubic with real coefficients has roots 2+i and 3, third root is 2-i. Polynomial is (x-3)[(x-2)^2+1].

Practice + Why

  • Construct polynomial from root set including one nonreal root (pair completion).
  • Check if a proposed root set can belong to real-coefficient polynomial.

L14. Vieta for Cubics

Motivation: relation-based solving and validation without full expansion every time.

Discussion

For monic cubic x^3+ax^2+bx+c with roots r,s,t: r+s+t=-a, rs+rt+st=b, rst=-c.

Solved Example

Given roots sum 4, pairwise sum 1, product -6, polynomial is x^3-4x^2+x+6.

Practice + Why

  • Compute 1/r + 1/s + 1/t from Vieta data (derived expressions).
  • Detect impossible root relations quickly.

L15. Vieta for Quartics and Coefficient Sanity Checks

Motivation: avoid expansion mistakes by using symmetric checks.

Discussion

For monic quartic, coefficients alternate signs with elementary symmetric sums of roots.

Solved Example

Roots 1,2,-1,3 give sum 5, pairwise sum 5, triple sum -5, product -6, so x^4-5x^3+5x^2+5x-6.

Practice + Why

  • Fill missing coefficient from partial root data (coefficient reasoning).
  • Use Vieta to verify an expanded quartic (error spotting).

L16. Given One Root: Deflate then Solve Remaining Polynomial

Motivation: many exam questions hide this as the shortest path.

Discussion

Use known root immediately to divide, then solve lower-degree remainder by familiar method.

Solved Example

If x=2 is a root of x^3-2x^2-5x+6, deflation gives x^2-5x+3, so remaining roots are (5+-sqrt(13))/2.

Practice + Why

  • Repeat on a quartic with one known root (workflow endurance).
  • Back-substitute to validate all roots (self-check habit).

L17. Polynomial Sign Charts and Factor-Interval Method

Motivation: needed for inequality and graph behavior questions.

Discussion

Mark real roots on line, test one point per interval, track sign flips (odd multiplicity flips, even stays).

Solved Example

For (x-1)^2(x+2), signs are negative on (-inf,-2), positive on (-2,1), positive on (1,inf).

Practice + Why

  • Solve (x-3)(x+1)^2 <= 0 (inequality from sign chart).
  • Predict sign without plugging many values (efficiency).

L18. Graph Behavior from Multiplicity and End Behavior

Motivation: fast sketching reduces algebra load in interpretation problems.

Discussion

Odd multiplicity crosses axis; even multiplicity touches. Degree parity and leading coefficient decide tails.

Solved Example

f(x)=-(x+1)^2(x-2)^3 has degree 5 with negative leading coefficient: left tail up, right tail down; touch at x=-1, cross at x=2.

Practice + Why

  • Sketch from factored form only (model-based plotting).
  • Infer possible factorization from a rough graph.

L19. Complex Equation Systems in a+bi Form

Motivation: convert one complex equation into two real equations.

Discussion

Set real and imaginary parts equal separately. Solve simultaneous real equations.

Solved Example

If (x+yi)(1+i)=3+5i, then (x-y)+(x+y)i=3+5i so x-y=3, x+y=5, giving x=4, y=1.

Practice + Why

  • Solve one linear and one quadratic complex system (equation translation).
  • Check by substitution into original complex form.

L20. Principal Argument and Quadrant-Consistency Repairs

Motivation: many marks are lost from wrong-angle quadrant errors.

Discussion

Compute reference angle from tan^-1(|b/a|) then correct by quadrant from signs of a,b.

Solved Example

For z=-2-2i, reference is pi/4, Q3 gives principal argument -3pi/4 (or 5pi/4 non-principal).

Practice + Why

  • Find principal arguments for mixed-sign coordinates (quadrant confidence).
  • Convert between (-pi,pi] and [0,2pi).

L21. Complex Quadratics and Imaginary Discriminant Cases

Motivation: extends quadratic routine with zero extra fear.

Discussion

If discriminant is negative, roots are complex conjugates in real-coefficient quadratics.

Solved Example

x^2+4x+13=0 gives D=16-52=-36, roots x=(-4+-6i)/2=-2+-3i.

Practice + Why

  • Solve 3 quadratics with negative discriminant quickly (pattern speed).
  • Write resulting factorization over reals and over complexes.

L22. Modulus and Argument Equations (Argand Locus Basics)

Motivation: geometric interpretation is tested in short but tricky questions.

Discussion

|z-a|=r is a circle centered at a. Fixed argument gives a ray from origin (or shifted point if transformed).

Solved Example

|z-(1+i)|=2 is circle center (1,1), radius 2. arg(z)=pi/4 is the line ray y=x, x>0.

Practice + Why

  • Draw intersection of modulus and argument conditions (locus synthesis).
  • Translate each condition into coordinate equation.

L23. De Moivre Expansion and Simplification Drills

Motivation: avoids repeated binomial expansion in high powers.

Discussion

Convert to polar first, apply De Moivre, then convert back only if asked.

Solved Example

(1+i)^8: write 1+i=sqrt(2)cis(pi/4), so power is (sqrt(2))^8 cis(2pi)=16.

Practice + Why

  • Compute (sqrt(3)-i)^6 by polar route (method locking).
  • Do one problem both polar and algebraic ways, compare steps.

L24. Vieta Reverse: Reconstruct Polynomial from Root-Sum Conditions

Motivation: common in "build equation" and parameter questions.

Discussion

Translate given symmetric root info directly into coefficients via Vieta.

Solved Example

Monic cubic with r+s+t=2, rs+rt+st=-5, rst=6 is x^3-2x^2-5x-6.

Practice + Why

  • Construct a quartic from four symmetric conditions (careful signs).
  • Given one coefficient wrong, repair it from root conditions.

Review and Capstone Lessons (Remaining Tracker Subtopics)

R1. Timed Mixed Quiz + Corrections

Subtopics covered: complex timed quiz, timed mixed quiz, mixed timed paper, strict mixed paper.

Motivation

Exam performance is mostly tool selection under time pressure.

Format

25 min quiz (5 mixed problems) + 20 min corrections + 10 min trigger-card update.

Practice Motivation

Every wrong answer is tagged: concept gap, algebra slip, sign/argument error, or time panic.

R2. Weak-Skill Repair: Roots and Complex Algebra

Subtopics covered: weak-skill repair set, weakest polynomial theorem, weakest complex algebra.

Motivation

Targeted repair beats random extra questions.

Discussion

Pick top two repeated errors from your log and solve 3 focused problems per error type.

Example

If you miss conjugate division signs, do 3 division-only questions with answer-check by reverse multiplication.

R3. Trigger Drills: Theorem Selection and Flash Cues

Subtopics covered: capstone corrections and flash triggers, final flash review cues.

Motivation

Speed comes from recognizing patterns in the first 10 seconds.

Discussion

Build cue cards like "integer coefficients + cubic -> rational root list first".

Practice

Do 12 prompt cards where you state method only, no full solution.

R4. Capstone Summary Sheet

Subtopics covered: capstone summary sheet, one-page master summary, final capstone corrections.

Motivation

Your final page should compress methods, common traps, and one representative worked line per method.

Must Include

  • Vieta signs template (quadratic/cubic/quartic).
  • Complex argument quadrant map.
  • Deflation workflow and check steps.

R5. Hard Set: Complex Arguments and Locus

Subtopics covered: hard-set on complex arguments and locus questions.

Motivation

These are high-yield and easy to lose marks if diagrams are skipped.

Example

Solve and sketch: |z-2|=3 and arg(z-1)=pi/3.

Practice

Three locus intersections with clear coordinate geometry interpretation.

R6. Timed Repair: Polynomial Factor Flow

Subtopics covered: timed mixed repair set for polynomial factor flow.

Motivation

Build a no-hesitation chain: candidates -> test -> deflate -> solve -> verify.

Practice

Two cubics and one quartic, 20-minute cap, each with mandatory back-substitution.
