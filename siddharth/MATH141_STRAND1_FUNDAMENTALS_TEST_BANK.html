Mixed Fundamentals Tests (Non-Trigonometry)

Each paper mixes indices, surds, logs, factorisation, algebraic fractions, equations, and coordinate geometry.

Mixed Test A (60 minutes)

  • Simplify: \((ac^2)^3 \div (a^4c)^2\).
  • Write with positive indices: \(a^{1/2}\times a^{-1}\).
  • Simplify: \(2\sqrt{175}-5\sqrt{63}\).
  • Rationalise: \(\dfrac{1}{\sqrt7+\sqrt3}\).
  • If \(\log_2 3=p,\ \log_2 5=q\), find \(\log_2 150\).
  • Factorise: \(x^2-7x+12\).
  • Simplify: \(\dfrac{1}{x^2-9x+20}+\dfrac{1}{x^2-11x+30}\).
  • Solve: \(10x^2+11x=6\).
  • Find equation of the line through \((1,2)\) and \((-1,3)\). Give gradient and intercepts.

Mixed Test B (60 minutes)

  • Simplify: \(x^{2a+b}\times x^{a-b}\).
  • Evaluate: \((25^{-3/4})^2\).
  • Simplify: \(3\sqrt{32}+2\sqrt{75}-5\sqrt{162}\).
  • Rationalise: \(\dfrac{3}{\sqrt5-2}\).
  • If \(\log_b N=x\), express \(\log_b(\sqrt N)\) in terms of \(x\).
  • Factorise: \(6x^2-7x-3\).
  • Simplify: \(\dfrac{x-1}{x}\div\left(1+\dfrac{1}{x}\right)\).
  • Solve: \(9^{x-4}+4=13^{x-2}\).
  • Find line through \((2,3)\) parallel to \(3x-7y=0\).

Mixed Test C (60 minutes)

  • Simplify: \(\left(\dfrac{a^3}{b^2}\right)^5\).
  • Evaluate: \(125^{2/3}\).
  • Simplify: \((\sqrt6+\sqrt2)^2\).
  • Simplify: \(\dfrac{\sqrt{15}}{\sqrt3}\).
  • Solve for \(y\): \(x+\ln y=\ln(x+y)\).
  • Factorise: \(x^2+4x-21\).
  • Simplify: \(\dfrac{x-3}{x+2}-\dfrac{x-2}{x+3}+\dfrac{1}{x-1}\).
  • Solve: \(y^{2/3}+2y^{1/3}=8\).
  • Find midpoint of \((4,5)\) and \((-2,11)\), then the point dividing \((1,3)\) to \((9,-7)\) in ratio \(3:2\).

Mixed Test D (60 minutes)

  • Given \(x=a^2b\), \(y=a\sqrt[3]{b}\), express \(b\) in terms of \(x,y\).
  • Write with positive indices: \(a^{-2x-1}\div x^{a-1}\).
  • Find \(x\): \(\sqrt{x}=\sqrt{72}-\sqrt8\).
  • Simplify: \(\dfrac{a^3-b^3}{a-b}-\dfrac{a^3+b^3}{a+b}\), then divide by \(\dfrac{4ab}{a^2-b^2}\).
  • Given \(\log_2 3=p,\log_2 5=q\), find \(\log_2(1.8)\).
  • Factorise fully: \(a^{12}-b^{12}\).
  • Solve: \(\dfrac{x-2}{3x+4}=\dfrac{2x-3}{x+1}\).
  • Line through \((1,-4)\) perpendicular to \(4x+y+9=0\).
  • Show \((1,2)\) is equidistant from \(3x+4y-8=0\) and \(4x-3y-1=0\).

Trigonometry-Only Tests (Separate)

This section is intentionally separated from the mixed fundamentals tests.

Trig Test T1 (60 minutes)

  • Verify: \(\sin\!\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta\right)=\cos\theta\), \(\cos(\pi-\theta)=-\cos\theta\), and \(\tan(\pi+\theta)=\tan\theta\).
  • Verify: \((\sin\theta+\cos\theta)^2=1+\sin2\theta\).
  • Find exact: \(\cos\!\left(\frac{5\pi}{12}\right)\), \(\sin\!\left(\frac{\pi}{8}\right)\).
  • If \(\sin\theta=\frac35\), \(\theta\) in QII, find \(\sin2\theta,\cos2\theta,\tan2\theta\).
  • Solve on \(0\le x<2\pi\): \(\cos x=-\frac12,\ \cos2x=-\frac12\).
  • Solve on \(0\le x<2\pi\): \(\sin x=\sin2x,\ \cos x=\cos2x\).
  • Use Law of Cosines for \(b=12,\ c=27,\ A=\frac\pi3\).
  • Use Law of Sines for \(c=10,\ A=\frac\pi4,\ B=\frac\pi3\).

Trig Test T2 (60 minutes)

  • Show: \(\sin3\theta=3\sin\theta-4\sin^3\theta\), \(\cos3\theta=4\cos^3\theta-3\cos\theta\).
  • Show: \(\sin(x+y)\sin(x-y)=\sin^2x-\sin^2y\).
  • From \(8x=5x+3x,\ 2x=5x-3x\), show \(\sin8x\sin2x=\cos^23x-\cos^25x\).
  • Write products as sums: \(\cos\theta\cos3\theta\), \(\sin5\theta\cos3\theta\).
  • Triangle area: \(a=5,\ b=6,\ C=\frac\pi6\).
  • Satellite application: orbit radius \(8000\) km, period \(2\) hours, use given geometry at \(12{:}20\) pm for distance.
  • Satellite beam problem at angle \(\frac\pi3\): find time through beam.
Tip: attempt mixed papers first, then do T1/T2 as focused trig consolidation.
