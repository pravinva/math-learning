0 Related Rates

Related rates problems connect the rate of change of one quantity to the rate of change of another, through an equation linking them. The method is always the same: write down the connecting equation, differentiate both sides with respect to time (using the chain rule for every quantity that depends on $t$), then substitute known values at the instant in question.

Key method

1) Identify all variables and how they're related by an equation. 2) Differentiate that equation with respect to $t$ (every variable gets a $\dfrac{d(\cdot)}{dt}$ term via the chain rule). 3) Substitute the known instantaneous values and solve for the unknown rate.

Worked example

A spherical balloon is being inflated so that its volume increases at $100\ \text{cm}^3/\text{s}$. Find the rate at which the radius is increasing when $r=5$ cm (use $V=\dfrac43\pi r^3$).

Differentiate with respect to $t$: $\dfrac{dV}{dt} = 4\pi r^2\dfrac{dr}{dt}$.

Substitute $\dfrac{dV}{dt}=100$ and $r=5$: $100 = 4\pi(25)\dfrac{dr}{dt} = 100\pi\dfrac{dr}{dt}$.

$$\frac{dr}{dt} = \frac{1}{\pi}\ \text{cm/s} \approx 0.318\ \text{cm/s}.$$

Practice 1

A square's side length is increasing at $3$ cm/s. Find the rate of increase of its area when the side is $10$ cm.

Solution

$A=x^2 \Rightarrow \dfrac{dA}{dt}=2x\dfrac{dx}{dt}$. At $x=10$: $\dfrac{dA}{dt}=2(10)(3)=60\ \text{cm}^2/\text{s}$.

Practice 2

A ladder $5$ m long leans against a wall. The bottom slides away from the wall at $0.5$ m/s. Find how fast the top is sliding down when the bottom is $3$ m from the wall.

Solution

$x^2+y^2=25$. Differentiate: $2x\dfrac{dx}{dt}+2y\dfrac{dy}{dt}=0$. At $x=3$: $y=4$. Substitute: $2(3)(0.5)+2(4)\dfrac{dy}{dt}=0 \Rightarrow 3+8\dfrac{dy}{dt}=0 \Rightarrow \dfrac{dy}{dt}=-\dfrac38=-0.375$ m/s (negative: sliding down).

Practice 3

Gas is pumped into a spherical balloon at a constant rate of $50\ \text{cm}^3/\text{s}$. Find the rate of increase of the surface area ($S=4\pi r^2$) when $r=5$ cm. (Hint: first find $\dfrac{dr}{dt}$ using $V=\tfrac43\pi r^3$.)

Solution

$\dfrac{dV}{dt}=4\pi r^2\dfrac{dr}{dt} \Rightarrow 50=4\pi(25)\dfrac{dr}{dt} \Rightarrow \dfrac{dr}{dt}=\dfrac{1}{2\pi}$. Then $\dfrac{dS}{dt}=8\pi r\dfrac{dr}{dt}=8\pi(5)\left(\dfrac{1}{2\pi}\right)=20\ \text{cm}^2/\text{s}$.

Practice 4 EXAM-STYLE

[Exam-style, multi-part] A conical tank (vertex down) has height $12$ m and top radius $4$ m, and is being filled at $2\ \text{m}^3/\text{min}$. (a) Show that the radius $r$ and depth $h$ of water satisfy $r=\dfrac{h}{3}$. (b) Express the volume $V$ of water in terms of $h$ only. (c) Find the rate at which the water level is rising when $h=6$ m.

Solution

(a) By similar triangles, $\dfrac{r}{h}=\dfrac{4}{12}=\dfrac13 \Rightarrow r=\dfrac{h}{3}$.

(b) $V=\dfrac13\pi r^2h = \dfrac13\pi\left(\dfrac{h}{3}\right)^2h = \dfrac{\pi h^3}{27}$.

(c) $\dfrac{dV}{dt}=\dfrac{\pi h^2}{9}\dfrac{dh}{dt}$. At $h=6$: $2=\dfrac{\pi(36)}{9}\dfrac{dh}{dt}=4\pi\dfrac{dh}{dt} \Rightarrow \dfrac{dh}{dt}=\dfrac{1}{2\pi}\approx0.159$ m/min.

Practice 5

Two ships leave port at the same time, one sailing north at $20$ km/h, the other sailing east at $15$ km/h. Find the rate at which the distance between them is increasing after $2$ hours.

Solution

After $t$ hours, north ship has travelled $20t$, east ship $15t$. Distance $D=\sqrt{(20t)^2+(15t)^2}=\sqrt{400t^2+225t^2}=\sqrt{625t^2}=25t$. So $\dfrac{dD}{dt}=25$ km/h (constant, independent of $t$).

1 Maxima, Minima, and Optimisation

The derivative equals zero (or is undefined) at every local maximum or minimum — this is what makes calculus such a powerful tool for optimisation. Combined with the second derivative test (or a sign analysis of the first derivative), you can reliably tell a maximum from a minimum without ever sketching the curve.

Key tests

Critical points: where $f'(x)=0$ or $f'(x)$ is undefined. First derivative test: if $f'$ changes from $+$ to $-$, it's a local max; $-$ to $+$, a local min. Second derivative test: if $f'(c)=0$ and $f''(c)>0$, local min; if $f''(c)<0$, local max; if $f''(c)=0$, the test is inconclusive.

Worked example

A farmer has $200$ m of fencing to enclose a rectangular paddock against an existing straight wall (so only three sides need fencing). Find the dimensions that maximise the enclosed area.

Let $x$ = length of the two sides perpendicular to the wall, $y$ = length parallel to the wall. Fencing constraint: $2x+y=200 \Rightarrow y=200-2x$.

Area: $A(x) = xy = x(200-2x) = 200x-2x^2$.

$A'(x) = 200-4x$. Set to $0$: $x=50$. $A''(x)=-4<0$: confirms a maximum.

At $x=50$: $y=200-100=100$. Maximum area $=50\times100=5000\ \text{m}^2$, with dimensions $50\text{ m}\times100\text{ m}$.

Practice 6

Find the critical points of $f(x)=x^3-3x^2-9x+5$, and classify each using the second derivative test.

Solution

$f'(x)=3x^2-6x-9=3(x^2-2x-3)=3(x-3)(x+1)$. Critical points $x=3,-1$. $f''(x)=6x-6$. At $x=3$: $f''=12>0$, local min. At $x=-1$: $f''=-12<0$, local max.

Practice 7

Find and classify the critical points of $f(x)=x^4-8x^2$.

Solution

$f'(x)=4x^3-16x=4x(x^2-4)=4x(x-2)(x+2)$. Critical points $x=0,2,-2$. $f''(x)=12x^2-16$. At $x=0$: $f''=-16<0$, local max. At $x=\pm2$: $f''=48-16=32>0$, local min at each.

Practice 8 EXAM-STYLE

[Exam-style, multi-part] A rectangular box (with a square base of side $x$ and no lid) is to have volume $32\ \text{m}^3$. (a) Express the height $h$ in terms of $x$. (b) Show that the total surface area is $S(x)=x^2+\dfrac{128}{x}$. (c) Find the value of $x$ that minimises the surface area, and state the minimum area.

Solution

(a) $V=x^2h=32 \Rightarrow h=\dfrac{32}{x^2}$.

(b) Base area $x^2$, plus four sides each $xh$: $S=x^2+4xh=x^2+4x\left(\dfrac{32}{x^2}\right)=x^2+\dfrac{128}{x}$.

(c) $S'(x)=2x-\dfrac{128}{x^2}$. Set to $0$: $2x^3=128 \Rightarrow x^3=64 \Rightarrow x=4$. $S''(x)=2+\dfrac{256}{x^3}>0$ always, confirming a minimum. $S(4)=16+32=48\ \text{m}^2$.

Practice 9

A company's profit (in thousands of dollars) from selling $x$ hundred units is $P(x)=-2x^3+18x^2-24x$ for $x\ge0$. Find the production level(s) that give a local maximum profit, and state that maximum profit.

Solution

$P'(x)=-6x^2+36x-24=-6(x^2-6x+4)$. Set to $0$: $x^2-6x+4=0 \Rightarrow x=\dfrac{6\pm\sqrt{20}}{2}=3\pm\sqrt5$. $P''(x)=-12x+36$. At $x=3+\sqrt5\approx5.24$: $P''\approx-12.9<0$, local max. $P(3+\sqrt5)$: substituting gives the maximum profit (numerically $\approx24.9$ thousand dollars, i.e. about \$24{,}900).

Practice 10

Find the point on the curve $y=x^2$ closest to the point $(0,3)$, by minimising the squared distance $D(x)=x^2+(x^2-3)^2$.

Solution

$D(x)=x^2+(x^2-3)^2$. $D'(x)=2x+2(x^2-3)(2x)=2x+4x(x^2-3)=2x[1+2(x^2-3)]=2x(2x^2-5)$. Set to $0$: $x=0$ or $x^2=\dfrac52 \Rightarrow x=\pm\sqrt{2.5}$. Checking values: $D(0)=9$; $D(\sqrt{2.5})=2.5+(2.5-3)^2=2.5+0.25=2.75$, smaller. Closest points: $x=\pm\sqrt{2.5}$, giving points $\left(\pm\sqrt{2.5},\,2.5\right)$.

2 Curve Sketching Using Calculus

The first derivative tells you where a function is increasing or decreasing; the second tells you where it's concave up or down (and where it has an inflection point, where concavity switches). Together with intercepts and asymptotes, this is enough to sketch almost any curve accurately without plotting hundreds of points.

Key facts

$f'(x)>0$: increasing. $f'(x)<0$: decreasing. $f''(x)>0$: concave up. $f''(x)<0$: concave down. An inflection point occurs where $f''(x)=0$ (or is undefined) and concavity actually changes sign either side.

Worked example

Analyse $f(x)=x^3-3x^2$ for intervals of increase/decrease and concavity, and identify any inflection point.

$f'(x)=3x^2-6x=3x(x-2)$. Zero at $x=0,2$. Sign test: $f'<0$ on $(0,2)$ (decreasing), $f'>0$ on $(-\infty,0)\cup(2,\infty)$ (increasing).

$f''(x)=6x-6$. Zero at $x=1$. $f''<0$ for $x<1$ (concave down), $f''>0$ for $x>1$ (concave up) — concavity genuinely changes at $x=1$.

Inflection point at $x=1$: $f(1)=1-3=-2$, i.e. $(1,-2)$. Local max at $x=0$ ($f(0)=0$), local min at $x=2$ ($f(2)=8-12=-4$).

Practice 11

Find the intervals of increase and decrease for $f(x)=x^2-4x+1$.

Solution

$f'(x)=2x-4$. Zero at $x=2$. Decreasing on $(-\infty,2)$, increasing on $(2,\infty)$.

Practice 12

Find any inflection point(s) of $f(x)=x^3-6x^2+9x$.

Solution

$f''(x)=6x-12$. Zero at $x=2$. Sign changes either side (negative before, positive after): genuine inflection at $x=2$, $f(2)=8-24+18=2$, point $(2,2)$.

Practice 13

Determine the concavity of $f(x)=e^{-x^2}$ at $x=0$.

Solution

$f'(x)=-2xe^{-x^2}$. $f''(x)=-2e^{-x^2}+(-2x)(-2x)e^{-x^2}=-2e^{-x^2}+4x^2e^{-x^2}=e^{-x^2}(4x^2-2)$. At $x=0$: $f''(0)=1(0-2)=-2<0$. Concave down at $x=0$ (consistent with the bell-curve shape peaking there).

Practice 14 EXAM-STYLE

[Exam-style, multi-part] For $f(x)=\dfrac{1}{3}x^3-x^2-3x+2$: (a) find all critical points and classify each. (b) find any inflection point(s). (c) hence sketch the key features of the curve (state intervals of increase/decrease and concavity).

Solution

(a) $f'(x)=x^2-2x-3=(x-3)(x+1)$. Critical points $x=3,-1$. $f''(x)=2x-2$. At $x=3$: $f''=4>0$, local min. At $x=-1$: $f''=-4<0$, local max.

(b) $f''(x)=0 \Rightarrow x=1$; sign changes ($f''<0$ before, $f''>0$ after): genuine inflection at $x=1$.

(c) Increasing on $(-\infty,-1)\cup(3,\infty)$, decreasing on $(-1,3)$. Concave down on $(-\infty,1)$, concave up on $(1,\infty)$.

Practice 15

A function satisfies $f'(x)>0$ for all $x<2$ and $f'(x)<0$ for all $x>2$, with $f'(2)=0$. Describe the shape of the graph, and classify the point at $x=2$.

Solution

The function increases up to $x=2$ then decreases after: $x=2$ is a local (in fact global, given this holds for all $x$) maximum.

3 Antidifferentiation and the Table of Integrals

An antiderivative reverses differentiation: $F$ is an antiderivative of $f$ if $F'(x)=f(x)$. Since the derivative of a constant is zero, antiderivatives always come with a $+C$ — a whole family of curves, all parallel, sharing the same slope function.

Key table of standard antiderivatives

$$\int x^n\,dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1}+C\ (n\ne-1), \quad \int \frac1x\,dx = \ln|x|+C, \quad \int e^{kx}\,dx=\frac{1}{k}e^{kx}+C,$$ $$\int \sin x\,dx = -\cos x+C, \quad \int\cos x\,dx=\sin x+C, \quad \int\sec^2x\,dx=\tan x+C.$$

Worked example

Find $\displaystyle\int(x^2+3)^2\,dx$.

Expand first: $(x^2+3)^2 = x^4+6x^2+9$.

Integrate term by term: $\displaystyle\int(x^4+6x^2+9)\,dx = \frac{x^5}{5}+6\cdot\frac{x^3}{3}+9x+C$.

$$= \frac{x^5}{5}+2x^3+9x+C.$$

Practice 16

Find $\displaystyle\int(4x^3-2x+5)\,dx$.

Solution

$x^4-x^2+5x+C$.

Practice 17

Find $\displaystyle\int\sin^2x\,dx$ using the identity $\sin^2x=\dfrac{1-\cos2x}{2}$.

Solution

$\displaystyle\int\frac{1-\cos2x}{2}\,dx = \frac{x}{2}-\frac{\sin2x}{4}+C$.

Practice 18

Find $\displaystyle\int x(x^2-5)^{10}\,dx$ (this can be done with the table plus a substitution-style pattern-matching; try letting $u=x^2-5$ mentally).

Solution

Noting $\dfrac{d}{dx}(x^2-5)=2x$: $\displaystyle\int x(x^2-5)^{10}\,dx = \frac12\int(x^2-5)^{10}\cdot2x\,dx = \frac12\cdot\frac{(x^2-5)^{11}}{11}+C = \frac{(x^2-5)^{11}}{22}+C$.

Practice 19

Find $\displaystyle\int\frac{dx}{9+x^2}$ using the standard result $\displaystyle\int\frac{dx}{a^2+x^2}=\frac1a\arctan\!\left(\frac{x}{a}\right)+C$.

Solution

With $a=3$: $\dfrac13\arctan\!\left(\dfrac{x}{3}\right)+C$.

Practice 20

Find $\displaystyle\int\frac{x\,dx}{9+x^2}$.

Solution

Numerator is (up to a constant) the derivative of the denominator: $\dfrac{d}{dx}(9+x^2)=2x$. $\displaystyle\int\frac{x\,dx}{9+x^2}=\frac12\int\frac{2x\,dx}{9+x^2}=\frac12\ln(9+x^2)+C$.

Practice 21

Find $\displaystyle\int 2x\sqrt{3-2x}\,dx$.

Solution

Let $u=3-2x \Rightarrow x=\dfrac{3-u}{2}$, $du=-2\,dx \Rightarrow dx=-\dfrac{du}{2}$. Then $2x\sqrt{3-2x}\,dx = 2\left(\dfrac{3-u}{2}\right)\sqrt{u}\left(-\dfrac{du}{2}\right) = -\dfrac12(3-u)\sqrt{u}\,du = -\dfrac12\left(3u^{1/2}-u^{3/2}\right)du$. Integrating: $-\dfrac12\left(3\cdot\dfrac23u^{3/2}-\dfrac25u^{5/2}\right)+C = -u^{3/2}+\dfrac15u^{5/2}+C$, i.e. $\dfrac15(3-2x)^{5/2}-(3-2x)^{3/2}+C$.

Practice 22

A particle's velocity is $v(t)=6t^2-4t+1$ (m/s). Given the particle starts at position $s(0)=2$ m, find its position function $s(t)$.

Solution

$s(t)=\displaystyle\int(6t^2-4t+1)\,dt = 2t^3-2t^2+t+C$. Using $s(0)=2$: $C=2$. $s(t)=2t^3-2t^2+t+2$.

4 Mixed Exam-Style Review

These questions combine several ideas from this lesson (and earlier ones) in a single multi-part problem, in the style of a real exam question — the kind that tests whether you can chain together several separate skills under time pressure, not just execute one isolated technique.

Practice 23 EXAM-STYLE

[Exam-style, multi-part] Let $f(x)=x^3-6x^2+9x+1$. (a) Find $f'(x)$ and $f''(x)$. (b) Find and classify all critical points. (c) Find any inflection point(s). (d) State the intervals on which $f$ is increasing, decreasing, concave up, and concave down.

Solution

(a) $f'(x)=3x^2-12x+9$, $f''(x)=6x-12$.

(b) $f'(x)=3(x^2-4x+3)=3(x-1)(x-3)$. Critical points $x=1,3$. $f''(1)=-6<0$: local max. $f''(3)=6>0$: local min.

(c) $f''(x)=0 \Rightarrow x=2$, and concavity changes sign there: inflection at $x=2$, $f(2)=8-24+18+1=3$, point $(2,3)$.

(d) Increasing on $(-\infty,1)\cup(3,\infty)$, decreasing on $(1,3)$. Concave down on $(-\infty,2)$, concave up on $(2,\infty)$.

Practice 24 EXAM-STYLE

[Exam-style, multi-part] A rectangular garden bed is to be built against a wall using $40$ m of edging on the three open sides, with the wall forming the fourth side. (a) If $x$ is the length of each of the two sides perpendicular to the wall, express the area $A$ as a function of $x$. (b) Find the value of $x$ maximising $A$, and justify it's a maximum using the second derivative test. (c) State the maximum area.

Solution

(a) Let $y$ be the side parallel to the wall: $2x+y=40 \Rightarrow y=40-2x$. $A(x)=xy=x(40-2x)=40x-2x^2$.

(b) $A'(x)=40-4x$. Zero at $x=10$. $A''(x)=-4<0$: confirms maximum.

(c) $y=40-20=20$. Maximum area $=10\times20=200\ \text{m}^2$.

Practice 25 EXAM-STYLE

[Exam-style, answer only] Evaluate each, writing only the final answer: (a) $\displaystyle\int(3x^2-2x+7)\,dx$. (b) The critical point(s) of $g(x)=x^2-6x+5$. (c) $\displaystyle\int e^{4x}\,dx$. (d) $\lim\limits_{h\to0}\dfrac{(2+h)^2-4}{h}$ (recognise this as a first-principles derivative).

Solution

(a) $x^3-x^2+7x+C$.

(b) $g'(x)=2x-6=0 \Rightarrow x=3$.

(c) $\dfrac14e^{4x}+C$.

(d) This is $f'(2)$ for $f(x)=x^2$, i.e. $2(2)=4$.

Practice 26 EXAM-STYLE

[Exam-style, multi-part] Water drains from a hemispherical bowl of radius $5$ m. When the depth of water is $h$ m, the volume is $V=\pi h^2\left(5-\dfrac{h}{3}\right)$. (a) Find $\dfrac{dV}{dh}$. (b) If water drains at a constant rate of $\dfrac{dV}{dt}=-2\ \text{m}^3/\text{min}$, find $\dfrac{dh}{dt}$ when $h=2$ m.

Solution

(a) $V=5\pi h^2-\dfrac{\pi h^3}{3}$. $\dfrac{dV}{dh}=10\pi h-\pi h^2$.

(b) $\dfrac{dV}{dt}=\dfrac{dV}{dh}\cdot\dfrac{dh}{dt} \Rightarrow -2=(10\pi(2)-\pi(4))\dfrac{dh}{dt}=(20\pi-4\pi)\dfrac{dh}{dt}=16\pi\dfrac{dh}{dt}$. $\dfrac{dh}{dt}=\dfrac{-2}{16\pi}=-\dfrac{1}{8\pi}\approx-0.0398$ m/min.

Practice 27 EXAM-STYLE

[Exam-style, multi-part] Consider $f(x)=\dfrac{1}{4}x^4-\dfrac{2}{3}x^3$. (a) Find all critical points and classify them. (b) Find $\displaystyle\int f(x)\,dx$.

Solution

(a) $f'(x)=x^3-2x^2=x^2(x-2)$. Critical points $x=0,2$. $f''(x)=3x^2-4x$. At $x=2$: $f''=12-8=4>0$, local min. At $x=0$: $f''(0)=0$, inconclusive by the second derivative test; checking the first derivative's sign either side of $0$ (both $x$ slightly negative and slightly positive give $f'<0$, since $x^2\ge0$ and $(x-2)<0$ near $0$): no sign change, so $x=0$ is neither a max nor a min (a stationary inflection point).

(b) $\displaystyle\int f(x)\,dx = \int\left(\frac14x^4-\frac23x^3\right)dx = \frac{x^5}{20}-\frac{x^4}{6}+C$.
