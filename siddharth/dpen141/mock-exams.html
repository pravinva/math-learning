Mock Mid-Sem (60 marks, 90 min)

Focus: Calculus fundamentals + differentiation + intro polar/integration.

Section A

Q1
10 marks

Simplify and rationalise: \( \dfrac{(16x^8)^{3/4}}{2-\sqrt3} \).

Show solution
\((16x^8)^{3/4}=8x^6\). So expression is \(\frac{8x^6}{2-\sqrt3}\cdot\frac{2+\sqrt3}{2+\sqrt3}=8x^6(2+\sqrt3)\).
Q2
10 marks

Decompose \( \dfrac{3x+5}{(x-1)(x+3)} \) into partial fractions.

Show solution
Set \(\frac{A}{x-1}+\frac{B}{x+3}\). Then \(3x+5=A(x+3)+B(x-1)\). With \(x=1\): \(8=4A\Rightarrow A=2\). With \(x=-3\): \(-4=-4B\Rightarrow B=1\). Result: \(\frac{2}{x-1}+\frac{1}{x+3}\).
Q3
12 marks

Use logarithmic differentiation to find \( \dfrac{d}{dx}\left[(x^2+1)^x\right] \).

Show solution
\(\ln y=x\ln(x^2+1)\). So \(\frac{y'}y=\ln(x^2+1)+x\frac{2x}{x^2+1}\). Hence \(y'=(x^2+1)^x\left[\ln(x^2+1)+\frac{2x^2}{x^2+1}\right]\).
Q4
14 marks

For \(r=2\cos(3\theta)\), find area of one petal.

Show solution
One petal: \(\theta\in[-\pi/6,\pi/6]\). \(A=\frac12\int r^2 d\theta=\frac12\int_{-\pi/6}^{\pi/6}4\cos^2(3\theta)\,d\theta=2\int\cos^2(3\theta)\,d\theta\). Use half-angle to get \(A=\pi/3\).
Q5
14 marks

Evaluate \( \int_0^\infty x e^{-x}\,dx \), and state if convergent/divergent.

Show solution
Let \(I(t)=\int_0^t xe^{-x}dx\). Integration by parts gives antiderivative \(-(x+1)e^{-x}\). So \(I=\lim_{t\to\infty}[-(t+1)e^{-t}+1]=1\). Convergent.

Mock Final (100 marks, 120 min)

Full syllabus: all strands including linear algebra and vector geometry.

Section A: Differentiation + Integration

Q1
16 marks

Evaluate \( \lim_{x\to0}\dfrac{\sin(5x)}{x} \) and \( \dfrac{d}{dx}\arctan(4x^3) \).

Show solution
\(\lim=5\). Derivative: \(\frac{12x^2}{1+16x^6}\).
Q2
18 marks

Using trig substitution, evaluate \( \int\sqrt{9-x^2}\,dx \).

Show solution
With \(x=3\sin\theta\), \(dx=3\cos\theta\,d\theta\): integral becomes \(9\int\cos^2\theta\,d\theta\). Final result: \(\frac{x}{2}\sqrt{9-x^2}+\frac92\arcsin\left(\frac x3\right)+C\).
Q3
16 marks

Given parametric equations \(x=t^2+1,\ y=t^3-3t\), find \(dy/dx\), and points where tangent is horizontal.

Show solution
\(\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{3t^2-3}{2t}\). Horizontal: \(dy/dt=0\Rightarrow t=\pm1\). Points: \((2,-2)\) and \((2,2)\).

Section B: Linear Algebra

Q4
20 marks

Solve the system via Gaussian elimination: \(x+2y-z=3,\ 2x+3y+z=4,\ x+y+2z=1\). Classify solution type.

Show solution
Row reduction gives one free variable. Let \(z=t\): \(y=2+3t,\ x=-1-5t\). Infinitely many solutions (one free variable).
Q5
15 marks

Find eigenvalues and eigenvectors of \(A=\begin{bmatrix}3&1\\0&2\end{bmatrix}\).

Show solution
Characteristic \((3-\lambda)(2-\lambda)=0\Rightarrow \lambda=3,2\). For \(\lambda=3\): eigenvector \([1,0]^T\). For \(\lambda=2\): eigenvector \([-1,1]^T\).

Section C: Vector Geometry

Q6
15 marks

For \(\mathbf a=(1,2,-1)\), \(\mathbf b=(3,0,2)\), find (i) \(\mathbf a\cdot\mathbf b\), (ii) angle between vectors, (iii) \(\mathbf a\times\mathbf b\).

Show solution
Dot = 1. \(|a|=\sqrt6,\ |b|=\sqrt{13}\), so \(\cos\theta=1/\sqrt{78}\), \(\theta\approx83.5^\circ\). Cross product \((4,-5,-6)\).
