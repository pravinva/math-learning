0 Vectors: Magnitude, Direction, and Position

A vector carries both a size (magnitude) and a direction — unlike a scalar, which is just a number. In 3D, $\mathbf{v}=(v_1,v_2,v_3)$ (or $v_1\mathbf{i}+v_2\mathbf{j}+v_3\mathbf{k}$) describes displacement along each coordinate axis.

Key formula

Magnitude: $|\mathbf{v}| = \sqrt{v_1^2+v_2^2+v_3^2}$. A position vector $\overrightarrow{OP}$ points from the origin to a point $P$. The vector from point $A$ to point $B$ is $\overrightarrow{AB} = \overrightarrow{OB}-\overrightarrow{OA} = B-A$ (subtract coordinates).

Worked example

For $A=(1,-2,3)$ and $B=(4,0,-1)$, find $\overrightarrow{AB}$ and its magnitude.

$\overrightarrow{AB} = B-A = (4-1,\ 0-(-2),\ -1-3) = (3,2,-4)$.

$|\overrightarrow{AB}| = \sqrt{9+4+16}=\sqrt{29}$.

Practice 1

Find the magnitude of $\mathbf{v}=(2,-3,6)$.

Solution

$|\mathbf{v}|=\sqrt{4+9+36}=\sqrt{49}=7$.

Practice 2

For $A=(2,1,-1)$ and $B=(5,-2,3)$, find $\overrightarrow{AB}$.

Solution

$\overrightarrow{AB}=(5-2,-2-1,3-(-1))=(3,-3,4)$.

Practice 3

Find a unit vector in the same direction as $\mathbf{v}=(3,4,0)$.

Solution

$|\mathbf{v}|=\sqrt{9+16}=5$. Unit vector: $\dfrac15(3,4,0)=(0.6,0.8,0)$.

Practice 4

A drone's position vector is $\mathbf{r}=(10,20,15)$ (metres). Find how far it is from the origin (its launch point).

Solution

$|\mathbf{r}|=\sqrt{100+400+225}=\sqrt{725}\approx26.9$ m.

Practice 5

Find the value(s) of $k$ for which $(k,3,4)$ has magnitude $\sqrt{50}$.

Solution

$k^2+9+16=50 \Rightarrow k^2=25 \Rightarrow k=\pm5$.

1 Vector Arithmetic

Vectors add, subtract, and scale entrywise, just like matrices — there's genuinely nothing new here mechanically, only a new geometric picture (tip-to-tail addition, scaling stretches or reverses direction).

Key idea

$\mathbf{a}+\mathbf{b}$ adds entrywise; $c\mathbf{a}$ scales every entry by $c$ (reversing direction if $c<0$). Two nonzero vectors are parallel exactly when one is a scalar multiple of the other.

Worked example

For $\mathbf{a}=(1,2,-1)$ and $\mathbf{b}=(3,-1,2)$, find $2\mathbf{a}-3\mathbf{b}$, and determine whether $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$ are parallel.

$2\mathbf{a}=(2,4,-2)$. $3\mathbf{b}=(9,-3,6)$. $2\mathbf{a}-3\mathbf{b}=(2-9,4-(-3),-2-6)=(-7,7,-8)$.

For parallel vectors, every ratio $\dfrac{a_i}{b_i}$ must match: $\dfrac13,\ \dfrac{2}{-1}=-2,\ \dfrac{-1}{2}$ — all different, so not parallel.

Practice 6

For $\mathbf{a}=(2,-1,3)$ and $\mathbf{b}=(1,4,-2)$, find $\mathbf{a}+\mathbf{b}$ and $\mathbf{a}-2\mathbf{b}$.

Solution

$\mathbf{a}+\mathbf{b}=(3,3,1)$. $\mathbf{a}-2\mathbf{b}=(2-2,-1-8,3+4)=(0,-9,7)$.

Practice 7

Determine whether $\mathbf{u}=(2,-4,6)$ and $\mathbf{v}=(-1,2,-3)$ are parallel.

Solution

$\mathbf{u}=-2\mathbf{v}$ (check: $-2(-1,2,-3)=(2,-4,6)$✓). Parallel (and pointing in opposite directions, since the scalar is negative).

Practice 8

Find the value of $k$ for which $(2,k,6)$ is parallel to $(1,3,k)$.

Solution

Parallel requires each entry of the first vector to equal the same constant $c$ times the corresponding entry of the second: $2=c(1),\ k=c(3),\ 6=c(k)$. From the first, $c=2$. Then $k=3c=6$. Checking the third: $6=c\cdot k = 2(6)=12$ — this fails, so $k=6$ does not actually work. Trying the other ordering ($6=ck$ solved for $c$ first: $c=6/k$) and combining with $k=3c=18/k \Rightarrow k^2=18 \Rightarrow k=\pm3\sqrt2$, then checking $c=2$ from the first equation must also hold: $2=6/k \Rightarrow k=3$, which doesn't match $\pm3\sqrt2$ either. All three conditions can only be satisfied simultaneously if $k=6$, since two of the three ratios (first and second entries) fully determine $c=2$ and $k=6$; the third entry then requires $6=2k=12$, which is false. So there is in fact no value of $k$ that makes these two vectors parallel.

Practice 9

A boat's velocity relative to water is $\mathbf{v}_b=(4,3)$ km/h, and the water current is $\mathbf{v}_c=(-1,2)$ km/h. Find the boat's velocity relative to the ground, $\mathbf{v}_b+\mathbf{v}_c$, and its speed.

Solution

$\mathbf{v}_b+\mathbf{v}_c=(3,5)$ km/h. Speed $=\sqrt{9+25}=\sqrt{34}\approx5.83$ km/h.

2 The Dot Product

The dot product turns two vectors into a single number that measures how much they point in the same direction — it's the key tool for finding angles between vectors and testing perpendicularity.

Key formulas

$$\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b} = a_1b_1+a_2b_2+a_3b_3 = |\mathbf{a}||\mathbf{b}|\cos\theta.$$

$\mathbf{a}\perp\mathbf{b} \iff \mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b}=0$. The angle between two vectors: $\cos\theta = \dfrac{\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b}}{|\mathbf{a}||\mathbf{b}|}$.

Worked example

Find the angle between $\mathbf{a}=(1,2,2)$ and $\mathbf{b}=(2,-1,2)$.

$\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b} = 1(2)+2(-1)+2(2) = 2-2+4=4$.

$|\mathbf{a}|=\sqrt{1+4+4}=3$, $\ |\mathbf{b}|=\sqrt{4+1+4}=3$.

$\cos\theta=\dfrac{4}{3\times3}=\dfrac49 \ \Rightarrow\ \theta=\cos^{-1}\!\left(\dfrac49\right)\approx63.6^\circ$.

Practice 10

Find $\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b}$ for $\mathbf{a}=(3,-2,1)$ and $\mathbf{b}=(1,4,2)$.

Solution

$3(1)+(-2)(4)+1(2)=3-8+2=-3$.

Practice 11

Determine whether $\mathbf{u}=(2,3,-1)$ and $\mathbf{v}=(1,-1,-1)$ are perpendicular.

Solution

$\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v}=2(1)+3(-1)+(-1)(-1)=2-3+1=0$. Perpendicular.

Practice 12

Find the value of $k$ for which $(2,k,1)$ and $(3,-1,2)$ are perpendicular.

Solution

$2(3)+k(-1)+1(2)=0 \Rightarrow 6-k+2=0 \Rightarrow k=8$.

Practice 13

Find the angle between $\mathbf{a}=(1,0,1)$ and $\mathbf{b}=(0,1,1)$, to the nearest degree.

Solution

$\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b}=0+0+1=1$. $|\mathbf{a}|=\sqrt2$, $|\mathbf{b}|=\sqrt2$. $\cos\theta=\dfrac{1}{2} \Rightarrow \theta=60^\circ$.

Practice 14

A force $\mathbf{F}=(10,5,-3)$ N acts on an object that moves along displacement $\mathbf{d}=(4,0,2)$ m. Find the work done, $W=\mathbf{F}\cdot\mathbf{d}$.

Solution

$W = 10(4)+5(0)+(-3)(2) = 40+0-6=34$ J.

Practice 15

Find the projection (scalar component) of $\mathbf{a}=(3,4,0)$ onto $\mathbf{b}=(1,0,0)$.

Solution

Scalar projection $=\dfrac{\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b}}{|\mathbf{b}|}=\dfrac{3}{1}=3$.

3 The Cross Product

Unlike the dot product (which gives a scalar), the cross product $\mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b}$ produces a new vector, perpendicular to both $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$, whose magnitude equals the area of the parallelogram they span. It only makes sense in three dimensions.

Key formula

$$\mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b} = \det\begin{pmatrix}\mathbf{i}&\mathbf{j}&\mathbf{k}\\a_1&a_2&a_3\\b_1&b_2&b_3\end{pmatrix} = (a_2b_3-a_3b_2)\mathbf{i}-(a_1b_3-a_3b_1)\mathbf{j}+(a_1b_2-a_2b_1)\mathbf{k}.$$

$|\mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b}| = |\mathbf{a}||\mathbf{b}|\sin\theta$ = area of the parallelogram with sides $\mathbf{a},\mathbf{b}$. $\mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b}=\mathbf{0}$ exactly when $\mathbf{a},\mathbf{b}$ are parallel.

Worked example

Find $\mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b}$ for $\mathbf{a}=(1,2,-1)$ and $\mathbf{b}=(3,0,2)$, and find the area of the parallelogram they span.

$\mathbf{i}$-component: $(2)(2)-(-1)(0)=4-0=4$. $\mathbf{j}$-component: $-\big[(1)(2)-(-1)(3)\big]=-(2+3)=-5$. $\mathbf{k}$-component: $(1)(0)-(2)(3)=0-6=-6$.

$\mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b}=(4,-5,-6)$.

Area $=|\mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b}|=\sqrt{16+25+36}=\sqrt{77}$.

Practice 16

Find $\mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b}$ for $\mathbf{a}=(1,0,0)$ and $\mathbf{b}=(0,1,0)$, and identify the result.

Solution

$\mathbf{i}$: $(0)(0)-(0)(1)=0$. $\mathbf{j}$: $-[(1)(0)-(0)(0)]=0$. $\mathbf{k}$: $(1)(1)-(0)(0)=1$. Result: $(0,0,1)=\mathbf{k}$, as expected ($\mathbf{i}\times\mathbf{j}=\mathbf{k}$).

Practice 17

Find $\mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b}$ for $\mathbf{a}=(2,1,-1)$ and $\mathbf{b}=(1,-1,2)$.

Solution

$\mathbf{i}$: $(1)(2)-(-1)(-1)=2-1=1$. $\mathbf{j}$: $-[(2)(2)-(-1)(1)]=-(4+1)=-5$. $\mathbf{k}$: $(2)(-1)-(1)(1)=-2-1=-3$. Result: $(1,-5,-3)$.

Practice 18

Find the area of the parallelogram spanned by $\mathbf{a}=(3,0,0)$ and $\mathbf{b}=(0,4,0)$, first using the cross product, then confirm it matches simple geometry.

Solution

$\mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b}$: $\mathbf{k}$-component $=(3)(4)-(0)(0)=12$; other components $0$. $|\mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b}|=12$. Matches: this is just a $3\times4$ rectangle, area $12$. ✓

Practice 19

Use the cross product to find a vector perpendicular to both $\mathbf{u}=(1,1,1)$ and $\mathbf{v}=(1,2,3)$.

Solution

$\mathbf{i}$: $(1)(3)-(1)(2)=1$. $\mathbf{j}$: $-[(1)(3)-(1)(1)]=-2$. $\mathbf{k}$: $(1)(2)-(1)(1)=1$. Result: $(1,-2,1)$, perpendicular to both.

Practice 20

Find the area of the triangle with vertices $A=(0,0,0)$, $B=(2,0,0)$, $C=(0,3,0)$, using $\overrightarrow{AB}\times\overrightarrow{AC}$.

Solution

$\overrightarrow{AB}=(2,0,0)$, $\overrightarrow{AC}=(0,3,0)$. Cross product: $\mathbf{k}$-component $=(2)(3)-(0)(0)=6$; others $0$. $|\overrightarrow{AB}\times\overrightarrow{AC}|=6$. Triangle area is half the parallelogram area: $\dfrac62=3$.
