Subtopic 1: Core + Substitution (10)

Why this matters

Most integration questions begin with one decision: is this directly integrable or does it need a quick substitution? This is the base layer for every harder section.

Recognition pattern

Use direct rules when terms already look like \(x^n\), \(e^{ax}\), \(\sin(ax)\), \(\cos(ax)\). Use substitution when you see an inner expression and its derivative nearby, e.g. \((x^2+7)\) with \(x\), or \((3x-1)\) inside trig/exp roots.

\[ \int f(g(x))g'(x)\,dx=\int f(u)\,du,\qquad u=g(x) \]

Worked Example A (pure substitution)

\[ \int \frac{2x}{x^2+5}\,dx,\quad u=x^2+5,\ du=2x\,dx \]
\[ \int \frac{2x}{x^2+5}\,dx=\int \frac{1}{u}\,du=\ln|u|+C=\ln(x^2+5)+C \]

Worked Example B (chain-rule reverse with trig)

\[ \int \cos(4x-3)\,dx,\quad u=4x-3,\ du=4\,dx,\ dx=\frac{1}{4}du \]
\[ \int \cos(4x-3)\,dx=\frac14\int \cos u\,du=\frac14\sin u + C = \frac14\sin(4x-3)+C \]
Things to remember: in substitution, change everything to \(u\), integrate, then substitute back. Always include \(+C\) for indefinite integrals.

Practice Set (10)

  1. \(\int (4x^3-5x+2)\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \int (4x^3-5x+2)\,dx =\int 4x^3\,dx-\int 5x\,dx+\int 2\,dx \] \[ =4\cdot\frac{x^4}{4}-5\cdot\frac{x^2}{2}+2x+C =x^4-\frac{5x^2}{2}+2x+C. \]
  2. \(\int \frac{3x}{x^2+7}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ u=x^2+7,\quad du=2x\,dx,\quad 3x\,dx=\frac32\,du. \] \[ \int \frac{3x}{x^2+7}\,dx =\frac32\int \frac1u\,du =\frac32\ln|u|+C =\frac32\ln(x^2+7)+C. \]
  3. \(\int e^{5x}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \text{Use } \int e^{ax}\,dx=\frac1a e^{ax}+C. \] \[ \int e^{5x}\,dx=\frac15 e^{5x}+C. \]
  4. \(\int \sin(3x)\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ u=3x,\ du=3\,dx,\ dx=\frac13du. \] \[ \int \sin(3x)\,dx=\frac13\int \sin u\,du =\frac13(-\cos u)+C =-\frac13\cos(3x)+C. \]
  5. \(\int \frac{2x+1}{x^2+x+4}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ u=x^2+x+4,\quad du=(2x+1)\,dx. \] \[ \int \frac{2x+1}{x^2+x+4}\,dx =\int \frac1u\,du =\ln|u|+C =\ln(x^2+x+4)+C. \]
  6. \(\int x\sqrt{x^2+9}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ u=x^2+9,\quad du=2x\,dx,\quad x\,dx=\frac12du. \] \[ \int x\sqrt{x^2+9}\,dx =\frac12\int u^{1/2}\,du =\frac12\cdot\frac{u^{3/2}}{3/2}+C =\frac13u^{3/2}+C =\frac13(x^2+9)^{3/2}+C. \]
  7. \(\int \frac{1}{x}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \int \frac1x\,dx=\ln|x|+C. \] (Absolute value is required because \(x\) can be positive or negative.)
  8. \(\int \cos(2x-1)\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ u=2x-1,\ du=2\,dx,\ dx=\frac12du. \] \[ \int \cos(2x-1)\,dx=\frac12\int \cos u\,du =\frac12\sin u + C =\frac12\sin(2x-1)+C. \]
  9. \(\int \frac{x^2}{x^3+1}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ u=x^3+1,\quad du=3x^2\,dx,\quad x^2\,dx=\frac13du. \] \[ \int \frac{x^2}{x^3+1}\,dx =\frac13\int \frac1u\,du =\frac13\ln|u|+C =\frac13\ln(x^3+1)+C. \]
  10. \(\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-4x^2}}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ u=2x,\ du=2\,dx,\ dx=\frac12du. \] \[ \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-4x^2}}\,dx =\frac12\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-u^2}}\,du =\frac12\arcsin(u)+C =\frac12\arcsin(2x)+C. \]

Subtopic 2: Integration by Parts (10)

Why this matters

By-parts is for products where substitution does not cleanly collapse the integrand. You trade one integral for another that is simpler.

\[ \int u\,dv = uv - \int v\,du \]

Choosing \(u\) and \(dv\)

Pick \(u\) as the term that becomes simpler after differentiation (like \(\ln x\), \(\arctan x\), polynomial). Pick \(dv\) as the part you can integrate reliably (like \(e^x,\sin x,\cos x\)).

Worked Example A

\[ \int x e^x\,dx,\quad u=x,\ dv=e^x dx,\ du=dx,\ v=e^x \]
\[ \int x e^x\,dx = xe^x-\int e^x\,dx = xe^x-e^x+C=e^x(x-1)+C \]

Worked Example B (log integral)

\[ \int \ln x\,dx,\quad u=\ln x,\ dv=dx,\ du=\frac1x dx,\ v=x \]
\[ \int \ln x\,dx = x\ln x-\int x\cdot\frac1x dx = x\ln x-\int 1\,dx = x\ln x-x+C \]
Things to remember: if the new integral is still a product, apply by-parts again. For \(\int e^x\sin x\,dx\), two rounds lead to an equation you solve algebraically.

Practice Set (10)

  1. \(\int x e^x\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ u=x,\ dv=e^x dx,\ du=dx,\ v=e^x. \] \[ \int x e^x\,dx=xe^x-\int e^x\,dx =xe^x-e^x+C=e^x(x-1)+C. \]
  2. \(\int x\cos x\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ u=x,\ dv=\cos x\,dx,\ du=dx,\ v=\sin x. \] \[ \int x\cos x\,dx=x\sin x-\int \sin x\,dx =x\sin x+\cos x+C. \]
  3. \(\int \ln x\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ u=\ln x,\ dv=dx,\ du=\frac1x dx,\ v=x. \] \[ \int \ln x\,dx=x\ln x-\int x\cdot\frac1x dx =x\ln x-\int 1\,dx =x\ln x-x+C. \]
  4. \(\int x^2 e^{2x}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ I=\int x^2e^{2x}dx,\ u=x^2,\ dv=e^{2x}dx,\ du=2x\,dx,\ v=\frac12e^{2x}. \] \[ I=\frac12x^2e^{2x}-\int x e^{2x}dx. \] Let \(J=\int x e^{2x}dx\). Then \[ J=\frac12xe^{2x}-\frac12\int e^{2x}dx =\frac12xe^{2x}-\frac14e^{2x}. \] So \[ I=\frac12x^2e^{2x}-\left(\frac12xe^{2x}-\frac14e^{2x}\right) =\frac{e^{2x}}{4}(2x^2-2x+1)+C. \]
  5. \(\int x\ln x\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ u=\ln x,\ dv=x\,dx,\ du=\frac1x dx,\ v=\frac{x^2}{2}. \] \[ \int x\ln x\,dx=\frac{x^2}{2}\ln x-\int \frac{x^2}{2}\cdot\frac1x\,dx =\frac{x^2}{2}\ln x-\frac12\int x\,dx =\frac{x^2}{2}\ln x-\frac{x^2}{4}+C. \]
  6. \(\int e^x\sin x\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ I=\int e^x\sin x\,dx. \] By parts with \(u=\sin x,\ dv=e^x dx\): \[ I=e^x\sin x-\int e^x\cos x\,dx. \] Let \(K=\int e^x\cos x\,dx\). By parts again: \[ K=e^x\cos x+\int e^x\sin x\,dx=e^x\cos x+I. \] Hence \[ I=e^x\sin x-(e^x\cos x+I)\Rightarrow 2I=e^x(\sin x-\cos x). \] \[ I=\frac{e^x}{2}(\sin x-\cos x)+C. \]
  7. \(\int x^2\cos x\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ I=\int x^2\cos x\,dx,\ u=x^2,\ dv=\cos x\,dx. \] \[ I=x^2\sin x-\int 2x\sin x\,dx. \] Let \(J=\int x\sin x\,dx\), then \[ J=-x\cos x+\int \cos x\,dx=-x\cos x+\sin x. \] So \[ I=x^2\sin x-2J =x^2\sin x-2(-x\cos x+\sin x) =x^2\sin x+2x\cos x-2\sin x+C. \]
  8. \(\int x\arctan x\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ u=\arctan x,\ dv=x\,dx,\ du=\frac{1}{1+x^2}dx,\ v=\frac{x^2}{2}. \] \[ \int x\arctan x\,dx =\frac{x^2}{2}\arctan x-\frac12\int \frac{x^2}{1+x^2}dx. \] \[ \frac{x^2}{1+x^2}=1-\frac{1}{1+x^2}. \] \[ \int x\arctan x\,dx =\frac{x^2}{2}\arctan x-\frac12\int\left(1-\frac{1}{1+x^2}\right)dx \] \[ =\frac{x^2}{2}\arctan x-\frac{x}{2}+\frac12\arctan x+C =\frac{x^2+1}{2}\arctan x-\frac{x}{2}+C. \]
  9. \(\int \ln(1+x)\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ u=\ln(1+x),\ dv=dx,\ du=\frac{1}{1+x}dx,\ v=x. \] \[ \int \ln(1+x)\,dx=x\ln(1+x)-\int \frac{x}{1+x}dx. \] \[ \frac{x}{1+x}=1-\frac{1}{1+x}. \] \[ \int \ln(1+x)\,dx=x\ln(1+x)-\int\left(1-\frac1{1+x}\right)dx \] \[ =x\ln(1+x)-x+\ln(1+x)+C =(x+1)\ln(1+x)-x+C. \]
  10. \(\int x^3 e^x\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ I=\int x^3e^x\,dx. \] Repeated by-parts reduces polynomial degree: \[ I=x^3e^x-3\int x^2e^x\,dx. \] Let \(J=\int x^2e^x\,dx\). Then \[ J=x^2e^x-2\int xe^x\,dx =x^2e^x-2(xe^x-e^x). \] So \[ J=e^x(x^2-2x+2). \] Therefore \[ I=x^3e^x-3e^x(x^2-2x+2) =e^x(x^3-3x^2+6x-6)+C. \]

Subtopic 3: Partial Fractions (10)

Why this matters

Rational functions are often not directly integrable. Partial fractions breaks one hard fraction into simple pieces you can integrate with log/arctan rules.

Setup pattern

Step 1: ensure numerator degree is lower than denominator degree (divide first if needed). Step 2: factor denominator. Step 3: assign constants to each factor block. Step 4: solve constants and integrate each term.

Worked Example A (distinct linear factors)

\[ \int \frac{5}{(x-1)(x+2)}\,dx \]
\[ \frac{5}{(x-1)(x+2)}=\frac{A}{x-1}+\frac{B}{x+2} \]
\[ 5=A(x+2)+B(x-1) \]
\[ x=1\Rightarrow A=\frac{5}{3},\quad x=-2\Rightarrow B=-\frac{5}{3} \]
\[ \int \frac{5}{(x-1)(x+2)}dx=\frac53\ln|x-1|-\frac53\ln|x+2|+C \]

Worked Example B (repeated linear factor)

\[ \int \frac{3x+1}{(x+1)^2}\,dx,\quad \frac{3x+1}{(x+1)^2}=\frac{A}{x+1}+\frac{B}{(x+1)^2} \]
\[ 3x+1=A(x+1)+B\Rightarrow A=3,\ B=-2 \]
\[ \int \left(\frac{3}{x+1}-\frac{2}{(x+1)^2}\right)dx=3\ln|x+1|+\frac{2}{x+1}+C \]
Things to remember: linear factor gives logs; irreducible quadratic terms usually lead to \(\arctan\). Keep absolute values on log terms.

Practice Set (10)

  1. \(\int \frac{1}{x^2-1}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \frac{1}{x^2-1}=\frac{1}{(x-1)(x+1)}=\frac{A}{x-1}+\frac{B}{x+1}. \] \[ 1=A(x+1)+B(x-1). \] Put \(x=1\Rightarrow A=\frac12\), \(x=-1\Rightarrow B=-\frac12\). \[ \int\frac{1}{x^2-1}dx =\frac12\int\frac{1}{x-1}dx-\frac12\int\frac{1}{x+1}dx =\frac12\ln\left|\frac{x-1}{x+1}\right|+C. \]
  2. \(\int \frac{3x+5}{x^2+x-2}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ x^2+x-2=(x+2)(x-1),\quad \frac{3x+5}{(x+2)(x-1)}=\frac{A}{x+2}+\frac{B}{x-1}. \] \[ 3x+5=A(x-1)+B(x+2). \] \(x=1\Rightarrow 8=3B\Rightarrow B=\frac83\), \(x=-2\Rightarrow -1=-3A\Rightarrow A=\frac13\). \[ \int \frac{3x+5}{x^2+x-2}dx =\frac13\ln|x+2|+\frac83\ln|x-1|+C. \] Equivalent log forms are acceptable.
  3. \(\int \frac{x+1}{x(x+4)}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \frac{x+1}{x(x+4)}=\frac{A}{x}+\frac{B}{x+4},\quad x+1=A(x+4)+Bx. \] \[ x+1=(A+B)x+4A \Rightarrow \begin{cases} A+B=1\\ 4A=1 \end{cases} \Rightarrow A=\frac14,\ B=\frac34. \] \[ \int \frac{x+1}{x(x+4)}dx =\frac14\ln|x|+\frac34\ln|x+4|+C. \]
  4. \(\int \frac{2x+1}{x^2+3x+2}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ x^2+3x+2=(x+1)(x+2),\quad \frac{2x+1}{(x+1)(x+2)}=\frac{A}{x+1}+\frac{B}{x+2}. \] \[ 2x+1=A(x+2)+B(x+1). \] \(x=-1\Rightarrow -1=A\), \(x=-2\Rightarrow -3=-B\Rightarrow B=3\). \[ \int \frac{2x+1}{x^2+3x+2}dx =-\ln|x+1|+3\ln|x+2|+C. \]
  5. \(\int \frac{5x+7}{(x+1)(x+3)}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \frac{5x+7}{(x+1)(x+3)}=\frac{A}{x+1}+\frac{B}{x+3}. \] \[ 5x+7=A(x+3)+B(x+1). \] \(x=-1\Rightarrow 2=2A\Rightarrow A=1\), \(x=-3\Rightarrow -8=-2B\Rightarrow B=4\). \[ \int \frac{5x+7}{(x+1)(x+3)}dx=\ln|x+1|+4\ln|x+3|+C. \]
  6. \(\int \frac{1}{x(x-2)}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \frac{1}{x(x-2)}=\frac{A}{x}+\frac{B}{x-2},\quad 1=A(x-2)+Bx. \] \(x=0\Rightarrow 1=-2A\Rightarrow A=-\frac12\), \(x=2\Rightarrow 1=2B\Rightarrow B=\frac12\). \[ \int \frac{1}{x(x-2)}dx=-\frac12\ln|x|+\frac12\ln|x-2|+C. \]
  7. \(\int \frac{x^2+1}{x(x^2+1)}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \frac{x^2+1}{x(x^2+1)}=\frac{1}{x}. \] \[ \int \frac{x^2+1}{x(x^2+1)}\,dx=\int \frac1x\,dx=\ln|x|+C. \]
  8. \(\int \frac{1}{(x+1)^2}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \int (x+1)^{-2}dx=\frac{(x+1)^{-1}}{-1}+C=-\frac1{x+1}+C. \]
  9. \(\int \frac{4x+1}{(2x-1)(x+2)}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \frac{4x+1}{(2x-1)(x+2)}=\frac{A}{2x-1}+\frac{B}{x+2}. \] \[ 4x+1=A(x+2)+B(2x-1). \] Set \(x=\frac12\): \(3=A\cdot\frac52\Rightarrow A=\frac65\). Set \(x=-2\): \(-7=-5B\Rightarrow B=\frac75\). \[ \int \frac{4x+1}{(2x-1)(x+2)}dx =\frac65\int\frac{1}{2x-1}dx+\frac75\int\frac{1}{x+2}dx \] \[ =\frac65\cdot\frac12\ln|2x-1|+\frac75\ln|x+2|+C =\frac35\ln|2x-1|+\frac75\ln|x+2|+C. \]
  10. \(\int \frac{3x^2+2x+4}{x(x^2+1)}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \frac{3x^2+2x+4}{x(x^2+1)}=\frac{A}{x}+\frac{Bx+C}{x^2+1}. \] \[ 3x^2+2x+4=A(x^2+1)+x(Bx+C)=(A+B)x^2+Cx+A. \] Match coefficients: \[ A=4,\ C=2,\ A+B=3\Rightarrow B=-1. \] So \[ \int \frac{3x^2+2x+4}{x(x^2+1)}dx =\int\left(\frac4x+\frac{-x+2}{x^2+1}\right)dx \] \[ =4\ln|x|-\frac12\ln(x^2+1)+2\arctan x+C. \]

Subtopic 4: Definite Integrals + Area (10)

Why this matters

Definite integrals are how calculus turns rates into total quantities (distance, accumulated charge, volume, area). For area between curves, the setup is usually more important than the integration.

Setup pattern

\[ \int_a^b f(x)\,dx = F(b)-F(a),\qquad \text{(Fundamental Theorem)} \]
\[ \text{Area between curves}=\int_a^b\left(y_{\text{top}}-y_{\text{bottom}}\right)\,dx \]

Worked Example A (definite integral)

\[ \int_0^2 (3x^2-1)\,dx=[x^3-x]_0^2=(8-2)-0=6 \]

Worked Example B (area between two curves)

\[ \text{Find area between }y=x\text{ and }y=x^2\text{ on }[0,1] \]
\[ \text{On }[0,1],\ x>x^2\Rightarrow A=\int_0^1(x-x^2)\,dx \]
\[ A=\left[\frac{x^2}{2}-\frac{x^3}{3}\right]_0^1=\frac12-\frac13=\frac16 \]
Things to remember: if graph crosses, split interval at intersections; area must be non-negative; signed integrals can be zero even when geometric area is not.

Practice Set (10)

  1. \(\int_0^2 (3x^2-1)\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \int_0^2 (3x^2-1)\,dx=[x^3-x]_0^2=(8-2)-(0-0)=6. \]
  2. \(\int_0^\pi \sin x\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \int_0^\pi \sin x\,dx=[-\cos x]_0^\pi=(-\cos\pi)-(-\cos0)=1-(-1)=2. \]
  3. Area between \(y=x\) and \(y=x^2\) on \([0,1]\)
    Show answer
    \[ \text{On }[0,1],\ x\ge x^2\Rightarrow A=\int_0^1(x-x^2)\,dx \] \[ =\left[\frac{x^2}{2}-\frac{x^3}{3}\right]_0^1 =\frac12-\frac13=\frac16. \]
  4. \(\int_1^e \frac{1}{x}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \int_1^e \frac1x\,dx=[\ln|x|]_1^e=\ln e-\ln 1=1-0=1. \]
  5. \(\int_0^{\pi/2}\cos(2x)\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \int_0^{\pi/2}\cos(2x)\,dx=\left[\frac12\sin(2x)\right]_0^{\pi/2} =\frac12(\sin\pi-\sin0)=0. \]
  6. Area between \(y=2x\) and \(y=x^2\)
    Show answer
    \[ \text{Intersections from }2x=x^2:\ x(x-2)=0\Rightarrow x=0,2. \] On \([0,2]\), \(2x\ge x^2\), so \[ A=\int_0^2(2x-x^2)\,dx=\left[x^2-\frac{x^3}{3}\right]_0^2 =4-\frac83=\frac43. \]
  7. \(\int_{-1}^1 x^3\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ x^3\text{ is odd},\ \int_{-a}^{a}\text{odd function}\,dx=0. \] Or directly: \[ \left[\frac{x^4}{4}\right]_{-1}^{1}=\frac14-\frac14=0. \]
  8. \(\int_0^1 \sqrt{x}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \int_0^1 x^{1/2}\,dx=\left[\frac{x^{3/2}}{3/2}\right]_0^1 =\left[\frac23x^{3/2}\right]_0^1 =\frac23. \]
  9. Area bounded by \(y=4-x^2\) and x-axis
    Show answer
    \[ 4-x^2=0\Rightarrow x=\pm2. \] \[ A=\int_{-2}^{2}(4-x^2)\,dx =\left[4x-\frac{x^3}{3}\right]_{-2}^{2} =\left(8-\frac83\right)-\left(-8+\frac83\right) =\frac{32}{3}. \]
  10. \(\int_0^1 xe^{x^2}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ u=x^2,\ du=2x\,dx,\ x\,dx=\frac12du. \] \[ \int_0^1 xe^{x^2}\,dx=\frac12\int_0^1 e^u\,du =\frac12[e^u]_0^1 =\frac12(e-1). \]

Subtopic 5: Improper + Mixed Extension (10)

Why this matters

Improper integrals appear when bounds are infinite or the integrand blows up at an endpoint. These are limit questions in disguise, and deciding convergence/divergence is a core MATH141 skill.

Setup pattern

\[ \int_a^\infty f(x)\,dx=\lim_{b\to\infty}\int_a^b f(x)\,dx \]
\[ \int_a^b f(x)\,dx\ \text{with singularity at }a =\lim_{t\to a^+}\int_t^b f(x)\,dx \]

Worked Example A (convergent)

\[ \int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x^2}\,dx =\lim_{b\to\infty}\left[-\frac1x\right]_1^b =\lim_{b\to\infty}\left(1-\frac1b\right)=1 \]

Worked Example B (divergent)

\[ \int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x}\,dx =\lim_{b\to\infty}[\ln x]_1^b =\lim_{b\to\infty}\ln b=\infty \]

Worked Example C (endpoint singularity but convergent)

\[ \int_0^1 \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}}\,dx =\lim_{t\to0^+}\int_t^1 x^{-1/2}\,dx =\lim_{t\to0^+}[2\sqrt{x}]_t^1 =2 \]
Things to remember: if any split piece diverges, whole integral diverges. Never plug infinite bounds directly into antiderivatives without first writing the limit.

Practice Set (10)

  1. \(\int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x^2}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x^2}\,dx=\lim_{b\to\infty}\int_1^b x^{-2}dx =\lim_{b\to\infty}\left[-\frac1x\right]_1^b =\lim_{b\to\infty}\left(1-\frac1b\right)=1. \] Convergent.
  2. \(\int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \int_1^\infty \frac1x\,dx=\lim_{b\to\infty}\int_1^b \frac1x\,dx =\lim_{b\to\infty}[\ln x]_1^b =\lim_{b\to\infty}\ln b=\infty. \] Divergent.
  3. \(\int_0^1 \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \int_0^1 \frac1{\sqrt{x}}dx=\lim_{t\to0^+}\int_t^1 x^{-1/2}dx =\lim_{t\to0^+}[2\sqrt{x}]_t^1 =\lim_{t\to0^+}(2-2\sqrt{t})=2. \] Convergent.
  4. \(\int_0^\infty e^{-x}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \int_0^\infty e^{-x}dx=\lim_{b\to\infty}\int_0^b e^{-x}dx =\lim_{b\to\infty}[-e^{-x}]_0^b =\lim_{b\to\infty}(1-e^{-b})=1. \] Convergent.
  5. \(\int_0^\infty xe^{-x}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \int xe^{-x}dx=-(x+1)e^{-x}+C \quad (\text{by parts}). \] So \[ \int_0^\infty xe^{-x}dx =\lim_{b\to\infty}\left[-(x+1)e^{-x}\right]_0^b =\lim_{b\to\infty}\left(1-(b+1)e^{-b}\right)=1. \] Convergent.
  6. \(\int_1^\infty \frac{\ln x}{x^2}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ I=\int \frac{\ln x}{x^2}dx,\quad u=\ln x,\ dv=x^{-2}dx. \] \[ du=\frac1x dx,\ v=-\frac1x \Rightarrow I=-\frac{\ln x}{x}+\int x^{-2}dx =-\frac{\ln x}{x}-\frac1x+C. \] Then \[ \int_1^\infty \frac{\ln x}{x^2}dx =\lim_{b\to\infty}\left[-\frac{\ln x}{x}-\frac1x\right]_1^b =0-(-1)=1. \] Convergent.
  7. \(\int_0^\infty \frac{1}{1+x^2}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \int_0^\infty \frac{1}{1+x^2}dx =\lim_{b\to\infty}[\arctan x]_0^b =\frac{\pi}{2}-0=\frac{\pi}{2}. \] Convergent.
  8. \(\int_0^1 \ln x\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ \int_0^1 \ln x\,dx=\lim_{t\to0^+}\int_t^1 \ln x\,dx. \] \[ \int \ln x\,dx=x\ln x-x+C. \] \[ \lim_{t\to0^+}[x\ln x-x]_t^1 =(-1)-\lim_{t\to0^+}(t\ln t-t)=-1. \] Convergent.
  9. \(\int_0^\infty \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}(1+x)}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ x=t^2,\ dx=2t\,dt,\ \sqrt{x}=t. \] \[ \int_0^\infty \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}(1+x)}dx =\int_0^\infty \frac{1}{t(1+t^2)}(2t\,dt) =2\int_0^\infty \frac{1}{1+t^2}dt \] \[ =2[\arctan t]_0^\infty=2\cdot\frac{\pi}{2}=\pi. \] Convergent.
  10. \(\int_0^\infty \frac{x}{(1+x^2)^2}\,dx\)
    Show answer
    \[ u=1+x^2,\ du=2x\,dx,\ x\,dx=\frac12du. \] Bounds: \(x=0\to u=1\), \(x\to\infty\to u\to\infty\). \[ \int_0^\infty \frac{x}{(1+x^2)^2}dx =\frac12\int_1^\infty u^{-2}du =\frac12\left[-\frac1u\right]_1^\infty =\frac12. \] Convergent.
