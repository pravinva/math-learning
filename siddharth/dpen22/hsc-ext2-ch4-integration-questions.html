Method playbook for this pack
1) First classify the integrand: basic form, chain form, rational form, trig form, or product form.
2) If chain form appears, try a direct reverse-chain or \(u\)-sub before expanding anything.
3) For rational expressions, simplify first, then partial fractions if needed.
4) For trig powers/products, convert to identities that reduce power or product complexity.
5) For products like polynomial\(\times\)exp/trig/log, consider integration by parts.
6) For definite integrals, keep exact values and use symmetry when possible.

  4. Evaluate \(\int (4x-1)^7\,dx\).
  5. Evaluate \(\int \frac{2x}{x^2+9}\,dx\).
  6. Evaluate \(\int \frac{dx}{x^2+6x+13}\,dx\).
  7. Evaluate \(\int_0^2 (3x^2-4x+1)\,dx\).
