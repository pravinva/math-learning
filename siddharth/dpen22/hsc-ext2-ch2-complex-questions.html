Method playbook for Complex Numbers
1) Decide representation first: Cartesian \(a+bi\) or polar \(r(\cos\theta+i\sin\theta)\).
2) For divide/simplify in Cartesian form, multiply by conjugate.
3) For powers/roots, switch to polar and use de Moivre directly.
4) For loci, translate each modulus/argument statement into geometry before doing algebra.
5) Keep exact angle values in radians unless a decimal is explicitly requested.

Test 1 - Algebra and Cartesian Form

  1. Simplify: \((3-2i)+(5+7i)\).
  2. Simplify: \((4+3i)(2-i)\).
  3. Simplify and write in \(a+bi\) form: \(\dfrac{5+2i}{1-3i}\).
  4. Find \(|z|\) and \(\arg z\) (principal value) for \(z=-1+\sqrt{3}i\).
  5. Solve: \(z^2-(4+2i)z+(11+2i)=0\).
  6. If \(z=2-5i\), evaluate \(z\bar z\) and \(\dfrac{1}{z}\).
  7. Solve for real \(x,y\): \((x+yi)(1+i)=7+i\).
  8. Find all \(z\) such that \(z+\bar z=8\) and \(z-\bar z=6i\).
  9. Find the set of points representing \(|z-2|=3\) and describe it geometrically.
  10. Find \(k\in\mathbb{R}\) so that \(z=1+ki\) satisfies \(|z|=\sqrt{10}\).

Test 2 - Polar Form and De Moivre Basics

  1. Write \(z=-\sqrt{3}+i\) in polar form \(r\text{cis}\theta\).
  2. Evaluate \((2\text{cis}\,\frac{\pi}{6})^5\) in polar form.
  3. Convert \((\cos\frac{5\pi}{4}+i\sin\frac{5\pi}{4})\) to Cartesian form.
  4. Find all cube roots of \(8\text{cis}\,\frac{\pi}{2}\).
  5. Solve \(z^4=16\).
  6. Solve \(z^6=-64i\) and sketch root positions on Argand plane.
  7. Given \(z_1=3\text{cis}\,\frac{\pi}{3}\), \(z_2=2\text{cis}\left(-\frac{\pi}{6}\right)\), find \(z_1z_2\) and \(\frac{z_1}{z_2}\).
  8. Find principal argument of \(z=(-1-i)^7\).
  9. Show that \(\left(\frac{1+i}{\sqrt2}\right)^8=1\).
  10. Find all \(z\) such that \(z^3=1\), then verify sum of roots is zero.

Test 3 - Complex Plane Geometry and Loci

  1. Describe geometrically: \(|z-1|=|z+3|\).
  2. Describe and sketch the region: \(|z-(1+i)|\le 2\).
  3. Describe the locus: \(\arg(z-2)=\frac{\pi}{4}\).
  4. Find equation in \(x,y\) for \(|z-2i|=|z+2i|\).
  5. Find equation in \(x,y\) for \(|z-(2+i)|=3\).
  6. Find the set of points with \(\Re(z)=2\) and \(|z|=5\).
  7. For \(z=x+yi\), interpret \(\Im\!\left(\frac{z-1}{z+1}\right)=0\) as a locus.
  8. Find all \(z\) satisfying \(|z-1|=2|z+1|\).
  9. Determine whether \(z=3-4i\) lies inside, on, or outside \(|z-2i|=5\).
  10. Find the intersection points of \(|z|=4\) and \(\Re(z)=2\).

Test 4 - De Moivre and Trig Connections

  1. Use de Moivre to expand \((\cos\theta+i\sin\theta)^4\).
  2. Hence derive identities for \(\cos4\theta\) and \(\sin4\theta\).
  3. Find exact value of \(\cos(5\pi/12)\) using angle methods.
  4. Solve \(\cos(3\theta)=0\) for \(0\le\theta\le2\pi\).
  5. Solve \(\sin(4\theta)=\frac{\sqrt3}{2}\) for \(0\le\theta\le2\pi\).
  6. If \(z=\text{cis}\,\theta\), show that \(z+\frac1z=2\cos\theta\).
  7. Find \((1+i)^{10}\) in \(a+bi\) form.
  8. Find all roots of \(z^5=32(\cos\pi+i\sin\pi)\).
  9. Compute \(\sum_{k=0}^{5}\text{cis}\left(\frac{k\pi}{3}\right)\).
  10. Show geometrically why roots of \(z^n=1\) form a regular \(n\)-gon.

Test 5 - Mixed Challenge and Readiness Bridge

  1. Solve \(z^2+2(1-i)z+10i=0\).
  2. Find principal value of \(\Log(-1+i\sqrt3)\).
  3. Find all fourth roots of \(1+i\).
  4. Solve \(|z-1|^2+|z+1|^2=10\).
  5. Find and classify region: \(\left|\frac{z-1}{z+1}\right|=1\).
  6. Show that product of all sixth roots of unity is \(-1\).
  7. Solve \(z^4+z^2+1=0\).
  8. If \(z=\text{cis}\,\theta\), show \(\Re(z^3)=4\cos^3\theta-3\cos\theta\).
  9. Find all \(z\) such that \(z+\frac{4}{z}\in\mathbb{R}\) with \(|z|=2\).
  10. For signal \(s(t)=\Re(e^{(i\omega-a)t})\), explain role of \(a\) and \(\omega\) and write \(s(t)\) explicitly.
