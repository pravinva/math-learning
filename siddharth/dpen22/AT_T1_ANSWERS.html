  1. Given \(A=\frac{\pi}{4}\), \(B=\frac{\pi}{6}\), \(b=4\). First find \(C\):
    \[ C=\pi-\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{\pi}{6}=\frac{7\pi}{12}. \] Use sine rule: \[ \frac{c}{\sin C}=\frac{b}{\sin B} \Rightarrow c=\frac{4\sin(7\pi/12)}{\sin(\pi/6)} =8\sin(105^\circ). \] \[ \sin(105^\circ)=\frac{\sqrt6+\sqrt2}{4} \Rightarrow c=2(\sqrt6+\sqrt2)\approx 7.73. \]
  2. Smallest side is \(a=2\), so smallest angle is \(A\). Use cosine rule: \[ a^2=b^2+c^2-2bc\cos A \Rightarrow \cos A=\frac{b^2+c^2-a^2}{2bc} =\frac{16+25-4}{2\cdot4\cdot5} =\frac{37}{40}. \] \[ A=\cos^{-1}\!\left(\frac{37}{40}\right)\approx 22.33^\circ. \]
  3. Let the base of pole be at the origin. Downhill point is \(20\) m away on a \(20^\circ\) slope, so relative horizontal and vertical offsets are: \[ \Delta x=20\cos20^\circ,\qquad \Delta y=20\sin20^\circ. \] The top of the vertical pole is \(10\) m above base, so vertical difference to downhill point is: \[ 10+\Delta y=10+20\sin20^\circ. \] Rope length: \[ L=\sqrt{(20\cos20^\circ)^2+(10+20\sin20^\circ)^2} \approx 25.24\text{ m}. \]
  4. \(y=2\sin(3x)\).
    Amplitude \(=|2|=2\).
    Period \(=\dfrac{2\pi}{3}\).
  5. \(y=\dfrac12\tan x\).
    For tangent, period \(=\pi\).
    Vertical stretch factor \(=\dfrac12\). (Tangent has no amplitude.)
  6. \(\tan x=\dfrac{4}{3}\) and \(\pi\le x\le \dfrac{3\pi}{2}\) (Quadrant III).
    Reference triangle gives hypotenuse \(5\). In QIII, cosine is negative: \[ \cos x=-\frac{3}{5}. \]
  7. \[ \frac{1}{1+\cos x}+\frac{1}{1-\cos x} =\frac{(1-\cos x)+(1+\cos x)}{1-\cos^2x} =\frac{2}{\sin^2x} =2\csc^2x. \]
  8. Check: \[ \sin\!\left(x+\frac{\pi}{4}\right) =\sin x\cos\frac{\pi}{4}+\cos x\sin\frac{\pi}{4} =\frac{\sqrt2}{2}(\sin x+\cos x). \] Statement is true.
  9. \[ \cos(\pi-x)=-\cos x,\qquad \sin\!\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\cos x. \] They are negatives, not equal in general. Statement is false.
  10. Show: \[ \frac{\sin2x}{\sin x}-\frac{\cos2x}{\cos x}=\sec x. \] First term: \[ \frac{\sin2x}{\sin x}=\frac{2\sin x\cos x}{\sin x}=2\cos x. \] Second term: \[ \frac{\cos2x}{\cos x} =\frac{2\cos^2x-1}{\cos x} =2\cos x-\sec x. \] So LHS: \[ 2\cos x-(2\cos x-\sec x)=\sec x. \] Done.
