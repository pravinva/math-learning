MATH141 Readiness Section (Complex Bridge)

What to focus on first for MATH141 readiness:
  1. Algebra speed: simplify and solve with \(i^2=-1\), conjugates, and modulus calculations.
  2. Geometric view: interpret \(z=a+bi\) as a point/vector in the plane and connect to vector operations.
  3. Polar fluency: move between Cartesian and polar form for products, powers, and roots.

Recommended 2-week bridge plan

  • Week 1: 2.1-2.3 + 12 mixed simplification/solve questions per day.
  • Week 2: 2.4-2.7 + 8 polar/de Moivre/roots questions per day.
  • Use this alongside the existing DPEN22 \(\rightarrow\) MATH141 packs in the index.
Reality check for course alignment: MATH141 itself is not a "complex numbers course", but this chapter builds the symbolic control and vector-style thinking that makes early university maths smoother.
