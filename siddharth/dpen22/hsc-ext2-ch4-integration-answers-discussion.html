Test 1 - Answers

  1. \(\int (6x^2-8x+5)\,dx=2x^3-4x^2+5x+C\)
  2. \(\int \frac{1}{x+4}\,dx=\ln|x+4|+C\)
  3. \(\int (3e^{2x}-4\cos x)\,dx=\frac{3}{2}e^{2x}-4\sin x+C\)
  4. \(\int (4x-1)^7\,dx=\frac{(4x-1)^8}{32}+C\)
  5. \(\int \frac{2x}{x^2+9}\,dx=\ln(x^2+9)+C\)
  6. \(\int \frac{dx}{x^2+6x+13}\,dx=\arctan(x+3)+C\)
  7. \(\int_0^2 (3x^2-4x+1)\,dx=2\)
  8. \(\int_0^\pi \sin x\,dx=2\)
  9. \(\int_1^e \frac{1}{x}\,dx=1\)
  10. Area between \(y=x^2\) and \(y=2x\): intersections \(x=0,2\), area \(=\int_0^2(2x-x^2)\,dx=\frac{4}{3}\)
Discussion: Test 1 is pure setup discipline. The biggest error is forgetting complete-the-square in Q6 and swapping top-minus-bottom in Q10.

Test 2 - Answers

  1. \(\int \frac{5x^4}{x^5+3}\,dx=\ln(x^5+3)+C\)
  2. \(\int \frac{3x+2}{x^2+2x+5}\,dx=\frac{3}{2}\ln(x^2+2x+5)-\arctan\!\left(\frac{x+1}{2}\right)+C\)
  3. \(\int \frac{x^2+4x+5}{x+1}\,dx=\frac{x^2}{2}+3x+2\ln|x+1|+C\)
  4. \(\int \frac{7x+1}{(x-2)(x+3)}\,dx=3\ln|x-2|+4\ln|x+3|+C\)
  5. \(\int \frac{2x+5}{x^2-4x+8}\,dx=\ln(x^2-4x+8)+9\arctan\!\left(\frac{x-2}{2}\right)+C\)
  6. \(\int \frac{1}{x(x+2)}\,dx=\frac12\ln\left|\frac{x}{x+2}\right|+C\)
  7. \(\int_0^1 \frac{2x}{x^2+1}\,dx=\ln 2\)
  8. \(\int_1^3 \frac{1}{(x-2)^2+1}\,dx=\frac{\pi}{2}\)
  9. \(\int_0^4 \frac{x+1}{\sqrt{x+5}}\,dx=\frac{38}{3}\)
  10. \(\int \frac{x^2+1}{x^2-1}\,dx=x+\ln\left|\frac{x-1}{x+1}\right|+C\)
Discussion: Q2 and Q5 are the key Ch4 move: split numerator into derivative-part + constant-part, then combine log and arctan pieces cleanly.

Test 3 - Answers

  1. \(\int \sin^2 x\,dx=\frac{x}{2}-\frac{\sin 2x}{4}+C\)
  2. \(\int \cos^2 x\,dx=\frac{x}{2}+\frac{\sin 2x}{4}+C\)
  3. \(\int \sin x\cos x\,dx=\frac{1}{2}\sin^2 x+C\)
  4. \(\int \sin^3 x\cos x\,dx=\frac{\sin^4 x}{4}+C\)
  5. \(\int \cos^3 x\,dx=\sin x-\frac{\sin^3 x}{3}+C\)
  6. \(\int \sin(3x)\cos(2x)\,dx=\frac{1}{2}\int[\sin(5x)+\sin x]\,dx=-\frac{\cos5x}{10}-\frac{\cos x}{2}+C\)
  7. \(\int \tan x\,dx=-\ln|\cos x|+C\)
  8. \(\int \sec^2(3x)\,dx=\frac13\tan(3x)+C\)
  9. \(\int_0^{\pi/2}\sin^2 x\,dx=\frac{\pi}{4}\)
  10. \(\int_0^\pi \sin x\cos x\,dx=0\)
Discussion: For trig powers, first decide odd/even strategy. For mixed-frequency trig (Q6), product-to-sum is usually the fastest safe route.

Test 4 - Answers

  1. \(\int xe^x\,dx=e^x(x-1)+C\)
  2. \(\int x\cos x\,dx=x\sin x+\cos x+C\)
  3. \(\int x\ln x\,dx=\frac{x^2}{2}\ln x-\frac{x^2}{4}+C\)
  4. \(\int x^2e^x\,dx=e^x(x^2-2x+2)+C\)
  5. \(\int_0^1 xe^{2x}\,dx=\frac{e^2+1}{4}\)
  6. \(\int_0^\pi x\sin x\,dx=\pi\)
  7. \(\int \ln x\,dx=x\ln x-x+C\)
  8. \(\int_0^1 x\ln(1+x)\,dx=\frac14\)
  9. \(\int_0^2 (x^2+1)e^x\,dx=e^2+1\)
  10. Average value on \([0,3]\): \(\frac{1}{3}\int_0^3(x^2+1)\,dx=4\)
Discussion: This test is mostly "choose \(u\), choose \(dv\)" correctly. If repeated parts appears (like \(x^2e^x\)), do by-parts recursively or tabular style.

Test 5 - Answers

  1. \(\int \frac{dx}{x^2-4x+13}\,dx=\frac13\arctan\!\left(\frac{x-2}{3}\right)+C\)
  2. \(\int \frac{x}{x^2+4x+8}\,dx=\frac12\ln(x^2+4x+8)-\arctan\!\left(\frac{x+2}{2}\right)+C\)
  3. \(\int \frac{x^2}{x^2+1}\,dx=x-\arctan x+C\)
  4. \(\int \frac{dx}{(x-1)(x^2+1)}=\frac12\ln|x-1|-\frac14\ln(x^2+1)-\frac12\arctan x+C\)
  5. \(\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{9-x^2}}\,dx=\arcsin\!\left(\frac{x}{3}\right)+C\)
  6. \(\int \frac{x^2}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\,dx=\frac12\arcsin x-\frac12x\sqrt{1-x^2}+C\)
  7. \(\int \sin^3 x\,dx=-\cos x+\frac{\cos^3 x}{3}+C\)
  8. \(\int_0^{\pi/2}\sin^3 x\,dx=\frac23\)
  9. \(\int_0^1 \frac{\ln(1+x)}{1+x}\,dx=\frac12(\ln2)^2\)
  10. \(v(t)=t^2-4t+3=(t-1)(t-3)\). Displacement on \([0,5]\): \(\int_0^5 v(t)\,dt=\frac{5}{3}\). Total distance: \(\int_0^1 v\,dt-\int_1^3 v\,dt+\int_3^5 v\,dt=\frac{23}{3}\).
Discussion: Test 5 is bridge-level. Q10 is the key modeling skill: displacement uses signed area, total distance uses absolute velocity by splitting at sign changes.
