LEVEL 1
Basic Antiderivatives
Power rule, exponential, logarithmic, trigonometric
1
Find \(\int (3x^2-4x+5)\,dx\).
Apply power rule and linearity.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(\int 3x^2\,dx=3\cdot\frac{x^3}{3}=x^3\)
2
\(\int 4x\,dx=4\cdot\frac{x^2}{2}=2x^2\)
3
\(\int 5\,dx=5x\)
ANSWER \(x^3-2x^2+5x+C\)
2
Find \(\int \left(\dfrac{2}{x^3}+\dfrac{3}{\sqrt{x}}\right)dx\).
Rewrite as negative/fractional exponents.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Rewrite: \(\int(2x^{-3}+3x^{-1/2})\,dx\)
2
\(=2\cdot\frac{x^{-2}}{-2}+3\cdot\frac{x^{1/2}}{1/2}=-x^{-2}+6x^{1/2}+C\)
ANSWER \(-\dfrac{1}{x^2}+6\sqrt{x}+C\)
3
Find \(\int (e^x+3^x)\,dx\).
Use formulas for e^x and a^x.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(\int e^x\,dx=e^x\)
2
\(\int 3^x\,dx=\frac{3^x}{\ln 3}\)
ANSWER \(e^x+\dfrac{3^x}{\ln 3}+C\)
4
Find \(\int \left(\sin x+\cos x\right)dx\).
Standard trig integrals.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(\int \sin x\,dx=-\cos x\)
2
\(\int \cos x\,dx=\sin x\)
ANSWER \(-\cos x+\sin x+C\)
5
Find \(\int \dfrac{5}{x}\,dx\).
Special case when n=-1.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(\int \frac{1}{x}\,dx=\ln|x|+C\)
2
Multiply by constant: \(5\ln|x|+C\)
ANSWER \(5\ln|x|+C\)
6
Find \(\int \sec^2 x\,dx\).
Standard trig integral.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(\int \sec^2 x\,dx=\tan x+C\)
ANSWER \(\tan x+C\)
7
Find \(\int e^{3x}\,dx\).
Chain rule in reverse - adjust for the constant.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Derivative of \(e^{3x}\) is \(3e^{3x}\)
2
So \(\int e^{3x}\,dx=\frac{1}{3}e^{3x}+C\)
ANSWER \(\dfrac{1}{3}e^{3x}+C\)
8
Find \(\int (2x+1)^3\,dx\).
Could expand, but substitution is cleaner.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Derivative of \((2x+1)^4\) is \(4(2x+1)^3\cdot 2=8(2x+1)^3\)
2
So \(\int (2x+1)^3\,dx=\frac{(2x+1)^4}{8}+C\)
ANSWER \(\dfrac{(2x+1)^4}{8}+C\)
9
Find \(\int \dfrac{4}{2x+5}\,dx\).
Pattern: ∫f'(x)/f(x) dx = ln|f(x)|+C.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Derivative of \(2x+5\) is \(2\)
2
\(\int \frac{4}{2x+5}\,dx=\frac{4}{2}\ln|2x+5|+C=2\ln|2x+5|+C\)
ANSWER \(2\ln|2x+5|+C\)
10
Find \(\int \sin(4x)\,dx\).
Adjust for the constant factor.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Derivative of \(-\cos(4x)\) is \(4\sin(4x)\)
2
\(\int \sin(4x)\,dx=-\frac{1}{4}\cos(4x)+C\)
ANSWER \(-\dfrac{1}{4}\cos(4x)+C\)
LEVEL 2
Substitution & Techniques
u-substitution, adjusting for constants
11
Find \(\int x\sqrt{x^2+1}\,dx\) using substitution.
Let u=x²+1.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Let \(u=x^2+1\), then \(du=2x\,dx\), so \(x\,dx=\frac{du}{2}\)
2
\(\int x\sqrt{x^2+1}\,dx=\int \sqrt{u}\cdot\frac{du}{2}=\frac{1}{2}\int u^{1/2}\,du\)
3
\(=\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{u^{3/2}}{3/2}=\frac{1}{3}u^{3/2}+C\)
4
Back-substitute: \(\frac{1}{3}(x^2+1)^{3/2}+C\)
ANSWER \(\dfrac{1}{3}(x^2+1)^{3/2}+C\)
12
Find \(\int \dfrac{x}{x^2+4}\,dx\).
Let u=x²+4.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Let \(u=x^2+4\), \(du=2x\,dx\), so \(x\,dx=\frac{du}{2}\)
2
\(\int \frac{x}{x^2+4}\,dx=\int \frac{1}{u}\cdot\frac{du}{2}=\frac{1}{2}\ln|u|+C\)
3
\(=\frac{1}{2}\ln(x^2+4)+C\) (no absolute value needed since \(x^2+4>0\))
ANSWER \(\dfrac{1}{2}\ln(x^2+4)+C\)
13
Find \(\int e^x\sin(e^x)\,dx\).
Let u=e^x.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Let \(u=e^x\), \(du=e^x\,dx\)
2
\(\int e^x\sin(e^x)\,dx=\int \sin u\,du=-\cos u+C\)
3
\(=-\cos(e^x)+C\)
ANSWER \(-\cos(e^x)+C\)
14
Find \(\int x^2(x^3+5)^7\,dx\).
Let u=x³+5.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Let \(u=x^3+5\), \(du=3x^2\,dx\), so \(x^2\,dx=\frac{du}{3}\)
2
\(\int x^2(x^3+5)^7\,dx=\int u^7\cdot\frac{du}{3}=\frac{1}{3}\cdot\frac{u^8}{8}=\frac{u^8}{24}+C\)
3
\(=\frac{(x^3+5)^8}{24}+C\)
ANSWER \(\dfrac{(x^3+5)^8}{24}+C\)
15
Find \(\int \dfrac{\cos x}{1+\sin x}\,dx\).
Let u=1+sin x.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Let \(u=1+\sin x\), \(du=\cos x\,dx\)
2
\(\int \frac{\cos x}{1+\sin x}\,dx=\int \frac{du}{u}=\ln|u|+C\)
3
\(=\ln|1+\sin x|+C\)
ANSWER \(\ln|1+\sin x|+C\)
16
Find \(\int \tan x\,dx\). (Hint: write as sin x/cos x)
Let u=cos x.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(\int \tan x\,dx=\int \frac{\sin x}{\cos x}\,dx\)
2
Let \(u=\cos x\), \(du=-\sin x\,dx\), so \(\sin x\,dx=-du\)
3
\(\int \frac{\sin x}{\cos x}\,dx=\int \frac{-du}{u}=-\ln|u|+C=-\ln|\cos x|+C\)
4
\(=\ln|\sec x|+C\) or \(-\ln|\cos x|+C\)
ANSWER \(-\ln|\cos x|+C\) or \(\ln|\sec x|+C\)
17
Find \(\int \dfrac{2x+3}{x^2+3x+1}\,dx\).
Notice the numerator is the derivative of the denominator.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Let \(u=x^2+3x+1\), \(du=(2x+3)\,dx\)
2
\(\int \frac{2x+3}{x^2+3x+1}\,dx=\int \frac{du}{u}=\ln|u|+C\)
3
\(=\ln|x^2+3x+1|+C\)
ANSWER \(\ln|x^2+3x+1|+C\)
18
Find \(\int x\sqrt{1-x^2}\,dx\).
Let u=1-x².
▶
SOLUTION
1
Let \(u=1-x^2\), \(du=-2x\,dx\), so \(x\,dx=-\frac{du}{2}\)
2
\(\int x\sqrt{1-x^2}\,dx=\int \sqrt{u}\cdot\left(-\frac{du}{2}\right)=-\frac{1}{2}\int u^{1/2}\,du\)
3
\(=-\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{u^{3/2}}{3/2}=-\frac{1}{3}u^{3/2}+C\)
4
\(=-\frac{1}{3}(1-x^2)^{3/2}+C\)
ANSWER \(-\dfrac{1}{3}(1-x^2)^{3/2}+C\)
19
Find \(\int xe^{x^2}\,dx\).
Let u=x².
▶
SOLUTION
1
Let \(u=x^2\), \(du=2x\,dx\), so \(x\,dx=\frac{du}{2}\)
2
\(\int xe^{x^2}\,dx=\int e^u\cdot\frac{du}{2}=\frac{1}{2}e^u+C\)
3
\(=\frac{1}{2}e^{x^2}+C\)
ANSWER \(\dfrac{1}{2}e^{x^2}+C\)
20
Find \(\int \sin^3 x\cos x\,dx\).
Let u=sin x.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Let \(u=\sin x\), \(du=\cos x\,dx\)
2
\(\int \sin^3 x\cos x\,dx=\int u^3\,du=\frac{u^4}{4}+C\)
3
\(=\frac{\sin^4 x}{4}+C\)
ANSWER \(\dfrac{\sin^4 x}{4}+C\)
LEVEL 3
Definite Integrals & FTC
Fundamental Theorem, definite integration with substitution
21
Evaluate \(\int_1^3 (2x+1)\,dx\).
FTC: find antiderivative, then F(3)-F(1).
▶
SOLUTION
1
Antiderivative: \(F(x)=x^2+x\)
2
\(\int_1^3 (2x+1)\,dx=[x^2+x]_1^3\)
3
\(=(9+3)-(1+1)=12-2=10\)
ANSWER \(10\)
22
Evaluate \(\int_0^\pi \sin x\,dx\).
Antiderivative of sin x is -cos x.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(\int_0^\pi \sin x\,dx=[-\cos x]_0^\pi\)
2
\(=-\cos\pi-(-\cos 0)=-(-1)-(-1)=1+1=2\)
ANSWER \(2\)
23
Evaluate \(\int_1^e \dfrac{1}{x}\,dx\).
This gives ln e - ln 1.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(\int_1^e \frac{1}{x}\,dx=[\ln x]_1^e\) (no absolute value since x>0)
2
\(=\ln e-\ln 1=1-0=1\)
ANSWER \(1\)
24
Evaluate \(\int_0^1 x\sqrt{x^2+1}\,dx\) using substitution.
Change limits when you substitute u=x²+1.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Let \(u=x^2+1\), \(du=2x\,dx\)
2
When x=0: u=1. When x=1: u=2
3
\(\int_0^1 x\sqrt{x^2+1}\,dx=\int_1^2 \sqrt{u}\cdot\frac{du}{2}=\frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{u^{3/2}}{3/2}\right]_1^2\)
4
\(=\frac{1}{3}[u^{3/2}]_1^2=\frac{1}{3}(2^{3/2}-1)=\frac{1}{3}(2\sqrt{2}-1)\)
ANSWER \(\dfrac{2\sqrt{2}-1}{3}\approx 0.609\)
25
If \(F(x)=\int_2^x (t^2+1)\,dt\), find F'(x).
FTC Part 1: derivative of accumulation function.
▶
SOLUTION
1
By FTC Part 1: \(\frac{d}{dx}\left[\int_a^x f(t)\,dt\right]=f(x)\)
2
\(F'(x)=x^2+1\)
ANSWER \(F'(x)=x^2+1\)
26
Evaluate \(\int_0^{\pi/2} \cos(2x)\,dx\).
Antiderivative is sin(2x)/2.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(\int \cos(2x)\,dx=\frac{\sin(2x)}{2}+C\)
2
\(\int_0^{\pi/2} \cos(2x)\,dx=\left[\frac{\sin(2x)}{2}\right]_0^{\pi/2}\)
3
\(=\frac{\sin\pi}{2}-\frac{\sin 0}{2}=\frac{0}{2}-\frac{0}{2}=0\)
ANSWER \(0\)
27
Evaluate \(\int_0^2 xe^{x^2}\,dx\).
Use substitution u=x², change limits.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Let \(u=x^2\), \(du=2x\,dx\)
2
When x=0: u=0. When x=2: u=4
3
\(\int_0^2 xe^{x^2}\,dx=\int_0^4 e^u\cdot\frac{du}{2}=\frac{1}{2}[e^u]_0^4=\frac{1}{2}(e^4-1)\)
ANSWER \(\dfrac{e^4-1}{2}\approx 26.8\)
28
Evaluate \(\int_1^4 \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x}}\,dx\).
Rewrite as x^(-1/2).
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(\int_1^4 x^{-1/2}\,dx=\left[\frac{x^{1/2}}{1/2}\right]_1^4=[2\sqrt{x}]_1^4\)
2
\(=2\sqrt{4}-2\sqrt{1}=4-2=2\)
ANSWER \(2\)
29
Evaluate \(\int_{-1}^1 x^3\,dx\).
Note that x³ is an odd function.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(\int_{-1}^1 x^3\,dx=\left[\frac{x^4}{4}\right]_{-1}^1\)
2
\(=\frac{1}{4}-\frac{1}{4}=0\)
3
This makes sense: odd function over symmetric interval → area=0
ANSWER \(0\)
30
Evaluate \(\int_0^{\ln 2} e^x\,dx\).
Simple exponential integral.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(\int_0^{\ln 2} e^x\,dx=[e^x]_0^{\ln 2}\)
2
\(=e^{\ln 2}-e^0=2-1=1\)

LEVEL 4
Area Applications
Area under curves, area between curves
31
Find the area under \(y=x^2\) from x=0 to x=2.
Area = ∫ from 0 to 2 of x² dx.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(A=\int_0^2 x^2\,dx=\left[\frac{x^3}{3}\right]_0^2\)
2
\(=\frac{8}{3}-0=\frac{8}{3}\) square units
ANSWER \(\dfrac{8}{3}\) square units
32
Find the area between \(y=x^2\) and \(y=x\) from x=0 to x=1.
Area = ∫(top - bottom) dx. Determine which is on top.
▶
SOLUTION
1
On [0,1]: x > x² (line is above parabola)
2
\(A=\int_0^1 (x-x^2)\,dx=\left[\frac{x^2}{2}-\frac{x^3}{3}\right]_0^1\)
3
\(=\frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{3}=\frac{3-2}{6}=\frac{1}{6}\)
ANSWER \(\dfrac{1}{6}\) square units
33
Find the area under \(y=\sin x\) from x=0 to x=π.
sin x ≥ 0 on this interval.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(A=\int_0^\pi \sin x\,dx=[-\cos x]_0^\pi\)
2
\(=-\cos\pi+\cos 0=-(-1)+1=2\)
ANSWER \(2\) square units
34
Find the area between \(y=x^2-4\) and the x-axis from x=-2 to x=2.
The parabola is below the x-axis, so take absolute value.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Since \(x^2-4\leq 0\) on [-2,2], \(A=\left|\int_{-2}^2 (x^2-4)\,dx\right|\)
2
\(\int_{-2}^2 (x^2-4)\,dx=\left[\frac{x^3}{3}-4x\right]_{-2}^2\)
3
\(=\left(\frac{8}{3}-8\right)-\left(\frac{-8}{3}+8\right)=\frac{8}{3}-8+\frac{8}{3}-8=\frac{16}{3}-16=-\frac{32}{3}\)
4
\(A=\left|-\frac{32}{3}\right|=\frac{32}{3}\)
ANSWER \(\dfrac{32}{3}\) square units
35
Find the area between \(y=e^x\) and \(y=1\) from x=0 to x=1.
e^x > 1 on this interval.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(A=\int_0^1 (e^x-1)\,dx=[e^x-x]_0^1\)
2
\(=(e-1)-(1-0)=e-2\)
ANSWER \(e-2\approx 0.718\) square units
36
Find the area enclosed by \(y=x^3-x\) and the x-axis.
Find zeros first: x(x²-1)=0. Split into regions.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Zeros: \(x(x-1)(x+1)=0\) → x=-1, 0, 1
2
On [-1,0]: \(x^3-x\geq 0\). On [0,1]: \(x^3-x\leq 0\)
3
\(A=\int_{-1}^0 (x^3-x)\,dx+\left|\int_0^1 (x^3-x)\,dx\right|\)
4
\(\left[\frac{x^4}{4}-\frac{x^2}{2}\right]_{-1}^0=0-\left(\frac{1}{4}-\frac{1}{2}\right)=\frac{1}{4}\)
5
\(\left[\frac{x^4}{4}-\frac{x^2}{2}\right]_0^1=\frac{1}{4}-\frac{1}{2}=-\frac{1}{4}\), so \(\left|-\frac{1}{4}\right|=\frac{1}{4}\)
6
Total: \(\frac{1}{4}+\frac{1}{4}=\frac{1}{2}\)
ANSWER \(\dfrac{1}{2}\) square units
37
Find the area between \(y=\sqrt{x}\) and \(y=x^2\) from x=0 to their intersection.
Find intersection by solving √x = x².
▶
SOLUTION
1
Intersection: \(\sqrt{x}=x^2\) → \(x=x^4\) → \(x^4-x=0\) → x=0 or x=1
2
On [0,1]: \(\sqrt{x}\geq x^2\)
3
\(A=\int_0^1 (\sqrt{x}-x^2)\,dx=\int_0^1 (x^{1/2}-x^2)\,dx\)
4
\(=\left[\frac{x^{3/2}}{3/2}-\frac{x^3}{3}\right]_0^1=\frac{2}{3}-\frac{1}{3}=\frac{1}{3}\)
ANSWER \(\dfrac{1}{3}\) square units
38
Find the area of the region bounded by \(y=\cos x\), the x-axis, x=0, and x=π/2.
cos x ≥ 0 on [0, π/2].
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(A=\int_0^{\pi/2} \cos x\,dx=[\sin x]_0^{\pi/2}\)
2
\(=\sin(\pi/2)-\sin 0=1-0=1\)
ANSWER \(1\) square unit
39
Find the area between \(y=4-x^2\) and \(y=x+2\).
Find intersection points first.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Intersection: \(4-x^2=x+2\) → \(x^2+x-2=0\) → (x+2)(x-1)=0 → x=-2, 1
2
On [-2,1]: parabola is above line
3
\(A=\int_{-2}^1 [(4-x^2)-(x+2)]\,dx=\int_{-2}^1 (2-x-x^2)\,dx\)
4
\(=\left[2x-\frac{x^2}{2}-\frac{x^3}{3}\right]_{-2}^1=\left(2-\frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{3}\right)-\left(-4-2+\frac{8}{3}\right)\)
5
\(=\frac{7}{6}-\left(\frac{-10}{3}\right)=\frac{7}{6}+\frac{20}{6}=\frac{27}{6}=\frac{9}{2}\)
ANSWER \(\dfrac{9}{2}\) square units
40
Find the area enclosed by one arch of \(y=\sin x\) (from 0 to π).
We already know this integral.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(A=\int_0^\pi \sin x\,dx=[-\cos x]_0^\pi\)
2
\(=-(-1)-(-1)=2\)
ANSWER \(2\) square units
LEVEL 5
Engineering & Advanced Applications
Velocity, displacement, volumes, real-world problems
41
A particle moves with velocity \(v(t)=3t^2-2t+1\) m/s. Find the displacement from t=0 to t=3 seconds.
Displacement = ∫v(t) dt.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Displacement \(s=\int_0^3 (3t^2-2t+1)\,dt\)
2
\(=[t^3-t^2+t]_0^3\)
3
\(=(27-9+3)-0=21\) meters
ANSWER \(21\) meters
42
Water flows into a tank at rate \(R(t)=5+2t\) liters/min. How much water enters in the first 10 minutes?
Total volume = ∫R(t) dt.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(V=\int_0^{10} (5+2t)\,dt=[5t+t^2]_0^{10}\)
2
\(=(50+100)-0=150\) liters
ANSWER \(150\) liters
43
A car's velocity is \(v(t)=20+6t-t^2\) m/s for 0≤t≤8. Find total distance traveled.
Check if v(t) changes sign. Distance = ∫|v(t)| dt.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Find when v=0: \(t^2-6t-20=0\) → t≈8.65 (outside [0,8])
2
v(t)>0 on [0,8], so distance = displacement
3
\(d=\int_0^8 (20+6t-t^2)\,dt=\left[20t+3t^2-\frac{t^3}{3}\right]_0^8\)
4
\(=160+192-\frac{512}{3}=352-170.67=181.33\) meters
ANSWER \(\dfrac{544}{3}\approx 181.3\) meters
44
The cross-sectional area of a solid is \(A(x)=\pi(4-x^2)\) m² at position x. Find volume from x=-2 to x=2.
Volume = ∫A(x) dx.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(V=\int_{-2}^2 \pi(4-x^2)\,dx=\pi\int_{-2}^2 (4-x^2)\,dx\)
2
\(=\pi\left[4x-\frac{x^3}{3}\right]_{-2}^2\)
3
\(=\pi\left[\left(8-\frac{8}{3}\right)-\left(-8+\frac{8}{3}\right)\right]=\pi\left[16-\frac{16}{3}\right]=\frac{32\pi}{3}\) m³
ANSWER \(\dfrac{32\pi}{3}\approx 33.5\) m³
45
A spring with natural length 0.5 m requires force \(F(x)=200x\) N to stretch x meters beyond natural length. Find work to stretch it from 0.5 m to 0.7 m (0.2 m extension).
Work = ∫F dx over the displacement.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Extension from 0 to 0.2 m. \(W=\int_0^{0.2} 200x\,dx\)
2
\(=[100x^2]_0^{0.2}=100(0.04)=4\) joules
ANSWER \(4\) joules
46
Electric charge flows at rate \(I(t)=0.5e^{-0.1t}\) amperes. Find total charge transferred from t=0 to t=10 seconds.
Charge Q = ∫I(t) dt (in coulombs).
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(Q=\int_0^{10} 0.5e^{-0.1t}\,dt\)
2
\(=0.5\cdot\frac{e^{-0.1t}}{-0.1}\Big|_0^{10}=-5[e^{-1}-e^0]\)
3
\(=-5(e^{-1}-1)=5(1-e^{-1})\approx 5(0.632)=3.16\) coulombs
ANSWER \(5(1-e^{-1})\approx 3.16\) coulombs
47
A tank drains at rate \(R(t)=-\sqrt{t}\) liters/min (negative = draining). If initially 100 L, how much remains after 16 minutes?
Amount drained = ∫|R(t)| dt.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Amount drained: \(\int_0^{16} \sqrt{t}\,dt=\int_0^{16} t^{1/2}\,dt\)
2
\(=\left[\frac{t^{3/2}}{3/2}\right]_0^{16}=\frac{2}{3}[16^{3/2}]=\frac{2}{3}(64)=\frac{128}{3}\approx 42.67\) L
3
Remaining: \(100-42.67=57.33\) L
ANSWER \(100-\dfrac{128}{3}=\dfrac{172}{3}\approx 57.3\) liters
48
Temperature in a room increases at rate \(T'(t)=3+0.5\cos(t)\) °C/hour. Find temperature increase from t=0 to t=π hours.
Total increase = ∫T'(t) dt.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(\Delta T=\int_0^\pi (3+0.5\cos t)\,dt\)
2
\(=[3t+0.5\sin t]_0^\pi\)
3
\(=(3\pi+0)-(0+0)=3\pi\approx 9.42\) °C
ANSWER \(3\pi\approx 9.42\) °C
49
A bacteria population grows at rate \(P'(t)=100e^{0.2t}\) cells/hour. How many new cells from t=0 to t=5 hours?
New cells = ∫P'(t) dt.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(\Delta P=\int_0^5 100e^{0.2t}\,dt\)
2
\(=100\cdot\frac{e^{0.2t}}{0.2}\Big|_0^5=500[e^1-e^0]\)
3
\(=500(e-1)\approx 500(1.718)=859\) cells
ANSWER \(500(e-1)\approx 859\) cells
50
A rocket's acceleration is \(a(t)=12-2t\) m/s² from t=0 to t=6 s. If initial velocity is 0, find: (a) velocity at t=6, (b) distance traveled.
v(t) = ∫a(t) dt, s(t) = ∫v(t) dt.
▶
SOLUTION
1
(a) \(v(t)=\int_0^t (12-2\tau)\,d\tau=[12\tau-\tau^2]_0^t=12t-t^2\)
2
\(v(6)=72-36=36\) m/s
