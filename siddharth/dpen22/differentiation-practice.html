LEVEL 1
Basic Rules & First Principles
1
Differentiate \(f(x)=\dfrac{3}{x^2}-\dfrac{2}{\sqrt{x}}+x^3\) and simplify.
Rewrite using negative and fractional exponents first.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Rewrite: \(3x^{-2}-2x^{-1/2}+x^3\)
2
Differentiate: \(-6x^{-3}+x^{-3/2}+3x^2\)
ANSWER \(3x^2+\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x^3}}-\dfrac{6}{x^3}\)
2
Differentiate \(f(x)=x^2\) from first principles and verify the result gives f′(3)=6.
Four-step method: f(x+h), subtract, divide by h, take limit.
▶
SOLUTION
1
f(x+h)=(x+h)²=x²+2xh+h²
2
Subtract f(x): 2xh+h²=h(2x+h)
3
Divide by h: 2x+h. Limit: 2x.
4
f′(3)=2(3)=6 ✓
ANSWER \(f'(x)=2x\). Confirmed: f′(3)=6.
LEVEL 2
Product, Quotient & Chain Rules
3
Differentiate \(f(x)=(x^3+1)\sin x\).
Product rule with u=x³+1 and v=sin x.
▶
SOLUTION
1
u=x³+1, v=sin x. u′=3x², v′=cos x
2
f′=3x²sin x+(x³+1)cos x
ANSWER \(3x^2\sin x+(x^3+1)\cos x\)
4
Differentiate \(f(x)=\dfrac{\ln x}{x^2}\).
Quotient rule with u=ln x and v=x².
▶
SOLUTION
1
u=ln x, v=x². u′=1/x, v′=2x
2
f′=\(\dfrac{(1/x)\cdot x^2-\ln x\cdot 2x}{x^4}=\dfrac{x-2x\ln x}{x^4}=\dfrac{1-2\ln x}{x^3}\)
ANSWER \(\dfrac{1-2\ln x}{x^3}\)
5
Differentiate \(f(x)=\sin^4(3x)\).
This is (\sin(3x))⁴ — apply chain rule twice (nested).
▶
SOLUTION
1
Outer: u⁴, inner: sin(3x). d/dx[u⁴]=4u³=4sin³(3x)
2
Multiply by d/dx[sin(3x)]=3cos(3x)
3
f′=4sin³(3x)·3cos(3x)=12sin³(3x)cos(3x)
ANSWER \(12\sin^3(3x)\cos(3x)\)
6
Differentiate \(f(x)=e^{x^2}\ln(3x+1)\).
Product rule, with chain rule needed for both factors.
▶
SOLUTION
1
u=e^{x²} → u′=2xe^{x²}. v=ln(3x+1) → v′=3/(3x+1)
2
f′=2xe^{x²}ln(3x+1)+e^{x²}·\dfrac{3}{3x+1}=e^{x^2}\!\left(2x\ln(3x+1)+\dfrac{3}{3x+1}\right)
ANSWER \(e^{x^2}\!\left(2x\ln(3x+1)+\dfrac{3}{3x+1}\right)\)
LEVEL 3
Higher Derivatives & Tangent Lines
7
Find \(f''(x)\) for \(f(x)=xe^x\) and show that \(f''(x)=f'(x)+e^x\).
Find f′ by product rule first, then differentiate again.
▶
SOLUTION
1
f′=eˣ+xeˣ=eˣ(1+x)
2
f′′=eˣ(1+x)+eˣ=(1+x+1)eˣ=(2+x)eˣ
3
Check: f′(x)+eˣ=(1+x)eˣ+eˣ=(2+x)eˣ=f′′(x) ✓
ANSWER \(f''(x)=(x+2)e^x\). Identity verified.
8
Find the equations of the tangent and normal to \(y=x\ln x\) at \(x=e\).
Find y(e) and y′(e) first. Product rule for y′.
▶
SOLUTION
1
y(e)=e·ln e=e. y′=ln x+1 (product rule). At x=e: y′=1+1=2.
2
Tangent: y−e=2(x−e) → y=2x−e.
3
Normal slope: −1/2. Normal: y−e=−(1/2)(x−e) → y=−x/2+3e/2.
ANSWER Tangent: \(y=2x-e\). Normal: \(y=-\dfrac{x}{2}+\dfrac{3e}{2}\)
LEVEL 4
Stationary Points & Curve Sketching
9
For \(f(x)=\dfrac{x^2-1}{x^2+1}\), find all stationary points, determine where f is increasing/decreasing, and state the range of f.
Quotient rule for f′. Note the denominator x²+1 is always positive.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Quotient rule: f′=\(\dfrac{2x(x^2+1)-(x^2-1)2x}{(x^2+1)^2}=\dfrac{4x}{(x^2+1)^2}\)
2
f′=0 → x=0. f(0)=−1. Stationary point at (0,−1).
3
f′<0 for x<0 (decreasing), f′>0 for x>0 (increasing). → (0,−1) is a global minimum.
4
As x→±∞: f→1 from below. So range: [−1,1).
ANSWER Min at (0,−1). Decreasing on (−∞,0), increasing on (0,∞). Range: [−1,1).
10
Sketch \(f(x)=x^3-6x^2+9x-4\), finding and classifying all stationary and inflection points.
Factor f′ as 3(x−a)(x−b). Use f′′ for classification and to find inflection.
▶
SOLUTION
1
f′=3x²−12x+9=3(x−1)(x−3)=0 → x=1,3.
2
f(1)=1−6+9−4=0. f(3)=27−54+27−4=−4.
3
f′′=6x−12. At x=1: f′′=−6<0 → local max (1,0). At x=3: f′′=6>0 → local min (3,−4).
4
Inflection: f′′=0 → x=2. f(2)=8−24+18−4=−2. Inflection at (2,−2).
5
y-intercept: f(0)=−4. End behaviour: positive cubic → up right, down left.
ANSWER Local max (1,0), local min (3,−4), inflection (2,−2), y-intercept (0,−4).
LEVEL 5
Optimisation & Extended Problems
11
A rectangle is inscribed in a semicircle of radius 5. Find the dimensions of the largest rectangle that can be inscribed.
Place the semicircle with centre at origin. A corner of the rectangle at (x, y) satisfies x²+y²=25. Express area in terms of x only, then differentiate.
▶
Diagram: semicircle radius 5, diameter along x-axis. Rectangle has base on diameter, two upper corners on semicircle.
SOLUTION
1
Upper corner at (x,y) on circle: y=√(25−x²). Rectangle dimensions: width=2x, height=y=√(25−x²).
2
A=2x√(25−x²). Domain: 0
3
A′=2√(25−x²)+2x·\dfrac{-x}{\sqrt{25-x^2}}=\dfrac{2(25-x^2)-2x^2}{\sqrt{25-x^2}}=\dfrac{2(25-2x^2)}{\sqrt{25-x^2}}
4
A′=0 → 25−2x²=0 → x=5/√2. y=√(25−25/2)=5/√2.
5
Width=2x=5√2, height=5/√2=5√2/2. Area=25.
ANSWER Width=\(5\sqrt{2}\) cm, height=\(\dfrac{5\sqrt{2}}{2}\) cm, max area=25 cm²
12
Differentiate \(y=\dfrac{(x^2+1)^3\sqrt{2x-1}}{(3x+2)^4}\) using logarithmic differentiation.
Take ln of both sides and use log laws to split into sums. Then differentiate term by term.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(\ln y=3\ln(x^2+1)+\frac{1}{2}\ln(2x-1)-4\ln(3x+2)\)
2
\(\dfrac{y'}{y}=\dfrac{6x}{x^2+1}+\dfrac{1}{2x-1}-\dfrac{12}{3x+2}\)
3
\(y'=y\cdot\left[\dfrac{6x}{x^2+1}+\dfrac{1}{2x-1}-\dfrac{12}{3x+2}\right]\)
ANSWER \(y'=\dfrac{(x^2+1)^3\sqrt{2x-1}}{(3x+2)^4}\cdot\left[\dfrac{6x}{x^2+1}+\dfrac{1}{2x-1}-\dfrac{12}{3x+2}\right]\)
13
A particle moves along a line with position \(s(t)=t^3-9t^2+24t-5\) (metres, \(t\geq 0\) seconds). Find when the particle is: (a) at rest, (b) moving in the positive direction, (c) at its maximum positive displacement.
Velocity = s′. Find when v=0, determine sign of v in each interval, and identify the max s.
▶
SOLUTION
1
v=s′=3t²−18t+24=3(t−2)(t−4). v=0 at t=2 and t=4.
2
(a) At rest: t=2 s and t=4 s.
3
(b) v>0 on [0,2) and (4,∞). Moving in positive direction: 0≤t<2 and t>4.
4
(c) s(2)=8−36+48−5=15 m. s(4)=64−144+96−5=11 m. Max positive displacement at t=2: s=15 m.
ANSWER (a) t=2,4 s   (b) 0≤t<2 and t>4   (c) Max 15 m at t=2 s
14
Prove that the function \(f(x)=x+e^{-x}\) is always convex (concave up) and find the global minimum value.
Show f′′>0 everywhere. Find f′=0 for the minimum.
▶
SOLUTION
1
f′=1−e^{−x}=0 → e^{−x}=1 → x=0. Stationary point at (0,1).
2
f′′=e^{−x}>0 for all x (since e^{−x} is always positive). So f is always concave up — confirmed convex.
3
Since f′′>0 everywhere, the stationary point at x=0 is a global minimum.
4
f(0)=0+e^0=1. Global minimum value is 1.
ANSWER f is convex everywhere (f′′=e^{−x}>0). Global minimum value = 1 at x=0.
