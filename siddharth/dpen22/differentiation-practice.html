LEVEL 1
Foundations & Basic Rules
First principles, power rule, notation, basic derivatives
1
Differentiate \(f(x)=x^2\) from first principles.
Use \(f'(x)=\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}\). Expand, simplify, take limit.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(f(x+h)=(x+h)^2=x^2+2xh+h^2\)
2
\(f(x+h)-f(x)=x^2+2xh+h^2-x^2=2xh+h^2=h(2x+h)\)
3
\(\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}=\frac{h(2x+h)}{h}=2x+h\)
4
\(f'(x)=\lim_{h\to 0}(2x+h)=2x\)
ANSWER \(f'(x)=2x\)
2
Differentiate \(f(x)=\sqrt{x}\) from first principles.
Write \(f(x)=x^{1/2}\). Use conjugate to rationalize the numerator.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(f'(x)=\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{\sqrt{x+h}-\sqrt{x}}{h}\)
2
Multiply by conjugate: \(\frac{\sqrt{x+h}-\sqrt{x}}{h}\cdot\frac{\sqrt{x+h}+\sqrt{x}}{\sqrt{x+h}+\sqrt{x}}=\frac{(x+h)-x}{h(\sqrt{x+h}+\sqrt{x})}=\frac{h}{h(\sqrt{x+h}+\sqrt{x})}\)
3
\(=\frac{1}{\sqrt{x+h}+\sqrt{x}}\)
4
\(f'(x)=\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{1}{\sqrt{x+h}+\sqrt{x}}=\frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}\)
ANSWER \(f'(x)=\dfrac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}\)
3
Differentiate \(y=\dfrac{3}{x^2}-\dfrac{2}{\sqrt{x}}+x^3-5\).
Rewrite with negative and fractional exponents, then apply power rule.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Rewrite: \(y=3x^{-2}-2x^{-1/2}+x^3-5\)
2
Apply power rule: \(\frac{dy}{dx}=3(-2)x^{-3}-2(-\frac{1}{2})x^{-3/2}+3x^2-0\)
3
\(=-6x^{-3}+x^{-3/2}+3x^2\)
4
Simplify: \(3x^2+\frac{1}{x^{3/2}}-\frac{6}{x^3}\)
ANSWER \(\dfrac{dy}{dx}=3x^2+\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x^3}}-\dfrac{6}{x^3}\)
4
Find \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\left[4x^5-7x^3+2x-9\right]\).
Apply power rule, constant multiple, sum/difference rules.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Differentiate term by term: \(4(5x^4)-7(3x^2)+2(1)-0\)
2
\(=20x^4-21x^2+2\)
ANSWER \(20x^4-21x^2+2\)
5
Differentiate \(f(x)=\dfrac{1}{x^3}+\sqrt[3]{x^2}\).
Rewrite as \(x^{-3}+x^{2/3}\).
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(f(x)=x^{-3}+x^{2/3}\)
2
\(f'(x)=-3x^{-4}+\frac{2}{3}x^{-1/3}\)
3
\(=-\frac{3}{x^4}+\frac{2}{3\sqrt[3]{x}}\)
ANSWER \(f'(x)=-\dfrac{3}{x^4}+\dfrac{2}{3\sqrt[3]{x}}\)
6
Find \(f'(2)\) if \(f(x)=3x^2-4x+7\).
Differentiate first, then substitute x=2.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(f'(x)=6x-4\)
2
\(f'(2)=6(2)-4=12-4=8\)
ANSWER \(f'(2)=8\)

Differentiate \(y=(2x+1)(x^2-3)\) by expanding first.
Expand the product before differentiating.

SOLUTION

\(\frac{dy}{dx}=6x^2+2x-6\)
ANSWER \(\dfrac{dy}{dx}=6x^2+2x-6\)

If \(s(t)=4t^3-2t^2+5t-1\) represents position, find the velocity at t=1.

▶

LEVEL 2
Product, Quotient & Chain Rules
Advanced differentiation rules for compositions and combinations
11
Differentiate \(f(x)=(x^2+1)(x^3-2x)\) using the product rule.
Let u=x²+1 and v=x³-2x. Apply (uv)'=u'v+uv'.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Let \(u=x^2+1\), \(v=x^3-2x\)
2
\(u'=2x\), \(v'=3x^2-2\)
3
\(f'(x)=u'v+uv'=(2x)(x^3-2x)+(x^2+1)(3x^2-2)\)
4
\(=2x^4-4x^2+3x^4-2x^2+3x^2-2=5x^4-3x^2-2\)
ANSWER \(f'(x)=5x^4-3x^2-2\)
12
Differentiate \(f(x)=(x^3+1)\sin x\).
Product rule with trig function.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(u=x^3+1\), \(v=\sin x\)
2
\(u'=3x^2\), \(v'=\cos x\)
3
\(f'(x)=3x^2\sin x+(x^3+1)\cos x\)
ANSWER \(f'(x)=3x^2\sin x+(x^3+1)\cos x\)
13
Differentiate \(f(x)=\dfrac{x^2+1}{x-3}\) using the quotient rule.
Use \(\left(\frac{u}{v}\right)'=\frac{u'v-uv'}{v^2}\).
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(u=x^2+1\), \(v=x-3\)
2
\(u'=2x\), \(v'=1\)
3
\(f'(x)=\frac{2x(x-3)-(x^2+1)(1)}{(x-3)^2}\)
4
\(=\frac{2x^2-6x-x^2-1}{(x-3)^2}=\frac{x^2-6x-1}{(x-3)^2}\)
ANSWER \(f'(x)=\dfrac{x^2-6x-1}{(x-3)^2}\)
14
Differentiate \(f(x)=\dfrac{\ln x}{x^2}\).
Quotient rule with logarithm.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(u=\ln x\), \(v=x^2\); \(u'=\frac{1}{x}\), \(v'=2x\)
2
\(f'(x)=\frac{\frac{1}{x}\cdot x^2-\ln x\cdot 2x}{x^4}\)
3
\(=\frac{x-2x\ln x}{x^4}=\frac{x(1-2\ln x)}{x^4}=\frac{1-2\ln x}{x^3}\)
ANSWER \(f'(x)=\dfrac{1-2\ln x}{x^3}\)
15
Differentiate \(f(x)=(3x+1)^5\) using the chain rule.
Outer function: u⁵. Inner function: 3x+1.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Let \(u=3x+1\), so \(f(x)=u^5\)
2
\(\frac{d}{du}[u^5]=5u^4\), \(\frac{du}{dx}=3\)
3
\(f'(x)=5u^4\cdot 3=15(3x+1)^4\)
ANSWER \(f'(x)=15(3x+1)^4\)
16
Differentiate \(f(x)=\sin(x^2)\).
Chain rule: outer is sin, inner is x².
▶
SOLUTION
1
Outer: \(\sin u\), inner: \(u=x^2\)
2
\(\frac{d}{du}[\sin u]=\cos u\), \(\frac{du}{dx}=2x\)
3
\(f'(x)=\cos(x^2)\cdot 2x=2x\cos(x^2)\)
ANSWER \(f'(x)=2x\cos(x^2)\)
17
Differentiate \(f(x)=\cos(3x-2)\).
Remember d/dx[cos x] = -sin x, then apply chain rule.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Outer: \(\cos u\), inner: \(u=3x-2\)
2
\(\frac{d}{du}[\cos u]=-\sin u\), \(\frac{du}{dx}=3\)
3
\(f'(x)=-\sin(3x-2)\cdot 3=-3\sin(3x-2)\)
ANSWER \(f'(x)=-3\sin(3x-2)\)
18
Differentiate \(f(x)=\sin^4(3x)\).
Nested chain rule: \([\sin(3x)]^4\). Apply twice.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Outermost: \(u^4\) where \(u=\sin(3x)\)
2
\(\frac{d}{du}[u^4]=4u^3=4\sin^3(3x)\)
3
\(\frac{d}{dx}[\sin(3x)]=\cos(3x)\cdot 3=3\cos(3x)\)
4
\(f'(x)=4\sin^3(3x)\cdot 3\cos(3x)=12\sin^3(3x)\cos(3x)\)
ANSWER \(f'(x)=12\sin^3(3x)\cos(3x)\)
19
Differentiate \(f(x)=\tan(x^3+2x)\).
d/dx[tan x]=sec²x. Chain rule for inner function.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Outer: \(\tan u\), inner: \(u=x^3+2x\)
2
\(\frac{d}{du}[\tan u]=\sec^2 u\), \(\frac{du}{dx}=3x^2+2\)
3
\(f'(x)=\sec^2(x^3+2x)\cdot(3x^2+2)\)
ANSWER \(f'(x)=(3x^2+2)\sec^2(x^3+2x)\)
20
Differentiate \(f(x)=e^{x^2}\ln(3x+1)\).
Product rule with both factors requiring chain rule.
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(u=e^{x^2}\), \(v=\ln(3x+1)\)
2
\(u'=e^{x^2}\cdot 2x=2xe^{x^2}\) (chain rule)
3
\(v'=\frac{1}{3x+1}\cdot 3=\frac{3}{3x+1}\) (chain rule)
4
\(f'(x)=2xe^{x^2}\ln(3x+1)+e^{x^2}\cdot\frac{3}{3x+1}\)
5
\(=e^{x^2}\left(2x\ln(3x+1)+\frac{3}{3x+1}\right)\)
ANSWER \(f'(x)=e^{x^2}\left(2x\ln(3x+1)+\dfrac{3}{3x+1}\right)\)

Find the point (2, y(2)) and the slope m=y'(2).

SOLUTION

\(y(2)=4-8+3=-1\). Point: (2,-1)
2
\(y'=2x-4\), so \(y'(2)=4-4=0\)

32
Find the equations of the tangent and normal to \(y=x\ln x\) at \(x=e\).
Tangent has slope y'(e). Normal has slope -1/y'(e).
▶
SOLUTION
1
\(y(e)=e\ln e=e\cdot 1=e\)
2
\(y'=\ln x+x\cdot\frac{1}{x}=\ln x+1\), so \(y'(e)=1+1=2\)
3
Tangent: \(y-e=2(x-e)\) → \(y=2x-e\)
4
Normal slope: \(-\frac{1}{2}\). Normal: \(y-e=-\frac{1}{2}(x-e)\) → \(y=-\frac{x}{2}+\frac{3e}{2}\)
ANSWER Tangent: \(y=2x-e\). Normal: \(y=-\dfrac{x}{2}+\dfrac{3e}{2}\)

41
Find two positive numbers whose sum is 20 and whose product is maximum.
Let x and (20-x) be the numbers. Maximize P=x(20-x).
▶
SOLUTION
1
Let the numbers be x and 20-x. Product: \(P(x)=x(20-x)=20x-x^2\)
2
\(P'(x)=20-2x=0\) → \(x=10\)
3
\(P''(x)=-2<0\) → maximum at x=10
4
The numbers are 10 and 10. Maximum product = 100
ANSWER The numbers are 10 and 10. Max product = 100
42
A farmer has 100 m of fence to enclose a rectangular field along a river (one side needs no fence). Find the maximum area.
Let x be width. Then length = 100-2x. Area A=x(100-2x).
▶
SOLUTION
1
Let width=x. Fence: x+length+x=100 → length=100-2x
2
Area: \(A(x)=x(100-2x)=100x-2x^2\)
3
\(A'(x)=100-4x=0\) → \(x=25\) m
4
Length=100-50=50 m. Max area=25×50=1250 m²
ANSWER Width=25 m, length=50 m. Max area=1250 m²
43
Find the point on the parabola \(y=x^2\) closest to the point (0,1).
Minimize distance squared: \(D^2=x^2+(x^2-1)^2\).
▶
SOLUTION
1
Point on parabola: (x,x²). Distance squared: \(D^2=x^2+(x^2-1)^2\)
2
\(D^2=x^2+x^4-2x^2+1=x^4-x^2+1\)
3
\(\frac{d(D^2)}{dx}=4x^3-2x=2x(2x^2-1)=0\) → \(x=0\) or \(x=\pm\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\)
4
Test: x=0 gives D²=1. \(x=\pm\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\) gives \(D^2=\frac{1}{4}+\frac{1}{2}=\frac{3}{4}\) (minimum)
5
When \(x=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\), \(y=\frac{1}{2}\). By symmetry, also \((-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}},\frac{1}{2})\)
ANSWER \(\left(\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}},\dfrac{1}{2}\right)\) and \(\left(-\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}},\dfrac{1}{2}\right)\)
44
A rectangle is inscribed in a semicircle of radius 5. Find the dimensions for maximum area.
Corner at (x,y) on circle: x²+y²=25. Area=2xy. Express in terms of x only.
▶
SOLUTION
1
Rectangle width=2x, height=y=√(25-x²)
2
