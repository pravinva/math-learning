DPEN22 Applied Reverse-Identity Training

These sets train Siddharth to spot patterns inside full problems (equations, simplification chains, and application models), then choose the reverse identity efficiently.
How to use each question:
1) Identify the structure first (double-angle, sum/difference, or product-sum).
2) Rewrite to a simpler target form.
3) Complete the algebra/trig solving and check domain/interval constraints.
Identity Toolbox (use for all sets)
Core Pythagorean:
\(\sin^2x+\cos^2x=1,\quad 1-\cos2x=2\sin^2x,\quad 1+\cos2x=2\cos^2x\)
Double-angle (both ways):
\(\sin2x=2\sin x\cos x\)
\(\cos2x=\cos^2x-\sin^2x=1-2\sin^2x=2\cos^2x-1\)
Angle addition/subtraction:
\(\sin(A\pm B)=\sin A\cos B\pm\cos A\sin B\)
\(\cos(A\pm B)=\cos A\cos B\mp\sin A\sin B\)
Product-to-sum / sum-to-product:
\(2\sin A\cos B=\sin(A+B)+\sin(A-B)\)
\(2\cos A\cos B=\cos(A+B)+\cos(A-B)\)
\(2\sin A\sin B=\cos(A-B)-\cos(A+B)\)
\(\cos A+\cos B=2\cos\!\left(\dfrac{A+B}{2}\right)\cos\!\left(\dfrac{A-B}{2}\right)\)
\(\cos A-\cos B=-2\sin\!\left(\dfrac{A+B}{2}\right)\sin\!\left(\dfrac{A-B}{2}\right)\)

Set 1 (Guided, 8 Problems)

  1. Simplify fully: \(\dfrac{1-\cos(2x)}{1+\cos(2x)}\).
    Observe: numerator and denominator both look like half-angle forms.
  2. Solve on \(0\le x\le 2\pi\): \(\cos^2x-\sin^2x=\dfrac12\).
    Observe: left side is a direct reverse double-angle pattern.
  3. Simplify: \(2\sin(3x)\cos(2x)-2\cos(3x)\sin(2x)\).
    Observe: match to \(\sin A\cos B-\cos A\sin B\).
  4. Rewrite as a product and then solve on \(0\le x\le 2\pi\): \(\cos(5x)-\cos(3x)=0\).
    Observe: difference of cosines is easier as product of sines.
  5. Show that \((1+\cos2\theta)^2-(\sin2\theta)^2=2(1+\cos2\theta)\cos2\theta\).
    Observe: treat as difference of squares in the first step.
  6. A vibration model gives \(y(t)=4\sin(2t)\cos t\). Rewrite as a sum of simple sinusoids.
    Observe: use product-to-sum so each term has one angle only.
  7. Solve on \(0\le x\le 2\pi\): \(1-2\sin^2x=-\dfrac{\sqrt2}{2}\).
    Observe: reverse to \(\cos2x\) before solving.
  8. Simplify \(\sin(4x)\cos x+\cos(4x)\sin x\), then evaluate at \(x=15^\circ\).
    Observe: angle-addition reverse pattern appears immediately.

Set 2 (Semi-Guided, 8 Problems)

  1. Simplify fully: \(\dfrac{\cos(4x)-1}{\sin(2x)}\) (where defined).
  2. Solve on \(0\le x\le 2\pi\): \(2\sin x\cos x=\dfrac{\sqrt3}{2}\).
  3. Condense and solve on \(0\le x\le 2\pi\): \(\sin(2x)\cos(3x)+\cos(2x)\sin(3x)=0\).
  4. Express as product, then solve: \(\cos(7x)+\cos(3x)=0\), \(0\le x\le 2\pi\).
  5. Simplify: \((\cos^2x-\sin^2x)^2+(2\sin x\cos x)^2\).
  6. A signal mixer output is \(v(t)=6\cos(5t)\cos(2t)\). Rewrite as a sum of two cosine signals.
  7. Solve on \(0\le x\le 2\pi\): \(\dfrac{1-\cos(2x)}{2}=\dfrac34\).
  8. Prove: \(\sin(5x)\cos x-\cos(5x)\sin x=\sin(4x)\).

Set 3 (Exam-Style Mixed, 8 Problems)

  1. Solve on \(0\le x\le 2\pi\): \(\cos^2x-\sin^2x=\sin x\).
  2. Simplify: \(\dfrac{1+\cos(2x)}{\sin x\cos x}\) (where defined).
  3. Given \(f(x)=\cos(3x)\cos(2x)-\sin(3x)\sin(2x)\), rewrite with one trig function and find all zeros on \(0\le x\le 2\pi\).
  4. Solve: \(2\cos(4x)\cos x=\cos(5x)\), \(0\le x\le 2\pi\).
  5. Simplify and solve on \(0\le x\le2\pi\): \(\cos(6x)-\cos(2x)=0\).
  6. A rotating component has torque model \(\tau(\theta)=8(\cos^2\theta-\sin^2\theta)\). Find all \(\theta\in[0,2\pi]\) where torque is zero.
  7. Rewrite \(g(x)=3(1-2\sin^2x)+2(2\sin x\cos x)\) as \(a\cos(2x)+b\sin(2x)\).
  8. Show that \(2\sin(4x)\cos(3x)-2\cos(4x)\sin(3x)=2\sin x\), then solve \(2\sin x=1\) on \(0\le x\le2\pi\).

Worked Solutions — Set 1

  1. \(\dfrac{1-\cos2x}{1+\cos2x}=\dfrac{2\sin^2x}{2\cos^2x}=\tan^2x\).
  2. \(\cos2x=\frac12\Rightarrow 2x=\frac{\pi}{3},\frac{5\pi}{3},\frac{7\pi}{3},\frac{11\pi}{3}\Rightarrow x=\frac{\pi}{6},\frac{5\pi}{6},\frac{7\pi}{6},\frac{11\pi}{6}\).
  3. \(2[\sin3x\cos2x-\cos3x\sin2x]=2\sin(3x-2x)=2\sin x\).
  4. \(\cos5x-\cos3x=-2\sin4x\sin x=0\Rightarrow \sin4x=0\) or \(\sin x=0\). On \([0,2\pi]\): \(x=\dfrac{n\pi}{4},\ n=0,1,\dots,8\).
  5. LHS \(=[(1+\cos2\theta)-\sin2\theta][(1+\cos2\theta)+\sin2\theta]\). Expanding with \(\sin^2+\cos^2=1\) gives \(2\cos2\theta(1+\cos2\theta)\), as required.
  6. \(4\sin2t\cos t=2[2\sin2t\cos t]=2[\sin3t+\sin t]=2\sin3t+2\sin t\).
  7. \(\cos2x=-\dfrac{\sqrt2}{2}\Rightarrow 2x=\dfrac{3\pi}{4},\dfrac{5\pi}{4},\dfrac{11\pi}{4},\dfrac{13\pi}{4}\Rightarrow x=\dfrac{3\pi}{8},\dfrac{5\pi}{8},\dfrac{11\pi}{8},\dfrac{13\pi}{8}\).
  8. \(\sin(4x+x)=\sin5x\). At \(x=15^\circ\): \(\sin75^\circ=\dfrac{\sqrt6+\sqrt2}{4}\).

Worked Solutions — Set 2

  1. \(\dfrac{\cos4x-1}{\sin2x}=\dfrac{-2\sin^22x}{\sin2x}=-2\sin2x\).
  2. \(\sin2x=\dfrac{\sqrt3}{2}\Rightarrow 2x=\dfrac{\pi}{3},\dfrac{2\pi}{3},\dfrac{7\pi}{3},\dfrac{8\pi}{3}\Rightarrow x=\dfrac{\pi}{6},\dfrac{\pi}{3},\dfrac{7\pi}{6},\dfrac{4\pi}{3}\).
  3. \(\sin(2x+3x)=\sin5x=0\Rightarrow x=\dfrac{n\pi}{5}\) for \(n=0,1,\dots,10\).
  4. \(\cos7x+\cos3x=2\cos5x\cos2x=0\Rightarrow \cos5x=0\) or \(\cos2x=0\), then list solutions on \([0,2\pi]\).
  5. \((\cos2x)^2+(\sin2x)^2=1\).
  6. \(6\cos5t\cos2t=3[2\cos5t\cos2t]=3[\cos7t+\cos3t]\).
  7. \(\sin^2x=\dfrac34\Rightarrow \sin x=\pm\dfrac{\sqrt3}{2}\Rightarrow x=\dfrac{\pi}{3},\dfrac{2\pi}{3},\dfrac{4\pi}{3},\dfrac{5\pi}{3}\).
  8. Direct reverse formula: \(\sin A\cos B-\cos A\sin B=\sin(A-B)\Rightarrow \sin(5x-x)=\sin4x\).

Worked Solutions — Set 3

  1. \(\cos2x=\sin x\). Use \(\cos2x=1-2\sin^2x\): \(1-2\sin^2x=\sin x\Rightarrow 2s^2+s-1=0\Rightarrow s=\dfrac12\) or \(-1\). So \(x=\dfrac{\pi}{6},\dfrac{5\pi}{6},\dfrac{3\pi}{2}\).
  2. \(\dfrac{1+\cos2x}{\sin x\cos x}=\dfrac{2\cos^2x}{\sin x\cos x}=2\cot x\).
  3. \(f(x)=\cos(3x+2x)=\cos5x\). Zeros: \(5x=\dfrac{\pi}{2}+k\pi\Rightarrow x=\dfrac{\pi}{10}+\dfrac{k\pi}{5}\), keep \(0\le x\le2\pi\).
  4. \(2\cos4x\cos x=\cos5x+\cos3x=\cos5x\Rightarrow \cos3x=0\). So \(3x=\dfrac{\pi}{2}+k\pi\Rightarrow x=\dfrac{\pi}{6}+\dfrac{k\pi}{3}\).
  5. \(\cos6x-\cos2x=-2\sin4x\sin2x=0\Rightarrow \sin4x=0\) or \(\sin2x=0\), then solve on interval.
  6. \(\tau(\theta)=8\cos2\theta\). Zero torque: \(\cos2\theta=0\Rightarrow 2\theta=\dfrac{\pi}{2},\dfrac{3\pi}{2},\dfrac{5\pi}{2},\dfrac{7\pi}{2}\Rightarrow \theta=\dfrac{\pi}{4},\dfrac{3\pi}{4},\dfrac{5\pi}{4},\dfrac{7\pi}{4}\).
  7. \(g(x)=3\cos2x+2\sin2x\), so \(a=3,\ b=2\).
  8. LHS \(=2\sin(4x-3x)=2\sin x\). Solve \(2\sin x=1\Rightarrow \sin x=\dfrac12\Rightarrow x=\dfrac{\pi}{6},\dfrac{5\pi}{6}\).
