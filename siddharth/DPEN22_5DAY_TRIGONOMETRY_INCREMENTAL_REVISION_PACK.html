DPEN22 5-Day Trigonometry Incremental Revision Pack

Canonical angles, inverse trig, graphs, identities, equations, sine/cosine rules, and compound angles
How to use: Do Day 1 to Day 5 in order. Each day has 4 non-simple questions (deeper exam-style). Complete all questions first, then check the answer key.

Day 1

Focus: Canonical angles, reference angles, signs, and coterminal control
Q1 (Exact values mix, no calculator):

Evaluate exactly without a calculator: \(E=\sin 330^\circ+\cos 240^\circ-\tan 225^\circ\).

Q2 (Reference angle + CAST):

Find exact values of \(\sin\left(-\frac{7\pi}{6}\right)\), \(\cos\left(\frac{13\pi}{6}\right)\), and \(\tan\left(-\frac{11\pi}{4}\right)\).

Q3 (Coordinate to angle):

A unit-circle point is \(\left(-\frac{\sqrt3}{2},-\frac12\right)\). Find all \(\theta\in[0,2\pi)\), and one positive and one negative coterminal angle.

Q4 (Odd/even trig application):

Use odd/even identities to simplify exactly: \(S=\sin(-75^\circ)+\cos(-60^\circ)-\tan(-45^\circ)\).

Day 2

Focus: Trig graphs (parameters, key points, asymptotes, reverse-engineering)
Q1 (Read parameters):

For \(y=-2\cos\!\left(3x-\frac{\pi}{2}\right)+1\), state amplitude, period, phase shift, midline, and range.

Q2 (Key-point sketching):

Find 5 key points for one cycle of \(y=3\sin\!\left(2\left(x-\frac{\pi}{4}\right)\right)-1\).

Q3 (Reverse engineer):

A sine graph has max \(5\), min \(-3\), period \(\pi\), and crosses midline increasing at \(x=\frac{\pi}{6}\). Find an equation.

Q4 (Tangent structure):

For \(y=\tan\!\left(2x+\frac{\pi}{3}\right)\), find period, center crossing, and two adjacent vertical asymptotes near \(x=0\).

Day 3

Focus: Identities and compound/double-angle fluency
Q1 (Identity proof):

Prove \(\dfrac{1-\cos2x}{\sin2x}=\tan x\), stating domain restrictions where needed.

Q2 (Identity simplify):

Simplify fully: \((\sin x+\cos x)^2+(\sin x-\cos x)^2\).

Q3 (Compound exact values):

Evaluate exactly: \(\sin75^\circ,\ \cos15^\circ,\ \tan15^\circ\).

Q4 (Double-angle from given ratio):

Given \(\sin A=\frac35\) and \(A\) acute, find \(\sin2A,\ \cos2A,\ \tan2A\).

Day 4

Focus: Trig equations + inverse trig principal values
Q1 (Quadratic trig equation):

Solve \(2\sin^2x-3\sin x+1=0\) for \(x\in[0,2\pi]\).

Q2 (Transformed-angle equation):

Solve \(\cos\!\left(2x-\frac{\pi}{3}\right)=\frac12\) for \(x\in[0,2\pi]\).

Q3 (Principal-value evaluation):

Evaluate: \(\arcsin\!\left(\sin\frac{7\pi}{6}\right),\ \arccos\!\left(\cos\frac{5\pi}{3}\right),\ \arctan(-\sqrt3)\).

Q4 (Inverse equations with checks):

Solve \( \arctan(2x-1)=\frac{\pi}{4}\) and \( \arcsin(3x-1)=\frac{\pi}{6}\), including validity checks.

Day 5

Focus: Sine/cosine rules and high-value mixed triangle scenarios
Q1 (SAS + area):

In \(\triangle ABC\), \(a=8,\ b=11,\ C=47^\circ\). Find \(c\) and the area.

Q2 (SSA ambiguous case):

Given \(a=9,\ b=11,\ A=40^\circ\), determine the number of possible triangles and find possible \(B\) values.

Q3 (SSS to angle + area):

For sides \(13,14,15\), find the largest angle and the area.

Q4 (Mixed trig + geometry):

A surveyor is \(60\) m from a tower. Angle of elevation to top is \(58^\circ\), and to a lower marker is \(31^\circ\). Find marker height and top-above-marker height.

Answer Key

Day 1

  • Q1 full working: \(\sin330^\circ=\sin(360^\circ-30^\circ)=-\sin30^\circ=-\frac12\). \(\cos240^\circ=\cos(180^\circ+60^\circ)=-\cos60^\circ=-\frac12\). \(\tan225^\circ=\tan(180^\circ+45^\circ)=\tan45^\circ=1\). So \(E=-\frac12-\frac12-1=-2\).
  • Q2 full working: \(\sin(-7\pi/6)=-\sin(7\pi/6)=-(-\frac12)=\frac12\). \(\cos(13\pi/6)=\cos(2\pi+\pi/6)=\cos(\pi/6)=\frac{\sqrt3}{2}\). \(\tan(-11\pi/4)=\tan(-11\pi/4+3\pi)=\tan(\pi/4)=1\).
  • Q3 full working: Coordinates \(\left(-\frac{\sqrt3}{2},-\frac12\right)\) give Q3 with reference angle \(\pi/6\), hence \(\theta=\pi+\pi/6=7\pi/6\). Coterminals: \(7\pi/6+2\pi=19\pi/6\) and \(7\pi/6-2\pi=-5\pi/6\).
  • Q4 full working: \(S=\sin(-75^\circ)+\cos(-60^\circ)-\tan(-45^\circ)\). Use odd/even: \(\sin(-75^\circ)=-\sin75^\circ,\ \cos(-60^\circ)=\cos60^\circ,\ \tan(-45^\circ)=-\tan45^\circ\). So \(S=-\sin75^\circ+\frac12+1\). Also \(\sin75^\circ=\frac{\sqrt6+\sqrt2}{4}\). Therefore \(S=\frac{6-\sqrt6-\sqrt2}{4}\).

Day 2

  • Q1 full working: Write \(y=a\cos(bx+c)+d\). Here \(a=-2,\ b=3,\ c=-\pi/2,\ d=1\). Amplitude \(=|a|=2\). Period \(=2\pi/|b|=2\pi/3\). Phase shift \(=-c/b=(\pi/2)/3=\pi/6\) right. Midline \(y=1\). Range \(d\pm|a|=[-1,3]\).
  • Q2 full working: \(y=3\sin(2(x-\pi/4))-1\). So \(a=3,\ d=-1,\ T=2\pi/2=\pi,\ T/4=\pi/4\). One cycle starts at \(x=\pi/4\): \(x=\pi/4,\pi/2,3\pi/4,\pi,5\pi/4\). Sine pattern \(0,1,0,-1,0\). Multiply by 3 then shift down 1 gives \(y=-1,2,-1,-4,-1\).
  • Q3 full working: Max \(5\), min \(-3\) \(\Rightarrow a=(5-(-3))/2=4,\ d=(5+(-3))/2=1\). Period \(\pi\Rightarrow b=2\). “Crosses midline increasing” means sine start at phase point \(x=h\), so \(h=\pi/6\). Hence \(y=4\sin(2(x-\pi/6))+1\).
  • Q4 full working: For \(\tan\), period \(=\pi/|b|=\pi/2\). Center crossing when \(2x+\pi/3=0\Rightarrow x=-\pi/6\). Asymptotes satisfy \(2x+\pi/3=\pi/2+k\pi\Rightarrow x=\pi/12+k\pi/2\). Closest adjacent around 0: \(k=-1\Rightarrow -5\pi/12\), \(k=0\Rightarrow \pi/12\).

Day 3

  • Q1 full working: \(\dfrac{1-\cos2x}{\sin2x}=\dfrac{2\sin^2x}{2\sin x\cos x}=\dfrac{\sin x}{\cos x}=\tan x\). Domain note: denominator \(\sin2x\neq0\), so \(x\neq n\pi/2\).
  • Q2 full working: Expand both squares: \((\sin x+\cos x)^2=\sin^2x+2\sin x\cos x+\cos^2x\), \((\sin x-\cos x)^2=\sin^2x-2\sin x\cos x+\cos^2x\). Add: \(2\sin^2x+2\cos^2x=2(\sin^2x+\cos^2x)=2\).
  • Q3 full working: \(\sin75^\circ=\sin(45^\circ+30^\circ)=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\frac{\sqrt3}{2}+\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\frac12=\frac{\sqrt6+\sqrt2}{4}\). \(\cos15^\circ=\cos(45^\circ-30^\circ)=\frac{\sqrt6+\sqrt2}{4}\). \(\tan15^\circ=\tan(45^\circ-30^\circ)=\frac{1-1/\sqrt3}{1+1/\sqrt3}=2-\sqrt3\).
  • Q4 full working: Given \(\sin A=3/5\), acute \(\Rightarrow \cos A=4/5\). Then \(\sin2A=2\sin A\cos A=2(3/5)(4/5)=24/25\). \(\cos2A=\cos^2A-\sin^2A=(16-9)/25=7/25\). \(\tan2A=\sin2A/\cos2A=(24/25)/(7/25)=24/7\).

Day 4

  • Q1 full working: \(2\sin^2x-3\sin x+1=0\Rightarrow (2\sin x-1)(\sin x-1)=0\). So \(\sin x=1/2\) or \(1\). In \([0,2\pi]\): \(\sin x=1/2\Rightarrow x=\pi/6,5\pi/6\); \(\sin x=1\Rightarrow x=\pi/2\).
  • Q2 full working: Set \(u=2x-\pi/3\). Then \(\cos u=1/2\Rightarrow u=\pm\pi/3+2k\pi\). Case 1: \(2x-\pi/3=\pi/3+2k\pi\Rightarrow x=\pi/3+k\pi\). Case 2: \(2x-\pi/3=-\pi/3+2k\pi\Rightarrow x=k\pi\). In \([0,2\pi]\): \(x=0,\pi/3,\pi,4\pi/3,2\pi\).
  • Q3 full working: \(\sin(7\pi/6)=-1/2\), principal arcsin value is \(-\pi/6\). \(\cos(5\pi/3)=1/2\), principal arccos in \([0,\pi]\) is \(\pi/3\). \(\arctan(-\sqrt3)\) in \((-\pi/2,\pi/2)\) is \(-\pi/3\).
  • Q4 full working: \(\arctan(2x-1)=\pi/4\Rightarrow 2x-1=\tan(\pi/4)=1\Rightarrow x=1\). Also \(\arcsin(3x-1)=\pi/6\Rightarrow 3x-1=\sin(\pi/6)=1/2\Rightarrow x=1/2\). Check: \(3(1/2)-1=1/2\in[-1,1]\), valid.

Day 5

  • Q1 full working: SAS use cosine rule: \(c^2=8^2+11^2-2(8)(11)\cos47^\circ=64+121-176\cos47^\circ\approx68.225\). So \(c\approx\sqrt{68.225}=8.26\). Area uses included angle: \(A=\frac12ab\sin C=\frac12(8)(11)\sin47^\circ\approx32.20\).
  • Q2 full working: Sine rule: \(\frac{\sin B}{11}=\frac{\sin40^\circ}{9}\Rightarrow \sin B=\frac{11\sin40^\circ}{9}\approx0.7856\). So \(B_1=\sin^{-1}(0.7856)\approx51.8^\circ\). Second angle \(B_2=180^\circ-51.8^\circ=128.2^\circ\). Check with \(A=40^\circ\): \(40+128.2=168.2<180\), so both valid \(\Rightarrow\) 2 triangles.
  • Q3 full working: Largest side \(15\Rightarrow\) largest angle opposite it, call it \(C\). \(\cos C=\frac{13^2+14^2-15^2}{2(13)(14)}=\frac{140}{364}=\frac{5}{13}\), hence \(C\approx67.4^\circ\). Then \(\sin C=\sqrt{1-(5/13)^2}=12/13\). Area \(=\frac12(13)(14)\frac{12}{13}=84\).
  • Q4 full working: Marker height \(h_m=60\tan31^\circ\approx36.05\) m. Top height \(h_t=60\tan58^\circ\approx96.03\) m. Height from marker to top \(=h_t-h_m\approx59.98\) m (about \(60.0\) m).
