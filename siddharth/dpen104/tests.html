In-Class Tests & Mock Exam
ENGG104 is assessed by two in-class tests (Quiz 1 & Quiz 2, 30% combined) and a final exam (40%). Below are practice versions of each, written to match the syllabus split — try them closed-book and timed, then reveal the full worked solutions.
Treat these like the real thing. Test 1 covers DC through inductance (Quiz 1 falls around Week 7). Test 2 covers AC (Quiz 2 around Week 12). The Mock Exam samples the whole subject. Work on paper, show every step, and check your method — not just the final number — against the solution.

In-Class Test 1 · Weeks 1-7
DC Circuits, Capacitance & Inductance
Closed book. Calculator allowed. Show all working.
8 questions~45 min50 marksGuides 1-3
Q1A 9 V battery drives 0.3 A through a resistor. Find (a) the resistance and (b) the power dissipated.5 marks
▶ Show solution
(a) \(R=V/I=9/0.3=30\,\Omega\). (b) \(P=VI=9\times0.3=2.7\,\text{W}\) (or \(I^2R=0.09\times30=2.7\,\text{W}\)).
Q2Resistors of 4 Ω, 6 Ω and 10 Ω are in series across 40 V. Find the current and the voltage across the 6 Ω resistor.6 marks
▶ Show solution
\(R_T=4+6+10=20\,\Omega\). \(I=40/20=2\,\text{A}\). \(V_6=IR_6=2\times6=12\,\text{V}\) (or VDR: \(40\times6/20=12\,\text{V}\)).
Q3Find the equivalent resistance of 12 Ω, 12 Ω and 6 Ω all in parallel.5 marks
▶ Show solution
\[\frac{1}{R_p}=\frac{1}{12}+\frac{1}{12}+\frac{1}{6}=\frac{1+1+2}{12}=\frac{4}{12}\]
\(R_p=3\,\Omega\).
Q4A 20 V source feeds 5 Ω in series with (10 Ω ∥ 10 Ω). Find the source current and the voltage across the parallel pair.7 marks
▶ Show solution
Parallel: \(10\parallel10=5\,\Omega\). \(R_T=5+5=10\,\Omega\). \(I_s=20/10=2\,\text{A}\). \(V_{pair}=I_s\times5=2\times5=\)\(10\,\text{V}\).
Q5State (a) Kirchhoff's Voltage Law and (b) Kirchhoff's Current Law, and say which conservation principle each expresses.4 marks
▶ Show solution
(a) KVL: the algebraic sum of voltages around any closed loop = 0 — conservation of energy. (b) KCL: the sum of currents into a node = sum out — conservation of charge.
Q6A network seen by a load gives \(R_{Th}=28\,\Omega\) and \(E_{Th}=32\,\text{V}\). Find the load current when \(R_L=4\,\Omega\), and the maximum power the network can deliver.7 marks
▶ Show solution
\(I_L=E_{Th}/(R_{Th}+R_L)=32/(28+4)=32/32=\)\(1\,\text{A}\). Max power at \(R_L=R_{Th}=28\,\Omega\): \(P_{max}=E_{Th}^2/(4R_{Th})=32^2/112=\)\(9.14\,\text{W}\).
Q7A 100 µF capacitor charges from a 24 V source through a 10 kΩ resistor. Find (a) the time constant, (b) the capacitor voltage after one time constant, (c) the initial charging current.8 marks
▶ Show solution
(a) \(\tau=RC=10^4\times100\times10^{-6}=1\,\text{s}\). (b) \(v_C=24(1-e^{-1})=24\times0.632=15.2\,\text{V}\). (c) \(i_0=E/R=24/10000=2.4\,\text{mA}\).
Q8An inductor \(L=0.8\,\text{H}\) carries a current changing at 50 A/s. (a) Find the induced voltage. (b) If it carries a steady 3 A, find the stored energy.8 marks
▶ Show solution
(a) \(v_L=L\,di/dt=0.8\times50=40\,\text{V}\). (b) \(W=\tfrac12LI^2=\tfrac12(0.8)(9)=3.6\,\text{J}\).
In-Class Test 2 · Weeks 8-12
AC Circuits
Closed book. Calculator allowed. Show all working.
8 questions~45 min50 marksGuide 4
Q1A sinusoidal voltage is \(v=311\sin(314t)\,\text{V}\). Find (a) the peak, (b) the RMS value, (c) the frequency, (d) the period.6 marks
▶ Show solution
(a) \(V_m=311\,\text{V}\). (b) \(V_{rms}=311/\sqrt2=220\,\text{V}\). (c) \(f=\omega/2\pi=314/6.283=50\,\text{Hz}\). (d) \(T=1/50=20\,\text{ms}\).
Q2Find the reactance of (a) a 0.15 H inductor and (b) a 22 µF capacitor, both at 50 Hz.6 marks
▶ Show solution
(a) \(X_L=2\pi fL=2\pi(50)(0.15)=47.1\,\Omega\). (b) \(X_C=1/(2\pi fC)=1/(2\pi\cdot50\cdot22\times10^{-6})=145\,\Omega\).
Q3A series circuit has \(R=12\,\Omega\) and \(X_L=16\,\Omega\). Find the impedance magnitude and the phase angle. Does voltage lead or lag current?6 marks
▶ Show solution
\(|Z|=\sqrt{12^2+16^2}=\sqrt{400}=\)\(20\,\Omega\). \(\theta=\tan^{-1}(16/12)=\)\(53.1^\circ\). Inductive → voltage leads current.
Q4The circuit in Q3 is driven by 120 V RMS. Find the current and the voltage across the inductor.6 marks
▶ Show solution
\(I=V/|Z|=120/20=\)\(6\,\text{A}\). \(V_L=IX_L=6\times16=\)\(96\,\text{V}\). (Check: \(V_R=6\times12=72\,\text{V}\); \(\sqrt{72^2+96^2}=120\,\text{V}\) ✓.)
Q5A series RLC has \(R=8\,\Omega\), \(X_L=30\,\Omega\), \(X_C=24\,\Omega\). Find \(|Z|\). What changes at resonance?7 marks
▶ Show solution
\(X=X_L-X_C=6\,\Omega\); \(|Z|=\sqrt{8^2+6^2}=\)\(10\,\Omega\). At resonance \(X_L=X_C\) so they cancel, \(Z=R=8\,\Omega\) (minimum) and current is maximum.
Q6A load draws 6 A from a 230 V supply at PF = 0.8 lagging. Find the apparent, real and reactive power.7 marks
▶ Show solution
\(S=VI=230\times6=\)\(1380\,\text{VA}\). \(P=S\cos\theta=1380\times0.8=\)\(1104\,\text{W}\). \(\sin\theta=0.6\Rightarrow Q=1380\times0.6=\)\(828\,\text{VAR}\).
Q7Explain "power factor" and one practical reason a utility penalises a low power factor.5 marks
▶ Show solution
PF \(=\cos\theta=P/S\): the fraction of apparent power that does real work. A low PF means more current for the same real power, so cables must be larger and \(I^2R\) line losses rise — costing the utility more, hence the penalty.
Q8A full-wave bridge rectifier is fed from a transformer giving 18 V RMS. Find the peak output and the average DC output (ideal diodes).7 marks
▶ Show solution
\(V_m=18\sqrt2=25.5\,\text{V}\). Full-wave: \(V_{DC}=0.637V_m=0.637\times25.5=\)\(16.2\,\text{V}\).
Mock Final Exam · Whole Subject
DPEN104 Mock Examination
Closed book. Calculator allowed. Attempt all questions. Show all working.
12 questions~2 hours100 marksAll guides
Q1Define charge, current and voltage, giving the SI unit and defining equation of each.6 marks
▶ Show solution
Charge \(Q\), coulomb (C), \(Q=It\). Current \(I\), ampere (A), \(I=Q/t\) (rate of charge flow). Voltage \(V\), volt (V), \(V=W/Q\) (energy per coulomb).
Q2A 60 W lamp runs on 240 V. Find its operating current and resistance.6 marks
▶ Show solution
\(I=P/V=60/240=0.25\,\text{A}\). \(R=V/I=240/0.25=960\,\Omega\) (or \(V^2/P=240^2/60=960\,\Omega\)).
Q3A 48 V source feeds 8 Ω in series with (24 Ω ∥ 12 Ω). Find \(R_T\), the source current, and the current in the 24 Ω.9 marks
▶ Show solution
\(24\parallel12=\tfrac{24\times12}{36}=8\,\Omega\); \(R_T=8+8=16\,\Omega\). \(I_s=48/16=3\,\text{A}\). \(V_{pair}=3\times8=24\,\text{V}\); \(I_{24}=24/24=\)\(1\,\text{A}\) (and \(I_{12}=2\,\text{A}\), sum = 3 A ✓).
Q4Convert a 36 V source in series with 9 Ω into an equivalent current source.5 marks
▶ Show solution
\(I=E/R=36/9=4\,\text{A}\) in parallel with the same \(9\,\Omega\).
Q5State the three steps of Thévenin's theorem, then find \(R_{Th}\) for a network where, with the source shorted, 10 Ω is in parallel with 40 Ω and that combination is in series with 20 Ω.9 marks
▶ Show solution
Steps: (1) remove the load; (2) kill sources and find \(R_{Th}\) at the terminals; (3) restore sources and find the open-circuit voltage \(E_{Th}\).
\[R_{Th}=\frac{10\times40}{10+40}+20=8+20=28\,\Omega\]
Q6Briefly explain the superposition theorem and how each source type is "turned off."6 marks
▶ Show solution
In a linear circuit, the response is the algebraic sum of the responses to each source acting alone. To turn off a voltage source → short it; a current source → open it. Internal resistances remain.
Q7A 47 µF capacitor charges from 30 V through 47 kΩ. Find (a) τ, (b) the voltage after 2τ, (c) the energy stored when fully charged.9 marks
▶ Show solution
(a) \(\tau=RC=47000\times47\times10^{-6}=2.21\,\text{s}\). (b) \(v_C=30(1-e^{-2})=30\times0.865=26.0\,\text{V}\). (c) \(W=\tfrac12CV^2=\tfrac12(47\mu)(30^2)=21.2\,\text{mJ}\).
Q8A 2 H inductor with series resistance 50 Ω is switched onto 100 V. Find (a) τ, (b) the final current, (c) the current after one time constant.8 marks
▶ Show solution
(a) \(\tau=L/R=2/50=0.04\,\text{s}\). (b) \(I_f=E/R=100/50=2\,\text{A}\). (c) \(i=2(1-e^{-1})=2\times0.632=1.26\,\text{A}\).
Q9A 230 V (RMS), 50 Hz supply drives a coil of \(R=30\,\Omega\) and \(L=0.1\,\text{H}\) in series. Find \(X_L\), \(|Z|\), the current and the phase angle.10 marks
▶ Show solution
\(X_L=2\pi(50)(0.1)=31.4\,\Omega\). \(|Z|=\sqrt{30^2+31.4^2}=\sqrt{900+986}=43.4\,\Omega\). \(I=230/43.4=5.30\,\text{A}\). \(\theta=\tan^{-1}(31.4/30)=46.3^\circ\) (V leads I).
Q10For the coil in Q9, find the real, reactive and apparent power and the power factor.8 marks
▶ Show solution
\(S=VI=230\times5.30=1219\,\text{VA}\). PF \(=\cos46.3^\circ=0.691\). \(P=S\times\text{PF}=1219\times0.691=842\,\text{W}\) (= \(I^2R=5.30^2\times30=843\,\text{W}\) ✓). \(Q=S\sin46.3^\circ=1219\times0.723=881\,\text{VAR}\).
Q11A series RLC has \(R=10\,\Omega\), \(L=20\,\text{mH}\), \(C=5\,\mu\text{F}\). Find the resonant frequency.6 marks
▶ Show solution
\[f_0=\frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}}=\frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{(20\times10^{-3})(5\times10^{-6})}}=\frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{10^{-7}}}\]
\(=\frac{1}{2\pi(3.16\times10^{-4})}=\)\(503\,\text{Hz}\).
Q12(a) Sketch/describe how a PN junction conducts in only one direction. (b) A silicon diode in series with 1.5 kΩ across 6 V — find the current (0.7 V model).8 marks
▶ Show solution
(a) Forward bias (+ to P) shrinks the depletion region so charge carriers cross → conducts above ~0.7 V. Reverse bias (+ to N) widens the depletion region → blocks. Hence one-way conduction. (b) \(I=(6-0.7)/1500=5.3/1500=3.53\,\text{mA}\).
