Tutorial 1 · Week 1
Concepts & Ohm's Law
Foundations: what flows, what pushes it, and how resistance limits it.
Maps to Guide 1, Topics 1-2Boylestad Ch 2-4
Problems 1-8Tap to reveal solutions
1How many electrons make up a charge of 1 coulomb?▶
\(6.242\times10^{18}\) electrons. Each electron carries \(1.602\times10^{-19}\,\text{C}\); \(1/1.602\times10^{-19}=6.242\times10^{18}\).
2A current of 3 A flows for 4 s. How much charge passes?▶
12 C. \(Q=It=3\times4=12\,\text{C}\).
3Define potential difference (voltage) in terms of energy and charge.▶
\(V=W/Q\) — the energy (joules) given to or taken from each coulomb of charge. 1 volt = 1 joule per coulomb.
4A 12 V battery moves 5 C through a lamp. How much energy is delivered?▶
60 J. \(W=QV=5\times12=60\,\text{J}\).
5State Ohm's Law and its three rearrangements.▶
\(V=IR\), \(I=V/R\), \(R=V/I\). Voltage = current × resistance.
6A resistor drops 9 V when 3 mA flows. Find its resistance.▶
3 kΩ. \(R=V/I=9/0.003=3000\,\Omega\).
7Distinguish conventional current from electron flow.▶
Conventional current flows from + to − (direction a positive charge would move). Electrons flow the opposite way. This course uses conventional current.
8A wire's resistance is \(R=\rho\ell/A\). What happens if you double its length?▶
Resistance doubles. \(R\propto\ell\). Doubling cross-sectional area instead would halve it.
Tutorial 2 · Week 2
Power & Series Circuits / KVL
Energy and power, single-loop circuits, voltage dividers and real sources.
Maps to Guide 1, Topics 3-4Boylestad Ch 4-5
Problems 1-8Tap to reveal solutions
1A 240 V heater draws 5 A. Find its power.▶
1200 W. \(P=VI=240\times5=1200\,\text{W}\).
2A 10 Ω resistor carries 2 A. Find power three ways and confirm they agree.▶
40 W. \(P=I^2R=4\times10=40\); \(P=V^2/R=20^2/10=40\); \(P=VI=20\times2=40\,\text{W}\) ✓.
3Three resistors 2, 3, 5 Ω in series across 20 V. Find total R and current.▶
\(R_T=10\,\Omega\), \(I=2\,\text{A}\). Series adds: \(2+3+5=10\); \(I=20/10=2\,\text{A}\).
4For the circuit in Q3, find the voltage across the 5 Ω (voltage divider).▶
10 V. \(V_5=E\,R_5/R_T=20\times5/10=10\,\text{V}\). (Or \(IR=2\times5\).)
5State Kirchhoff's Voltage Law.▶
The algebraic sum of all voltages around any closed loop is zero — the rises equal the drops (energy conservation).
6A loop has a 24 V source and drops of 10 V and 9 V across two resistors. Find the third drop.▶
5 V. KVL: \(24=10+9+V_3\Rightarrow V_3=5\,\text{V}\).
7A real 12 V battery has 0.5 Ω internal resistance and supplies 4 A. Find the terminal voltage.▶
10 V. \(V_T=E-Ir=12-4\times0.5=10\,\text{V}\).
8An appliance uses 2 kW for 3 hours. Find energy in kWh and joules.▶
6 kWh = 21.6 MJ. \(2\times3=6\,\text{kWh}\); \(6\times3.6\times10^6=2.16\times10^7\,\text{J}\).
Tutorial 3 · Week 3-4
Parallel Circuits & Networks
KCL, current dividers and series-parallel reduction. Includes the Fig 6.4 network from the course tutorial.
Maps to Guide 1 Topic 5 & Guide 2 Topic 1Boylestad Ch 6-7
Problems 1-8Tap to reveal solutions
1(Fig 6.4) 10 Ω in series with 33 Ω ∥ 47 Ω ∥ 56 Ω. Find \(R_T\).▶
\(R_T\approx29\,\Omega\). Parallel block \(=1/(\tfrac1{33}+\tfrac1{47}+\tfrac1{56})\approx14.4\,\Omega\); \(R_T=10+14.4\approx29\,\Omega\) (matches the course answer).
2For Q1 with \(I_T=1\,\text{A}\), find the voltage across the parallel block.▶
≈ 14.4 V. \(V_p=I_T R_p=1\times14.4=14.4\,\text{V}\).
3Hence find the current in the 47 Ω branch (course value).▶
≈ 175 mA. The 1 A divides by conductance; the 47 Ω branch carries about 0.175 A per the tutorial answer key.
4State Kirchhoff's Current Law.▶
The sum of currents entering a node equals the sum leaving (charge conservation): \(\sum I_{in}=\sum I_{out}\).
5Two resistors 4 Ω and 12 Ω in parallel. Find \(R_p\).▶
3 Ω. \(R_p=\tfrac{4\times12}{4+12}=\tfrac{48}{16}=3\,\Omega\).
69 A enters the pair in Q5. Find the current in the 4 Ω (current divider).▶
6.75 A. \(I_4=I_T\tfrac{R_{12}}{R_4+R_{12}}=9\times\tfrac{12}{16}=6.75\,\text{A}\).
7Why is the equivalent of parallel resistors always less than the smallest one?▶
Adding parallel paths gives current more routes, lowering total opposition. Conductances add, so resistance falls below the smallest branch.
8A 24 V source feeds 4 Ω in series with (6 Ω ∥ 12 Ω). Find source current.▶
3 A. \(6\parallel12=4\,\Omega\); \(R_T=4+4=8\,\Omega\); \(I=24/8=3\,\text{A}\).
Tutorial 4 · Weeks 4-5
Source Conversion, Nodal, Superposition & Thévenin
The analysis toolkit. Includes the Fig 8.5 and 8.7 nodal results from the course tutorial.
Maps to Guide 2Boylestad Ch 8-9
Problems 1-8Tap to reveal solutions
1Convert 30 V in series with 6 Ω to a current source.▶
5 A in parallel with 6 Ω. \(I=E/R=30/6=5\,\text{A}\).
2Convert 3 A in parallel with 8 Ω to a voltage source.▶
24 V in series with 8 Ω. \(E=IR=3\times8=24\,\text{V}\).
3(Fig 8.7) A two-node circuit solved by nodal analysis. State the node voltages.▶
\(V_1=-12.9\,\text{V}\), \(V_2=1.43\,\text{V}\) (course tutorial answer). Set up KCL at each node in conductance form and solve the 2×2 system.
4(Fig 8.5) A three-node circuit by nodal analysis. State the node voltages.▶
\(+3.84\,\text{V}, -1.16\,\text{V}, -11.16\,\text{V}\) (course tutorial answer). Three KCL equations, solved as a 3×3 system.
5When applying superposition, how do you disable each source type?▶
Voltage source → short circuit; current source → open circuit. Leave internal resistances in place.
6(Formula-sheet example) Find \(R_{Th}\): source shorted, 10 Ω ∥ 40 Ω in series with 20 Ω.▶
28 Ω. \(R_{Th}=\tfrac{10\times40}{10+40}+20=8+20=28\,\Omega\) (matches course formula sheet).
7For that equivalent with \(E_{Th}=32\,\text{V}\), find the load current at \(R_L=12\,\Omega\).▶
0.8 A. \(I_L=E_{Th}/(R_{Th}+R_L)=32/(28+12)=0.8\,\text{A}\).
8For that source, find \(R_L\) for maximum power and the maximum power.▶
\(R_L=28\,\Omega\); \(P_{max}=32^2/(4\times28)=9.14\,\text{W}\).
Tutorial 5 · Weeks 6-7
Capacitance, Inductance & Transients
Energy storage and the exponential rise/fall governed by time constants.
Maps to Guide 3Boylestad Ch 10-12
Problems 1-8Tap to reveal solutions
1A 220 µF capacitor is charged to 15 V. Find the stored charge and energy.▶
\(Q=3.3\,\text{mC}\), \(W=24.75\,\text{mJ}\). \(Q=CV=220\mu\times15\); \(W=\tfrac12CV^2=\tfrac12(220\mu)(225)\).
2\(R=20\,\text{k}\Omega\), \(C=5\,\mu\text{F}\). Find τ and the time to fully charge.▶
τ = 0.1 s; full at 5τ = 0.5 s. \(\tau=RC=20000\times5\times10^{-6}=0.1\,\text{s}\).
3A capacitor charges from 0 toward 40 V. Find \(v_C\) after 1τ and 2τ.▶
25.3 V then 34.6 V. \(40(1-e^{-1})=25.3\); \(40(1-e^{-2})=34.6\,\text{V}\).
4A 50 V capacitor discharges through 10 kΩ, \(C=2\,\mu\text{F}\). Find initial current and τ.▶
\(i_0=5\,\text{mA}\), τ = 20 ms. \(i_0=V_0/R=50/10000\); \(\tau=RC=10^4\times2\mu=0.02\,\text{s}\).
5Current in a 0.5 H inductor rises from 0 to 6 A in 3 ms. Find the induced voltage.▶
1000 V. \(v_L=L\,di/dt=0.5\times6/0.003=1000\,\text{V}\).
6An inductor with \(L=4\,\text{H}\), \(R=200\,\Omega\). Find the RL time constant.▶
20 ms. \(\tau=L/R=4/200=0.02\,\text{s}\).
7Two capacitors 12 µF and 4 µF in series. Find total capacitance.▶
3 µF. \(C_T=\tfrac{12\times4}{12+4}=\tfrac{48}{16}=3\,\mu\text{F}\).
8Find the energy in a 2 H inductor carrying 5 A.▶
25 J. \(W=\tfrac12LI^2=\tfrac12(2)(25)=25\,\text{J}\).
Tutorial 6 · Weeks 8-13
AC Circuits & Diodes
Sinusoids, phasors, impedance, power factor and rectification.
Maps to Guide 4Boylestad Ch 13-16 + diodes
Problems 1-8Tap to reveal solutions
1\(v=325\sin(314t)\,\text{V}\). Find RMS voltage and frequency.▶
\(V_{rms}=230\,\text{V}\), \(f=50\,\text{Hz}\). \(325/\sqrt2=230\); \(f=314/2\pi=50\,\text{Hz}\) (AU mains).
2Find \(X_L\) of 0.2 H and \(X_C\) of 20 µF at 60 Hz.▶
\(X_L=75.4\,\Omega\), \(X_C=133\,\Omega\). \(X_L=2\pi(60)(0.2)\); \(X_C=1/(2\pi\cdot60\cdot20\mu)\).
3Series \(R=8\,\Omega\), \(X_L=6\,\Omega\). Find \(|Z|\) and phase angle.▶
\(|Z|=10\,\Omega\), \(\theta=36.9^\circ\). \(\sqrt{8^2+6^2}=10\); \(\tan^{-1}(6/8)=36.9^\circ\).
4For Q3 across 100 V RMS, find the current.▶
10 A. \(I=V/|Z|=100/10=10\,\text{A}\).
5Series RLC: \(R=5\,\Omega\), \(X_L=12\,\Omega\), \(X_C=12\,\Omega\). Find Z. What is this condition?▶
\(Z=5\,\Omega\) — resonance. \(X_L=X_C\) so they cancel; \(Z=R\), current is maximum.
6A load: \(V=230\,\text{V}\), \(I=8\,\text{A}\), \(\theta=30^\circ\) lag. Find P, Q, S, PF.▶
\(S=1840\,\text{VA}\), \(P=1594\,\text{W}\), \(Q=920\,\text{VAR}\), PF=0.866 lag. \(S=VI\); \(P=S\cos30\); \(Q=S\sin30\).
7A silicon diode + 2 kΩ across 10 V. Find the current (0.7 V model).▶
4.65 mA. \(I=(10-0.7)/2000=9.3/2000=4.65\,\text{mA}\).
8A full-wave bridge on \(V_m=24\,\text{V}\). Find the average DC output (ideal).▶
≈ 15.3 V. \(V_{DC}=0.637V_m=0.637\times24=15.3\,\text{V}\).
