Mock Mid-Sem (60 marks, 90 min)

Coverage: DC fundamentals, KCL/KVL, transients, basic AC impedance.

Section A: Core Calculations

Q1
8 marks

A 24 V source supplies two series resistors: 5 Ω and 7 Ω. Find (i) circuit current, (ii) voltage drop across each resistor, (iii) power in each resistor.

Show Solution
$I=24/(5+7)=2$ A. Drops: $V_1=10$ V, $V_2=14$ V. Powers: $P_1=I^2R_1=20$ W, $P_2=28$ W.
Q2
10 marks

At a node, 6.5 A enters. Three branch currents leave: 1.2 A, 2.0 A, and $I_x$. Then this node is connected to a 12 Ω branch with voltage 18 V. Find $I_x$ and check whether KCL and Ohm's law are both consistent.

Show Solution
KCL gives $I_x=6.5-1.2-2.0=3.3$ A leaving. For 18 V across 12 Ω, branch current should be $I=1.5$ A. Not consistent with 3.3 A, so either given branch voltage or branch resistance/current belongs to different branch/data set.
Q3
12 marks

For an RC charging circuit with $R=8$ kΩ, $C=25$ µF and $V_s=20$ V, calculate (i) time constant, (ii) capacitor voltage at $t=\tau$, (iii) time to reach 95% of final value (use standard first-order approximation).

Show Solution
$\tau=RC=8000\times25\times10^{-6}=0.20$ s. At $t=\tau$, $v_C=V_s(1-e^{-1})=20(0.632)=12.64$ V. 95% reached at about $3\tau=0.60$ s.
Q4
10 marks

A series RLC circuit has $R=10$ Ω, $X_L=18$ Ω, $X_C=6$ Ω on a 120 V rms source. Find (i) impedance magnitude, (ii) current rms, (iii) phase angle and identify whether circuit is net inductive/capacitive.

Show Solution
Net reactance $X=12$ Ω. $|Z|=\sqrt{10^2+12^2}=15.62$ Ω. $I=120/15.62=7.68$ A. $\phi=\tan^{-1}(12/10)=50.2^\circ$, inductive (current lags).

Section B: Concept + Math Reasoning

Q5
10 marks

A 2 kW single-phase load at 240 V operates first at pf 0.65 then improved to pf 0.95. Compute line current in both cases and percentage reduction. Briefly explain why this matters in cables.

Show Solution
$I=P/(V\cdot pf)$. At 0.65: $I_1=2000/(240*0.65)=12.82$ A. At 0.95: $I_2=8.77$ A. Reduction $=(12.82-8.77)/12.82=31.6\%$. Lower current means reduced $I^2R$ losses and voltage drop.
Q6
10 marks

A resistor is rated 0.5 W. It is accidentally connected across 15 V. Determine minimum safe resistance and explain one practical design safeguard to prevent this failure mode.

Show Solution
$R_{min}=V^2/P=225/0.5=450$ Ω. Safeguard: series current limiting (or fuse/protection resistor) ensuring maximum fault power below resistor rating.

Mock Final (100 marks, 120 min)

Coverage: full DPEN104 including network theorems, AC power, machines, and measurement/reporting.

Section A: Network Analysis

Q1
15 marks

A source network has Thevenin equivalent $V_{th}=18$ V, $R_{th}=3$ Ω. For $R_L=6$ Ω, find load current, load voltage, load power, and source current.

Show Solution
$I=18/(3+6)=2$ A. $V_L=IR_L=12$ V. $P_L=I^2R_L=24$ W. Source current equals series current = 2 A.
Q2
15 marks

Convert this to Norton equivalent and determine $R_L$ for maximum load power. Compute that maximum power.

Show Solution
$I_N=V_{th}/R_{th}=6$ A, $R_N=3$ Ω. For max power transfer, $R_L=R_{th}=3$ Ω. $P_{max}=V_{th}^2/(4R_{th})=18^2/(12)=27$ W.

Section B: AC and Power Factor

Q3
20 marks

A 230 V rms supply feeds a load with $R=20$ Ω and net reactance $X=15$ Ω (inductive). Find $|Z|$, current, phase angle, real power, reactive power, apparent power, and power factor.

Show Solution
$|Z|=\sqrt{20^2+15^2}=25$ Ω. $I=230/25=9.2$ A. $\phi=\tan^{-1}(15/20)=36.87^\circ$. $S=VI=2116$ VA. pf $=\cos\phi=0.8$ lagging. $P=S\cdot pf=1692.8$ W. $Q=S\sin\phi=1269.6$ var.
Q4
10 marks

Power factor correction target is 0.95 for same real power as Q3. Find new supply current and percentage reduction.

Show Solution
$I_{new}=P/(V\cdot0.95)=1692.8/(230*0.95)=7.75$ A. Reduction from 9.2 A is 15.8%.

Section C: Machines + Measurement

Q5
20 marks

A motor draws 12 A at 240 V with pf 0.88 and has efficiency 82%. Compute electrical input power, mechanical output power, and total losses. Then find shaft torque at 145 rad/s.

Show Solution
$P_{in}=VIpf=2534.4$ W. $P_{out}=0.82P_{in}=2078.2$ W. Losses $=456.2$ W. Torque $\tau=P/\omega=2078.2/145=14.33$ N·m.
Q6
20 marks

An RC lab predicts $\tau_{theory}=0.150$ s, measured average is $\tau_{meas}=0.163$ s. Compute percent error. Give two technical causes and one improvement for next experiment.

Show Solution
Percent error $=|0.163-0.150|/0.150\times100=8.67\%$. Causes: component tolerance and oscilloscope/timebase reading uncertainty (or lead/contact resistance). Improvement: use precision components and repeated trials with averaged curve fitting.
