1) Advanced Optimization and Word Problems

Motivation: MATH141 exam questions often begin as words, not formulas. Your real job is model building first, differentiation second, interpretation third.

Why minima/maxima are optimization

Optimization means: among all allowed designs, pick the one that gives the best value of a target quantity.

  • Smallest material cost -> minimum
  • Largest strength or volume -> maximum

So in math language, optimization is exactly a minima/maxima problem under constraints.

Why we set derivative to zero

At an interior turning point of a smooth function, the tangent is horizontal. A horizontal tangent has slope \(0\), and slope is the derivative.

\[ \text{candidate optimum (interior)} \Rightarrow f'(x)=0 \]

That is why solving \(f'(x)=0\) gives critical points. They are candidates, not final answers.

Important: \(f'(x)=0\) does not automatically mean max or min. You must still classify using sign change, second derivative, or endpoint comparison (for closed intervals / constrained domains).
Mini Intuition Example — Why slope zero matters

Take \(f(x)=(x-3)^2+2\). This is a bowl shape with lowest point at \(x=3\).

\[ f'(x)=2(x-3),\quad f'(3)=0 \]

Left of \(3\), slope is negative (function decreasing). Right of \(3\), slope is positive (function increasing). So \(x=3\) is a minimum.

Pattern (always use this 6-step routine)

  1. Define variables clearly with units.
  2. Write the objective function to minimize/maximize.
  3. Use constraints to reduce objective to one variable.
  4. Differentiate and solve critical points.
  5. Check feasibility and classify (min/max).
  6. State final answer in context words with units.
Solved Example — Cylindrical Tank

Find dimensions minimizing surface area of a closed cylinder with fixed volume \(V=1000\text{ cm}^3\).

\[ V=\pi r^2h=1000 \Rightarrow h=\frac{1000}{\pi r^2} \]
\[ S(r)=2\pi r^2+2\pi rh=2\pi r^2+\frac{2000}{r} \]
\[ S'(r)=4\pi r-\frac{2000}{r^2}=0 \Rightarrow 4\pi r^3=2000 \Rightarrow r^3=\frac{500}{\pi} \]

Then \(h=\frac{1000}{\pi r^2}=2r\). So minimum occurs when height equals diameter.

Engineering Word Problem — Beam Section

A rectangular beam has perimeter \(40\) cm. Maximize cross-sectional area \(A=wh\).

\[ 2w+2h=40 \Rightarrow h=20-w,\quad A(w)=w(20-w)=20w-w^2 \]
\[ A'(w)=20-2w=0 \Rightarrow w=10,\ h=10,\ A_{\max}=100\text{ cm}^2 \]
Things to remember: (1) Objective and constraint are different equations. (2) Keep units all the way through. (3) Always translate result back to sentence form.

Practice Problems (Optimization)

  1. An open-top box has square base and volume \(500\text{ cm}^3\). Minimize material area.
  2. A fence of length 120 m encloses a rectangle against a straight wall (3 sides fenced). Max area?
  3. A wire of length 100 cm is cut into two pieces: one square, one circle. Choose cut to minimize total area enclosed.
  4. A pipeline route uses on-land and underwater segments with different costs per meter. Build and optimize cost model.
Answer Sketches
1) Use \(V=x^2h\), area \(A=x^2+4xh\), obtain single-variable and optimize.
2) Let width \(x\), length \(120-2x\), maximize \(A=x(120-2x)\Rightarrow x=30\).
3) Write perimeters + area formulas, reduce to one variable, differentiate and compare endpoints.
4) Use geometry + weighted cost \(C=c_1d_1+c_2d_2\), then differentiate w.r.t. landing point.
Solved Example — Open Top Box

Find dimensions minimizing material for an open-top box with square base and volume \(108\text{ m}^3\).

\[ x^2h=108\Rightarrow h=\frac{108}{x^2},\quad A(x)=x^2+4xh=x^2+\frac{432}{x} \]
\[ A'(x)=2x-\frac{432}{x^2}=0\Rightarrow 2x^3=432\Rightarrow x=6,\ h=3 \]

Minimum area design: base \(6\times 6\), height \(3\).

Solved Example — Closest Point Problem

Find point on parabola \(y=x^2\) closest to \((0,3)\).

\[ D^2=x^2+(x^2-3)^2=x^4-5x^2+9,\quad \frac{d}{dx}D^2=4x^3-10x=2x(2x^2-5) \]

Critical points \(x=0,\pm\sqrt{5/2}\). Compare \(D^2\): minimum at \(x=\pm\sqrt{5/2}\), giving points \((\pm\sqrt{5/2},5/2)\).

Extended Practice (Optimization, 8 more)

  1. A cylinder has fixed surface area \(200\pi\). Maximize volume.
  2. A sheet \(30\) by \(20\) has equal corner squares removed and folded to an open box. Maximize volume.
  3. For \(x>0\), minimize \(f(x)=x+\frac{16}{x}\).
  4. Find dimensions of rectangle of max area inscribed under \(y=12-x^2\) above x-axis.
  5. A norm \(||v||^2=x^2+y^2\) with constraint \(x+2y=8\): find minimum norm vector.
  6. Minimize cost \(C(x)=50x+\frac{1800}{x}\) for \(x>0\).
  7. A right circular cone has fixed volume \(V\). Minimize total surface area.
  8. Split 60 m fence into two adjacent rectangles sharing one side. Max total area.
Answer Sketches
1) At optimum \(h=2r\).
2) Model \(V(x)=x(30-2x)(20-2x)\), solve cubic critical point in domain.
3) \(x=4,\ f_{\min}=8\).
4) Max at \(x=2\), width \(4\), height \(8\), area \(32\).
5) \((x,y)=\left(\frac{8}{5},\frac{16}{5}\right)\).
6) \(x=6,\ C_{\min}=600\).
7) Use one-variable reduction from \(V=\frac13\pi r^2h\), differentiate area function.
8) Set common and outer lengths, reduce to one variable and optimize.

2) Integration Core (Indefinite, Definite, Applications)

Motivation: Differentiation asks “instant change.” Integration asks “total accumulation.” MATH141 tests both algebraic technique and physical interpretation.

Pattern A: Indefinite Integrals

  • Match integrand pattern (power, trig, exp, log).
  • Check if substitution is needed.
  • Include \(+C\).
Solved Example — Indefinite
\[ \int \left(3x^2+\frac{2}{x}-4e^x\right)\,dx=x^3+2\ln|x|-4e^x+C \]

Pattern B: Definite Integrals and Area

  1. Find antiderivative \(F\).
  2. Compute \(F(b)-F(a)\).
  3. For geometric area, use absolute value / top-bottom logic.
Solved Example — Area Between Curves

Find area between \(y=x\) and \(y=x^2\) on \([0,1]\).

\[ A=\int_0^1(x-x^2)\,dx=\left[\frac{x^2}{2}-\frac{x^3}{3}\right]_0^1=\frac{1}{6} \]

Pattern C: Applications

Word Problem — Velocity to Displacement

Given \(v(t)=4t-3\), find displacement from \(t=1\) to \(t=5\).

\[ s=\int_1^5(4t-3)\,dt=\left[2t^2-3t\right]_1^5=(50-15)-(2-3)=36 \]
Things to remember: (1) Definite integral gives signed area. (2) “Distance travelled” may need \(\int |v(t)|dt\). (3) \(+C\) only for indefinite integrals.

Practice Problems (Integration)

  1. Evaluate \(\int (5x^4-3\sin x+2^x)\,dx\).
  2. Evaluate \(\int_0^{\pi}\sin x\,dx\).
  3. Find area between \(y=2x\) and \(y=x^2\).
  4. If \(a(t)=6t\), \(v(0)=4\), find \(v(t)\) and displacement on \(0\le t\le2\).
Answer Sketches
1) \(x^5+3\cos x+\frac{2^x}{\ln 2}+C\).
2) \(2\).
3) Intersections \(x=0,2\), area \(\int_0^2(2x-x^2)dx=\frac{4}{3}\).
4) \(v=3t^2+4\), displacement \(\int_0^2(3t^2+4)dt=16\).
Solved Example — Exponential Application

If current \(I(t)=3e^{-0.2t}\), charge from \(t=0\) to \(t=10\):

\[ Q=\int_0^{10}3e^{-0.2t}\,dt=3\left[\frac{e^{-0.2t}}{-0.2}\right]_0^{10}=15(1-e^{-2}) \]
Solved Example — Integration by Parts

Evaluate \(\int x\ln x\,dx\).

\[ u=\ln x,\ dv=x\,dx\Rightarrow du=\frac1x dx,\ v=\frac{x^2}{2} \]
\[ \int x\ln x\,dx=\frac{x^2}{2}\ln x-\frac12\int x\,dx =\frac{x^2}{2}\ln x-\frac{x^2}{4}+C \]

Extended Practice (Integration, 8 more)

  1. \(\int \frac{2x+1}{x^2+x+1}\,dx\)
  2. \(\int_0^2 (x^3-2x)\,dx\)
  3. Area between \(y=x^2+1\) and \(y=2x+3\).
  4. \(\int x e^x\,dx\)
  5. \(\int \frac{1}{x^2-1}\,dx\) (partial fractions)
  6. Given \(v(t)=t^2-4t+3\), find distance on \(0\le t\le5\).
  7. \(\int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x^p}\,dx\): state convergence condition.
  8. If \(a(t)=4t+1,\ v(0)=2,\ s(0)=0\), find \(s(t)\).
Answer Sketches
1) \(\ln(x^2+x+1)+C\).
2) \(0\).
3) Intersections \(x=1\pm\sqrt3\), integrate top-bottom.
4) \(e^x(x-1)+C\).
5) \(\frac12\ln\left|\frac{x-1}{x+1}\right|+C\).
6) Split where \(v=0\), use \(\int |v|dt\).
7) Converges iff \(p>1\).
8) \(v=2t^2+t+2,\ s=\frac{2}{3}t^3+\frac12t^2+2t\).

3) Complex Numbers (Cartesian, Polar, Exponential)

Motivation: Complex numbers are a full MATH141 strand and critical in engineering signals/phasors. No limits prerequisite needed.

Pattern (core workflow)

  1. Start with \(z=a+bi\).
  2. Compute modulus \(r=\sqrt{a^2+b^2}\).
  3. Find argument \(\theta\) with correct quadrant.
  4. Write \(z=r(\cos\theta+i\sin\theta)=re^{i\theta}\).
Solved Example — Convert to Polar/Exponential

Convert \(z=-1+i\sqrt{3}\).

\[ r=\sqrt{1+3}=2,\quad \theta=\frac{2\pi}{3} \]
\[ z=2\left(\cos\frac{2\pi}{3}+i\sin\frac{2\pi}{3}\right)=2e^{i2\pi/3} \]
Word Problem — De Moivre Root Context

Find all cube roots of \(8e^{i\pi}\) (engineering phase split).

\[ z_k=8^{1/3}e^{i(\pi+2k\pi)/3}=2e^{i(\pi+2k\pi)/3},\ k=0,1,2 \]
Things to remember: (1) Argument must respect quadrant. (2) Multiple arguments differ by \(2\pi n\). (3) Root formulas require all \(k\) values.

Practice Problems (Complex)

  1. Convert \(3-3i\) to polar and exponential forms.
  2. Compute \((1+i)^8\) using polar form.
  3. Find all 4th roots of \(16\).
  4. Solve \(z^3=-27i\) in exponential form.
Answer Sketches
1) \(r=3\sqrt2,\ \theta=-\pi/4\) (or \(7\pi/4\)).
2) \(16\).
3) \(2e^{ik\pi/2},\ k=0,1,2,3\).
4) \(-27i=27e^{-i\pi/2}\), roots \(3e^{i(-\pi/6+2k\pi/3)}\).
Solved Example — Multiply in Polar Form

\((2e^{i\pi/3})(5e^{-i\pi/6})=10e^{i(\pi/3-\pi/6)}=10e^{i\pi/6}\).

Solved Example — Division in Polar Form

\(\frac{6e^{i5\pi/6}}{3e^{i\pi/3}}=2e^{i(5\pi/6-\pi/3)}=2e^{i\pi/2}=2i\).

Extended Practice (Complex, 8 more)

  1. Convert \(-4+4i\) to \(re^{i\theta}\).
  2. Find \((\sqrt3+i)^6\).
  3. Find all cube roots of \(8(\cos\pi+i\sin\pi)\).
  4. Solve \(z^4=16i\).
  5. Find principal argument of \(-2-2\sqrt3\,i\).
  6. Show \( |z_1z_2|=|z_1||z_2| \) using polar form.
  7. Compute \( (1-i)^{10} \).
  8. Express \( \frac{1+i}{1-i} \) in Cartesian and exponential form.
Answer Sketches
1) \(4\sqrt2e^{i3\pi/4}\).
2) \(64\).
3) \(2e^{i(\pi/3+2k\pi/3)}\).
4) \(2e^{i(\pi/8+k\pi/2)},\ k=0,1,2,3\).
5) \(-2\pi/3\) (or \(4\pi/3\)).
6) Multiply moduli directly in polar.
7) Use polar \(1-i=\sqrt2e^{-i\pi/4}\).
8) \(i\), and exponential \(e^{i\pi/2}\).

4) Linear Algebra Strand (Matrices + Determinants + Vectors)

Motivation: MATH141 calculus and linear algebra run together. This strand can be studied now with no limits dependency.

Pattern A: Matrix solving workflow

  • Write augmented matrix.
  • Row-reduce systematically.
  • Classify solution type (unique/infinite/none).
Solved Example — 2x2 Inverse
\[ A=\begin{bmatrix}2&1\\3&4\end{bmatrix},\ \det A=8-3=5,\ A^{-1}=\frac{1}{5}\begin{bmatrix}4&-1\\-3&2\end{bmatrix} \]

Pattern B: Dot/Cross product in 3D

Solved Example — Dot and Cross

\(\mathbf{a}=(1,2,3),\ \mathbf{b}=(2,-1,4)\)

\[ \mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b}=1\cdot2+2(-1)+3\cdot4=12 \]
\[ \mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b}= \begin{vmatrix}\mathbf{i}&\mathbf{j}&\mathbf{k}\\1&2&3\\2&-1&4\end{vmatrix} =(11,2,-5) \]
Word Problem — Plane from 3 points

Given \(P,Q,R\), build vectors \(\overrightarrow{PQ},\overrightarrow{PR}\), take cross product for normal \(\mathbf{n}\), then use \(\mathbf{n}\cdot(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{P})=0\).

Things to remember: (1) \(\det=0\) means no inverse. (2) Dot product tests alignment/orthogonality. (3) Cross product gives normal direction and area magnitude.

Practice Problems (Linear Algebra + Vectors)

  1. Solve \(2x+y=5,\ 3x-2y=4\) using matrix inverse.
  2. Row reduce a 3x3 system and classify solution type.
  3. Find eigenvalues of \(\begin{bmatrix}4&1\\2&3\end{bmatrix}\).
  4. Given \(A(1,0,2)\), \(B(3,1,0)\), find direction vector of line \(AB\).
  5. Find equation of plane through \(P(1,1,1)\) with normal \((2,-1,3)\).
Answer Sketches
1) \(x=2,\ y=1\).
2) Depends on reduced form: unique/infinite/none from pivots/consistency.
3) \(\lambda=5,2\).
4) \((2,1,-2)\).
5) \(2(x-1)-1(y-1)+3(z-1)=0\Rightarrow 2x-y+3z-4=0\).
Solved Example — 3x3 Gaussian Elimination

Solve \(\begin{cases}x+y+z=6\\2x-y+3z=14\\x+2y-z=2\end{cases}\).

\[ \text{RREF}\Rightarrow (x,y,z)=(4,1,1) \]
Solved Example — Distance from Point to Plane

Distance from \((1,2,3)\) to plane \(2x-y+2z-5=0\):

\[ d=\frac{|2(1)-2+2(3)-5|}{\sqrt{2^2+(-1)^2+2^2}}=\frac{1}{3} \]

Extended Practice (Linear Algebra + Vectors, 8 more)

  1. Find inverse of \(\begin{bmatrix}1&2\\5&3\end{bmatrix}\).
  2. Solve a 3x3 system by elimination and state rank.
  3. Find determinant of \(\begin{bmatrix}2&1&0\\3&-1&4\\1&2&5\end{bmatrix}\).
  4. Find eigenvectors for \(\lambda=2\) of \(\begin{bmatrix}4&1\\2&3\end{bmatrix}\).
  5. Compute angle between \((1,2,2)\) and \((2,1,-2)\).
  6. Find equation of line through \((1,0,2)\) parallel to \((3,-1,4)\).
  7. Find equation of plane through \(A(1,0,0),B(0,1,0),C(0,0,1)\).
  8. Classify two lines as intersecting/parallel/skew from parametric forms.
Answer Sketches
1) \(\frac{-1}{7}\begin{bmatrix}3&-2\\-5&1\end{bmatrix}\).
2) Use echelon form; rank from pivot count.
3) \(-39\).
4) Solve \((A-2I)v=0\Rightarrow v=(1,-2)\) up to scale.
5) \(\cos\theta=\frac{(1)(2)+(2)(1)+(2)(-2)}{\sqrt9\sqrt9}=0\Rightarrow 90^\circ\).
6) \(r=(1,0,2)+t(3,-1,4)\).
7) \(x+y+z=1\).
8) Compare direction vectors + solve parameter equations.
