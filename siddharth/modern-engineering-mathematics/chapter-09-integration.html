Chapter 9

Integration

Integration is the reverse of differentiation and the mathematics of accumulation. This chapter builds method choice confidence with clear, exam-friendly examples.

9.1 Antiderivatives and Indefinite Integrals

Find a function whose derivative gives the integrand.
$$\int x^n\,dx=\frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1}+C\ (n\neq -1),\qquad \int \frac{1}{x}\,dx=\ln|x|+C$$
Worked Example

\(\int(6x^2-4x+7)\,dx=2x^3-2x^2+7x+C\).

9.2 Definite Integrals and Area

A definite integral gives net accumulation between bounds.
$$\int_a^b f(x)\,dx = F(b)-F(a)\quad\text{where }F'(x)=f(x)$$

Engineering interpretation

  • Integrate velocity to get displacement.
  • Integrate load intensity to get resultant load.
  • Integrate power over time to get energy.
Worked Example

\(\int_0^3 (2x+1)\,dx=[x^2+x]_0^3=(9+3)-0=12\).

9.3 Method Choice: Substitution and Parts

Use substitution for composition and parts for products.
$$\int f(g(x))g'(x)\,dx=\int f(u)\,du,\qquad \int u\,dv=uv-\int v\,du$$
Substitution Example

\(\int 2x\cos(x^2)\,dx\). Let \(u=x^2\), \(du=2x\,dx\):

$$\int 2x\cos(x^2)\,dx=\int \cos u\,du=\sin u + C = \sin(x^2)+C$$
Integration by Parts Example

\(\int x e^x\,dx\), choose \(u=x,\ dv=e^x\,dx\):

$$\int x e^x\,dx=xe^x-\int e^x\,dx=xe^x-e^x+C=e^x(x-1)+C$$

Chapter 9 Practice

Try first, then open solutions.

Set (8) — Integration

  1. \(\int (4x^3-5)\,dx\).
    Show solution
    \(x^4-5x+C\).
  2. \(\int_1^3 2x\,dx\).
    Show solution
    \([x^2]_1^3=8\).
  3. \(\int \frac{1}{x}\,dx\).
    Show solution
    \(\ln|x|+C\).
  4. \(\int \cos(5x)\,dx\).
    Show solution
    \(\frac{1}{5}\sin(5x)+C\).
  5. \(\int x(x^2+1)^3\,dx\).
    Show solution
    Use \(u=x^2+1\): \(\frac{(x^2+1)^4}{8}+C\).
  6. \(\int x\ln x\,dx\).
    Show solution
    By parts: \(\frac{x^2}{2}\ln x-\frac{x^2}{4}+C\).
  7. If \(v(t)=3t^2\), find displacement from \(t=0\) to \(t=2\).
    Show solution
    \(\int_0^2 3t^2 dt=[t^3]_0^2=8\).
  8. \(\int_0^\pi \sin x\,dx\).
    Show solution
    \([-\cos x]_0^\pi=2\).
