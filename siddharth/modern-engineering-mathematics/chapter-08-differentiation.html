Chapter 8

Differentiation

This chapter explains derivatives slowly and visually: what they mean, how to compute them, and where they appear in engineering models (velocity, acceleration, optimization, and sensitivity).

8.1 Meaning of a Derivative

Derivative = instantaneous rate of change = slope of tangent.
$$f'(x)=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}$$

Why this matters

In mechanics and circuits, you need the rate at one instant, not just average change over a large interval. Derivatives answer that local-rate question.

Worked Example

If \(s(t)=3t^2+2t\), then velocity is \(v(t)=\frac{ds}{dt}=6t+2\), and acceleration is \(a(t)=\frac{dv}{dt}=6\).

8.2 Rules and Chain Rule

Use derivative rules to avoid redoing first-principles every time.
$$\frac{d}{dx}(x^n)=nx^{n-1},\quad (uv)'=u'v+uv',\quad \left(\frac{u}{v}\right)'=\frac{u'v-uv'}{v^2},\quad \frac{d}{dx}f(g(x))=f'(g(x))g'(x)$$

Chain rule mental model

Differentiate outer function first, keep inner unchanged, then multiply by derivative of inner.

Worked Example

\(y=(2x^3-5)^4\). Let outer \(u^4\), inner \(u=2x^3-5\).

$$\frac{dy}{dx}=4(2x^3-5)^3\cdot(6x^2)=24x^2(2x^3-5)^3$$

8.3 Implicit and Parametric Differentiation

Differentiate equations not explicitly solved for \(y\).

Implicit method

Differentiate both sides with respect to \(x\), and whenever you differentiate \(y\), include \(\frac{dy}{dx}\).

Worked Example

For \(x^2+y^2=25\):

$$2x+2y\frac{dy}{dx}=0\quad\Rightarrow\quad \frac{dy}{dx}=-\frac{x}{y}$$

Parametric method

When \(x=x(t)\), \(y=y(t)\), compute \(\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{dy/dt}{dx/dt}\).

Worked Example

\(x=t^2+1,\ y=t^3-2t\). Then \(\frac{dx}{dt}=2t,\ \frac{dy}{dt}=3t^2-2\), so:

$$\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{3t^2-2}{2t}$$

Chapter 8 Practice

Try first, then open solutions.

Set (8) — Differentiation

  1. Differentiate \(y=5x^4-3x+1\).
    Show solution
    \(y'=20x^3-3\).
  2. Differentiate \(y=\sin(3x)\).
    Show solution
    \(y'=3\cos(3x)\).
  3. Differentiate \(y=\frac{x^2+1}{x}\).
    Show solution
    \(y'=1-\frac{1}{x^2}\).
  4. Differentiate \(y=(x^2+4)^5\).
    Show solution
    \(y'=10x(x^2+4)^4\).
  5. Find slope at \(x=2\) for \(y=x^3-2x\).
    Show solution
    \(y'=3x^2-2\Rightarrow y'(2)=10\).
  6. Implicitly differentiate \(xy=7\).
    Show solution
    \(x\frac{dy}{dx}+y=0\Rightarrow \frac{dy}{dx}=-\frac{y}{x}\).
  7. Given \(x=t^2,\ y=t^4\), find \(dy/dx\).
    Show solution
    \(\frac{dy/dt}{dx/dt}=\frac{4t^3}{2t}=2t^2\).
  8. If \(s(t)=4t^3\), find velocity at \(t=2\).
    Show solution
    \(v=ds/dt=12t^2\Rightarrow v(2)=48\).
