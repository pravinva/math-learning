Chapter 10

Polar Coordinates

Polar form gives position using distance and direction. It is ideal for circular motion, rotating systems, and many curves that look complicated in Cartesian form.

10.1 Polar Description of a Point

A point is given by \((r,\theta)\): radius and angle.
$$x=r\cos\theta,\qquad y=r\sin\theta,\qquad r^2=x^2+y^2$$

Why useful

When geometry is radial or rotational, polar form can simplify equations and integrals significantly.

Worked Example

Convert \((r,\theta)=(6,30^\circ)\): \(x=6\cos30^\circ=3\sqrt3,\ y=6\sin30^\circ=3\).

10.2 Conversion Between Cartesian and Polar

Convert both ways and track quadrant correctly.
$$r=\sqrt{x^2+y^2},\qquad \theta=\tan^{-1}\!\left(\frac{y}{x}\right)\ \text{(with quadrant check)}$$
Worked Example

For \((x,y)=(-3,3)\): \(r=\sqrt{18}=3\sqrt2\). Reference angle is \(45^\circ\), but point is in quadrant II, so \(\theta=135^\circ\).

Equivalent polar forms

\((r,\theta)\equiv(r,\theta+360^\circ k)\equiv(-r,\theta+180^\circ)\). This explains why one Cartesian point can have many polar pairs.

10.3 Polar Curves and Area

Sketch common polar curves and compute enclosed area.
$$A=\frac12\int_{\alpha}^{\beta} r^2\,d\theta$$
Area Example

Find area inside \(r=2\) from \(\theta=0\) to \(\pi\):

$$A=\frac12\int_0^\pi 4\,d\theta=2\pi$$
Curve Example

\(r=1+\cos\theta\) is a cardioid-type curve. Symmetry and key-angle points (\(0,\pi/2,\pi\)) help sketch quickly.

Chapter 10 Practice

Try first, then open solutions.

Set (8) — Polar Coordinates

  1. Convert \((r,\theta)=(5,60^\circ)\) to Cartesian.
    Show solution
    \((x,y)=\left(\frac{5}{2},\frac{5\sqrt3}{2}\right)\).
  2. Convert \((x,y)=(4,4)\) to polar (principal angle).
    Show solution
    \(r=4\sqrt2,\ \theta=45^\circ\).
  3. Convert \((x,y)=(-2,-2)\) to polar.
    Show solution
    \(r=2\sqrt2,\ \theta=225^\circ\).
  4. Write an equivalent polar pair for \((3,40^\circ)\) with negative radius.
    Show solution
    \((-3,220^\circ)\).
  5. Find area for \(r=3\), \(0\le\theta\le\pi/2\).
    Show solution
    \(A=\frac12\int_0^{\pi/2}9\,d\theta=\frac{9\pi}{4}\).
  6. For \(r=2\cos\theta\), what Cartesian curve is it?
    Show solution
    Circle \(x^2+y^2=2x\) or \((x-1)^2+y^2=1\).
  7. If \(x= r\cos\theta,\ y=r\sin\theta\), show \(r^2=x^2+y^2\).
    Show solution
    Square and add: \(x^2+y^2=r^2(\cos^2\theta+\sin^2\theta)=r^2\).
  8. Find area inside \(r=1+\cos\theta\), \(0\le\theta\le\pi\).
    Show solution
    \(A=\frac12\int_0^\pi (1+\cos\theta)^2 d\theta=\frac{3\pi}{4}\).
