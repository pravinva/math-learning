A. First Principles (Mental Model)

  • DPEN100 Dynamics: asks "if forces act, how does motion change?" -> acceleration is generally not zero.
  • DPEN102 Statics: asks "how can forces balance?" -> acceleration is zero and body is in equilibrium.
\[ \text{Dynamics: } \sum \vec F = m\vec a \qquad\qquad \text{Statics: } \sum \vec F = \vec 0,\ \sum \vec M = \vec 0 \]

B. Why "Moment" and "Moment of Inertia"?

1) Moment of a force

"Moment" means turning influence about a point/axis. A force can translate a body and also try to rotate it. The rotational influence is the moment.

\[ M_O = F d_{\perp} \quad\text{(2D scalar)} \qquad\text{or}\qquad \vec M_O = \vec r \times \vec F \]

If the line of action passes through the point, \(d_{\perp}=0\), so moment is zero.

2) Moment of inertia

Also called "second moment" because it uses distance squared. It measures how far area (or mass) is spread from an axis.

  • Area MOI (statics/beam bending): \(I=\int r^2 dA\)
  • Mass MOI (dynamics/rotation): \(I=\int r^2 dm\)
Intuition: Far-away area/mass counts a lot more because of \(r^2\). That is why deep beams are stiffer in bending than shallow ones.

C. Core Statics Terms (Intuitive Glossary)

D. DPEN102 Formula Block (Statics + Mechanics)

1) Equilibrium + moments

\[ \sum F_x=0,\quad \sum F_y=0,\quad \sum M_O=0 \]
\[ M_O = F d_{\perp},\qquad \vec M_O=\vec r\times\vec F,\qquad M_{\text{couple}}=Fd \]

2) Distributed loads and resultants

\[ R=\int w(x)\,dx,\qquad x_R = \frac{1}{R}\int x\,w(x)\,dx \]

3) Friction

\[ F_f\le \mu_s N\ \text{(static)},\qquad F_f=\mu_k N\ \text{(kinetic)} \]

4) Stress/strain and material response

\[ \sigma=\frac{P}{A},\quad \varepsilon=\frac{\Delta L}{L},\quad \sigma=E\varepsilon \]
\[ \tau=\frac{V}{A}\ \text{(average/simple)},\qquad \gamma\approx\phi\ (\text{radians}) \]

5) Centroid and area moment of inertia

\[ \bar x=\frac{1}{A}\int x\,dA,\quad \bar y=\frac{1}{A}\int y\,dA \]
\[ I_x=\int y^2\,dA,\quad I_y=\int x^2\,dA,\quad J_O=I_x+I_y \]
\[ I = I_c + Ad^2 \quad\text{(parallel-axis theorem)} \]

6) Beams (shear and bending)

\[ \frac{dV}{dx}=-w(x),\qquad \frac{dM}{dx}=V(x) \]

E. DPEN100 Formula Block (Dynamics Bridge)

1) Motion definitions

\[ v=\frac{ds}{dt},\qquad a=\frac{dv}{dt}=\frac{d^2s}{dt^2},\qquad a\,ds=v\,dv \]

2) Constant acceleration (SUVAT)

\[ v=u+at,\quad s=ut+\frac{1}{2}at^2,\quad v^2=u^2+2as,\quad s=\frac{u+v}{2}t \]

3) Particle dynamics and path components

\[ \sum \vec F = m\vec a,\qquad a_t=\frac{dv}{dt},\qquad a_n=\frac{v^2}{\rho} \]

4) Projectile essentials (no drag)

\[ x=u\cos\theta\ t,\quad y=u\sin\theta\ t-\frac{1}{2}gt^2,\quad R=\frac{u^2\sin 2\theta}{g} \]

F. FBD and Truss Checklist (Weak-Student Safe Mode)

  1. Draw one body only; cut away everything else.
  2. Replace supports with unknown reactions correctly.
  3. Add dimensions, angles, and sign convention before equations.
  4. Take moments about a smart point to eliminate unknowns.
  5. For trusses, solve joint-by-joint and keep tension/compression signs consistent.
If you feel stuck: Do not start algebra. Rebuild the FBD first. Most statics errors are diagram errors, not math errors.
