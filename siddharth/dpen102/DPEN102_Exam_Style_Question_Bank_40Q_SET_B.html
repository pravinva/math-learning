A. Vectors + FBD (Q1-Q5)

Q18 marks

Resolve 12 kN at \(40^\circ\) and 9 kN at \(210^\circ\). Find resultant.

Answer
  1. \(R_x=12\cos40+9\cos210=9.193-7.794=1.399\).
  2. \(R_y=12\sin40+9\sin210=7.713-4.5=3.213\).
  3. \(|R|=\sqrt{1.399^2+3.213^2}=3.50\text{ kN}\), \(\theta=66.5^\circ\).
Q28 marks

Block weight 700 N on horizontal surface. Pull \(P\) at \(30^\circ\), \(\mu_s=0.28\). Find \(P\) at impending motion.

Answer
  1. \(N=700-P\sin30=700-0.5P\).
  2. \(P\cos30=0.28N=0.28(700-0.5P)\).
  3. \(0.866P=196-0.14P\Rightarrow 1.006P=196\Rightarrow P=194.8\text{ N}\).
Q38 marks

State full FBD checklist before writing equilibrium equations.

Answer
  1. Isolate single body.
  2. Replace supports with correct reactions.
  3. Include all applied loads/couples/self-weight.
  4. Choose axes and sign convention.
  5. Mark dimensions/perpendicular arms.
Q48 marks

Two-force member in frame: describe force direction and why.

Answer

Forces at both ends are equal/opposite/collinear with member axis. Otherwise a net moment would exist, violating equilibrium.

Q58 marks

Convert vector \(\vec F=10\hat i-24\hat j\) to magnitude-angle form.

Answer
  1. \(|F|=\sqrt{10^2+24^2}=26\text{ N}\).
  2. \(\theta=\tan^{-1}(24/10)=67.4^\circ\), Quadrant IV because \(y<0\): \(\theta=-67.4^\circ\) from +x.

Q610 marks

Beam span 9 m. Point loads: 10 kN at 2 m, 14 kN at 7 m from A. Find reactions.

Answer
  1. \(\Sigma M_A:9B_y-10(2)-14(7)=0\Rightarrow B_y=13.11\text{ kN}\).
  2. \(\Sigma F_y:A_y+B_y-24=0\Rightarrow A_y=10.89\text{ kN}\).

Q810 marks

Simply supported beam 8 m with UDL 4 kN/m over full length and 6 kN at midspan. Find reactions.

Q98 marks

Why is \(V=dM/dx\) useful during exams?

Answer

It links diagram shapes directly: zero shear gives moment extrema; area under shear gives moment change; lets you check consistency quickly.

Q1010 marks

Cantilever 3 m with triangular load from zero at free end to 6 kN/m at fixed end. Find fixed shear and moment.

Answer
  1. Resultant \(=\frac12(3)(6)=9\text{ kN}\).
  2. Acts at \(1/3\) of base from high-intensity end \(\Rightarrow 1\text{ m}\) from fixed end.
  3. Fixed shear \(=9\text{ kN}\), fixed moment \(=9(1)=9\text{ kN·m}\).

C. Trusses (Q11-Q15)

Q1110 marks

Symmetric truss with 24 kN at center top node. Find support reactions.

Answer

By symmetry, vertical reactions split equally: 12 kN each. No net horizontal load \(\Rightarrow A_x=0\).

Q1210 marks

Method of joints: node has known reaction 12 kN up and diagonal at \(45^\circ\). Find diagonal if no other vertical member at node.

Answer

\(\Sigma F_y:12+F_d\sin45=0\Rightarrow F_d=-16.97\text{ kN}\) compression.

Q138 marks

Explain one advantage of method of sections over method of joints.

Answer

Sections can find selected internal member forces directly without solving every joint sequentially, saving time for distant members.

Q148 marks

If section cut crosses more than 3 unknown member forces in 2D, what issue occurs?

Answer

Only three independent equilibrium equations exist in 2D; more than three unknowns makes one cut section statically indeterminate.

Q1510 marks

At unloaded joint with three members where two are collinear, determine likely zero-force member type.

Answer

The non-collinear member is zero-force (provided no external load or support reaction at that joint).

D. SFD/BMD + Interpretation (Q16-Q20)

Q168 marks

UDL segment with \(w=5\text{ kN/m}\) over 2 m causes what shear change?

Answer

\(\Delta V=-wL=-10\text{ kN}\) (downward change in sign convention where downward load is negative).

Q1710 marks

Given \(V=20-4x\), \(M(0)=3\), find \(M(5)\).

Answer
  1. \(M=\int(20-4x)dx=20x-2x^2+C\).
  2. \(C=3\). So \(M(5)=100-50+3=53\text{ kN·m}\).
Q188 marks

How does a clockwise applied couple appear on BMD?

Answer

As an instantaneous jump (step) in bending moment equal to applied couple magnitude with sign by convention.

Q198 marks

Can bending moment be nonzero when shear is zero?

Answer

Yes. Shear zero indicates local max/min (or flat) of moment, not necessarily moment zero.

Q2010 marks

Name two reasons students get wrong sign on BMD.

Answer
  1. Inconsistent sagging/hogging sign convention.
  2. Incorrect shear sign tracking across load jumps.

E. Friction + Impending Motion (Q21-Q25)

Q2110 marks

Block 300 N on rough level plane, \(\mu_s=0.5\). Find max static friction and required push just to move.

Answer

\(N=300\text{ N}\), \(F_{s,max}=\mu_sN=150\text{ N}\). Push needed at threshold = 150 N.

Q2210 marks

Same block with upward pull 80 N at \(60^\circ\). Find new \(N\) and \(F_{s,max}\).

Answer
  1. Vertical pull component \(=80\sin60=69.3\text{ N}\).
  2. \(N=300-69.3=230.7\text{ N}\).
  3. \(F_{s,max}=0.5(230.7)=115.4\text{ N}\).
Q238 marks

Why can pulling upward reduce required motion force?

Answer

It reduces normal reaction \(N\), lowering friction limit \(\mu N\).

Q248 marks

Define coefficient of kinetic friction.

Answer

\(\mu_k=F_k/N\), ratio of sliding friction to normal force once relative motion occurs.

Q2510 marks

2 kg block on 20° incline, \(\mu_k=0.15\), sliding down. Find acceleration.

Answer
  1. Down-slope: \(mg\sin20=6.71\text{ N}\).
  2. \(N=mg\cos20=18.43\text{ N}\), friction up-slope \(=2.76\text{ N}\).
  3. Net \(=3.95\text{ N}\), \(a=3.95/2=1.98\text{ m/s}^2\) down slope.

F. Centroids + Inertia (Q26-Q30)

Q2610 marks

Composite area: two rectangles 100x20 stacked over 20x80 web centered. Find \(\bar y\) from bottom.

Answer
  1. \(A_f=2000,\ y_f=90\). \(A_w=1600,\ y_w=40\).
  2. \(\bar y=(2000\cdot90+1600\cdot40)/3600=67.78\text{ mm}\).
Q278 marks

State \(I_x\) for rectangle and circle about centroidal axes.

Answer

Rectangle: \(I_x=bh^3/12\). Circle: \(I=\pi d^4/64\) about centroidal diametral axis.

Q2810 marks

Rectangle 120x60 mm about base axis \(x_b\). Find \(I_{x_b}\).

Answer
  1. \(I_c=bh^3/12=120(60)^3/12=2.16\times10^6\text{ mm}^4\).
  2. \(A=7200\), \(d=30\).
  3. \(I_{x_b}=I_c+Ad^2=2.16\times10^6+6.48\times10^6=8.64\times10^6\text{ mm}^4\).
Q296 marks

Why does \(I\) have units of length\(^4\)?

Answer

Integral definition \(I=\int y^2\,dA\): area contributes length\(^2\), multiplied by squared distance length\(^2\), giving length\(^4\).

Q308 marks

Briefly explain neutral axis in bending.

Answer

Line in cross-section where longitudinal bending stress is zero; separates tension and compression zones.

G. Stress/Strain + Material Response (Q31-Q35)

Q3110 marks

Aluminum bar area 500 mm\(^2\), load 45 kN, \(E=70\) GPa, length 2.0 m. Find stress, strain, extension.

Answer
  1. \(\sigma=45{,}000/(500\times10^{-6})=90\text{ MPa}\).
  2. \(\epsilon=\sigma/E=90/70{,}000=0.001286\).
  3. \(\delta=\epsilon L=0.001286(2.0)=0.00257\text{ m}=2.57\text{ mm}\).
Q328 marks

Define engineering strain and true strain (concept only).

Answer

Engineering strain uses original length: \(\Delta L/L_0\). True strain accumulates incremental strain on instantaneous length: \(\int dL/L\).

Q338 marks

What does factor of safety 2.5 imply?

Answer

Working stress is limited to about \(1/2.5\) of reference strength (yield or ultimate, per design basis).

Q3410 marks

Rod diameter 20 mm experiences 0.4 mm extension over 800 mm. If \(E=200\) GPa, find load.

Answer
  1. \(\epsilon=0.4/800=0.0005\).
  2. \(\sigma=E\epsilon=200{,}000\times0.0005=100\text{ MPa}\).
  3. \(A=\pi(20)^2/4=314.16\text{ mm}^2\).
  4. \(P=\sigma A=100\times314.16=31.4\text{ kN}\).
Q358 marks

Why is brittle failure riskier in sudden-impact conditions?

Answer

Brittle materials absorb less plastic deformation energy before fracture; they can fail abruptly with little warning under impact/stress concentrations.

H. Work-Energy + Dynamics Integration (Q36-Q40)

Q3610 marks

Mass 8 kg moving at 6 m/s is brought to stop by constant braking force over 9 m. Find force magnitude.

Answer
  1. \(\Delta KE=0-\frac12mv^2=-144\text{ J}\).
  2. \(W=Fs\cos180=-F(9)=-144\Rightarrow F=16\text{ N}\).
Q3710 marks

2.5 kg block pulled 4 m by 30 N on rough horizontal surface (\(\mu_k=0.2\)). Find final speed from rest.

Answer
  1. Friction \(=0.2(2.5)(9.81)=4.905\text{ N}\).
  2. Net work \(=(30-4.905)4=100.38\text{ J}\).
  3. \(\frac12mv^2=100.38\Rightarrow v=\sqrt{2(100.38)/2.5}=8.96\text{ m/s}\).
Q388 marks

State work-energy theorem in one sentence.

Answer

Net work done by all forces on a particle/system equals change in kinetic energy.

Q3910 marks

Spring \(k=400\text{ N/m}\) compressed 0.15 m launches 1 kg block on frictionless surface. Find launch speed.

Answer
  1. Stored spring energy \(=\frac12kx^2=\frac12(400)(0.15)^2=4.5\text{ J}\).
  2. \(\frac12mv^2=4.5\Rightarrow v=3.0\text{ m/s}\).
Q4012 marks

Cart 20 kg starts at 2 m/s and descends 1.5 m vertically; rolling resistance does 120 J negative work. Find final speed.

Answer
  1. Initial \(KE_i=\frac12(20)(2^2)=40\text{ J}\).
  2. Potential drop adds \(mgh=(20)(9.81)(1.5)=294.3\text{ J}\).
  3. Resistive work \(-120\text{ J}\).
  4. \(KE_f=40+294.3-120=214.3\text{ J}\).
  5. \(\frac12(20)v^2=214.3\Rightarrow v=\sqrt{21.43}=4.63\text{ m/s}\).
