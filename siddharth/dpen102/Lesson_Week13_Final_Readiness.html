0 Readiness checklist

Before sitting the final assessment, be honestly able to tick every box below. Any unchecked box is exactly where to spend remaining study time.

  • Vectors, FBD, reactions: can resolve any angled force into components and draw a correct, complete FBD without hesitation.
  • Stress & internal actions: can find \(N,V,M\) at a cut, and check a section against an allowable stress.
  • Trusses + SFD/BMD: can find global reactions, identify zero-force members, run a method-of-sections cut, and build a full shear/moment diagram.
  • Friction, centroid, energy: can identify which friction state applies, find a composite centroid and second moment of area, and apply energy conservation for spring/gravity problems.
  • Timed communication quality: can present a full 6-step solution (Define→Data/Diagram→Theory→Estimate→Solve→Verify) under time pressure, not just get the right number.
1 Formula reference sheet

One compact reference, organised by week. Try rewriting this from memory before checking it, that act of recall is worth more than re-reading it passively.

Weeks 1–3 · Foundations
\(\Sigma F=ma\) · \(W=mg\) · equilibrium: \(\Sigma F_x=0,\ \Sigma F_y=0\)
\(F_x=F\cos\theta,\ F_y=F\sin\theta\) · \(F=\sqrt{F_x^2+F_y^2}\) · \(M=F\times d,\ \Sigma M_O=0\)
Week 4 · Stress, Strain, Internal Actions
\(\sigma=F/A\) · \(\varepsilon=\Delta L/L_0\) · \(\sigma_{allow}=\sigma_y/FoS\) · \(A_{req}=F/\sigma_{allow}\)
At a cut: \(\Sigma F_x=0\to N\), \(\Sigma F_y=0\to V\), \(\Sigma M_{cut}=0\to M\)
Week 5 · SFD / BMD
\(dV/dx=w(x)\) · \(dM/dx=V(x)\) · \(M(x)=\int V\,dx\) · \(M_{max}\) where \(V=0\)
Full-span UDL, simply supported: \(M_{max}=wL^2/8\)
Week 6 · Trusses
Zero-force rules: 2 non-collinear members, unloaded joint → both zero; 3 members, 2 collinear, unloaded joint → third is zero.
Method of sections: cut ≤3 unknowns, take moments about the intersection of two unknowns to isolate the third.
Week 7 · Friction
\(F_s\le\mu_sN\) · \(F_k=\mu_kN\) · angle of repose: \(\tan\theta=\mu_s\)
Wedge (1 friction surface): \(P=W\tan(\theta+\varphi),\ \varphi=\arctan\mu\) · Belt: \(T_2/T_1=e^{\mu\beta}\) (\(\beta\) in radians)
Week 8 · Centroids & Second Moment of Area
\(\bar x=\dfrac{\Sigma A_ix_i}{\Sigma A_i},\ \bar y=\dfrac{\Sigma A_iy_i}{\Sigma A_i}\) (holes as negative area)
Rectangle: \(I=bh^3/12\) · Parallel axis: \(I_{other}=I_{centroidal}+Ad^2\)
Week 9 · Work & Energy
\(W_{net}=\Delta K,\ K=\tfrac12mv^2\) · \(U_g=mgh\) · \(U_s=\tfrac12kx^2\)
Conservation: \(K_1+U_{s1}+U_{g1}=K_2+U_{s2}+U_{g2}\)
Week 11 · Design Checks
Pass/fail: \(\sigma_{work}\le\sigma_{allow}\) · \(F_{max}=\sigma_{allow}\times A\) · Bending: \(\sigma=Mc/I\)
2 Final mixed mock · 12 questions

Run this as one continuous timed sitting. It deliberately includes the harder combined-topic questions from across the whole course.

F1Wk 7

A 50 kg crate rests on a 25° incline, \(\mu_s=0.35\). Does it stay, and if not, find the acceleration (\(\mu_k=0.25\)).

Solution

\(W=490\text{ N}\). \(N=444.15\text{ N}\). \(F_s^{max}=155.45\text{ N}\). Required \(=207.1\text{ N}>155.45\): slides.

\(f_k=111.04\text{ N}\). Net \(=207.1-111.04=96.06\text{ N}\). \(a=96.06/50=1.921\text{ m/s}^2\).

F2Wk 5

A simply-supported beam, span 8 m, UDL \(w=60\text{ N/m}\) full span plus a 400 N point load at 5 m from A. Find the reactions.

Solution

UDL total \(=480\text{ N}\) at 4 m. \(\Sigma M_A\): \(R_B(8)=480(4)+400(5)=3920 \Rightarrow R_B=490\text{ N}\). \(R_A=480+400-490=390\text{ N}\).

F3Wk 5

Using F2's beam, find \(M\) at \(x=3\text{ m}\).

Solution

\(V(3)=390-60(3)=210\text{ N}\). UDL 0–3 totals 180 N at 1.5 m from the cut. \(M(3)=390(3)-180(1.5)=1170-270=900\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}\).

F4Wk 6

A truss member force comes out \(-640\text{ N}\) assuming tension. State its actual condition.

Solution

640 N compression.

F5Wk 7

A wedge, \(\theta=18^\circ,\mu=0.28\), lifts a 600 N block (frictionless floor). Find \(P\).

Solution

\(\varphi=\arctan(0.28)=15.64^\circ\). \(P=600\tan(33.64^\circ)=600(0.6656)=399.4\text{ N}\).

F6Wk 8

A T-section: flange area 2500 (\(y=210\text{ mm}\), own \(I=80{,}000\text{ mm}^4\)), stem area 3000 (\(y=100\text{ mm}\), own \(I=8{,}000{,}000\text{ mm}^4\)). Find the composite \(\bar y\) and composite \(I\).

Solution

Total area \(=5500\). \(\bar y=\dfrac{2500(210)+3000(100)}{5500}=150\text{ mm}\).

\(d_1=60,\ d_2=50\). \(I_{1,total}=80{,}000+2500(60)^2=9{,}080{,}000\text{ mm}^4\). \(I_{2,total}=8{,}000{,}000+3000(50)^2=15{,}500{,}000\text{ mm}^4\).

Composite \(I=24{,}580{,}000\text{ mm}^4\).

F7Wk 9

A 3 kg block, spring \(k=700\text{ N/m}\) compressed 0.22 m, launches up a frictionless 22° incline. Find the distance travelled before stopping.

Solution

\(U_s=\dfrac12(700)(0.0484)=16.94\text{ J}\). \(h=16.94/(3\times9.8)=0.5762\text{ m}\). \(d=h/\sin22^\circ=0.5762/0.3746=1.538\text{ m}\).

F8Wk 4 / 11

A rod carries an internal axial force \(N=52\text{ kN}\). \(\sigma_y=290\text{ MPa}\), \(FoS=2.9\). Find the minimum diameter.

Solution

\(\sigma_{allow}=100\text{ MPa}\). \(A_{req}=52{,}000/100=520\text{ mm}^2\). \(d=\sqrt{4(520)/\pi}=25.74\text{ mm}\).

F9Wk 6 / 9

A 20 kg block on a frictionless horizontal surface is connected over a pulley to a 12 kg hanging block. Find the acceleration and tension.

Solution

Total mass \(=32\text{ kg}\), driving force \(=12(9.8)=117.6\text{ N}\). \(a=117.6/32=3.675\text{ m/s}^2\). \(T=20(3.675)=73.5\text{ N}\) (check: \(12(9.8)-12(3.675)=73.5\text{ N}\) ✓).

F10Wk 5

A beam, pin-roller, span 10 m, two point loads: 250 N at 3 m, 350 N at 7 m. Find the reactions.

Solution

\(\Sigma M_A\): \(R_B(10)=250(3)+350(7)=3200 \Rightarrow R_B=320\text{ N}\). \(R_A=600-320=280\text{ N}\).

F11Wk 9

A 2 kg ball is thrown and reaches a max height of 6 m. Find the launch speed, then find its speed at half that height (3 m).

Solution

\(v_{launch}=\sqrt{2(9.8)(6)}=10.84\text{ m/s}\). Total energy \(=mgh_{max}=117.6\text{ J}\).

At \(h=3\): \(U_g=2(9.8)(3)=58.8\text{ J}\). \(K=117.6-58.8=58.8\text{ J}\). \(v=\sqrt{2(58.8)/2}=7.67\text{ m/s}\).

F12Capstone

In 2–3 sentences: what is the single most important habit for passing a DPEN102 exam question, regardless of topic?

Solution

Always start with a correct, complete FBD (or full data extraction) before writing a single equation, and always finish with a verify/sanity-check step, units, sign, order of magnitude. Nearly every avoidable error across all thirteen weeks traces back to skipping one of these two bookends.

Course complete. Thirteen weeks, from Newton's three laws through composite section design, all built on the same handful of ideas reused again and again: draw the FBD, apply equilibrium, check the material, verify the answer. That habit, more than any single formula, is what actually carries across into every later engineering subject.
