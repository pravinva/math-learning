0 Timing protocol & the ten error killers

Run each paper as a genuine timed set (60–90 minutes), not a leisurely read-through. Three passes: first, solve the high-confidence questions quickly; second, work through medium-difficulty ones with full structure; final, attempt the hardest items with explicit stated assumptions. After each paper, tag every mistake by type, concept, sign, units, diagram, algebra, or interpretation, and check it against the list below.

Top 10 error killers
  1. Units not converted first. Mixing mm and m, or N and kN, mid-calculation.
  2. A force missing from the FBD. Usually weight, friction, or one support reaction.
  3. Inconsistent moment sign convention. Mixing clockwise and counter-clockwise within the same equation.
  4. \(\mu_s\) vs \(\mu_k\) confusion. Using the static coefficient after the object has already started sliding, or vice versa.
  5. Assuming \(N=W\) automatically. Forgetting other vertical forces (angled ropes, applied pushes) also affect \(N\).
  6. Wrong angle reference. Confusing "from horizontal" with "from vertical" in incline or vector problems.
  7. Skipping reactions before internal actions. Attempting \(V\), \(M\), or a truss cut before the global reactions are known.
  8. Tension/compression sign mix-up. Losing track of which assumed direction (tension) a positive or negative result actually confirms.
  9. Using a piece's own centroidal \(I\) instead of applying the parallel axis theorem to shift it to the composite axis.
  10. Not recognising a physically impossible result (negative KE, negative required friction) as a signal to re-solve for the actual limiting case.
PAPER A · 10 questions, mixed weeks · target 60–75 minutes
A1Wk 1

A 6 kg block rests on a table. Find the normal force.

Solution

\(N=6(9.8)=58.8\text{ N}\).

A2Wk 2

Convert 3400 N to kN.

Solution

\(3.4\text{ kN}\).

A3Wk 3

A force of 120 N acts at 35° above horizontal. Find its components.

Solution

\(F_x=120\cos35^\circ=98.3\text{ N}\). \(F_y=120\sin35^\circ=68.8\text{ N}\).

A4FBD / Wk 3

A 40 kg crate is pulled by a horizontal rope on a frictionless floor, accelerating at 1.5 m/s². Find the tension.

Solution

\(T=ma=40(1.5)=60\text{ N}\).

A5Wk 4

A rod, diameter 14 mm, carries 25 kN. Find the stress.

Solution

\(A=\pi(7)^2=153.9\text{ mm}^2\). \(\sigma=25{,}000/153.9=162.4\text{ MPa}\).

A6Wk 5

A beam, span 7 m, single 300 N load at 3 m from A. Find the reactions.

Solution

\(R_B=300(3)/7=128.57\text{ N}\). \(R_A=171.43\text{ N}\).

A7Wk 6

At joint P, two non-collinear members meet, no external load. State their forces.

Solution

Both are zero-force members (Rule 1).

A8Wk 7

A 12 kg block on an 18° incline, \(\mu_s=0.4\). Does it stay in place?

Solution

\(W=117.6\text{ N}\). \(N=111.85\text{ N}\). \(F_s^{max}=44.74\text{ N}\). Required \(=36.34\text{ N}\). \(36.34<44.74\): stays.

A9Wk 8

Two rectangles: \(A_1=15\) at \((2,3)\), \(A_2=10\) at \((6,1)\). Find the composite centroid.

Solution

\(\bar x=\dfrac{15(2)+10(6)}{25}=3.6\). \(\bar y=\dfrac{15(3)+10(1)}{25}=2.2\).

A10Wk 9

A 4 kg block starts at rest, 60 J of net work is done on it. Find the final speed.

Solution

\(v=\sqrt{2(60)/4}=\sqrt{30}=5.48\text{ m/s}\).

PAPER B · 10 questions, mixed weeks · target 60–75 minutes
B1Wk 1

A 12 kg object accelerates at 3 m/s². Find the net force.

Solution

\(F=12(3)=36\text{ N}\).

B2Wk 2

Convert 0.045 m to mm.

Solution

\(45\text{ mm}\).

B3Wk 3

A force has components \(F_x=-8\text{ N}, F_y=-6\text{ N}\). Find its magnitude and angle from the positive \(x\)-axis.

Solution

\(F=\sqrt{64+36}=10\text{ N}\). Reference angle \(=36.87^\circ\); Quadrant III: \(\theta=180+36.87=216.87^\circ\).

B4FBD

A 60 N sign hangs symmetrically from two ropes, each at 50° above horizontal. Find the tension in each.

Solution

\(2T\sin50^\circ=60 \Rightarrow T=60/(2\times0.766)=39.16\text{ N}\).

B5Wk 4

A column, area 1800 mm², carries 150 kN. Find the stress in MPa.

Solution

\(\sigma=150{,}000/1800=83.33\text{ MPa}\) (N/mm² = MPa shortcut).

B6Wk 5

A cantilever, fixed at A, length 2.5 m, single 200 N load at the free end. Find the reaction moment at A.

Solution

\(M_A=200(2.5)=500\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}\).

B7Wk 6

A truss spans 10 m, pin-roller, 600 N load at 6 m from A. Find the reactions.

Solution

\(R_B=600(6)/10=360\text{ N}\). \(R_A=240\text{ N}\).

B8Wk 7

A belt wraps 200° (3.49 rad), \(\mu=0.3\). \(T_1=180\text{ N}\). Find \(T_2\).

Solution

\(\mu\beta=0.3(3.49)=1.047\). \(e^{1.047}=2.849\). \(T_2=180(2.849)=512.8\text{ N}\).

B9Wk 8

A rectangle, \(b=90\text{ mm}, h=160\text{ mm}\). Find the centroidal \(I\).

Solution

\(I=\dfrac{90(160)^3}{12}=30{,}720{,}000\text{ mm}^4\).

B10Wk 9

A spring \(k=350\text{ N/m}\), compressed 0.12 m, launches a 0.6 kg block (frictionless). Find the launch speed.

Solution

\(U_s=\dfrac12(350)(0.0144)=2.52\text{ J}\). \(v=\sqrt{2(2.52)/0.6}=\sqrt{8.4}=2.898\text{ m/s}\).
