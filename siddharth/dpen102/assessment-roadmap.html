Topic-to-Assessment Mapping

Vectors + Resolution: heavily tested in quiz + MCQ, foundation for all force components in written problems.
FBD + Equilibrium: core of mid-session and final written questions (reactions, support forces, force balance).
Moments + Couples + Trusses: appears in both MCQ and longer structural force-analysis parts.
Shear Force + Bending Moment: strong final exam topic; practice full diagram workflow and sign conventions.
Friction + Centroids: common MCQ and short numeric questions in sample papers.
Stress/Strain + Safety Factor + Energy: conceptual plus calculations, often linked with material behavior and design checks.

6-Step Written-Question Method

1. Define: restate what is known/unknown and the system boundary.
2. Draw FBD: include all external loads, reactions, axes, dimensions, and sign convention.
3. Theory + assumptions: equilibrium, rigid body assumptions, two-force member rules, etc.
4. Estimate directions/magnitudes: state engineering expectation before solving.
5. Solve with equations: use sum Fx = 0, sum Fy = 0, sum M = 0 and show substitutions cleanly.
6. Verify: check signs, units, reasonableness, and whether equations rebalance.
