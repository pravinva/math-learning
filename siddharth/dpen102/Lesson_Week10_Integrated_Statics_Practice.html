Integrated 1Weeks 1–4

A 15 kg sign hangs from the far end of a horizontal, weightless boom pinned to a wall. A cable runs from that same end to a higher point on the wall, at 35° to the boom, boom length 2 m. (a) Find the cable tension. (b) If the cable's allowable stress is 150 MPa, find the minimum cable diameter.

Solution

(a) Moments about the pin (both weight and cable act at the same end, 2 m out): \(T\sin35^\circ(2)=15(9.8)(2) \Rightarrow T\sin35^\circ=147 \Rightarrow T=147/0.5736=256.3\text{ N}\).

(b) \(A_{req}=256.3/(150\times10^6)=1.709\times10^{-6}\text{ m}^2=1.709\text{ mm}^2\). \(d=\sqrt{4(1.709)/\pi}=1.475\text{ mm}\).

Verify: a sub-2mm cable seems slender for 15kg, but the allowable stress of 150MPa is high and the load is modest, so the small size checks out.

Integrated 2Weeks 4–5

A simply-supported beam, span 5 m, carries a single 2000 N load at 2 m from A. (a) Find the reactions. (b) Find \(V\) and \(M\) just left of the load. (c) Describe the shape of the SFD and BMD.

Solution

(a) \(\Sigma M_A\): \(R_B(5)=2000(2)=4000 \Rightarrow R_B=800\text{ N}\). \(R_A=1200\text{ N}\).

(b) \(V=R_A=1200\text{ N}\). \(M=R_A(2)=2400\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}\).

(c) SFD: starts at \(+1200\text{ N}\), drops instantly to \(1200-2000=-800\text{ N}\) at the load, stays constant to \(x=5\), then jumps back to zero via \(R_B\). BMD: rises linearly from 0 to a peak of \(2400\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}\) exactly at the load (where the SFD's sign flips), then falls linearly back to zero at B.

Integrated 3Weeks 4 & 6

Using the Week 6 truss (span 16 m, height 3 m, panel width 4 m, pin at A, roller at I, single 800 N load at D) and its panel-2 top-chord result \(F_{BD}=1066.67\text{ N}\) compression: if that member's material has \(\sigma_y=250\text{ MPa}\) and required \(FoS=2.5\), find the minimum cross-sectional area needed.

Solution

\(\sigma_{allow}=250/2.5=100\text{ MPa}\). \(A_{req}=1066.67/(100\times10^6)=1.067\times10^{-5}\text{ m}^2=10.67\text{ mm}^2\).

Verify: a genuine method-of-sections result (Week 6) now drives an allowable-stress sizing calculation (Week 4), exactly how the two weeks connect in practice.

Integrated 4Weeks 7 & 9

A 5 kg block starts at rest at the top of a 30° incline and slides 4 m down it (measured along the incline), \(\mu_k=0.2\). Using energy methods, find its speed at the bottom.

Solution

Height dropped: \(h=4\sin30^\circ=2\text{ m}\). \(mgh=5(9.8)(2)=98\text{ J}\).

Friction force \(=\mu_kN=\mu_k\,mg\cos\theta=0.2(5)(9.8)(\cos30^\circ)=8.487\text{ N}\). Energy lost to friction over 4 m: \(8.487(4)=33.95\text{ J}\).

\(K_{final}=98-33.95=64.05\text{ J} \Rightarrow v=\sqrt{2(64.05)/5}=\sqrt{25.62}=5.06\text{ m/s}\).

Integrated 5Weeks 4 & 8

A beam's cross-section is the T-shape from Week 8 (composite centroid \(\bar y=125.71\text{ mm}\), composite \(I=22{,}643{,}787\text{ mm}^4\), total section height 200 mm). If this section carries an internal bending moment \(M=15\text{ kN}\cdot\text{m}\), find the maximum bending stress using \(\sigma=Mc/I\) (the natural extension of Week 8's \(I\) into an actual stress check).

Solution

The larger extreme-fibre distance is on the bottom: \(c=125.71\text{ mm}\) (the top is only \(200-125.71=74.29\text{ mm}\) from the centroid, smaller).

\(M=15{,}000{,}000\text{ N}\cdot\text{mm}\). \(\sigma=\dfrac{Mc}{I}=\dfrac{15{,}000{,}000(125.71)}{22{,}643{,}787}=\dfrac{1{,}885{,}650{,}000}{22{,}643{,}787}=83.28\text{ MPa}\).

Integrated 6Weeks 4 & 7

Using Week 7's wedge worked example (\(P=277.3\text{ N}\) to lift a 500 N block, \(\theta=15^\circ,\mu=0.25\)), the lifted block is supported by a rod of diameter 12 mm. Find the stress in the rod under the 500 N load.

Solution

\(A=\pi(0.006)^2=1.131\times10^{-4}\text{ m}^2\). \(\sigma=500/(1.131\times10^{-4})=4.42\times10^6\text{ Pa}=4.42\text{ MPa}\).

Integrated 7Weeks 4 & 5

A cantilever beam, fixed at A, length 3 m, carries a UDL \(w=200\text{ N/m}\) over its full length plus a 400 N point load at the free end. (a) Find the reaction force and moment at A. (b) Find \(V\) and \(M\) at midspan (1.5 m from A).

Solution

(a) UDL total \(=600\text{ N}\) at 1.5 m from A. \(R_{Ay}=600+400=1000\text{ N}\). \(M_A=600(1.5)+400(3)=900+1200=2100\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}\).

(b) Using the free-end side (length 1.5 m): UDL on this segment \(=200(1.5)=300\text{ N}\) at 0.75 m from the cut; tip load 400 N at 1.5 m from the cut.

\(V=300+400=700\text{ N}\). \(M=300(0.75)+400(1.5)=225+600=825\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}\).

Integrated 8Weeks 1 & 7

A 25 kg crate on a 20° incline is connected by a rope (parallel to the slope) to a hanging 10 kg mass over a frictionless pulley at the top. \(\mu_s\) between crate and incline is 0.3. Determine whether the system is in equilibrium.

Solution

Crate: \(W=245\text{ N}\). Down-slope component \(=245\sin20^\circ=83.79\text{ N}\). \(N=245\cos20^\circ=230.23\text{ N}\). \(F_s^{max}=0.3(230.23)=69.07\text{ N}\).

Hanging mass pulls with \(98\text{ N}\), tending to drag the crate up the incline (since \(98>83.79\)). Required friction to prevent motion \(=98-83.79=14.21\text{ N}\), acting down-slope.

Since \(14.21\text{ N} \ll F_s^{max}=69.07\text{ N}\): the system is in equilibrium, static friction easily accommodates the small imbalance. \(a=0\).

Integrated 9Weeks 7 & 9

A 3 kg block slides down a frictionless 25° incline from rest, travels 2 m, then compresses a spring (\(k=1000\text{ N/m}\)) aligned along the incline. Find the maximum spring compression.

Solution

At maximum compression \(x\), the block is momentarily at rest, so ALL height dropped (over the full \(2+x\) distance along the slope) converts to spring PE:

\(mg(2+x)\sin\theta=\dfrac12kx^2 \Rightarrow 3(9.8)(2+x)(0.4226)=500x^2 \Rightarrow 12.42(2+x)=500x^2\).

\(500x^2-12.42x-24.84=0\). Solving: \(x=\dfrac{12.42+\sqrt{12.42^2+4(500)(24.84)}}{1000}=\dfrac{12.42+223.24}{1000}=0.236\text{ m}\).

Integrated 10Weeks 4 & 7

A cable runs over a drum behaving like a belt-friction system (wrap angle \(\pi\) rad, \(\mu=0.2\)) to hold a load, with a worker holding the free end at 200 N. (a) Find the maximum load \(W\) it can support without slipping. (b) That load hangs from a rod with allowable stress 120 MPa, find the minimum rod diameter.

Solution

(a) \(T_2/T_1=e^{\mu\beta}=e^{0.2\pi}=e^{0.628}=1.874\). \(W=T_2=200(1.874)=374.8\text{ N}\).

(b) \(A_{req}=374.8/(120\times10^6)=3.123\times10^{-6}\text{ m}^2=3.123\text{ mm}^2\). \(d=\sqrt{4(3.123)/\pi}=1.994\text{ mm}\).
