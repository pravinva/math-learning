Section A: Vectors, FBD, Equilibrium (Q1-Q5)

Q1
8 marks

At a joint, two cables exert forces \(F_1=8\text{ kN}\) at \(35^\circ\) above +x and \(F_2=6\text{ kN}\) at \(120^\circ\) from +x. Find the resultant magnitude and direction.

Worked solution
  1. \(F_{1x}=8\cos35^\circ=6.553,\quad F_{1y}=8\sin35^\circ=4.589\text{ kN}\).
  2. \(F_{2x}=6\cos120^\circ=-3,\quad F_{2y}=6\sin120^\circ=5.196\text{ kN}\).
  3. Resultant components: \(R_x=3.553,\ R_y=9.785\text{ kN}\).
  4. \(|R|=\sqrt{R_x^2+R_y^2}=\sqrt{3.553^2+9.785^2}=10.41\text{ kN}\).
  5. \(\theta=\tan^{-1}(R_y/R_x)=\tan^{-1}(9.785/3.553)=70.0^\circ\) above +x.
Q2
8 marks

A 450 N crate rests on a rough horizontal floor. A force \(P\) is applied at \(25^\circ\) above horizontal. If \(\mu_s=0.35\), find \(P\) at impending motion.

Worked solution
  1. Vertical equilibrium: \(N + P\sin25^\circ - 450=0 \Rightarrow N=450-P\sin25^\circ\).
  2. At impending motion: \(P\cos25^\circ=F_f=\mu_s N=0.35(450-P\sin25^\circ)\).
  3. \(P(0.9063+0.35\times0.4226)=157.5\).
  4. \(P(1.0542)=157.5\Rightarrow P=149.4\text{ N}\).
Q3
8 marks

For the frame below, resolve the 5 kN force along members AB and AC where AB is at \(30^\circ\) and AC at \(110^\circ\) from +x.

Worked solution

Use oblique resolution with simultaneous equations in x/y:

  1. Let components be \(F_{AB},F_{AC}\) along respective member directions.
  2. \(F_{AB}\cos30^\circ + F_{AC}\cos110^\circ = 5\).
  3. \(F_{AB}\sin30^\circ + F_{AC}\sin110^\circ = 0\).
  4. From second: \(0.5F_{AB}+0.9397F_{AC}=0 \Rightarrow F_{AB}=-1.879F_{AC}\).
  5. Substitute into first: \(-1.627F_{AC}-0.342F_{AC}=5 \Rightarrow F_{AC}=-2.54\text{ kN}\).
  6. \(F_{AB}=4.78\text{ kN}\). (Negative sign means AC component acts opposite assumed direction.)
Q4
8 marks

A pin at A and roller at B support a beam. Loads: 6 kN downward at 2 m from A and 9 kN downward at 5 m from A. Span AB = 8 m. Find reactions \(A_x,A_y,B_y\).

Worked solution
  1. No horizontal load \(\Rightarrow A_x=0\).
  2. \(\Sigma M_A=0:\ 8B_y-6(2)-9(5)=0 \Rightarrow 8B_y=57 \Rightarrow B_y=7.125\text{ kN}\).
  3. \(\Sigma F_y=0:\ A_y+B_y-15=0 \Rightarrow A_y=7.875\text{ kN}\).
Q5
8 marks

Explain why a complete FBD must include support reactions, and list three common FBD errors that cause wrong equilibrium results.

Worked solution

Support reactions are external forces from constraints; without them, \(\Sigma F=0,\Sigma M=0\) is incomplete.

Common errors:

  1. Forgetting one reaction component at a pin support.
  2. Using wrong direction/sign for applied inclined forces.
  3. Taking moments with wrong lever arm (not perpendicular distance).

Section B: Moments, Beams, Reactions (Q6-Q10)

Q6
10 marks

Simply supported beam AB, span 10 m, UDL \(w=3\text{ kN/m}\) over full span, plus 12 kN point load at 3 m from A. Find reactions.

Worked solution
  1. UDL resultant \(=wL=30\text{ kN}\) at midspan (5 m from A).
  2. \(\Sigma M_A=0:\ 10B_y-30(5)-12(3)=0\Rightarrow B_y=18.6\text{ kN}\).
  3. \(\Sigma F_y=0:\ A_y+B_y-42=0\Rightarrow A_y=23.4\text{ kN}\).
Q7
10 marks

For Q6, find shear immediately left and right of x=3 m, and locate where shear crosses zero.

Worked solution
  1. \(V(3^-)=A_y-w(3)=23.4-9=14.4\text{ kN}\).
  2. Jump by -12 kN at point load: \(V(3^+)=2.4\text{ kN}\).
  3. For \(x>3\): \(V=23.4-3x-12=11.4-3x\).
  4. Set \(V=0\Rightarrow x=3.8\text{ m}\) from A.
Q8
10 marks

For Q6, determine maximum bending moment.

Worked solution
  1. Maximum \(M\) occurs where \(V=0\), at \(x=3.8\text{ m}\).
  2. \(M(x)=A_yx-\frac{wx^2}{2}-12(x-3)\) for \(x>3\).
  3. \(M_{max}=23.4(3.8)-\frac{3(3.8)^2}{2}-12(0.8)=57.54\text{ kN·m}\).
Q9
10 marks

A cantilever length 4 m carries UDL 2 kN/m over entire span and 5 kN tip load. Find fixed-end shear and moment.

Worked solution
  1. UDL resultant \(=8\text{ kN}\) acting at 2 m from fixed end.
  2. Fixed shear \(V_f=8+5=13\text{ kN}\) (upward at support).
  3. Fixed moment \(M_f=8(2)+5(4)=16+20=36\text{ kN·m}\) (hogging sign convention).
Q10
8 marks

State and justify two checks to validate a completed SFD/BMD before finalizing an exam answer.

Worked solution
  1. Area under SFD between two points equals BMD change between those points.
  2. Final BMD at simple support should return to zero; end shear sum must match total load.

Section C: Trusses (Q11-Q15)

Q11
10 marks

For a symmetric king-post truss span 6 m, height 2 m, 18 kN downward at apex, find support reactions.

Worked solution

By symmetry: \(A_y=B_y=9\text{ kN}\), \(A_x=0\).

Q12
12 marks

Using method of joints at left support joint for Q11, find force in left rafter and left tie member.

Worked solution
  1. Rafter angle: \(\tan\theta=2/3\Rightarrow \sin\theta=0.5547,\cos\theta=0.8321\).
  2. \(\Sigma F_y=0:\ 9+F_r\sin\theta=0\Rightarrow F_r=-16.23\text{ kN}\) (compression).
  3. \(\Sigma F_x=0:\ F_t+F_r\cos\theta=0\Rightarrow F_t=13.50\text{ kN}\) (tension).
Q13
10 marks

Method of sections: cut through three members including central vertical. Determine central vertical force.

Worked solution

Take moments about intersection of the two other cut members so only vertical member remains. For this symmetric loading, central vertical carries \(18\text{ kN}\) tension.

Q14
8 marks

Explain how to identify zero-force members quickly in an exam, with one valid joint condition.

Worked solution

At a joint with no external load/reaction and only two non-collinear members, both member forces are zero. Also: with three members where two are collinear and no external load, the non-collinear member is zero-force.

Q15
8 marks

A member force is found negative assuming tension. What does this mean physically?

Worked solution

The member is actually in compression with magnitude equal to absolute value of the computed force.

Section D: SFD/BMD Interpretation (Q16-Q20)

Q16
8 marks

State differential relationships between load \(w\), shear \(V\), and moment \(M\).

Worked solution

\(\dfrac{dV}{dx}=-w,\quad \dfrac{dM}{dx}=V\).

Q17
10 marks

Given \(V(x)=12-2x\) kN on \(0\le x\le6\), and \(M(0)=0\), derive \(M(x)\) and \(M(6)\).

Worked solution
  1. \(M=\int(12-2x)\,dx=12x-x^2+C\).
  2. \(M(0)=0\Rightarrow C=0\).
  3. \(M(6)=72-36=36\text{ kN·m}\).
Q18
8 marks

Where does bending moment become maximum in a beam segment?

Worked solution

Where \(V=0\) (and changes sign in many practical cases).

Q19
8 marks

What geometric shape does SFD take under pure UDL? What shape does BMD take?

Worked solution

Under constant UDL: SFD is linear, BMD is parabolic.

Q20
10 marks

Give a 4-step procedure to build SFD and BMD under mixed UDL + point loads.

Worked solution
  1. Find support reactions from equilibrium.
  2. Traverse beam left-to-right computing shear jumps/slopes.
  3. Integrate shear areas to get bending moments.
  4. Apply boundary checks at supports and load points.

Section E: Friction Systems (Q21-Q25)

Q21
10 marks

Block \(W=600\text{ N}\) on \(30^\circ\) incline, \(\mu_s=0.25\). Find if it slips down when no extra force is applied.

Worked solution
  1. Down-slope component \(=W\sin30=300\text{ N}\).
  2. \(N=W\cos30=519.6\text{ N}\).
  3. \(F_{max}=\mu_sN=129.9\text{ N}\).
  4. Since \(300>129.9\), static friction insufficient; block slips down.
Q22
8 marks

Define angle of friction and angle of repose, and state their relation.

Worked solution

\(\tan\phi=\mu\). Angle of repose equals angle of friction at impending motion: \(\theta_r=\phi\).

Q23
10 marks

A 200 N block is pushed on horizontal floor with 80 N at \(20^\circ\) downward. \(\mu_k=0.3\). Find kinetic friction and acceleration (mass 20 kg).

Worked solution
  1. \(N=200+80\sin20=227.36\text{ N}\).
  2. \(F_k=\mu_kN=68.21\text{ N}\).
  3. Horizontal push \(=80\cos20=75.18\text{ N}\).
  4. Net \(=75.18-68.21=6.97\text{ N}\Rightarrow a=6.97/20=0.349\text{ m/s}^2\).
Q24
6 marks

Why does pushing downward on a crate usually increase friction?

Worked solution

Downward component raises normal force \(N\), and friction capacity is proportional to \(N\): \(F=\mu N\).

Q25
10 marks

Two blocks stacked: top 50 N on lower 150 N. \(\mu=0.4\) between top/bottom only. Find max horizontal force on top without slipping.

Worked solution
  1. Normal at interface \(=50\text{ N}\).
  2. Max static friction \(=0.4(50)=20\text{ N}\).
  3. Thus max applied force transferable without slip is \(20\text{ N}\).

Section F: Centroids and Second Moment (Q26-Q30)

Q26
10 marks

Rectangle \(200\times100\) mm with centered circular hole diameter 40 mm. Find centroid location from bottom-left corner.

Worked solution

By symmetry, centroid remains at geometric center \((100,\ 50)\) mm.

Q27
12 marks

Find \(\bar{y}\) of T-section: flange \(120\times20\) mm on top of web \(20\times100\) mm, web centered.

Worked solution
  1. \(A_f=2400,\ y_f=110\) mm from base.
  2. \(A_w=2000,\ y_w=50\) mm.
  3. \(\bar y=\dfrac{A_fy_f+A_wy_w}{A_f+A_w}=\dfrac{2400(110)+2000(50)}{4400}=82.73\text{ mm}\).
Q28
10 marks

For a rectangle \(b=80\) mm, \(h=160\) mm, find \(I_x\) about centroidal horizontal axis.

Worked solution

\(I_x=\dfrac{bh^3}{12}=\dfrac{80(160)^3}{12}=27.31\times10^6\text{ mm}^4\).

Q29
8 marks

State parallel-axis theorem and one typical use in ENGG102 problems.

Worked solution

\(I=I_c+Ad^2\). Used when transferring centroidal second moment of a component to a common reference axis for composite sections.

Q30
8 marks

Why are centroid and second moment both needed in beam design checks?

Worked solution

Centroid locates neutral axis and section geometry; second moment quantifies bending stiffness and stress distribution via \(\sigma=\dfrac{My}{I}\).

Section G: Stress and Strain (Q31-Q35)

Q31
10 marks

A steel rod area \(400\text{ mm}^2\) carries tensile load 80 kN. Find normal stress.

Worked solution

\(\sigma=P/A=80{,}000/(400\times10^{-6})=200\text{ MPa}\).

Q32
10 marks

For Q31, if \(E=200\text{ GPa}\), find strain and extension over 1.2 m length.

Worked solution
  1. \(\epsilon=\sigma/E=200\times10^6/200\times10^9=0.001\).
  2. \(\delta=\epsilon L=0.001(1.2)=0.0012\text{ m}=1.2\text{ mm}\).
Q33
8 marks

Define proportional limit, yield stress, and factor of safety.

Worked solution

Proportional limit: end of linear \(\sigma\)-\(\epsilon\). Yield: start of permanent deformation. FoS \(=\sigma_{allow}/\sigma_{working}\) or \(\sigma_y/\sigma_{working}\).

Q34
10 marks

Bar in compression: \(P=120\text{ kN}\), diameter 30 mm. Find stress and comment on sign convention.

Worked solution
  1. \(A=\pi d^2/4=\pi(30)^2/4=706.86\text{ mm}^2\).
  2. \(\sigma=120000/706.86=169.8\text{ MPa}\).
  3. Compression is often taken as negative stress by sign convention.
Q35
8 marks

State two reasons why stress concentration matters in real components even if average stress seems safe.

Worked solution
  1. Local peak stress near holes/notches can exceed yield while average stress is low.
  2. Fatigue cracks usually initiate at geometric stress raisers.

Section H: Work-Energy and Dynamics (Q36-Q40)

Q36
10 marks

A 10 kg block starts from rest and is pulled by constant 80 N over 6 m on frictionless floor. Find final speed.

Worked solution
  1. Work done \(W=Fs=80(6)=480\text{ J}\).
  2. \(\Delta KE=W\Rightarrow \frac12mv^2=480\).
  3. \(v=\sqrt{2(480)/10}=9.80\text{ m/s}\).
Q37
10 marks

A 2 kg mass dropped from 5 m (no air drag). Find impact speed using energy.

Worked solution
  1. \(mgh=\frac12mv^2\Rightarrow v=\sqrt{2gh}\).
  2. \(v=\sqrt{2(9.81)(5)}=9.90\text{ m/s}\).
Q38
8 marks

Differentiate conservative and non-conservative forces with one ENGG102 example each.

Worked solution

Conservative: path-independent work (gravity, spring). Non-conservative: path-dependent dissipation (friction).

Q39
10 marks

A cart (mass 50 kg) moves at 4 m/s and climbs a ramp, stopping after gaining height \(h\) with no losses. Find \(h\).

Worked solution
  1. \(\frac12mv^2=mgh\Rightarrow h=v^2/(2g)\).
  2. \(h=16/(19.62)=0.815\text{ m}\).
Q40
12 marks

A 5 kg slider moves 3 m up a \(25^\circ\) incline under 120 N pull parallel to incline. \(\mu_k=0.2\). Find final speed from rest.

Worked solution
  1. Applied work: \(W_P=120(3)=360\text{ J}\).
  2. Weight component down slope: \(mg\sin25=20.73\text{ N}\Rightarrow W_g=-20.73(3)=-62.2\text{ J}\).
  3. Normal \(N=mg\cos25=44.45\text{ N}\Rightarrow F_k=\mu N=8.89\text{ N}\Rightarrow W_f=-26.7\text{ J}\).
  4. Net work \(=360-62.2-26.7=271.1\text{ J}=\Delta KE=\frac12mv^2\).
  5. \(v=\sqrt{2(271.1)/5}=10.41\text{ m/s}\).

Attempt mixed sets (for example: 2 from each section) under timed conditions, then check working quality against the model solutions. In exams, method marks are critical: show FBD, define sign convention, write governing equations, substitute cleanly, and add unit checks.
