Learning Outcomes

What this subject expects by the end.
SLO1: Apply fundamental physical principles in simple structure design tasks.
SLO2: Analyse forces acting on and within structural elements.
SLO3: Apply logical engineering design practices to mechanics problems.
SLO4: Demonstrate self-directed learning and problem-solving discipline.
SLO5: Present calculations and analysis professionally.
SLO6/SLO7: Team participation and understanding engineering responsibility.

Weekly Study Path (Recommended)

Weeks 1-3: Vectors, force resolution, Newton-law foundations, and clean sign conventions.
Weeks 4-6: FBD quality, support reactions, moments, and solving equilibrium systems quickly.
Weeks 6-8: Mid-session preparation: mixed MCQs + long-form frame/beam response questions.
Weeks 8-11: Trusses, shear force/bending moment diagrams, centroid and friction problem sets.
Weeks 11-13: Stress/strain/FOS + full timed exam practice with verification and write-up quality.

Exam Readiness Checklist

FBD discipline: Every force/reaction/axis shown before equations.
Method discipline: Define -> FBD -> Theory -> Solve -> Verify (especially written questions).
Diagram discipline: SFD/BMD with correct jumps, slopes, and extremum logic.
Units discipline: Keep N-kN, mm-m, MPa-GPa conversions consistent.
Communication discipline: Final answers include direction, sign meaning, and engineering interpretation.
