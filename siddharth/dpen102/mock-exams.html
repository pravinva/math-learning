Mock Mid-Session (100 marks, 120 min)

Pattern: 10 MCQ (50 marks) + 2 written questions (25 marks each). This section now includes worked solutions with method + verification.

Section A: MCQ-style Concepts + Quick Calculations

A1
5 marks

Resolve a 25 N force acting at $30^\circ$ above the +x-axis into x and y components.

Show Solution
Answer: $F_x=21.65$ N, $F_y=12.50$ N
  1. Use component form: $F_x=F\cos\theta$, $F_y=F\sin\theta$.
  2. $F_x=25\cos30^\circ=21.65$ N.
  3. $F_y=25\sin30^\circ=12.50$ N.
A2
5 marks

At the top of a projectile path, what are the vertical velocity and vertical acceleration values?

Show Solution
Answer: $v_y=0$, $a_y=-g$
  1. At peak, velocity changes direction, so instant vertical velocity is zero.
  2. Acceleration is governed by gravity only, so still downward.
A3
5 marks

Choose the true relation between shear force and bending moment along a beam.

Show Solution
Answer: $V=\frac{dM}{dx}$
  1. From beam differential relations: $\frac{dV}{dx}=-w$ and $\frac{dM}{dx}=V$.
  2. So slope of BMD is shear value at each x.
A4
5 marks

If a 2-force member is in pure tension, what can you say about forces at its two ends?

Show Solution
Answer: Equal, opposite, collinear with member axis
  1. Two-force member equilibrium requires only axial force transmission.
  2. Any non-collinear pair would create a moment and violate equilibrium.
A5
5 marks

Two vectors are $\vec A=(9\hat i-6\hat j)$ N and $\vec B=(7\hat i+9\hat j)$ N. Find $\vec C=\vec A+\vec B$ magnitude and angle from +x.

Show Solution
Answer: $\vec C=(16\hat i+3\hat j)$ N, $|\vec C|=16.28$ N, $\theta=10.6^\circ$
  1. Add components: $C_x=9+7=16$, $C_y=-6+9=3$.
  2. Magnitude: $|\vec C|=\sqrt{16^2+3^2}=16.28$ N.
  3. Angle: $\theta=\tan^{-1}(3/16)=10.6^\circ$ above +x.
A6
5 marks

Earth weight is 135 lbf. What is mass in SI units? (Use $1\text{ N}=0.22481\text{ lbf}$ and $g=9.81\text{ m/s}^2$.)

Show Solution
Answer: $m\approx61.2$ kg
  1. Convert weight: $W=135/0.22481=600.5$ N.
  2. Mass: $m=W/g=600.5/9.81=61.2$ kg.
A7
5 marks

What does the slope of the linear elastic stress-strain curve represent?

Show Solution
Answer: Young's Modulus, $E$
  1. By definition in elastic range: $\sigma=E\epsilon$.
  2. Hence slope $d\sigma/d\epsilon=E$.
A8
5 marks

A sled is pulled with constant net horizontal force on frictionless ground. Which quantity is constant?

Show Solution
Answer: Acceleration is constant
  1. Newton 2nd law: $F=ma$.
  2. If net force and mass are constant, acceleration is constant.
A9
5 marks

In a beam, where does bending moment reach local extrema?

Show Solution
Answer: Where shear force is zero
  1. $V=dM/dx$.
  2. At a local max/min of $M$, derivative is zero, so $V=0$.
A10
5 marks

A 30 kN force acts 2.5 m from a pivot, perpendicular to the lever arm. Find moment about pivot.

Show Solution
Answer: $M=75$ kN·m
  1. Moment magnitude: $M=Fd$ for perpendicular force.
  2. $M=30\times2.5=75$ kN·m.

Section B: Written Analysis

B1
25 marks

A low-mass frame has support C (pin) and support F on a $45^\circ$ incline (single reaction normal to slope). Horizontal loads: $F_A=80$ kN at 2 m above C and $F_B=40$ kN at 5 m above C. Support F is 4 m to the right of C. Find all support reactions.

Show Solution
Final reactions: $C_x=30$ kN (right), $C_y=-90$ kN (down), $F_x=90$ kN (right), $F_y=90$ kN (up), $R_F=127.3$ kN
  1. FBD: Unknowns $C_x,C_y$ at C and one reaction $R_F$ normal to slope. Since slope is $45^\circ$, components satisfy $F_x=F_y=R_F/\sqrt2$.
  2. Moment about C: $\Sigma M_C=0$: $+F_y(4)-80(2)-40(5)=0$.
  3. Solve: $4F_y-160-200=0 \Rightarrow F_y=90$ kN.
  4. Then $F_x=90$ kN (45-degree component equality).
  5. Horizontal force balance: $\Sigma F_x=0$: $C_x+90-80-40=0 \Rightarrow C_x=30$ kN.
  6. Vertical force balance: $\Sigma F_y=0$: $C_y+90=0 \Rightarrow C_y=-90$ kN (downward).
  7. Reaction magnitude at F: $R_F=\sqrt{90^2+90^2}=127.3$ kN.
  8. Verification: Substitute into all three equilibrium equations; each balances to zero.
Marking idea: FBD 5, moment equation 6, force equations 6, solving + units 5, verification 3.
B2
25 marks

Beam AC is pinned at C and connected to a two-force strut at B ($45^\circ$). Loads: $F_1=100$ kN downward at A (3 m left of C), $F_2=200$ kN downward at B (2 m left of C), and clockwise couple $M_1=50$ kN·m. Then, for the same geometry, find FoS for a strut diameter 100 mm with $\sigma_y=250$ MPa.

Show Solution
Reactions: $T=459.6$ kN in strut, $C_x=-325$ kN, $C_y=-25$ kN. FoS $=4.27$
  1. FBD: Replace strut by axial force $T$ at B with components $T\cos45^\circ$ (x) and $T\sin45^\circ$ (y).
  2. Moment about C: $\Sigma M_C=0$: $+100(3)+200(2)-50-T\sin45^\circ(2)=0$.
  3. Thus $300+400-50-1.414T=0 \Rightarrow T=459.6$ kN.
  4. Vertical balance: $\Sigma F_y=0$: $C_y+T\sin45^\circ-100-200=0$.
  5. $C_y+325-300=0 \Rightarrow C_y=-25$ kN.
  6. Horizontal balance: $\Sigma F_x=0$: $C_x+T\cos45^\circ=0 \Rightarrow C_x=-325$ kN.
  7. Stress in strut: $A=\pi d^2/4=\pi(0.1)^2/4=0.007854$ m$^2$.
  8. $\sigma=T/A=459600/0.007854=58.5$ MPa.
  9. Factor of Safety: $\text{FoS}=\sigma_y/\sigma=250/58.5=4.27$.
Marking idea: setup/FBD 5, equilibrium equations 8, solve reactions 5, stress + FoS 5, verification/units 2.

Section A: Mixed MCQ

F1
4 marks

Given a beam with UDL and point load, identify the correct SFD shape and sign transitions near supports and load points.

Show Solution
Answer rule: UDL gives linear slope in SFD; point load gives vertical jump.
F2
4 marks

Find the centroid location of a composite L-area from a chosen datum using area decomposition.

Show Solution
Method: $\bar{x}=\frac{\sum A_ix_i}{\sum A_i}$, $\bar{y}=\frac{\sum A_iy_i}{\sum A_i}$.
F3
4 marks

A block on a 30-degree incline is connected via pulley to a hanging mass. Derive expression for coefficient of static friction at impending motion.

Show Solution
Method: apply $\Sigma F_{\parallel}=0$, $\Sigma F_{\perp}=0$, use $F_f=\mu_sN$.
F4
4 marks

For a roller coaster rising from 50 m to 60 m without friction, what happens to kinetic energy?

Show Solution
Answer: kinetic energy decreases by $mg(10)$.
F5
4 marks

If truss height decreases while span and load stay fixed, what happens to diagonal member forces?

Show Solution
Answer: diagonal forces increase (flatter diagonals need larger force components).
F6
4 marks

Second moment of area units are:

Show Solution
Answer: length to the fourth power (e.g., mm$^4$).
F7
4 marks

For a simply supported beam under symmetric loading, what can be said about support reactions?

Show Solution
Answer: reactions are equal by symmetry.
F8
4 marks

When can a truss member be identified as zero-force by inspection?

Show Solution
Answer: at an unloaded joint with two non-collinear members, both are zero-force members.
F9
4 marks

True or False: If shear changes sign, bending moment has an extremum near that section.

Show Solution
Answer: True.
F10
4 marks

A point load acting exactly at a pin support creates what direct moment about that support?

Show Solution
Answer: zero (zero lever arm).

Section B: Long Questions

F4
30 marks

Beam A-B-C has overhang AB = 3 m and span BC = 6 m. Point load 20 kN downward at A. Uniform load 60 kN/m acts over the last 3 m of BC (from x=4.5 m to x=7.5 m measured from A). Supports at B (roller) and C (pin). Find reactions, SFD key ordinates, zero-shear location, and max $M^+$ / max $M^-$.

Show Solution
Key results: $R_B=70$ kN up, $R_C=130$ kN up; max negative moment at B is $-60$ kN·m.
  1. Equivalent UDL load: $W=60\times3=180$ kN, acting at midpoint of loaded segment: $x=6.0$ m from A.
  2. Take moments about B ($x=3$ m), CCW positive: $+20(3)-180(3)+R_C(6)=0$.
  3. $60-540+6R_C=0 \Rightarrow R_C=130$ kN.
  4. Vertical equilibrium: $R_B+R_C-20-180=0 \Rightarrow R_B=70$ kN.
  5. Shear just right of A: $V=-20$ kN. At B jump +70, so $V_{B+}=+50$ kN.
  6. Across unloaded BC segment up to start of UDL (x=4.5): shear remains +50 kN.
  7. In UDL region, shear decreases linearly with slope $-60$ kN/m, so zero shear occurs after $\Delta x=50/60=0.833$ m from load start. Thus $x=5.333$ m.
  8. Moment at B from overhang point load: $M_B=-20(3)=-60$ kN·m (hogging, max negative).
  9. Max positive moment occurs where shear crosses zero in loaded zone; evaluate by area under shear from B to $x=5.333$ m.
  10. Verification: end moment at C is zero for pin/roller system.
Marking idea: reactions 8, shear logic 8, BMD logic 8, extrema + verification 6.
F5
30 marks

For a planar truss, external loads are $P=25$ kN vertical and $H=10$ kN horizontal. Determine forces in members BC, CE, and DE using method of sections, and label each as tension or compression.

Show Solution
Model method answer: use global reactions, then one section cut through BC-CE-DE.
  1. Find support reactions from whole-truss equilibrium: $\Sigma F_x=0$, $\Sigma F_y=0$, $\Sigma M=0$.
  2. Pass a section through BC, CE, DE only and isolate one side of truss.
  3. Take moment about joint where two cut members intersect to solve third cut force directly.
  4. Use remaining $\Sigma F_x=0$ and $\Sigma F_y=0$ to solve the other two member forces.
  5. Sign convention: assume all cut forces tension (pulling away). Negative result means compression.
  6. Final line must report magnitudes + T/C labels + one equilibrium check.
Marking idea: reactions 6, clean section cut 6, member force equations 12, classification + check 6.
High-score workflow: (1) draw clean FBD first, (2) lock sign convention, (3) keep units consistent, (4) state assumptions, (5) add a final verification line.
