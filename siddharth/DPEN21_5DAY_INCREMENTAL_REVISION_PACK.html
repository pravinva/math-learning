DPEN21 10-Day Incremental Revision Pack

Circles, absolute value, discriminant, parabola, hyperbola, lines, logs, exponentials, and function composition
How to use: Do each day in order. Keep old topics alive while adding new ones. Complete questions first; check answers at the end.

Day 1

Focus: Circles (intro) + memory anchors for logs and exponentials
Q1 (Log):

Solve \( \log_2 x + \log_2 4 = 5 \).

Q2 (Exponential graph):

Sketch \(y=2^x\), and label y-intercept, horizontal asymptote, domain, and range.

Q3 (Circle):

For \((x-2)^2+(y+1)^2=9\), find centre and radius.

Day 2

Focus: Circles (intercepts + domain/range), plus daily log/exp revision
Q1 (Log):

Solve \( \log x - \log 2 = 1 \).

Q2 (Exponential graph):

Sketch \(y=2^{x-1}-3\), label asymptote and intercepts.

Q3 (Circle):

Find domain and range of \((x-1)^2+(y+2)^2=16\).

Day 3

Focus: Composite/odd-even functions + 2 absolute value questions, while retaining log/exp/circle
Q1 (Log):

Solve \( \log_3(2x-1)=2 \).

Q2 (Exponential):

Solve \( e^x=25 \) and leave exact form.

Q3 (Circle):

Find x-intercepts of \((x-1)^2+y^2=4\).

Q4 (Functions):

If \(f(x)=x^3-x\), \(g(x)=x^2+1\): state odd/even for each and find \((f\circ g)(x)\).

Q5 (Absolute value 1):

Solve \( |x-3|=5 \).

Q6 (Absolute value 2):

Find vertex, x-intercepts, and range of \(y=|x-2|-3\).

Day 4

Focus: Add parabola + discriminant, keep all previous topics alive
Q1 (Log):

Solve \( \log x-\log(x-1)=1 \) with domain check.

Q2 (Exponential):

Solve \(2^{x+1}=4^{3x-2}\).

Q3 (Circle):

Convert \(x^2+y^2-4x+6y+9=0\) to standard form and identify centre/radius.

Q4 (Functions):

Determine whether \(h(x)=x^4+1\) is odd, even, or neither.

Q5 (Absolute value):

Solve \( |2x-3| \ge 5 \).

Q6 (Parabola graph):

Sketch \(y=(x-1)^2-4\). Label vertex, axis of symmetry, and intercepts.

Q7 (Discriminant):

For \(x^2-6x+13=0\), use discriminant to classify roots.

Day 5

Focus: Add hyperbola + lines, full mixed revision
Q1 (Log):

Solve \( \log_2 16+\log_2 x=\log_2 32 \).

Q2 (Exponential graph):

Sketch \(y=3^x\), state domain/range/asymptote.

Q3 (Circle):

For \((x+2)^2+(y-1)^2=9\), find domain and range.

Q4 (Functions):

If \(f(x)=|x|\), \(g(x)=x-2\), find \((f\circ g)(x)\) and where it equals 0.

Q5 (Parabola):

Find nature of roots of \(x^2-4x+4=0\), then state vertex of \(y=x^2-4x+4\).

Q6 (Hyperbola):

For \(y=2+\frac{4}{x-3}\), find centre, asymptotes, intercepts, domain, and range.

Q7 (Lines):

Find equation, gradient, and intercepts of line through \((1,2)\), \((-1,3)\).

Q8 (Lines):

Find line through \((2,3)\) parallel to \(3x-7y=0\).

Day 6

Focus: Rational-log equations with domain checks + full mixed consolidation
Q1 (Log):

Solve \( \log_3(x-1)+\log_3(x-4)=2 \), with full domain justification.

Q2 (Exponential):

Solve \(e^{2x}-5e^x+6=0\).

Q3 (Circle):

Find the equation of the circle with diameter endpoints \(A(2,-1)\), \(B(8,5)\).

Q4 (Functions):

If \(f(x)=\dfrac{1}{x-1}\), \(g(x)=x^2-4\), find \((f\circ g)(x)\) and state its domain.

Q5 (Absolute value):

Solve \( |x+1|+|x-3|=6 \).

Q6 (Parabola):

For \(y=x^2-6x+13\), find vertex, minimum value, and axis of symmetry.

Q7 (Hyperbola):

For \(y=-1+\dfrac{6}{x+2}\), find centre, asymptotes, and intercepts.

Q8 (Lines):

Find the equation of the line through \((3,-2)\) perpendicular to \(4x+3y-7=0\).

Day 7

Focus: Multi-log equations + graph interpretation under constraints
Q1 (Log):

Solve \( \log_2(x+3)-\log_2(x-1)=\log_2 5 \), and verify the accepted root(s).

Q2 (Exponential):

Solve \(3^{2x}-10\cdot3^x+9=0\).

Q3 (Circle):

Find the tangent to \(x^2+y^2=25\) at \((3,4)\).

Q4 (Functions):

If \(f(x)=2x+1\), \(g(x)=\sqrt{x-2}\), find \((g\circ f)(x)\) and its domain.

Q5 (Absolute value):

Solve \( |2x-1|<|x+4| \).

Q6 (Parabola):

Find \(k\) such that \(y=x^2+kx+9\) has vertex on the line \(x=2\).

Q7 (Hyperbola):

For \(y=4-\dfrac{8}{x-1}\), find the point where the graph meets the x-axis.

Q8 (Lines):

Find the acute angle between \(2x-y+3=0\) and \(x+2y-4=0\).

Day 8

Focus: Log equations with fractional shifts + advanced coordinate review
Q1 (Log):

Solve \( \log_5(x+1)+\log_5(x-3)=1+\log_5 2 \), showing domain first.

Q2 (Exponential):

Solve \(5^{x+1}=2^{2x}\).

Q3 (Circle):

Find the circle through \((4,0)\), \((0,4)\), and \((0,0)\).

Q4 (Functions):

Let \(f(x)=|x-1|\), \(g(x)=x^2-4\). Solve \((f\circ g)(x)=0\).

Q5 (Absolute value):

Solve \( |x-2|\ge 2x-5 \).

Q6 (Parabola):

If \(y=ax^2+bx+4\) has roots \(x=1\) and \(x=4\), find \(a\) and \(b\).

Q7 (Hyperbola):

For \(y=1+\dfrac{k}{x-2}\), given it passes through \((4,5)\), find \(k\) and the y-intercept.

Q8 (Lines):

Find the distance between parallel lines \(3x-4y+7=0\) and \(3x-4y-9=0\).

Day 9

Focus: Two-sided log expressions + heavy mixed algebraic geometry
Q1 (Log):

Solve \( \log_3(2x-1)+\log_3(x-2)=\log_3(4x-5) \), with explicit domain filtering.

Q2 (Exponential):

Solve \(2^{x+2}+2^x=40\).

Q3 (Circle):

Find equation of the circle with centre \((1,-2)\) tangent to the line \(3x+4y+1=0\).

Q4 (Functions):

Given \(f(x)=x^2-1\), find \(f^{-1}(x)\) after restricting domain of \(f\) to \(x\ge0\).

Q5 (Absolute value):

Solve \( |x-4|+|2x+1|=9 \).

Q6 (Parabola + discriminant):

Find all \(k\) such that \(x^2-2(k+1)x+k^2-1=0\) has two distinct real roots.

Q7 (Hyperbola):

For \(y=-3+\dfrac{12}{x-4}\), find all points where \(y=0\) and \(y=3\).

Q8 (Lines):

Find the line that passes through the intersection of \(x+y=5\), \(2x-y=1\), and has gradient \(-3\).

Day 10

Focus: Exam-style nontrivial logs + final cumulative challenge set
Q1 (Log):

Solve \( \log_2(x^2-5x+6)=\log_2 x \), showing all domain restrictions and rejecting invalid roots.

Q2 (Exponential):

Solve \(9^x-10\cdot3^x+9=0\).

Q3 (Circle):

Find the equation of the circle passing through \((2,1)\), \((6,1)\), \((4,5)\).

Q4 (Functions):

If \(f(x)=\dfrac{x+1}{x-2}\), solve \(f(x)=3\) and state the domain/range of \(f\).

Q5 (Absolute value):

Solve \( |x-1|=|3x+5| \).

Q6 (Parabola):

Find the equation of a parabola with roots \(x=-1\) and \(x=5\), passing through \((0,-10)\).

Q7 (Hyperbola):

For \(y=2+\dfrac{m}{x+1}\), find \(m\) if the graph passes through \((3,1)\), then give asymptotes and centre.

Q8 (Lines):

Find the equation of the perpendicular bisector of the segment joining \(A(1,4)\) and \(B(7,-2)\).

Day 6

  • Q1: Domain \(x>4\). \((x-1)(x-4)=9 \Rightarrow x^2-5x-5=0\). Valid root: \(x=\frac{5+3\sqrt5}{2}\).
  • Q2: Let \(u=e^x\): \(u^2-5u+6=0\Rightarrow u=2,3\). So \(x=\ln2\) or \(x=\ln3\).
  • Q3: Centre \((5,2)\), radius \(3\sqrt2\), equation \((x-5)^2+(y-2)^2=18\).
  • Q4: \((f\circ g)(x)=\dfrac{1}{x^2-5}\), domain \(x\neq\pm\sqrt5\).
  • Q5: \(x\le -2\) or \(x\ge 4\).
  • Q6: \(y=(x-3)^2+4\), vertex \((3,4)\), minimum \(4\), axis \(x=3\).
  • Q7: Centre \((-2,-1)\), asymptotes \(x=-2,\ y=-1\), x-int \((4,0)\), y-int \((0,2)\).
  • Q8: Required gradient \(=\frac34\), line \(3x-4y-17=0\).

Day 7

  • Q1: Domain \(x>1\). \(\dfrac{x+3}{x-1}=5\Rightarrow x=2\) (valid).
  • Q2: Let \(u=3^x\): \(u^2-10u+9=0\Rightarrow u=1,9\). So \(x=0,2\).
  • Q3: Radius to \((3,4)\) has slope \(\frac43\), tangent slope \(-\frac34\). Equation: \(3x+4y-25=0\).
  • Q4: \((g\circ f)(x)=\sqrt{2x-1}\), domain \(x\ge\frac12\).
  • Q5: \((2x-1)^2<(x+4)^2\Rightarrow x\in\left(-1,\frac53\right)\).
  • Q6: Vertex x-coordinate is \(-\frac{k}{2}=2\Rightarrow k=-4\).
  • Q7: Set \(y=0\): \(0=4-\frac{8}{x-1}\Rightarrow x=3\). Point \((3,0)\).
  • Q8: Slopes \(m_1=2,\ m_2=-\frac12\). \(\tan\theta=\left|\frac{m_1-m_2}{1+m_1m_2}\right|\) undefined \(\Rightarrow \theta=90^\circ\).

Day 8

  • Q1: Domain \(x>3\). \((x+1)(x-3)=10\Rightarrow x^2-2x-13=0\). Valid root: \(x=1+\sqrt{14}\).
  • Q2: \(5\cdot5^x=4^x\Rightarrow \left(\frac45\right)^x=5\Rightarrow x=\frac{\ln 5}{\ln(4/5)}\).
  • Q3: \((x-2)^2+(y-2)^2=8\).
  • Q4: \(|x^2-5|=0\Rightarrow x=\pm\sqrt5\).
  • Q5: Always true for \(x\ge\frac52\). For \(x<\frac52\), solve \(x-2\le 5-2x\Rightarrow x\le\frac73\). Final: \(x\le\frac73\) or \(x\ge\frac52\).
  • Q6: \(y=a(x-1)(x-4)\). Using \((0,4)\): \(4a=4\Rightarrow a=1\). So \(b=-5\).
  • Q7: \(5=1+\frac{k}{2}\Rightarrow k=8\). y-intercept at \(x=0\): \(y=1+\frac8{-2}=-3\), so \((0,-3)\).
  • Q8: Distance \(=\frac{|7-(-9)|}{\sqrt{3^2+(-4)^2}}=\frac{16}{5}\).

Day 9

  • Q1: Domain \(x>2\). \((2x-1)(x-2)=4x-5\Rightarrow 2x^2-9x+7=0\Rightarrow x=1,\frac72\). Valid root: \(x=\frac72\).
  • Q2: \(4\cdot2^x+2^x=40\Rightarrow 5\cdot2^x=40\Rightarrow 2^x=8\Rightarrow x=3\).
  • Q3: Radius \(=\) distance from centre to line \(=\frac{|3(1)+4(-2)+1|}{5}=\frac45\). Equation \((x-1)^2+(y+2)^2=\frac{16}{25}\).
  • Q4: \(y=x^2-1\), \(x\ge0\Rightarrow x=\sqrt{y+1}\). Hence \(f^{-1}(x)=\sqrt{x+1}\), domain \(x\ge-1\).
  • Q5: Breakpoints at \(-\frac12\) and \(4\). Solutions: \(x=-\frac{14}{3}\) or \(x=4\).
  • Q6: Discriminant \(D=[-2(k+1)]^2-4(k^2-1)=8k+8\). For two distinct real roots: \(D>0\Rightarrow k>-1\).
  • Q7: For \(y=0\): \(x=8\). For \(y=3\): \(3=-3+\frac{12}{x-4}\Rightarrow x=6\). Points \((8,0)\), \((6,3)\).
  • Q8: Intersection of lines is \((2,3)\). With gradient \(-3\): \(y-3=-3(x-2)\Rightarrow 3x+y-9=0\).

Day 10

  • Q1: Domain from logs: \(x>0,\ x\neq2,3\). Equation gives \(x^2-5x+6=x\Rightarrow x^2-6x+6=0\Rightarrow x=3\pm\sqrt3\). Both satisfy domain.
  • Q2: Let \(u=3^x\): \(u^2-10u+9=0\Rightarrow u=1,9\). So \(x=0,2\).
  • Q3: By symmetry centre \((4,2)\), radius squared \(=5\). Equation \((x-4)^2+(y-2)^2=5\).
  • Q4: \( \frac{x+1}{x-2}=3\Rightarrow x=\frac72\). Domain \(x\neq2\). Range \(y\neq1\).
  • Q5: \((x-1)^2=(3x+5)^2\Rightarrow x=-2\) or \(x=-1\).
  • Q6: \(y=a(x+1)(x-5)\). Use \((0,-10)\): \(-5a=-10\Rightarrow a=2\). So \(y=2(x+1)(x-5)\).
  • Q7: \(1=2+\frac{m}{4}\Rightarrow m=-4\). Asymptotes \(x=-1,\ y=2\), centre \((-1,2)\).
  • Q8: Midpoint \(M(4,1)\). Slope \(AB=-1\), so perpendicular slope \(1\). Equation \(y-1=x-4\Rightarrow y=x-3\).
