195 minutes, two parts, and a scoring rule that rewards you for being confident about the parts you already know.
The Final Examination isn't automatically compulsory in every part — the subject outline gives you real flexibility here, and understanding exactly how that flexibility works can save you significant exam-period stress.

Note The pass requirement, and why it matters here ▶
Critical rule: you need a minimum of 24.5/70 (35%) across your combined examination component (the best-of-each-part total) to pass MATH142 — regardless of your assignment and workshop marks. Falling short caps your grade at a Technical Fail.

This is exactly why the subject outline stresses aiming to do well in every assessment component, not just enough to scrape by on non-exam marks — the exam floor is a hard requirement, separate from your overall percentage.

Trig integrals — even powers$\sin^2x=\dfrac{1-\cos2x}{2}$, $\cos^2x=\dfrac{1+\cos2x}{2}$
Trig substitution$\sqrt{a^2-x^2}\to x=a\sin\theta$; $\sqrt{a^2+x^2}\to x=a\tan\theta$; $\sqrt{x^2-a^2}\to x=a\sec\theta$
Partial fractionslinear: $\dfrac{A}{x-a}$; repeated: $\sum\dfrac{A_k}{(x-a)^k}$; irreducible quadratic: $\dfrac{Bx+C}{x^2+bx+c}$
Improper integrals$\int_a^\infty f\,dx=\lim_{t\to\infty}\int_a^t f\,dx$; converges iff the limit is finite
Reference Phase 1 — Numerical Integration & Polar (Weeks 3–4) ▶
Trapezoidal Rule$T_n=\dfrac{h}{2}[f(x_0)+2f(x_1)+\cdots+2f(x_{n-1})+f(x_n)]$, error $\propto h^2$
Simpson's Rule$S_n=\dfrac{h}{3}[f(x_0)+4f(x_1)+2f(x_2)+\cdots+f(x_n)]$, $n$ even, error $\propto h^4$
Polar conversion$x=r\cos\theta,\ y=r\sin\theta$; $r^2=x^2+y^2,\ \tan\theta=y/x$
Rose petals$r=a\cos(n\theta)$: $n$ odd → $n$ petals; $n$ even → $2n$ petals

Polar area$A=\dfrac12\int_\alpha^\beta r^2\,d\theta$
Disk/washer volume$V=\pi\int_a^b\big([R(x)]^2-[r(x)]^2\big)dx$

Reference Phase 2 — First-Order Differential Equations (Week 7–8) ▶
Separable DEs$\dfrac{dy}{dx}=g(x)h(y) \implies \int\dfrac{1}{h(y)}dy=\int g(x)dx$
Exponential growth/decay$y=y_0e^{kt}$
Newton's Law of Cooling$T=T_a+(T_0-T_a)e^{-kt}$
Integrating factor$y'+p(x)y=q(x)$: $\mu(x)=e^{\int p(x)dx}$, then $y=\dfrac{1}{\mu}\left[\int \mu q\,dx+C\right]$

SHM$x''+\omega^2x=0 \implies x=C_1\cos\omega t+C_2\sin\omega t$, $\omega=\sqrt{k/m}$
Reference Phase 3 — Limits & L'Hôpital's Rule (Week 10) ▶
Indeterminate forms$\frac00,\ \frac{\infty}{\infty},\ 0\cdot\infty,\ \infty-\infty,\ 0^0,\ 1^\infty,\ \infty^0$

Reference Phase 3 — Sequences & Series Convergence Tests (Week 11) ▶
Geometric series$\sum_{n=0}^\infty ar^n=\dfrac{a}{1-r}$ ($|r|<1$)
Divergence Test$\lim a_n\ne0 \implies$ series diverges
Integral Test$\sum a_n$ converges $\iff \int_1^\infty f(x)dx$ converges
Comparison / Limit Comparison$0\le a_n\le b_n$; or $L=\lim\dfrac{a_n}{b_n}\in(0,\infty)$ → same behaviour
Ratio / Root Test$L=\lim\left|\dfrac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}\right|$ or $L=\lim\sqrt[n]{|a_n|}$: $L<1$ conv., $L>1$ div., $L=1$ inconclusive

Integration
Techniques & Applications
Phase 1 core skills, mixed
1
Find $\int x\sin(2x)\,dx$.
Integration
▶
Solution

$u=x,dv=\sin2x\,dx$: $-\dfrac{x\cos2x}{2}+\dfrac{\sin2x}{4}+C$

2
Decompose $\dfrac{2x+1}{(x-1)(x+2)}$ into partial fractions.
Integration
▶
Solution

At $x=1$: $3=3A\implies A=1$; at $x=-2$: $-3=-3B\implies B=1$: $\dfrac{1}{x-1}+\dfrac{1}{x+2}$

3
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int_1^\infty \dfrac{1}{x^3}\,dx$.
Integration
▶
Solution

$\lim_{t\to\infty}\left[-\dfrac{1}{2x^2}\right]_1^t=\dfrac12$ — converges

4
Estimate $\int_0^2 x^2\,dx$ using Simpson's Rule with $n=2$.
Integration
▶
Solution

$h=1$; $f=0,1,4$; $S_2=\dfrac13[0+4+4]=\dfrac83$ — exact, since $x^2$ is a parabola

5
Convert $(2,2\sqrt3)$ to polar coordinates.
Integration
▶
Solution

$r=4$; $\tan\theta=\sqrt3\implies\theta=\pi/3$: $(4,\pi/3)$

6
Find the area enclosed by $r=2\cos\theta$.
Integration
▶
Solution

$A=\dfrac12\int_{-\pi/2}^{\pi/2}4\cos^2\theta\,d\theta=\pi$

7
Find the arc length of $y=x^{3/2}$ from $x=0$ to $x=1$.
Integration
▶
Solution

$f'(x)=\tfrac32\sqrt x$; $L=\int_0^1\sqrt{1+\tfrac94x}\,dx=\left[\tfrac{8}{27}(1+\tfrac94x)^{3/2}\right]_0^1=\tfrac{8}{27}\left(\left(\tfrac{13}{4}\right)^{3/2}-1\right)$

8
Find $\int \dfrac{1}{x^2-4}\,dx$ using partial fractions.
Integration
▶
Solution

$\dfrac{1}{(x-2)(x+2)}=\dfrac{1/4}{x-2}-\dfrac{1/4}{x+2}$: $\dfrac14\ln\left|\dfrac{x-2}{x+2}\right|+C$

9
Estimate $\int_0^1 e^{x^2}\,dx$ using the Trapezoidal Rule with $n=2$.
Integration
▶
Solution

$h=0.5$; $f(0),f(0.5),f(1)=1,1.284,2.718$; $T_2=0.25[1+2.568+2.718]=1.5715$

10
Find the area between $r=3$ and $r=1+2\cos\theta$ (identify intersection angles only, do not evaluate).
Integration
▶
Solution

$3=1+2\cos\theta\implies\cos\theta=1\implies\theta=0$ — the curves only touch at $\theta=0$, so intersection setup needs care; typically such problems specify a bounded region between two crossing angles rather than a single touch point

11
Find the volume when $y=\sin x$, $0\le x\le\pi$, is rotated about the $y$-axis using shells.
Integration
▶
Solution

$V=2\pi\int_0^\pi x\sin x\,dx$; by parts: $u=x,dv=\sin x\,dx$: $2\pi[-x\cos x+\sin x]_0^\pi=2\pi(\pi)=2\pi^2$

Differential Equations
First & Second Order
Phase 2 core skills, mixed
12
Solve $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=2xy$.
Differential Equations
▶
Solution

Separate: $\dfrac1y dy=2x\,dx \implies \ln|y|=x^2+C_1 \implies y=Ce^{x^2}$

13
Solve $y'+2y=10$.
Differential Equations
▶
Solution

$\mu=e^{2x}$: $\dfrac{d}{dx}[e^{2x}y]=10e^{2x} \implies y=5+Ce^{-2x}$

14
Solve $y''-y'-2y=0$.
Differential Equations
▶
Solution

$r^2-r-2=(r-2)(r+1)=0$: $y=C_1e^{2x}+C_2e^{-x}$

15
Solve $y''+4y'+4y=0$.
Differential Equations
▶
Solution

$(r+2)^2=0\implies r=-2$: $y=(C_1+C_2x)e^{-2x}$

16
A spring-mass system satisfies $x''+25x=0$, $x(0)=1$, $x'(0)=0$. Find $x(t)$.
Differential Equations
▶
Solution

$\omega=5$: $x(0)=C_1=1$; $x'(0)=5C_2=0\implies C_2=0$: $x=\cos5t$

17
Solve $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=y^2$, $y(0)=1$ (careful: this is separable but the solution isn't exponential).
Differential Equations
▶
Solution

$\dfrac{1}{y^2}dy=dx \implies -\dfrac1y=x+C$; at $x=0,y=1$: $C=-1$: $y=\dfrac{1}{1-x}$

18
A tank has volume $V(t)=50+t$ with outflow rate 1L/min; brine at 2g/L flows in at 2L/min. Write the DE for $A(t)$ (do not solve).
Differential Equations
▶
Solution

$\dfrac{dA}{dt}=2(2)-1\cdot\dfrac{A}{50+t}=4-\dfrac{A}{50+t}$

19
Solve $y''-6y'+9y=0$.
Differential Equations
▶
Solution

$(r-3)^2=0\implies r=3$: $y=(C_1+C_2x)e^{3x}$

20
Solve $y''+y=0$, $y(\pi/2)=1$, $y'(\pi/2)=0$.
Differential Equations
▶
Solution

$y=C_1\cos x+C_2\sin x$; $y(\pi/2)=C_2=1$; $y'=-C_1\sin x+C_2\cos x$, $y'(\pi/2)=-C_1=0\implies C_1=0$: $y=\sin x$

Limits & Series

Find $\lim_{n\to\infty}\dfrac{3n+2}{5n-1}$.
Limits & Series

$\to\dfrac35$

25
Write the first three terms of the Maclaurin series for $\cos(2x)$.
Limits & Series
▶
Solution

$1-\dfrac{(2x)^2}{2}+\dfrac{(2x)^4}{24}=1-2x^2+\dfrac{2x^4}{3}$

26
Evaluate $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\dfrac{x^3}{e^x}$ using repeated L'H\u00f4pital's Rule.
Limits & Series
▶
Solution

$\dfrac{\infty}{\infty}$ three times: $\lim\dfrac{3x^2}{e^x}\to\lim\dfrac{6x}{e^x}\to\lim\dfrac{6}{e^x}=0$

27
Apply the Limit Comparison Test to $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^\infty \dfrac{1}{n^2+n}$ against $\dfrac{1}{n^2}$.
Limits & Series
▶
Solution

$L=\lim\dfrac{n^2}{n^2+n}=1$, finite and positive; $\sum1/n^2$ converges, so this series converges too

28
Find the radius of convergence of $\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^\infty \dfrac{(x+1)^n}{3^n}$.
Limits & Series
▶
Solution

$L=\lim\left|\dfrac{(x+1)^{n+1}/3^{n+1}}{(x+1)^n/3^n}\right|=\dfrac{|x+1|}{3}<1\implies|x+1|<3$: $R=3$

29
Write the Maclaurin series for $\dfrac{1}{1+x^2}$ using the known series for $\dfrac{1}{1-x}$ (substitute $-x^2$).
Limits & Series
▶
Solution

$\sum_{n=0}^\infty(-x^2)^n=\sum_{n=0}^\infty(-1)^nx^{2n}$

Cross-Phase Challenge
Combining Integration, DEs, and Series
The kind of question the Final Exam actually tests
30
Solve the separable DE $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=\dfrac{x}{y}\cos(x^2)$, requiring an integration technique from Phase 1 to finish (u-substitution).
Cross-Phase Challenge
▶
Solution

$y\,dy=x\cos(x^2)dx$; let $u=x^2$, $du=2x\,dx$: $\int y\,dy=\tfrac12\int\cos u\,du \implies \tfrac{y^2}{2}=\tfrac12\sin(x^2)+C \implies y^2=\sin(x^2)+C'$

31
A second-order DE $y''+9y=0$ has solution $y=C_1\cos3x+C_2\sin3x$. Find the Maclaurin series for this solution's first three terms if $C_1=1,C_2=0$ (i.e. for $\cos3x$), using the known cosine series.
Cross-Phase Challenge
▶
Solution

Substitute $3x$ for $x$ in $\cos x=1-\tfrac{x^2}{2}+\tfrac{x^4}{24}-\cdots$: $\cos3x=1-\tfrac{9x^2}{2}+\tfrac{81x^4}{24}-\cdots$

32
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int_1^\infty \dfrac{1}{x^2}e^{-1/x}\,dx$ using substitution (Phase 1) and then determine, using the improper integral result, whether $\sum_{n=1}^\infty \dfrac{1}{n^2}e^{-1/n}$ converges (Phase 3, Integral Test).
Cross-Phase Challenge
▶
Solution

Let $u=-1/x$, $du=\tfrac{1}{x^2}dx$: $\int e^u du = e^{-1/x}$, evaluated $\lim_{t\to\infty}[e^{-1/x}]_1^t = 1-e^{-1}$, finite — the integral converges, so by the Integral Test the corresponding series converges too

33
A population satisfies the logistic-like separable DE $\dfrac{dP}{dt}=kP$ with $P(0)=100$, $k=0.05$. Use L'H\u00f4pital's Rule to evaluate $\displaystyle\lim_{t\to\infty}\dfrac{P(t)}{e^{0.05t}}$, confirming it matches the solution's coefficient.
Cross-Phase Challenge
▶
Solution

$P(t)=100e^{0.05t}$ (from separating and solving); $\dfrac{P(t)}{e^{0.05t}}=100$ for all $t$ — the limit is trivially $100$ (no indeterminate form actually arises here, since both grow at the exact same rate), confirming $P(t)$'s leading coefficient directly

34
Determine whether $\displaystyle\int_1^\infty \dfrac{\ln x}{x^2}\,dx$ converges, using integration by parts (Phase 1) inside an improper integral (also Phase 1) — this result also underlies a series convergence question via the Integral Test (Phase 3).
Cross-Phase Challenge
▶
Solution

By parts, $u=\ln x,dv=x^{-2}dx$: $\int\dfrac{\ln x}{x^2}dx=-\dfrac{\ln x}{x}+\int\dfrac{1}{x^2}dx=-\dfrac{\ln x}{x}-\dfrac1x+C$; evaluating the limit as $t\to\infty$ of $\left[-\dfrac{\ln x}{x}-\dfrac1x\right]_1^t \to (0-0)-(0-1)=1$ — converges, and by the Integral Test $\sum\dfrac{\ln n}{n^2}$ converges too
