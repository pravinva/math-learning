TOPIC 4.1
Angle of Inclination
Explore the relationship between the gradient of a line and the angle it makes with the positive x-axis.
DEFINITION Angle of Inclination

The angle of inclination of a line is the angle \( \theta \) measured anti-clockwise from the positive x-axis to the line.

The angle of inclination is always in the range \( 0° \leq \theta \leq 180° \).

\( 0° \leq \theta \leq 180° \)
PATTERN Gradient and Angle Relationship

The gradient \( m \) of a line is related to its angle of inclination \( \theta \) by the formula:

\( m = \tan \theta \)

This means:

  • If \( m > 0 \), then \( 0° < \theta < 90° \) (acute angle)
  • If \( m < 0 \), then \( 90° < \theta < 180° \) (obtuse angle)
  • If \( m = 0 \), then \( \theta = 0° \) (horizontal line)
  • If the line is vertical, then \( \theta = 90° \) (gradient undefined)
EXAMPLE Finding the Angle of Inclination

Question: Find the angle of inclination of the line passing through the points \( A(1, 2) \) and \( B(4, 8) \).

Solution:

Step 1: Find the gradient using \( m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} \)

\( m = \frac{8 - 2}{4 - 1} = \frac{6}{3} = 2 \)

Step 2: Use \( m = \tan \theta \) to find \( \theta \)

\( 2 = \tan \theta \)

\( \theta = \tan^{-1}(2) \)

\( \theta \approx 63.4° \)

Answer: The angle of inclination is approximately \( 63.4° \).

NOTE Working with Obtuse Angles

When the gradient is negative, the angle of inclination is obtuse (between 90° and 180°).

For example, if \( m = -1 \), then:

\( \tan \theta = -1 \)

Your calculator will give \( \theta = -45° \), but we need \( 0° \leq \theta \leq 180° \).

So we add 180°: \( \theta = -45° + 180° = 135° \)

General rule: If your calculator gives a negative angle, add 180° to get the angle of inclination.

1
Find the angle of inclination of the line passing through \( A(2, 3) \) and \( B(5, 9) \).
HINT: First find the gradient using \( m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} \), then use \( \theta = \tan^{-1}(m) \).
SOLUTION

Step 1: Find the gradient

\( m = \frac{9 - 3}{5 - 2} = \frac{6}{3} = 2 \)

Step 2: Find the angle

\( \theta = \tan^{-1}(2) \approx 63.4° \)

Answer: \( \theta \approx 63.4° \)

2
A line has an angle of inclination of \( 30° \). What is its gradient?
HINT: Use the formula \( m = \tan \theta \).
SOLUTION

\( m = \tan(30°) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \approx 0.577 \)

Answer: \( m = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \approx 0.577 \)

3
Find the angle of inclination of the line passing through \( P(-2, 5) \) and \( Q(3, -1) \).
HINT: The gradient will be negative, so the angle will be obtuse. Remember to add 180° to the calculator result.
SOLUTION

Step 1: Find the gradient

\( m = \frac{-1 - 5}{3 - (-2)} = \frac{-6}{5} = -1.2 \)

Step 2: Find the angle

\( \tan \theta = -1.2 \)

Calculator gives: \( \theta \approx -50.2° \)

Since this is negative, add 180°: \( \theta = -50.2° + 180° = 129.8° \)

Answer: \( \theta \approx 129.8° \)

4
A line has gradient \( m = \sqrt{3} \). Find its angle of inclination.
HINT: This is a standard angle. Think about special triangles.
SOLUTION

\( m = \tan \theta = \sqrt{3} \)

\( \theta = \tan^{-1}(\sqrt{3}) = 60° \)

Answer: \( \theta = 60° \)

5
Find the equation of the line passing through \( (2, 5) \) with an angle of inclination of \( 45° \).
HINT: First find the gradient using \( m = \tan(45°) \), then use the point-gradient form \( y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \).
SOLUTION

Step 1: Find the gradient

\( m = \tan(45°) = 1 \)

Step 2: Use point-gradient form

\( y - 5 = 1(x - 2) \)

\( y - 5 = x - 2 \)

\( y = x + 3 \)

Answer: \( y = x + 3 \)

6
A line has an angle of inclination of \( 120° \). What is its gradient?
HINT: Use \( m = \tan(120°) \). The gradient will be negative since the angle is obtuse.
SOLUTION

\( m = \tan(120°) = -\sqrt{3} \approx -1.732 \)

Answer: \( m = -\sqrt{3} \approx -1.732 \)

7
Find the angle of inclination of the line \( 2y - 3x + 6 = 0 \).
HINT: First rearrange to slope-intercept form \( y = mx + c \) to find the gradient.
SOLUTION

Step 1: Rearrange to find gradient

\( 2y - 3x + 6 = 0 \)

\( 2y = 3x - 6 \)

\( y = \frac{3}{2}x - 3 \)

So \( m = \frac{3}{2} = 1.5 \)

Step 2: Find the angle

\( \theta = \tan^{-1}(1.5) \approx 56.3° \)

Answer: \( \theta \approx 56.3° \)

8
Two lines have angles of inclination \( 30° \) and \( 60° \). Find the product of their gradients.
HINT: Find each gradient separately using \( m = \tan \theta \), then multiply them.
SOLUTION

Step 1: Find gradient of first line

\( m_1 = \tan(30°) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \)

Step 2: Find gradient of second line

\( m_2 = \tan(60°) = \sqrt{3} \)

Step 3: Find the product

\( m_1 \times m_2 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \times \sqrt{3} = 1 \)

Answer: The product is \( 1 \)

9
A line passes through the origin and has an angle of inclination of \( 135° \). Write its equation.
HINT: Find the gradient first. Since the line passes through the origin, \( c = 0 \) in \( y = mx + c \).
SOLUTION

Step 1: Find the gradient

\( m = \tan(135°) = -1 \)

Step 2: Write the equation

Since it passes through the origin: \( y = mx \)

\( y = -x \)

Answer: \( y = -x \) or \( x + y = 0 \)

10
Find the angle of inclination of the line passing through \( M(0, 4) \) and \( N(4, 0) \).
HINT: This line has a negative gradient. Don't forget to add 180° to your calculator result.
SOLUTION

Step 1: Find the gradient

\( m = \frac{0 - 4}{4 - 0} = \frac{-4}{4} = -1 \)

Step 2: Find the angle

\( \tan \theta = -1 \)

Calculator gives: \( \theta = -45° \)

Add 180°: \( \theta = -45° + 180° = 135° \)

Answer: \( \theta = 135° \)

11
A line has gradient \( m = -\frac{1}{2} \). Is its angle of inclination acute or obtuse? Find the exact value.
HINT: Since the gradient is negative, the angle is obtuse (between 90° and 180°).
SOLUTION

Since \( m = -\frac{1}{2} < 0 \), the angle is obtuse.

\( \tan \theta = -\frac{1}{2} \)

Calculator gives: \( \theta \approx -26.6° \)

Add 180°: \( \theta = -26.6° + 180° = 153.4° \)

Answer: Obtuse, \( \theta \approx 153.4° \)

12
Find the equation of the line with angle of inclination \( 150° \) passing through \( (3, -2) \).
HINT: First find \( m = \tan(150°) \), then use point-gradient form.
SOLUTION

Step 1: Find the gradient

\( m = \tan(150°) = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \approx -0.577 \)

Step 2: Use point-gradient form

\( y - (-2) = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(x - 3) \)

\( y + 2 = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(x - 3) \)

\( y = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}x + \frac{3}{\sqrt{3}} - 2 \)

\( y = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}x + \sqrt{3} - 2 \)

Answer: \( y = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}x + \sqrt{3} - 2 \) or \( y \approx -0.577x - 0.268 \)

TOPIC 4.2
Collinear Points
Learn how to determine whether three or more points lie on the same straight line.
DEFINITION Collinear Points

Three or more points are said to be collinear if they all lie on the same straight line.

To test if points are collinear, we check whether the gradients between consecutive pairs of points are equal.

PATTERN Testing for Collinearity

For three points \( A \), \( B \), and \( C \) to be collinear:

\( m_{AB} = m_{BC} = m_{AC} \)

You only need to show that two of these gradients are equal.

For example, if \( m_{AB} = m_{BC} \), then \( A \), \( B \), and \( C \) are collinear.

Remember: Use the gradient formula \( m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} \)

EXAMPLE Testing if Points are Collinear

Question: Determine whether the points \( A(1, 2) \), \( B(3, 6) \), and \( C(5, 10) \) are collinear.

Solution:

Step 1: Find the gradient from A to B

\( m_{AB} = \frac{6 - 2}{3 - 1} = \frac{4}{2} = 2 \)

Step 2: Find the gradient from B to C

\( m_{BC} = \frac{10 - 6}{5 - 3} = \frac{4}{2} = 2 \)

Step 3: Compare the gradients

Since \( m_{AB} = m_{BC} = 2 \), the points are collinear.

NOTE Finding Unknown Coordinates

You can use collinearity to find unknown coordinates.

Example: If \( A(2, 3) \), \( B(4, 7) \), and \( C(6, k) \) are collinear, find \( k \).

Solution:

Since the points are collinear: \( m_{AB} = m_{BC} \)

\( \frac{7 - 3}{4 - 2} = \frac{k - 7}{6 - 4} \)

\( \frac{4}{2} = \frac{k - 7}{2} \)

\( 2 = \frac{k - 7}{2} \)

\( 4 = k - 7 \)

\( k = 11 \)

Practice Problems
1
Are the points \( A(1, 3) \), \( B(2, 5) \), and \( C(4, 9) \) collinear?
HINT: Find \( m_{AB} \) and \( m_{BC} \). If they're equal, the points are collinear.
SOLUTION

Step 1: Find \( m_{AB} \)

\( m_{AB} = \frac{5 - 3}{2 - 1} = \frac{2}{1} = 2 \)

Step 2: Find \( m_{BC} \)

\( m_{BC} = \frac{9 - 5}{4 - 2} = \frac{4}{2} = 2 \)

Step 3: Compare

Since \( m_{AB} = m_{BC} = 2 \), the points are collinear.

2
Are the points \( P(0, 1) \), \( Q(2, 4) \), and \( R(5, 8) \) collinear?
HINT: Calculate both gradients carefully and compare them.
SOLUTION

Step 1: Find \( m_{PQ} \)

\( m_{PQ} = \frac{4 - 1}{2 - 0} = \frac{3}{2} = 1.5 \)

Step 2: Find \( m_{QR} \)

\( m_{QR} = \frac{8 - 4}{5 - 2} = \frac{4}{3} \approx 1.33 \)

Step 3: Compare

Since \( m_{PQ} \neq m_{QR} \), the points are NOT collinear.

3
If \( A(1, 2) \), \( B(3, 6) \), and \( C(5, k) \) are collinear, find the value of \( k \).
HINT: Set \( m_{AB} = m_{BC} \) and solve for \( k \).
SOLUTION

Step 1: Find \( m_{AB} \)

\( m_{AB} = \frac{6 - 2}{3 - 1} = \frac{4}{2} = 2 \)

Step 2: Set up equation

Since collinear: \( m_{BC} = m_{AB} \)

\( \frac{k - 6}{5 - 3} = 2 \)

\( \frac{k - 6}{2} = 2 \)

\( k - 6 = 4 \)

\( k = 10 \)

Answer: \( k = 10 \)

4
The points \( M(-2, 5) \), \( N(0, 1) \), and \( P(3, y) \) are collinear. Find \( y \).
HINT: First find the gradient from M to N, then use it to find y.
SOLUTION

Step 1: Find \( m_{MN} \)

\( m_{MN} = \frac{1 - 5}{0 - (-2)} = \frac{-4}{2} = -2 \)

Step 2: Set up equation

\( m_{NP} = m_{MN} \)

\( \frac{y - 1}{3 - 0} = -2 \)

\( \frac{y - 1}{3} = -2 \)

\( y - 1 = -6 \)

\( y = -5 \)

Answer: \( y = -5 \)

5
Show that the points \( A(2, -1) \), \( B(5, 5) \), and \( C(6, 7) \) are collinear.
HINT: Calculate two gradients and show they are equal.
SOLUTION

Step 1: Find \( m_{AB} \)

\( m_{AB} = \frac{5 - (-1)}{5 - 2} = \frac{6}{3} = 2 \)

Step 2: Find \( m_{BC} \)

\( m_{BC} = \frac{7 - 5}{6 - 5} = \frac{2}{1} = 2 \)

Conclusion: Since \( m_{AB} = m_{BC} = 2 \), the points are collinear. ✓

6
Are the points \( (0, 0) \), \( (3, -6) \), and \( (-2, 4) \) collinear?
HINT: One of the points is the origin, which makes calculations simpler.
SOLUTION

Let \( A(0, 0) \), \( B(3, -6) \), \( C(-2, 4) \)

Step 1: Find \( m_{AB} \)

\( m_{AB} = \frac{-6 - 0}{3 - 0} = \frac{-6}{3} = -2 \)

Step 2: Find \( m_{BC} \)

\( m_{BC} = \frac{4 - (-6)}{-2 - 3} = \frac{10}{-5} = -2 \)

Step 3: Conclusion

Since \( m_{AB} = m_{BC} = -2 \), the points are collinear.

7
If \( P(a, 2) \), \( Q(3, 5) \), and \( R(5, 7) \) are collinear, find \( a \).
HINT: First find the gradient from Q to R, then use it to find a.
SOLUTION

Step 1: Find \( m_{QR} \)

\( m_{QR} = \frac{7 - 5}{5 - 3} = \frac{2}{2} = 1 \)

Step 2: Set up equation

\( m_{PQ} = m_{QR} \)

\( \frac{5 - 2}{3 - a} = 1 \)

\( \frac{3}{3 - a} = 1 \)

\( 3 = 3 - a \)

\( a = 0 \)

Answer: \( a = 0 \)

8
Four points \( A(1, 2) \), \( B(2, 4) \), \( C(3, 6) \), and \( D(5, 10) \) are given. Are all four points collinear?
HINT: For four points to be collinear, all consecutive pairs must have the same gradient.
SOLUTION

Step 1: Find \( m_{AB} \)

\( m_{AB} = \frac{4 - 2}{2 - 1} = \frac{2}{1} = 2 \)

Step 2: Find \( m_{BC} \)

\( m_{BC} = \frac{6 - 4}{3 - 2} = \frac{2}{1} = 2 \)

Step 3: Find \( m_{CD} \)

\( m_{CD} = \frac{10 - 6}{5 - 3} = \frac{4}{2} = 2 \)

Conclusion: Since all gradients equal 2, all four points are collinear.

9
The points \( A(2, 1) \), \( B(x, 4) \), and \( C(8, 7) \) lie on a straight line. Find the value of \( x \).
HINT: Use \( m_{AB} = m_{AC} \) to set up an equation.
SOLUTION

Step 1: Find \( m_{AC} \)

\( m_{AC} = \frac{7 - 1}{8 - 2} = \frac{6}{6} = 1 \)

Step 2: Set up equation

\( m_{AB} = m_{AC} \)

\( \frac{4 - 1}{x - 2} = 1 \)

\( \frac{3}{x - 2} = 1 \)

\( 3 = x - 2 \)

\( x = 5 \)

Answer: \( x = 5 \)

10
Prove that the points \( (3, 8) \), \( (5, 2) \), and \( (6, -1) \) are collinear.
HINT: Show that the gradients between pairs of points are equal.
SOLUTION

Let \( A(3, 8) \), \( B(5, 2) \), \( C(6, -1) \)

Step 1: Find \( m_{AB} \)

\( m_{AB} = \frac{2 - 8}{5 - 3} = \frac{-6}{2} = -3 \)

Step 2: Find \( m_{BC} \)

\( m_{BC} = \frac{-1 - 2}{6 - 5} = \frac{-3}{1} = -3 \)

Proof: Since \( m_{AB} = m_{BC} = -3 \), the three points are collinear. ✓

11
If \( A(-1, y) \), \( B(2, 3) \), and \( C(5, 6) \) are collinear, find \( y \).
HINT: Find the gradient from B to C first, then use it to find y.
SOLUTION

Step 1: Find \( m_{BC} \)

\( m_{BC} = \frac{6 - 3}{5 - 2} = \frac{3}{3} = 1 \)

Step 2: Set up equation

\( m_{AB} = m_{BC} \)

\( \frac{3 - y}{2 - (-1)} = 1 \)

\( \frac{3 - y}{3} = 1 \)

\( 3 - y = 3 \)

\( y = 0 \)

Answer: \( y = 0 \)

12
The points \( P(k, 2k) \), \( Q(3, 8) \), and \( R(4, 11) \) are collinear. Find the value of \( k \).
HINT: First find the gradient from Q to R, then use it with point P to find k.
SOLUTION

Step 1: Find \( m_{QR} \)

\( m_{QR} = \frac{11 - 8}{4 - 3} = \frac{3}{1} = 3 \)

Step 2: Set up equation

\( m_{PQ} = m_{QR} \)

\( \frac{8 - 2k}{3 - k} = 3 \)

\( 8 - 2k = 3(3 - k) \)

\( 8 - 2k = 9 - 3k \)

\( -2k + 3k = 9 - 8 \)

\( k = 1 \)

Answer: \( k = 1 \)
