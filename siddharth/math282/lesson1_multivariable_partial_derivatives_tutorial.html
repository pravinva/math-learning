Multivariable Functions and Partial Derivatives

Full walkthrough: from "what changes with what?" intuition to gradients, directional derivatives, tangent planes, chain rule, and optimization tests.

1) Why this topic exists

Single-variable calculus answers: "how does output change when one input changes?" In engineering, outputs usually depend on several inputs at once:

  • temperature in a plate: \(T(x,y)\)
  • pressure in a flow: \(p(x,y,z)\)
  • cost from multiple factors: \(C(q_1,q_2)\)
Key idea: partial derivatives are the controlled way to vary one variable while freezing the others. This is why they are central in optimization and modeling.

2) Build-up concepts (step by step)

2.1 Functions of two variables

A function \(f(x,y)\) maps a point in the \(xy\)-plane to a height \(z\). You can think of this as a surface in 3D. Level curves \(f(x,y)=k\) are contour lines on that surface.

2.2 Partial derivatives

\[ f_x=\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}, \quad f_y=\frac{\partial f}{\partial y} \]

\(f_x\) is slope in the \(x\)-direction (with \(y\) fixed). \(f_y\) is slope in the \(y\)-direction (with \(x\) fixed).

2.3 Gradient and direction

\[ \nabla f=(f_x,f_y) \]

The gradient points in the direction of fastest increase, and its magnitude gives steepest rate of increase.

3) Method routines (scaffold)

Routine A — compute partials reliably
  1. Circle the variable you are differentiating with respect to.
  2. Treat all other variables as constants.
  3. Differentiate term-by-term and simplify only at the end.
Routine B — tangent plane at \((a,b)\)
  1. Find \(f(a,b)\), \(f_x(a,b)\), \(f_y(a,b)\).
  2. Use linearization: \[ z=f(a,b)+f_x(a,b)(x-a)+f_y(a,b)(y-b) \]
Routine C — chain rule with parameter \(t\)

\[ z=f(x,y),\ x=x(t),\ y=y(t)\Rightarrow \frac{dz}{dt}=f_x\frac{dx}{dt}+f_y\frac{dy}{dt} \]

4) Guided examples (fully worked)

Example 1: partial derivatives without shortcuts

Find \(f_x,f_y\) for \(f(x,y)=x^2y+3xy^2-4y+7\).

  1. \(f_x=2xy+3y^2\) (because \(-4y\) and \(7\) are constants in \(x\)).
  2. \(f_y=x^2+6xy-4\).
Answer: \(f_x=2xy+3y^2,\quad f_y=x^2+6xy-4\).

Example 2: gradient and directional meaning

For \(f=x^2-3xy+4y^2\), find \(\nabla f(1,-1)\).

  1. \(f_x=2x-3y,\ f_y=-3x+8y\).
  2. At \((1,-1)\): \(f_x=5,\ f_y=-11\).
  3. \(\nabla f(1,-1)=(5,-11)\).
Interpretation: steepest increase points toward vector \((5,-11)\).

Example 3: tangent plane from first principles

Find tangent plane to \(z=x^2+xy+y^2\) at \((1,2)\).

  1. \(f(1,2)=7\).
  2. \(f_x=2x+y\Rightarrow f_x(1,2)=4\).
  3. \(f_y=x+2y\Rightarrow f_y(1,2)=5\).
  4. \(z=7+4(x-1)+5(y-2)\).

Example 4: chain rule with moving point

\(z=x^2y+y^3,\ x=t^2,\ y=2t-1\). Find \(dz/dt\).

  1. \(f_x=2xy,\ f_y=x^2+3y^2\).
  2. \(x'=2t,\ y'=2\).
  3. \(dz/dt=f_xx'+f_yy'=(2xy)(2t)+(x^2+3y^2)(2)\).
  4. Substitute \(x=t^2,\ y=2t-1\).

Example 5: implicit derivative in multivariable context

\(x^2+xy+y^2=9\). Find \(dy/dx\).

  1. Differentiate: \(2x+(x\,y'+y)+2y\,y'=0\).
  2. \((x+2y)y'=-(2x+y)\).
  3. \(y'=-(2x+y)/(x+2y)\).

5) Common mistakes and recovery checks

  • Mistake: differentiating \(y\) while finding \(f_x\). Recovery: write "treat \(y\) as constant" above the line.
  • Mistake: dropping evaluation point in tangent plane. Recovery: compute \(f(a,b), f_x(a,b), f_y(a,b)\) as a separate mini-table.
  • Mistake: introducing \(\nabla f\) without direction meaning. Recovery: always append "steepest increase direction".

6) Practice set (with progression)

A. Fundamentals

  1. Find \(f_x,f_y\) for \(f=x^3y+x\ln y\).
  2. Find \(f_{xx},f_{yy},f_{xy}\) for \(f=x^2y+y^3\).
  3. Find \(\nabla f(2,1)\) for \(f=x^2+2xy-y^2\).
  4. Find tangent plane to \(z=x^2-y^2+2x\) at \((1,2)\).

B. Applied

  1. If \(T(x,y)=100-2x^2-y^2\), find rate of change in direction \(\mathbf{u}=(3,4)/5\) at \((2,1)\).
  2. \(z=e^{xy}\), \(x=\cos t\), \(y=t^2\): find \(dz/dt\).
  3. Find \(dy/dx\) from \(x^2+y^2=25\).
  4. Use second derivative test to classify critical point of \(f=x^2+y^2\) at \((0,0)\).
