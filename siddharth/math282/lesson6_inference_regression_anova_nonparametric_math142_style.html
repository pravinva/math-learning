MATH282 · Week 11-13
Inference, Regression, ANOVA, Nonparametric Tests
Full decision pipeline: confidence intervals, hypothesis tests, model interpretation, and group-comparison strategy.
Why this matters: In practice, we infer from samples. This week turns data into defensible decisions with uncertainty and model-fit awareness.
PatternBuild it: estimate, test, model, compare▶

Core flow:

  1. Estimate unknown parameter with CI.
  2. Test claim with p-value decision.
  3. Model relation via regression and residual checks.
  4. Compare many means with ANOVA or nonparametric alternatives.
\[ \text{CI}=\hat\theta \pm (\text{critical})(\text{SE}) \]
GuidedGuided routine for hypothesis testing▶
  1. State \(H_0\) and \(H_1\) in context words first.
  2. Select statistic: z / t / F depending on setup.
  3. Compute p-value and compare with \(\alpha\).
  4. Conclude with practical interpretation (not just "reject").
  5. If ANOVA significant, run post-hoc pairwise analysis.
ExampleWorked examples▶
Example 1 — 95% CI for mean

\(\bar x=52,\sigma=10,n=64\Rightarrow SE=1.25\).

CI: \(52\pm1.96(1.25)=(49.55,54.45)\).

Example 2 — one-sample t-test

\(\bar x=101,s=6,n=25,\mu_0=100\Rightarrow t=0.833\) (df 24), p-value large, fail to reject \(H_0\).

Example 3 — regression interpretation

\(\hat y=4+1.8x,\ R^2=0.81\): slope means +1 in \(x\) predicts +1.8 in \(y\); model explains 81% variation.

Example 4 — ANOVA decision

\(F=5.9,\ p=0.008\): reject equal means, then perform post-hoc comparisons.

NoteCommon mistakes and recovery checks▶
Recovery 1
Never say "accept \(H_0\)"; use "fail to reject \(H_0\)".
Recovery 2
Large \(R^2\) does not prove causation; check design and assumptions.
Recovery 3
ANOVA significance only says "at least one differs"; it does not identify which pair.
