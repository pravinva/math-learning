Lesson 6 Tutorial · Weeks 11-13

Statistical Inference, Regression, ANOVA, Nonparametric Methods

End-to-end tutorial from confidence intervals and tests to linear modeling and multi-group comparison.

1) Why this topic exists

You never observe the full population in practice. Inference quantifies uncertainty in conclusions from samples; regression and ANOVA make model-based comparisons.

Decision lens: estimate (intervals), test (hypotheses), model (regression), compare groups (ANOVA/nonparametric).

2) Build-up concepts

2.1 Confidence intervals

\[ \text{CI} = \text{estimate} \pm (\text{critical value})(\text{SE}) \]

2.2 Hypothesis testing

State \(H_0,H_1\), compute test statistic, obtain p-value, compare with \(\alpha\), conclude in context.

2.3 Regression and ANOVA

  • Regression: relationship between predictor \(x\) and response \(y\).
  • ANOVA: test equality of means across 3+ groups.

3) Method scaffold

Routine A — CI for mean
  1. Pick z or t based on known/unknown \(\sigma\).
  2. Compute standard error.
  3. Build interval and interpret in words.
Routine B — hypothesis test
  1. Write \(H_0, H_1\).
  2. Compute statistic \(z/t/F\).
  3. Use p-value; do not mix with confidence-level language.
Routine C — regression/ANOVA
  1. Fit model, inspect residual behavior.
  2. Interpret slope and \(R^2\) cautiously.
  3. If ANOVA significant, follow with post-hoc comparisons.

4) Guided worked examples

Example 1: CI for mean (known \(\sigma\))

\(\bar x=52,\ \sigma=10,\ n=64\), 95% CI for \(\mu\).

  1. SE \(=10/\sqrt{64}=1.25\).
  2. Critical \(z=1.96\).
  3. CI \(=52\pm1.96(1.25)=52\pm2.45\Rightarrow(49.55,54.45)\).

Example 2: one-sample t-test

\(\bar x=101,\ s=6,\ n=25,\ H_0:\mu=100,\ H_1:\mu\ne100\).

  1. \(t=(101-100)/(6/\sqrt{25})=0.833\), df \(=24\).
  2. p-value large (\(>0.05\)); fail to reject \(H_0\).

Example 3: linear regression interpretation

Given \(\hat y=4+1.8x,\ R^2=0.81\).

  1. Slope: each +1 in \(x\) predicts +1.8 in \(y\).
  2. \(R^2=0.81\): 81% of variation explained by linear model.
  3. At \(x=5\), prediction is \(13\).

Example 4: ANOVA conclusion

ANOVA output: \(F=5.9,\ p=0.008\), \(\alpha=0.05\).

  1. Since \(p<0.05\), reject equal-means null.
  2. At least one group mean differs. Continue with post-hoc pairwise comparisons.

5) Common mistakes and fixes

  • Mistake: "accept \(H_0\)". Fix: say "fail to reject \(H_0\)".
  • Mistake: causal interpretation from correlation. Fix: state "association" unless causal design exists.
  • Mistake: ANOVA significance but no follow-up. Fix: add post-hoc testing step.
