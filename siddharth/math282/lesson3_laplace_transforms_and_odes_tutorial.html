Laplace Transforms and ODE Initial Value Problems

From transform basics to shift theorems, Heaviside and delta inputs, and complete IVP solving workflow.

1) Why Laplace is used

Differential equations are hard because derivatives are coupled with unknown functions. Laplace transform converts derivatives into algebra in \(s\)-space:

\[ \mathcal{L}\{y'\}=sY(s)-y(0), \quad \mathcal{L}\{y''\}=s^2Y(s)-sy(0)-y'(0) \]

Key advantage: initial conditions are inserted directly during transform, not at the end.

2) Build-up concepts

2.1 Definition

\[ \mathcal{L}\{f(t)\}=F(s)=\int_0^\infty e^{-st}f(t)\,dt \]

2.2 Common table entries

  • \(\mathcal{L}\{1\}=1/s\), \(\mathcal{L}\{t^n\}=n!/s^{n+1}\)
  • \(\mathcal{L}\{e^{at}\}=1/(s-a)\)
  • \(\mathcal{L}\{\sin bt\}=b/(s^2+b^2)\), \(\mathcal{L}\{\cos bt\}=s/(s^2+b^2)\)

2.3 Shift/input functions

  • \(\mathcal{L}\{u(t-a)g(t-a)\}=e^{-as}G(s)\)
  • \(\mathcal{L}\{\delta(t-a)\}=e^{-as}\)

3) Method scaffold

Routine — solve \(y\)-IVP via Laplace
  1. Transform each ODE term and include initial conditions immediately.
  2. Solve for \(Y(s)\).
  3. Decompose \(Y(s)\) (partial fractions if needed).
  4. Invert term-by-term to recover \(y(t)\).
  5. Quick check: verify \(y(0)\), and optionally plug back into ODE.

4) Guided worked examples

Example 1: first-order IVP

\(y'+3y=6,\ y(0)=1\)

  1. \(sY-1+3Y=6/s\Rightarrow (s+3)Y=(s+6)/s\).
  2. \(Y=(s+6)/(s(s+3))=2/s-1/(s+3)\).
  3. \(y(t)=2-e^{-3t}\).

Example 2: second-order IVP

\(y''+4y=8,\ y(0)=0,\ y'(0)=0\)

  1. \(s^2Y+4Y=8/s\Rightarrow Y=8/[s(s^2+4)]\).
  2. Partial fractions: \(Y=2/s-2s/(s^2+4)\).
  3. \(y(t)=2-2\cos 2t\).

Example 3: delayed ramp input

\(f(t)=u(t-2)(t-2)\). Find transform.

  1. Recognize shifted \(g(t)=t\) with \(G(s)=1/s^2\).
  2. \(F(s)=e^{-2s}/s^2\).

Example 4: impulse forcing

\(2y'+y=\delta(t-3),\ y(0)=0\)

  1. \((2s+1)Y=e^{-3s}\Rightarrow Y=e^{-3s}/(2s+1)\).
  2. Inverse with shift: \(y(t)=u(t-3)\frac12 e^{-(t-3)/2}\).

5) Common mistakes and fixes

  • Mistake: forgetting initial-condition terms in \(\mathcal{L}\{y'\}\), \(\mathcal{L}\{y''\}\). Fix: write transform identities before starting.
  • Mistake: wrong sign in shift theorem. Fix: memorize "delay in time = multiply by \(e^{-as}\)".
  • Mistake: inverse transform without decomposition. Fix: always simplify \(Y(s)\) first.
