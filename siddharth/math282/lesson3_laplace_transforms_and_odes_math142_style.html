MATH282 · Week 3-5
Laplace Transforms for Differential Equations
Deep tutorial: transform tables, IVP solving mechanics, shift theorems, Heaviside steps, and delta impulses.
Why this matters: Laplace converts calculus-heavy ODE solving into algebra, and naturally handles discontinuous and impulse inputs common in engineering systems.
PatternBuild it: derivatives become algebra in \(s\)-space▶
\[ \mathcal L\{y'\}=sY-y(0),\qquad \mathcal L\{y''\}=s^2Y-sy(0)-y'(0) \]

So an equation like \(y''+3y'+2y=f(t)\) becomes a linear equation in \(Y(s)\). Initial conditions are inserted immediately.

Shift inputs are encoded by exponentials:

\[ \mathcal L\{u(t-a)g(t-a)\}=e^{-as}G(s),\qquad \mathcal L\{\delta(t-a)\}=e^{-as} \]
GuidedGuided lesson routine (IVP algorithm)▶
  1. Transform every term and substitute initial values right away.
  2. Isolate \(Y(s)\) completely.
  3. Use partial fractions or shift identities.
  4. Inverse transform term-by-term.
  5. Final check: evaluate \(y(0)\), and sanity-check behavior.
ExampleWorked examples (with reasoning)▶
Example 1 — first-order IVP

\(y'+3y=6,\ y(0)=1\).

\((s+3)Y=(s+6)/s\Rightarrow Y=2/s-1/(s+3)\).

\[ y(t)=2-e^{-3t} \]
Example 2 — second-order IVP

\(y''+4y=8,\ y(0)=0,\ y'(0)=0\).

\(Y=8/[s(s^2+4)] = 2/s - 2s/(s^2+4)\).

\[ y(t)=2-2\cos(2t) \]
Example 3 — delayed ramp

\(f(t)=u(t-2)(t-2)\).

\(F(s)=e^{-2s}\cdot(1/s^2)=e^{-2s}/s^2\).

Example 4 — impulse forcing

\(2y'+y=\delta(t-3),\ y(0)=0\).

\((2s+1)Y=e^{-3s}\Rightarrow Y=e^{-3s}/(2s+1)\).

\[ y(t)=u(t-3)\frac12e^{-(t-3)/2} \]
NoteCommon mistakes and recovery checks▶
Recovery 1
Never write \(\mathcal L\{y''\}=s^2Y\) alone unless both initial terms are zero and explicitly checked.
Recovery 2
If you see \(e^{-as}\), expect a delayed \(u(t-a)\) in time domain.
Recovery 3
Before inverse Laplace, simplify \(Y(s)\). Raw unsimplified forms hide table matches.
